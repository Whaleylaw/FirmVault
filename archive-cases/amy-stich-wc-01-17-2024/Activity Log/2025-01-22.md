Good afternoon Amy,
    I hate to hear that you have been so sick and hope you are starting to feel better. I definitely understand your frustration with the physical therapy and trying to find a proper position somewhere. Unfortunately, there is not a 'checklist' so to speak for you reaching MMI. That is a medical decision that you are most welcome to discuss with your physician. Be honest with him about your work situation and if he is not comfortable with placing you at MMI, he may recommend a Home Based exercise program that will allow you to still treat (at your convenience, in your home) and only have to check in & measure progress on occasions. I understand the recovery process is daunting and lengthy, but it is in your best interest. However, please feel free to discuss this openly with him and make the best decision for you.
    Once you have reached MMI, we will schedule an appointment with an independent doctor to assess any impairment you may have. We will also request your physician for his assessment in the form of a treating narrative. Once those documents are received, we can begin the settlement portion of your claim. Please be aware that the Insurance company may schedule you a visit with their own independent doctor. We will help ensure that you receive plenty of notice for these appointments, but want you to be aware & can plan accordingly moving forward.
    Please try to attend as many therapy appointments as you can within your treatment plan. You are always welcome to discuss these appointments and possibly move your re-evaluation appointment directly with your provider. We just asked that you keep us posted as you have been. If you have any questions at all along the way, please do not hesitate to reach out.
