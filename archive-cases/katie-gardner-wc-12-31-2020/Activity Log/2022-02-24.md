    We have sent correspondence to the Adjuster, Mr. Foster, requesting a
valid denial. In his correspondence he only stated that the incident did
not "fall within the provisions of the Workers' Compensation Act" which is
extremely vague. There are 4 'provisions' to the WC Act of 1923. All of
which your claim incompasses. A) The worker must have sustained personal
injury. B) Personal Injury must have been caused by an accident (later
cumulative trauma & occupational disease were added) C) The accident must
have arisen out of and in the course of employment. D) The personal injury
caused must have resulted in either a total or partial disability for a
period exceeding 3 days or resulted in death.
     Based on the information you provided, this claim fits all 4 of these
provisions and should be accepted as compensable. If your WC Carrier is not
willing to change their position, we will file your claim with the Kentucky
Department of Workers' Claims to be adjudicated by an administrative law
judge. We will keep you posted as things progress.
     In preparation for filing your claim, please provide us with a
complete list of any and all employment you have had in the past 15 years
as well as a complete list of any and all physicians or medical providers
you have seen for the same 15 year period. If you have any questions,
please feel free to contact us directly to discuss them in further detail.
