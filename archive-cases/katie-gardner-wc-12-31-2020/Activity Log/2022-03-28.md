but our understanding is this herniation is new. Our client simply stated
that she had felt this type of pain before and the supervisor and/or
reporting party ran with that statement and did not afford Mrs. Gardner the
opportunity for further evaluation.
       The fact Mrs. Gardner did receive STD benefits is not any type of
indication that the injury was not work related, simply that workers'
compensation had been denied and that Mrs. Gardner did in fact suffer a
diabiliting injury. As we are sure you are aware, if Mrs. Gardner's prior
injury had not yielded an impairment rating or had not been disabling
within the prior 12 months; it is not considered 'pre-existing' or
uncompensable. Further, if there was a pre-existing injury with an ratable
impairment, the additional damage is still compensable. Yet, again, Mrs.
Gardner was not afforded the opportunity for further evaluation before her
claim was denied and blocked at the employer level.
       The fact Mrs. Gardner was placed off work from 01/03/2021 until
01/05/2021 initially and later placed off work again on or about
01/20/2021, were notices to her employer of her injuries. The fact that
Mrs. Gardner's immediate supervisor failed to report & blocked the claim
from being reported appropriately does not constitute a failure to notice,
further, Mrs. Gardner is well within the two year statute of limitations
for presenting her claim.

received.
