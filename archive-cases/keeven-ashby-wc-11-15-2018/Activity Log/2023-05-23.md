> In terms of back-due TTD, I have consistently held my hat on the plain
> language of the statute. KRS 342.0011(11)(a) defines Temporary Total
> Disability as: ?the condition of an employee who has not reached maximum
> medical improvement from an injury and has not reached a level of
> improvement that would permit a return to employment.?
>
> Thus, I have taken the position that if those two elements apply, the
> employer/insurer has no right to terminate TTD benefits. I have seen this
> when an individual misses a doctor?s appointment and they cut off TTDs, but
> the same concept applies here. I have seen no support or precedent from
> insurers/defense attorneys that anything other than the above elements has
> any bearing on TTD benefits. I would definitely make the argument that he
> is entitled to back-due TTDs with interest from May-January while he was
> not at MMI (clearly not ? he was waiting for surgery) and not able to
> return to work (kept off until the surgery). One thing that could really
> help in this regard would be a doctor?s record or note that says Keevan
> needs to wait X amount of time after quitting to undergo the surgery.
>
>
