Good afternoon Robert,

Thank you for your email and for extending the offer of $17,500.
After reviewing your email, I would like to address a few points regarding liability. While you noted that the hole was next to a "bright yellow parking stop," it is important to emphasize that the hole itself was not marked, identified, or warned of in any way. The parking stop may have been visible, but it did nothing to alert pedestrians to the dangerous condition created by the unmarked, unlit hole where Ms. Campbell fell.
Additionally, although the photos were taken during the daytime, this incident occurred in the evening, and the hole was both unlit and without warning signs or barriers. This further diminishes the argument that the hazard was "open and obvious." These factors point to Golden Corral’s duty to ensure the safety of its premises, particularly in areas near the entrance where foot traffic is expected.
Moreover, your reference to the hole being located in "the front portion of the space not typically used for walking" does not seem relevant. It is foreseeable that pedestrians will navigate various parts of a parking lot, especially near the entrance of a business. The duty of care owed by Golden Corral extends to all areas where pedestrians are likely to walk, and a reasonable person would not expect to encounter a hidden hazard in this location, particularly without any warnings or barriers in place.
Critically, Ms. Campbell sustained a significant broken bone as a result of this fall, which underscores the seriousness of the incident and its impact on her. Given the circumstances of the fall and the severity of her injuries, we believe a higher settlement offer is warranted.
However, in an effort to reach an amicable resolution in this case, we are prepared to decrease our demand to $70,000.00 for full and final resolution.
Thank you for your consideration in this regard and I hope to hear from you soon.
