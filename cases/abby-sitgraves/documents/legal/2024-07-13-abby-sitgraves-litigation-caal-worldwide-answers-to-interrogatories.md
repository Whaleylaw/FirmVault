Comes Defendant, Caal Worldwide, Inc. (hereinafter “Caal’’), by counsel, and for its Answers to Plaintiffs First Set of Interrogatories and Requests for Production of Documents, states as follows:

GENERAL OBJECTIONS

Caal makes the following general objections to the Interrogatories and Requests for Production of Documents, and the general objections are incorporated into each response to each specific Interrogatory and/or Request. The assertion of the same, similar or additional objections in the individual objections to the Interrogatories and/or Requests or the failure to assert any additional objections to any Request, does not waive any of Caal’s general objections. Caal’s

general objections are as follows:




Caal objects to the Interrogatories and Requests to the extent they require Caal to disclose information or material which is protected from disclosure by any privilege, including but not limited to the attorney-client privilege and the attorney work product doctrine. The responses set forth below exclude any reference to privileged information or material. Any inadvertent disclosure of such information or material is not intended to be and shall not be deemed a waiver of any applicable privilege or protection.

Caal objects to the Interrogatories and Requests to the extent they require Caal to disclose information that is proprietary, private, sensitive, or confidential. Such information will not intentionally be disclosed absent an appropriate confidentiality agreement and/or protective order (and/or appropriate court authorization).

Caal objects to the Interrogatories and Requests to the extent they seek information that is not relevant or reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of admissible evidence.

Caal objects to the Interrogatories and Requests to the extent that they are overly broad, unduly burdensome, cumulative or duplicative, or impose an unwarranted or extreme expense on Caal.

Caal objects to the Interrogatories and Requests to the extent they seek to impose obligations beyond the scope of permissible discovery under the Kentucky Rules of Civil Procedure.

Caal objects to the Interrogatories and Requests to the extent that they require Caal to produce documents that are not in its possession, custody or control, or are equally available to Plaintiffs.

Caal objects to the Interrogatories and Requests to the extent they are vague, ambiguous or

unintelligible.
