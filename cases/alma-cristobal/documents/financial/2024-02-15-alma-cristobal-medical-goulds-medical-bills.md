FAQ- Frequently Asked Questions
-What is EMS Billing?
EMS Billing is a service fee charged by the provider for any of the following: ambulance transportation to a hospital/facility,
calls for assistance, non-transports.
-Why am | being charged for this ambulance transport, calls for assistance or non/transport?
Your municipality or transport company chargesa fee for transport or medica! services you received. The initial
ambulance transport billing goes directly to your insurance provider, if available. If Medicare, Medicaid, or a private health
insurer does not pay the bill, the patient is billed. You may receive an Explanation of Benefits (EOB) from your insurance
provider. This is not a bill. Your insurance provider may not reimburse the township for the full fee. Any unpaid balance on
the bill after the claim has been processed by your insurance provider may be your responsibility.
-No Insurance, what can |do? Will my inability to pay effect my access to Emergency Medical Services?
If you call 911 for a medical emergency you will NOT be denied ambulance ormedical services based on your financial situation. If you
cannot pay your bill contact our office. Most municipalities and transport companies have hardship plans available.
