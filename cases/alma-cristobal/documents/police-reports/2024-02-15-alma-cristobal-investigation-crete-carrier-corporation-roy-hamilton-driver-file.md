DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION PHYSICAL POLICY

Company policy at Crete Carrier Corporation and its operating division Shaffer Trucking (“the Company”) requires that all Department of Transportation re-certification physicals be completed by a Company approved DOT physician at one of the Company’s terminal locations. The Company pays for Company driver re-certification physicals. Owner operator/lease driver re-certification physicals will be charged back to the owner of the tractor.

Drivers who have a Body Mass Index (BMI) of 35 kg/m2 or greater based on information as determined from the DOT physical completed by a Company approved DOT physician will be required to complete a sleep study, unless already diagnosed with Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA). Drivers diagnosed with OSA as a result of such sleep study and who are prescribed a PAP treatment device will be required to regularly transmit usage data and comply with the treatment

standards.

Drivers diagnosed with a OSA and using a PAP treatment device are often required by DOT physicians to submit a usage history showing sufficient usage of the PAP device prior to being qualified by the DOT physician. Therefore, prior to a recertification physical, any driver utilizing a PAP device will be required to have obtained a usage history for the DOT physician's review during the recertification physical. Usage history varies by DOT physician. Therefore, a driver should be prepared to supply a minimum history of 30 days, and in some cases, may be required to supply 90

or more days of history.

Drivers who are physically qualified to operate a commercial motor vehicle with a diagnosis of diabetes mellitus treated with insulin for control under 49 CFR 391.41 (b)(3) under must self-monitor blood glucose levels in accordance with the specific treatment plan prescribed by the driver’s treating clinician. Such levels must be measured with an electronic glucometer that stores all readings, that records the date and time of readings, and from which data can be electronically downloaded. Any driver with a diagnosis of diabetes mellitus treated with insulin for control must, as soon as reasonably practicable, report to the Company and the driver’s treating clinician any occurrence of a severe hypoglycemic episode. A severe hypoglycemic episode is one that requires the assistant of others, or results in loss of consciousness, seizure,

or coma.

Company policy at Crete Carrier Corporation and its operating division Shaffer Trucking (“the Company”) requires that all Department of Transportation re-certification physicals be completed by a Company approved DOT physician at one of the Company’s terminal locations. The Company pays for Company driver re-certification physicals. Owner operator/lease driver re-certification physicals will be charged back to the owner of the tractor.

Drivers who have a Body Mass Index (BMI) of 35 kg/m2 or greater based on information as determined from the DOT physical completed by a Company approved DOT physician will be required to complete a sleep study, unless already diagnosed with Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA). Drivers diagnosed with OSA as a result of such sleep study and who are prescribed a PAP treatment device will be required to regularly transmit usage data and comply with the treatment standards.

Drivers diagnosed with a OSA and using a PAP treatment device are often required by DOT physicians to submit a usage history showing sufficient usage of the PAP device prior to being qualified by the DOT physician. Therefore, prior to a recertification physical, any driver utilizing a PAP device will be required to have obtained a usage history for the DOT physician's review during the recertification physical. Usage history varies by DOT physician. Therefore, a driver should be prepared to supply a minimum history of 30 days, and in some cases, may be required to supply 90 or more days of history.

Drivers who are physically qualified to operate a commercial motor vehicle with a diagnosis of diabetes mellitus treated with insulin for control under 49 CFR 391.41 (b)(3) under must self-monitor blood glucose levels in accordance with the specific treatment plan prescribed by the driver’s treating clinician. Such levels must be measured with an electronic glucometer that stores all readings, that records the date and time of readings, and from which data can be electronically downloaded. Any driver with a diagnosis of diabetes mellitus treated with insulin for control must, as soon as reasonably practicable, report to the Company and the driver’s treating clinician any occurrence of a severe hypoglycemic episode. A severe hypoglycemic episode is one that requires the assistant of others, or results in loss of consciousness, seizure,

or coma.

DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION PHYSICAL POLICY

Company policy at Crete Carrier Corporation and its operating division Shaffer Trucking (“the Company”) requires that all Department of Transportation re-certification physicals be completed by a Company approved DOT physician at one of the Company’s terminal locations. The Company pays for Company driver re-certification physicals. Owner operator/lease driver re-certification physicals will be charged back to the owner of the tractor.

Drivers who have a Body Mass Index (BMI) of 35 kg/m2 or greater based on information as determined from the DOT physical completed by a Company approved DOT physician will be required to complete a sleep study, unless already diagnosed with Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA). Drivers diagnosed with OSA as a result of such sleep study and who are prescribed a PAP treatment device will be required to regularly transmit usage data and comply with the treatment standards.

Drivers diagnosed with a OSA and using a PAP treatment device are often required by DOT physicians to submit a usage history showing sufficient usage of the PAP device prior to being qualified by the DOT physician. Therefore, prior to a recertification physical, any driver utilizing a PAP device will be required to have obtained a usage history for the DOT physician's review during the recertification physical. Usage history varies by DOT physician. Therefore, a driver should be prepared to supply a minimum history of 30 days, and in some cases, may be required to supply 90 or more days of history.

Drivers who are physically qualified to operate a commercial motor vehicle with a diagnosis of diabetes mellitus treated with insulin for control under 49 CFR 391.41 (b)(3) under must self-monitor blood glucose levels in accordance with the specific treatment plan prescribed by the driver’s treating clinician. Such levels must be measured with an electronic glucometer that stores all readings, that records the date and time of readings, and from which data can be electronically downloaded. Any driver with a diagnosis of diabetes mellitus treated with insulin for control must, as soon as reasonably practicable, report to the Company and the driver’s treating clinician any occurrence of a severe hypoglycemic episode. A severe hypoglycemic episode is one that requires the assistant of others, or results in loss of consciousness, seizure, or coma.
