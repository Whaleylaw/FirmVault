GENERAL OBJECTIONS
1. The objections and statements set forth in this section apply to each of the 
interrogatories and requests for production propounded and are not necessarily repeated in 
response to each individual interrogatory or request. The assertion of the same, similar or 
additional objections and specific objections to an individual interrogatory or request, or the failure 
to assert any additional objection to an interrogatory or request, does not waive any of the 
objections set forth in this section or the following sections.
2. By providing information or documents in response to an interrogatory or request, 
Defendant does not concede that any such information is relevant, material or admissible in 
evidence. Defendant reserves any rights it may have to the use of such information or documents 
as evidence.
3. Defendant generally objects to the interrogatories and requests to the extent that 
they seek information or documents protected by the attorney-client privilege, the work product 
doctrine, or any other applicable privilege or rule of confidentiality provided by law. Nothing in 

these responses is intended as or shall in any way be deemed a waiver of any privilege or right of 
confidentiality. In response to each interrogatory and request, Defendant will not undertake to 
provide privileged or otherwise protected information or documents.
4. Defendant objects to each interrogatory and request to the extent that it calls for 
information or documents within the propounding party's knowledge or to which the propounding 
party has equal access, or which the propounding party already has or will have in its possession 
on the grounds that such interrogatories and requests are unduly burdensome, oppressive, 
harassing and vexatious and go beyond any legitimate need of the propounding party for discovery. 
5. Defendant objects to each interrogatory and request to the extent that it calls for 
information or documents that does not seek information that is admissible or reasonably 
calculated to lead to the discovery of admissible evidence and is not reasonably related to any 
claim or defense in this action on the grounds that such interrogatories and requests are unduly 
burdensome, oppressive, harassing and vexatious and go beyond any legitimate need of the 
propounding party for discovery. 
6. In responding to these interrogatories and requests, Defendant does not undertake 
any obligations beyond those required by the Kentucky Rules of Civil Procedure.
7. Defendant rejects the propounding party’s instructions and definitions in favor of 
the applicable rules of procedure and the commonly understood meanings of the terms utilized.
