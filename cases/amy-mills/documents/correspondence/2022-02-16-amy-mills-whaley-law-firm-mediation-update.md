Dear Amy: 
 We are writing to let you know that we have arranged a non-binding mediation hearing in an effort to resolve 
your claim. It is likely that we have explained this already, but mediation is a process by which the parties attempt to 
resolve a claim with the assistance of an impartial, third person. The third party, usually a retired judge or an attorney 
trained in mediation, points out the strengths and weaknesses of the claims made by both the Plaintiff and Defendant. 
Often, this hearing will result in a settlement of the Plaintiff's claim. Thus, it is possible that we will reach a settlement
of your claim at the mediation hearing. 
 No judgment is entered for either party. A successful mediation depends on the willingness of both the Plaintiff 
and Defendant to make concessions. Neither party will walk away totally happy, but by reaching a settlement at the 
mediation, both parties will have avoided the risks associated with a trial. 
Your mediation hearing is scheduled to take place on February 16, 2022 at 1:00 pm. The mediation hearing is 
to be held via Zoom and the Judge will provide the link to the meeting just prior to the mediation date. 
The parties split the cost of the hearing. Typically, the cost of settling a claim through mediation is about 10% 
of the cost of a trial. 
In the near future, we will be preparing a mediation statement to be forwarded to the mediator prior to the 
mediation hearing. We may be contacting you if we need your assistance preparing this document. Please plan to attend 
the mediation hearing at the date and place previously listed in this letter. You will need to contact our office thirty (30) 
minutes before the hearing is scheduled to begin to discuss any questions you may have.
