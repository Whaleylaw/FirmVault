Dear Amy Mills:

	As I told you during our initial meeting, attempts to settle your claim will begin with our delivery of a "proposed settlement" letter.  This letter will ask for a specific sum of money to compensate you for your injuries.  We will enclose copies of your medical records and bills to support your claim.  Gathering medical records/bills takes time, and can result in a delay in settling your claim.  This delay can be avoided if you request the medical records and bills from each medical care provider once you are done with treatment for that medical provider.  That way, when you are released from your final treatment, we will already have all your medical records and bills other than those from your last medical care provider.
	Please review the attached medical treatment inventory.  Section (I) summarizes the information in our files about the medical care providers from whom you have received treatment for your accident-related injuries.  
	If this list does not include all the medical providers from whom you received treatment for your accident-related injuries, add the omitted names and addresses in Section (II).  (One way of providing this information is to send us one bill from each provider.)  In addition, please supply information about the dates of your treatment with each provider.
We will need a complete total of all your injury-related costs.  To that end, please forward any receipts for costs you incurred as a result of your injury, such as prescriptions you were given during treatment.
	I have enclosed a self-addressed (postage paid) envelope for your convenience in returning the requested information to our office.
	If you have any questions regarding the information requested, I am available to speak with you about your case at any time.  If I am not available, my answering service will take your call.  I will return your call as soon as possible.  
	Thank you for your prompt attention to this matter.

							Sincerely,

							Aaron Whaley
							Whaley Law Firm
