DEFINITIONS
The following definitions are applicable to these Requests for Production of Documents:
(A) Identity/Identification/Identity of:
i) When used in reference to an individual person, it means his/her full name, present
or last known business affiliation and position, last known residential address, last known
telephone number, date of birth, and social security number.
ii) When used in reference to a corporation, firm or other entity, or sole proprietorship,
it means its full name, form of organization, last or present known address, and last or present
known telephone number.
iii) When used in reference to a document, it means the type of document (e.g., letter,
memorandum, contract, or any other similar document), its author or originator, the date it was
originated, its recipients, its present location by address and its custodian; and ifthe document was,

but no longer is, in your possession or subject to your control, state what disposition was made of
it.
iv) When used in reference to an oral communication, it means the full name(s) of all
of the participants to the oral communication, their business affiliation and business address at the
time, their present or last known position, business affiliation and residential address, the location
of each participant at the time the oral communication took place, which participants initiated the
oral communication and the date the oral communication took place.
(B) Oral Communication means any utterance heard by another person, whether in
person, by telephone or otherwise.
(C) Person includes an individual, corporation, partnership or any other entity.
(D) Document. The term “document” shall mean the original writings (handwritten,
typed, or otherwise produced) and any non-identical copy ofany written, printed, typed, electronic
data of any type whether created, received, or stored on any personal or business device, recorded
or graphic matter (whether different from the original because of notes made upon such copy or
otherwise), formal or informal, in the possession, custody or control of you, your agents,
representatives or attorneys known to exist by you, your agents, representatives or attorneys; and
photographic prints or negatives, microfilm, microfiche, or other photographic matter; sound
reproduction systems or tapes, and all letters, copies of letters, envelopes, memos, scratch sheets,
work sheets, notes, statements, checks, check stubs or other evidence of payment, insurance
policies, invoices, bills, orders, reports, summaries, investigation, opinions, (including expert
opinions), trade letters, books, comparisons, diaries, articles, booklets, manuals, brochures,
pamphlets, circulars, bulletins, and notices. Documents shall also include any physical or tangible
evidence within the possession or control of you, your agents, representatives or attorneys.

The term “document” includes any copy(ies) ofthe foregoing in which any mark, alteration
or additional writing or other change from theoriginal or from any other copy, has been made; and
it includes any and all documents in the possession of you or any of your agents, representatives,
or attorneys, or any other person acting on your behalf.
(E) Financial Statements. The term "Financial Statements" shall mean Balance Sheets,
Statements ofIncome, Statements of Shareholder's Equity, Statements of Cash Flow, and all other
like documents and reports prepared for and/or by any ofyou.
(F) Claim of Privilege. If any answer, information, or document is withheld under
claim of privilege or claim ofa similar kind or nature, furnish the reasons wherefore, including
cases, statutes or regulations upon which you rely in asserting said privilege in sufficient detail to
allow the Court to rule upon the claim of privilege.
