V. MOTION FOR CONTEMPT
AND TO IMPOSE SANCTIONS PURSUANT TO CR 37.02
FORCHT BANK DEFENDANT
***** ***** *****
Comes the Defendant, Forcht Bank, by and through counsel, and, and pursuant to CR 
37.02, and respectfully moves this Court to find the Plaintiff in contempt and impose sanctions 
against the Plaintiff for violating the Court’s December 17, 2024 order compelling the Plaintiff to 
answer discovery requests regarding her recent medical providers, treatment records and bills. In 
support of said Motion, Defendant states as follows:
A trial court has broad discretion in addressing violations of its orders regarding discovery. 
Turner v. Andrew, 413 S.W.3d 272, 279 (Ky. 2013). CR 37.02 provides, in part, that “If a party . 
. . fails to obey an order to provide or permit discovery . . .” the court may impose a number of 
sanctions, including but not limited to the exclusion of evidence, the striking of pleadings, and 
dismissal of the action. CR 37.02(2); Nowicke v. Cent. Bank & Tr. Co., 551 S.W.2d 809, 810 (Ky. 
App. 1977). Additionally, CR 37.02(3) provides that “the court shall require the party failing to 
obey the order or the attorney advising him or both to pay the reasonable expenses, including 
attorney’s fees, caused by the failure, unless the court finds that the failure was substantially 
justified or that other circumstances make an award of expenses unjust.” Id.
This matter arises out of a slip and fall that occurred on April 26, 2019 at Forcht Bank in 
Corbin, Kentucky. Plaintiff has continued to seek medical treatment for injuries she attributes to 
her fall, but recently she has refused to provide records and bills in support of her claims. This 

2
Defendant served discovery on the Plaintiff on August 2, 2024 requesting that she provide details 
regarding her medical providers, treatment records and bills from January 1, 2023 to the present 
she claims are related to her fall. See Defendant’s Notice of Service attached hereto as Exhibit 1. 
Defense Counsel emailed Plaintiff’s Counsel on September 16, 2024 as the discovery responses 
were past due and questioned as to when to expect the responses would be received. See email 
correspondence attached hereto as Exhibit 2. Counsel advised that Plaintiff had an MRI on 
September 11, 2024, but he did not have the records. Id. Defense Counsel emailed again on 
October 17, 2024 requesting updated treatment records for the Plaintiff. Plaintiff’s Counsel 
advised that he did not have any new records, however, stated that the MRI on her shoulder showed 
a new tear of her labrum allegedly at the suture of her previous surgery, and that Plaintiff was 
going through physical therapy. Id. On November 25, 2024, Defense Counsel again reached out 
to Plaintiff’s Counsel asking for updated treatment records as well as who Plaintiff was treating 
with so that those records could be requested. Id. Counsel advised that he did not have any records 
to provide, but stated that Plaintiff had completed physical therapy and was scheduled to have 
surgery on her shoulder on January 3, 2025. Id. Defense Counsel asked Plaintiff’s Counsel who 
Plaintiff’s providers were for her physical therapy and who was doing her surgery. Id. Plaintiff’s 
Counsel responded with the following:
I hope this email finds you well. I spoke to Amy and we can agree 
to provide you with all the information and responses to your 
interrogatories and request for production, including who she is 
treating with at the moment after January 3rd. January 3rd is the date 
of her upcoming shoulder surgery and she would like to wait until 
after that to reveal the doctor’s identity This doctor has said that he 
will not take her as a patient if she is in litigation and she doesn’t 
want her surgery canceled.

3
Id. Defense Counsel advised that we could not agree to hide the fact that she was in litigation from 
a medical provider who would be performing a surgery that she was intending to claim as related 
to her pending litigation. Id. The email exchange among counsel is attached in full as Exhibit 2.
As Plaintiff refused to identify her medical providers or provide any additional records or 
bills, Defense Counsel then moved the Court to Compel Plaintiff to answer the discovery 
propounded on August 2, 2024 requesting that she provide details regarding her medical providers, 
treatment records and bills from January 1, 2023 to the present she claims are related to her fall. 
The Court entered an Order on December 17, 2024 ordering the discovery be provided within 10 
days. See Order attached hereto as Exhibit 3. Plaintiff’s Counsel served responses to the discovery 
on December 26, 2024; however, the responses are deficient, and therefore, do not comply with 
this Court’s Order. See Plaintiff’s discovery responses attached hereto as Exhibit 4. They are not 
verified, they do not identify any new or recent medical providers or treatment of the Plaintiff, and 
no bills or treatment records were provided. Id.
The last date of medical treatment the Defendant has for the Plaintiff is January 6, 2024. 
Since that time, Plaintiff has willfully undermined and frustrated this Defendant’s discovery efforts
while at the same time she has deliberately misrepresented her circumstances to a medical provider 
(or providers) regarding her pending litigation in attempts to claim additional injuries and damages
and hold this Defendant responsible for same. See Exhibit 2. This is plainly supported by the 
record. See Exhibits 1-4. In light of the attached correspondence with Plaintiff’s Counsel and the 
discovery responses which were provided, it is clear that Plaintiff has not complied, and never 
intended to comply, with the Court’s December 17, 2024 Order. Our Civil Rules support that 
sanctions should be imposed where the record indicates a willful failure to comply with discovery 
procedures. The Court should not tolerate this intentional and egregious misconduct. Therefore, 

4
the Defendant requests that the Plaintiff be prevented from admitting any medical treatment 
records or bills or related evidence after January 6, 2024 in support her claims. Such a sanction is 
proper under the rules as CR 37.02(2)(b) provides that a trial court may refuse to admit a party’s 
evidence to support a claim if the party has failed to comply with a discovery order.
WHEREFORE, the Defendant, Forcht Bank, respectfully requests the imposition of 
sanctions pursuant to CR 37.02, including exclusion of any further medical treatment records, 
bills or related evidence after January 6, 2024, or as the Court deems appropriate against Plaintiff 
for her misconduct under these circumstances and willful disregard to comply with this Court’s 
December 17, 2024 Order.
