1.Information provided in these responses are based upon information that is currently reasonably available to the Plaintiff, and her counsel, and Plaintiff expressly reserves the right to supplement these responses as required by the Kentucky Rules of Civil Procedure. 

2.Plaintiff objects to the Defendants’ Interrogatories to the extent any of them request the disclosure of information protected by the attorney/client privilege, work product privilege, and/or any other applicable privileges recognized by the Rules of Civil Procedure and/or Kentucky case law. 

3.Plaintiff objects to the Defendants’ Interrogatories to the extent they seek: (1) information beyond the scope of the Civil Rules of Procedure; (2) discovery that is unduly burdensome, broad, oppressive, expensive, unreasonably cumulative, or duplicative; (3) information that is public information/record, and/or information easily obtainable from other sources that is more convenient, or just as readily accessible by the Defendants, as it is for the Plaintiff; (4) information that is not the possession of the Plaintiff and/or not in Plaintiff’s control; and (5) information/discovery that is directed to non-parties.

4.The Plaintiff objects to the Defendants’ Interrogatories on the grounds that they are vague and ambiguous. 

5.The Plaintiff objects to the Defendants’ Interrogatories to the extent they request information that is of a private and personal nature unrelated to this litigation, as such information is not relevant nor reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of relevant and/or admissible evidence. 

6.The Plaintiff objects to the Defendants’ Interrogatories to the extent any of them seek legal conclusions and/or assertions/interpretations of law. 

7.The Plaintiff adopts and incorporates by reference each and every foregoing objection to all her responses and answers to the Defendants’ Interrogatories as if fully set forth therein.
