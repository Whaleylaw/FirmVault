RE: Amy Mills v Forcht Bank – Knox County Circuit Court – Division II 
Dear Amy: 
 We are writing to let you know that we have arranged a non-binding mediation in an effort to resolve 
your claim. It is likely that we have explained this already, but mediation is a process by which the parties attempt 
to resolve a claim with the assistance of an impartial, third person. The third party, usually a retired judge or an 
attorney trained in mediation, points out the strengths and weaknesses of the claims made by both the Plaintiff 
and Defendant. Often, this mediation will result in a settlement of the Plaintiff's claim. Thus, it is possible that we 
will reach a settlement of your claim at the mediation. 
 No judgment is entered for either party. A successful mediation depends on the willingness of both the 
Plaintiff and Defendant to make concessions. Neither party will walk away totally happy, but, by reaching a 
settlement at the mediation, both parties will have avoided the risks associated with a trial. The parties split the 
cost of the hearing. Typically, the cost of settling a claim through mediation is about 10% of the cost of a trial. 
Your mediation is scheduled to take place on September 21, 2023 at 10:00 a.m. via a Zoom Meeting. The 
Zoom link and login information will be provided by the mediation services closer to the actual mediation date. 
 In the near future, we will be preparing a mediation statement to be forwarded to the mediator prior to 
the mediation. we may be contacting you if we need your assistance preparing this document. Please plan to 
attend the mediation on the date and time referenced above, and contact our office thirty (30) minutes before the 
mediation is scheduled to begin to discuss any questions you may have.
