GUIDELINES FOR VIDEO CONFERENCING
Mediations are confidential, involving only the intended participants, so be sure to be alone
in a secure room (no third parties present and make sure the conversation cannot be overheard by
any third party).
You will need a computer, tablet or cell phone with a camera, microphone and internet
connection. If you are using a laptop, tablet or cell phone PLEASE be sure to keep it plugged in so
you will have enough power to be present throughout the mediation.
Please plan to be available 10 minutes prior to the start of the mediation so that we can gather
together for an initial session and work through any technical challenges. I will send all participants
an email containing a link to connect to the mediation.
We will begin with the mediator’s opening statement, after which the mediator will ask that
you and your client sign the Mediation Agreement and Release, scan it and return it.
Provide all counsel and the mediator with a cell phone number.
After our opening session, you will be assigned to a breakout room: Plaintiff’s room (#1)
or Defendant’s room (#2). When you are assigned to your room, a window will pop up on your
screen, saying “join breakout room?.” The screen is not available for very long so please be sure to
be paying attention. If you don’t see it, hover on the menu at the bottom of the screen for “breakout
session” and click on it. The conversations in the breakout rooms are strictly confidential involving
only the people assigned to those rooms.
Recordings of mediation sessions are strictly prohibited.
During breakout sessions when the mediator is present, you and your client(s) are asked to
refrain from any other activities (any cell phone use or other distractions) as you would if we were
sitting around a table together.
