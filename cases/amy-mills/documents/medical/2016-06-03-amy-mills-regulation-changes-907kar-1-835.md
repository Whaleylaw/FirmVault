Additional Changes
 The MWMA portal must be used to apply for waiver services.
 MAP 115 must be completed and uploaded in MWMA.
 MAP 10 can only be completed by: a physician; an advanced practice registered nurse (APRN); a 
physician assistant (PA); or an intellectual disability professional (IDP).
 Minor home adaptation requests need a MAP 95 uploaded into MWMA for PA.
 Employees are required to have a criminal background check through the Kentucky Administrative Office 
of the Courts (or equivalent out-of-state agency), Caregiver Misconduct Registry Check and Central 
Registry Check. Submission to the Nurse Aide Abuse Registry is still required.
 Section 8 added - Person-Centered Service Plan requirements.
o Must be established for each participant in accordance with the participant’s wishes and rights, 
ensuring dignity and respect.
o Must be both entered into and updated in MWMA by the participant’s case manager.

 Section 9 added - Case Management requirements.
o Education and experience required to be documented.
o Must act in the best interest of the participant while ensuring dignity and respect.
o Must adhere to agency policies and procedures.
o Document services provided in MWMA, including:
 Monthly person-centered service plan
 Monthly summary
 Significant events such as hospital admittance, relocation, etc. 
o Conflict free case management shall be a scenario in which a provider including any subsidiary, 
partnership, not-for-profit, or for-profit business entity that has a business interest in the provider 
who renders case management to a participant shall not also provide another 1915(c) home and 
community based waiver service to that same participant unless the provider is the only willing 
and qualified Michelle P. waiver provider within thirty (30) miles of the participant’s residence.
 Exemptions to conflict free case management can be made.
 Incident reports are now divided into two classes: Incident and Critical Incident. 
o Incidents will be required to be submitted through MWMA. At this time, please continue 
reporting critical and non-critical incidents through the current method outside of MWMA until 
further notice.
o Abuse, Neglect, or Exploitation are automatically classified as Critical and must be entered 
immediately into MWMA.
o Other Critical Incidents must be entered within 8 hours.
o Deaths must be entered immediately as part of the Critical Incident process. Mortality reviews are 
due within 14 days – see Section 11 (6) for further details.
 Electronic signatures must meet requirements in KRS 369.101 to 369.120 only.
