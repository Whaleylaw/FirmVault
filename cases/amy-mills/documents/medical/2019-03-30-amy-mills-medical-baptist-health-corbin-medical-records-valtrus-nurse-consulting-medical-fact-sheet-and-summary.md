<u>**The Facts**</u>

# This case involves a now 39-year-old female, Ms. Amy Mills, who claimed to have slipped on a wet surface and fell striking her head on the hard floor at a bank on 04.26.2019. Ms. Mills stated that it was raining that day and the rain came in the door, hence, making the floor slippery. She also stated there were no rugs or 'wet floor' signs at entrance.

# 

# Ms. Mills has a past medical history of amblyopia, emmetropia, hypermetropia, astigmatism, headaches, and occipital neuralgia. She is a current every day smoker, history of alcohol use, and drinks 32 ounces of coffee every day. 

# 

# She immediately went to Baptist Health Corbin emergency department complaining of neck, left shoulder, right hand, back and left hip pain with an intensity of 8 to 10 out of 10 pain scale. The pain on her left shoulder radiated to her left hand. She also reported vomiting after incident.  Upon examination, she was found to have decreased range of motion (ROM) and tenderness on the left shoulder, cervical and back, decreased ROM on the left hip, and tenderness on the right hand. X-rays of the left shoulder, right hand, left hip, left humerus, lumbar spine, sacrum, coccyx, and left right ankle were negative for acute fracture or destructive bony abnormality. Computed tomography (CT) scan of the cervical spine was negative as well. She was given a shoulder immobilizer and prescribed Norco for the pain.

# 

# On 04.29.2019, Ms. Mills saw her family practitioner at Grace Community Health Center, Theresa Gross APRN, regarding her emergency room visit. She described the incident and added that when she hit her head, it felt like time lapsed and she had urinary incontinence at the time of fall.  She was anxious, in pain, and crying during the physical exam. She was found to have a limping gait, cervical spine ROM with moderate pain and with spasms; moderate pain with motion in lumbar spine, left hip, bilateral foot/ankle left shoulder, and left elbow; and right hand with swelling. It was recommended for her to start physical therapy in two weeks, as well as obtain repeat x-rays if her symptoms did not improve. Ms. Mills was prescribed new medications, Nabumetone and Robaxin. 

# 

# Ms. Mills returned to see Theresa on 05.09.2016 complaining of neck and shoulder pain, headaches, and being unable to hear out of her left ear. It was recommended for her to obtain MRIs of the right upper extremity, left shoulder, cervical spine, and brain. Tramadol was also prescribed to be added to her pain regimen. 

# 

# On 05.16.2019, Ms. Mills presented with worsening left parietal headache accompanied with nausea, vomiting, and photophobia, aggravated by bright lights and noise. She was wearing glasses during the physical examination due to the photophobia. She continued to have moderate pain with motion on her neck and bilateral shoulders. She reported not being able to work due to her symptoms.

# Legal Nurse Discussion, Opinion, and Recommendations for Brain Injury Litigation Based on the Medical Records Provided

# 

# Based on the review of medical records, it is my opinion that Ms. Mills may have suffered mild traumatic brain injury (mTBI). She has complained of mildly impaired short-term memory, confusion, headaches, fatigue, depression, anxiety, dizziness, and vision changes. It was also noted during Dr. Nazar’s evaluation that she may have suffered some loss of consciousness. 

# 

# Measurements of mild traumatic injury include loss of consciousness for less than 30 minutes or no loss of consciousness, memory loss less that lasted less than 24 hours and a Glasgow Coma Scale of 13 to 15 (National Institutes of Health). Though there is not sufficient documentation and assessment from providers about this claim of mTBI, further assessments by brain injury specialists and providers would agree with this writer’s opinion. 

# 

# I am surprised that Theresa Gross APRN and Elizabeth Norcutt APRN diagnosed her with Post Concussive Syndrome and not have managed her for mTBI. They were focused on her treating her migraines. 

# 

# Ms. Mills verbalized the want for a neuropsychological assessment with Theresa Gross APRN, but the provider did not acknowledge referring her to this specialist. She would also benefit from seeing a neuropsychiatrist, neuro-optometrist, and neurocognitive specialist. They may possibly order a 3T MRI with Diffusor Tensor Imaging (DTI) or Susceptibility-Weighted Imaging (SWI), Cognitive Behavioral Therapy and other treatments.

# 

# However, it can be argued that her headaches and vision changes have been an ongoing problem prior to the accident. But even though this is a comorbidity, it could have been exacerbated by the fall. Her 32 oz a day of drinking coffee can also be brought up as this may cause headaches.  Another issue I see is her inconsistencies with her stories and verbalization of symptoms with different providers, mainly with regards to her neck pain. She complained of neck pain after her ACDF surgery to her PCP but reported resolved neck pain to her neurosurgeon and orthopedic spine surgeon. This can be mistaken for malingering. But only a neuropsychologist can assess this.
