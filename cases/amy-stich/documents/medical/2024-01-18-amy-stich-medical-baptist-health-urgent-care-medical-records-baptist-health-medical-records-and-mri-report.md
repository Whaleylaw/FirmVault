Joint Pain
Joint pain may be caused by many things. Joint pain is likely to go away when you follow
instructions from your health care provider for relieving pain at home. However, joint pain can
also be caused by conditions that require more treatment. Common causes of joint pain include:
Bruising in the area of the joint.
Injury caused by repeating certain movements too many times.
Age-related joint wear and tear.
Buildup of uric acid crystals in the joint (gout).
Inflammation of the joint.
Other forms of arthritis.
Infections of the joint or of the bone.
Your health care provider may recommend that you take pain medicine or wear a supportive device like an elastic
bandage, sling, or splint. If your jointpain continues, you may need lab or imaging tests to diagnose the cause of your
joint pain.
Follow these instructions at home:
Managing pain, stiffness, and swelling
e If directed, put ice on the painful area. To do this:
o If you have a removable elastic bandage, sling, or splint, take it off as told by your
doctor. —— =<
o Putice in a plastic bag. an’ =
o Place a towel between your skin and the bag.
o Leave the ice on for 20 minutes, 2—3 times a day.
o Remove the ice if your skin turns bright red. This is very important. If you cannot
feel pain, heat, or cold, you have a greater risk of damage to the area.
e Move your fingers and toes often to reduce stiffness and swelling.
e Raise the injured area above the level of your heart while you are sitting or lying down.
If directed, apply heat to the painful area as often as told by your health care provider. Use the heat source that your
health care provider recommends, such as a moist heat pack or a heating pad.
e Place a towel between your skin and the heat source.
e Leave the heat on for 20-30 minutes.
e Remove the heat if your skin turns bright red. This is especially important if you are unable to feel pain, heat, or
cold. You have a greater risk of getting burned.
