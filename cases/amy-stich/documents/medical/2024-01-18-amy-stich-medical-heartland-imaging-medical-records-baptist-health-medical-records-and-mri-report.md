Joint Pain
Joint pain may be caused by many things. Joint pain is likely to go away when you follow
instructions from your health care provider for relieving pain at home. However, joint pain can
also be caused by conditions that require more treatment. Common causes of joint pain include:
Bruising in the area of the joint.
Injury caused by repeating certain movements too many times.
Age-related joint wear and tear.
Buildup of uric acid crystals in the joint (gout).
Inflammation of the joint.
Other forms of arthritis.
Infections of the joint or of the bone.
Your health care provider may recommend that you take pain medicine or wear a supportive device like an elastic
bandage, sling, or splint. If your jointpain continues, you may need lab or imaging tests to diagnose the cause of your
joint pain.
Follow these instructions at home:
