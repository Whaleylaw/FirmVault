Description of procedure: Patient taken to the operating room where after adequate general
anesthetic been obtained the left hand and arm were prepared and draped and placed ona
hand table. A sterile tourniquet was placed on the left upper arm the left arm was elevated for
exsanguination and after an intraoperative timeout the tourniquet inflated to 250. We now
placed about a 3 cm incision over the antecubital fossa about a fingerbreadth distal to the
flexor crease. Subcutaneous tissues were dissected and cautery was used for hemostasis the
basilic vein was retracted medially and the lateral antebrachial cutaneous nerve is well. We
now dissected down where we found the biceps tendon this was subsequently dissected
distally to its attachment will we did find there is a partial avulsion from the bicipital tuberosity.
The tendon was released and then tagged with 1.3 suture tape in a grasping fashion we
debrided the bicipital tuberosity and then drilled a guidepin we had measured the tendon to be
approximately 6-1/2 and then drilled a 7 unicortical tunnel into the bicipital tuberosity. We now
removed the bicortical guidepin and placing the biceps button onto the suture then placed this
through the socket beyond the far cortex until held its position. Wound was thoroughly
irrigated to remove all reaming prior tothis however. We now used flex the elbow and using a
tension slide technique pulled the bicep into the tunnel and the radius and knotted this we then
used a free needle to pass the suture back through the tendon and knotted this. This point the
wound was irrigated the tourniquet was deflated pressure was held for hemostasis we then
closed subcutaneous tissues with 2-0 Vicryl and the skin with a STRATAFIX cuticular. A sterile
dressing was applied patient placed into a controlled range of motion brace aroused from
anesthetic and sent to recovery room thank you
