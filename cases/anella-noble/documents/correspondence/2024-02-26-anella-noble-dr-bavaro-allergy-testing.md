Dear The Whaley Law Firm,

This letter is being sent to provide additional information on mandatory, federal reporting requirements and to request information from your client in order to comply with those requirements. Section III of the Medicare, Medicaid and SCHIP Extension Act of 2007 imposes on insurers mandatory reporting of Medicare beneficiaries who receive settlements, judgments, awards or other payment from liability insurance, no-fault insurance or workers' compensation. The reporting process limits the number of instances where the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) must take action to enforce the Medicare Lien. Providing the reporting information benefits all parties involved.

Medicare is a secondary payer of medical expenses incurred by Medicare Beneficiaries under the Medicare program. The Medicare Beneficiary, the Beneficiary's attorney and the primary insurer responsible for making payment of medical expenses incurred by the Beneficiary all responsible for reporting claim and payment information to CMS. The reporting allows CMS to coordinate payments so that it does not pay medical expenses when it is an insurer's responsibility. lf Medicare pays medical expenses covered by a primary insurer, this is a conditional payment that Medicare is entitled to recover from the Beneficiary, the Beneficiary’s attorney and the primary insurer if the primary insurer does not protect Medicare's interest as the Secondary Payer. The Medicare law imposes an automatic lien on all conditional payments. The Medicare Lien applies to all settlements, judgments, awards or other payments made by insurers to claimants who are Medicare Beneficiaries. The Medicare Lien can be enforced against the Medicare Beneficiary, the Beneficiary's lawyer and the insurer.
