January 5, 2021

Anella Noble
1900 South Floyd St. , Unit 521
Louisville, KY  40208

Dear Anella
	Since you probably do not know what to expect as I process your injury claim, I would like to acquaint you with the way I handle cases of this type.  In my experience, it is helpful if you know in advance what to expect as my staff and I process your claim.  Although my office represents many injured victims, my support staff is always available to assist you with any questions or concerns you have regarding your claim.
	Your actions during this period play an important part in the process.  For example, people outside of this office may contact you.  If anyone should attempt to contact you on behalf of the Defendant, such as an insurance adjuster, insurance investigator, attorney, or anyone else, do not talk to this person.  You should not communicate with any representative of the Defendant, nor should you communicate directly with any potential Defendant.  Please direct them to my office.  If they attempt to correspond with you in writing, please do not return any information to the Defendant or their insurance company.  Forward the written documents directly to my office.  I will communicate directly with the Defendants and their representatives as I work with you on your injury claim.

	Once you have been released from medical care, I will request all final information from the doctors.  This information will include copies of their files, x-rays, and a final statement regarding your condition or permanent injury.  Please let me know when you have been released from any of the physicians treating you.  It is imperative that I obtain medical records as soon as possible after you are released from medical treatment.
	Throughout your recovery, please keep all of your medical bills, prescription receipts, and all other receipts related to your injury.  Please provide those to me when you are released from a doctor’s care.  Additionally, if you are incurring lost wages, it is important to keep track of the time you miss from work.  Included in this tally is any time you miss from work while seeking medical attention.  It is important that you have an accurate idea of the approximate amount of total wages lost as a result of your injury.  I will formally request a lost wage verification statement from your employer once you have returned to work.  Please let us know when you return to work so we can send a lost wage verification form to your employer.
	Once you have been released from doctor’s care and we have gathered all of your medical records and lost wage information, we will send the Defendant's insurance company a proposed settlement letter.  Your case cannot move forward toward settlement until we have the medical records to support your claim.  While my staff and I do our best to gather the necessary medical records in a timely manner, we are often at the mercy of the medical provider.  In many cases, it takes four (4) to six (6) weeks to acquire the relevant medical records.  For this reason, we request the medical records as soon as we have the name, dates of treatment, and address of the medical provider who treated your injuries.  Do not wait until you have completed treatment with your last doctor to provide us with the names of previous doctors who treated your injuries.  It is very important that you provide us with not only the names and dates, but also the addresses of all medical providers who treat your injuries during your recovery.  Instead of calling us, we ask that you simply send us a note with the name, dates of treatment, and address of any medical provider who treats you for your injuries.  Please take time to do this throughout the period of your recovery.  One means of doing this is to send us a bill from each medical provider, but make sure it contains this information.  As long as you send us this information promptly, we can move your claim forward toward settlement as you recover from your injuries.
	It is important that you understand that no case is settled until the exact nature of your injuries and medical condition has been determined.  Keep in mind that it is impossible to evaluate your injury claim accurately until you have concluded your medical care and treatment.
	Please be assured that I am moving forward aggressively through the investigation and work up of your file in order to get the best results possible.  We will be able to evaluate your claim as soon as the investigation is completed and all necessary medical information obtained.  
Usually it takes four (4) to six (6) weeks from the date you are released from medical treatment.  We will then send a settlement demand to the defendant’s insurance company.  After that, negotiations will begin.  As with claim evaluation, this process occurs usually within four (4) to six (6) weeks from the date the settlement demand is sent.  We will attempt to arrive at a reasonable settlement of your claim through negotiation.  Your patience and cooperation are necessary as we work together during these steps toward settlement.
	From past experience, I have found that if I try to press for a settlement, the insurance companies interpret this action as an eagerness to settle on your part.  Given their interpretation, they feel that they can take a more stubborn position and attempt to settle your case for less than full value.  Experience has taught me that a patient attitude, confident negotiations and a firm demand is the best way to deal with insurance companies.  Once I have received a reasonable offer, I will contact you with the insurance company's offer and we will fashion a reply demand.  Hopefully, this negotiation process will lead to a settlement that adequately compensates you for your injuries.  We may wish to attempt to settle your claim through mediation or arbitration.  I will explain each of these procedures to you if it becomes necessary.
	In some cases, it becomes necessary to file a complaint against the responsible party.  Usually this is not required, but if it becomes necessary, we will explain the litigation process to you at that time.
	One of the most difficult requests I make of my clients is that they remain patient.  I know that this whole experience can be very traumatic and difficult, and it is hard to wait for it to end.  Keep in mind that, however lengthy the process may be, the goal at all times is to provide you with fair compensation for your injury.
	I hope this letter has offered some insight into what to expect as we pursue your injury claim.  We are all working hard to serve you to the best or our ability.  It is my goal to make your experience with this office a pleasant one.  Every client is important to this firm.
	I am available to speak with you about your case at any time.  If I am not available, I will return your call as soon as possible.  I look forward to working with you throughout this process.
							Sincerely,
