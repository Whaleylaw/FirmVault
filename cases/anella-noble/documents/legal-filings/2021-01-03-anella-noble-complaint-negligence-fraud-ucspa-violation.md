Comes the Plaintiff, Anella Noble, by Counsel, and for her Complaint against the abovenamed Defendant, states as follows:
COUNT ONE: NEGLIGENCE
1. Plaintiff, Anella Noble, at all times herein referred to, was a resident of Jefferson 
County, Louisville, Kentucky and involved in a motor vehicle collision in Jefferson County, 
Louisville, Kentucky.
2. Defendant, Virginia P. Sewell, at all times herein referred to, was a resident of Jefferson

County, Louisville, Kentucky and the registered owner of a vehicle that struck the vehicle in 
which Plaintiff was a passenger.
3. Defendant, State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance Company, had an insurance policy with 
coverage in effect covering the vehicle which struck the vehicle in which the Plaintiff was a passenger. 
4. Defendant, Unknown Driver, was operating the vehicle that struck the vehicle in which Plaintiff 
was a passenger, he fled from the scene immediately following the collision. 
5. On or about January 3, 2021 in Jefferson County, Kentucky, the Defendant, Unknown 
Driver, operated a vehicle protected with a policy of insurance coverage issued by Defendant, 
State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance Company, in a negligent manner causing the vehicle
he was operating to strike the vehicle in which Plaintiff Anella Noble was a passenger. As a 
direct and proximate result of the negligence of the Defendants, the Plaintiff sustained serious 
injuries, medical damages; and was caused to suffer great pain, both mental and physical; 
damage in an amount which exceeds the jurisdictional minimum of this court.
6. Pursuant to KRS 304.39-062(b), threshold requirements have been met by the Plaintiff 
in that Plaintiff have incurred over $1,000.00 in medical expenses and sustained serious injuries.
COUNT TWO: FRAUD
7. The plaintiff incorporates the claims above as if fully restated here.
8. That Defendant, Virginia P. Sewell, engaged in fraud in an attempt to avoid insurance 
liability to Plaintiff.
9. That Defendant, Virginia P. Sewell, knowingly provided false material representations 
regarding the whereabouts and driver of the vehicle is said collision.
10. That said false material representations led her insurance company to wrongly deny 
coverage to Plaintiff.
11. That the Plaintiff had no knowledge of the true identity and said fraud caused consequent

12. That Defendant should be subject to significant punitive damages over and above its

willful and reckless.
COUNT THREE UCSPA VIOLATION
13. The plaintiff incorporates the claims above as if fully restated here.
14. The Defendant, Virginia P. Sewell, was insured by a policy of liability insurance with 
State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance on the date of the accident.
15. That Defendant, State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance Company, having made 
minimal contacts with the State of Kentucky, herein is subject to the Unfair Claims Settlement 
Practices Act.
16. That Defendant, State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance Company, has failed to fairly 
and properly investigate this claim and has wrongly denied coverage with no basis and solely 
upon the word of its insured.
17. That Defendant, State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance Company, has violated UCSPA 
pursuant to KRS 304.12-230 and other applicable law in that it did not:
a. Pay this valid claim after conducting a reasonable investigation; and,
b. Affirm coverage of this claim within a reasonable time after the proof of loss was 
submitted to them.
18. That this Plaintiff has incurred and will continue to incur loss or damage as a result of 
the Defendant, State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance Company’s, violation of the UCSPA 
and such loss has caused and will continue to cause Plaintiff additional consequential damages, 
including, without limitation, the cost of attorney fees and other expenses in order to prosecute
