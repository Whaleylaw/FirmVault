FACTS
Plaintiff, Anella Noble, claims that she was a passenger in a motor 
vehicle which was involved in a motor vehicle collision on January 3, 2021 
with a vehicle owned by Defendant, Virginia P. Sewell. (Plaintiff’s Complaint, 
¶2) Plaintiff alleges that the vehicle was operated by an unknown individual, 
identified in the pleading as “unknown driver.” (Plaintiff’s Complaint ¶5) There
is no allegation that Virginia Sewell was operating the vehicle which she owned 
at the time of the subject collision. 
The Complaint alleges that Ms. Sewell “knowingly provided false material 
representations regarding the whereabouts and driver of the vehicle is (sic) said 
collision.” (Plaintiff’s Complaint, ¶9). As a result of the purported

LEGAL AUTHORITY
Plaintiff has failed to state a cognizable legal claim against Virginia 
Sewell upon which relief can be granted. As such, the claim against Ms. Sewell 
should be dismissed pursuant to CR 12.02(f). 
i. VIRGINIA SEWELL’S OWNERSHIP OF THE VEHICLE DOES 
 NOT CREATE A CAUSE OF ACTION AGAINST HER.
Plaintiff alleges that Ms. Sewell owned the vehicle involved in an accident 
on January 3, 2021. However, it is long established in Kentucky that mere 
ownership of an automobile is insufficient to impose liability upon its owner for 
an accident. Farmer v. Stidham, Ky., 439 S.W.2d 71 (1969). As such, Ms. 
Sewell’s ownership, as alleged in numerical paragraph 2 of Plaintiff’s 
Complaint, does not give rise to a viable cause of action.
ii. PLAINTIFF HAS FAILED TO PLEAD ACTIONABLE FRAUD.
Kentucky Civil Rule 9.02 requires “In all averments of fraud or mistake, 
the circumstances constituting fraud or mistake shall be stated with 
particularity.” While minute detail is not required, Plaintiff must plead “the 
time, the place, the substance of the false representations, the facts 
misrepresented and the identification of what was obtained by the fraud.” Scott 
v. Farmers State Bank, Ky. 410 S.W.2d 717, 722 (1966); see also Keeton v. 
Lexington Truck Sales, Inc., Ky.App., 275 S.W.3d 723 (2008). No information 
has been set forth in the pleading regarding the alleged time of the 
misrepresentation, the place of the misrepresentation or what she purportedly

gained by the alleged misrepresentation. The only arguably substantive 
representation is a vague suggestion that she made a misrepresentation
regarding the “whereabouts and driver of the vehicle . . .” (Plaintiff’s
Complaint, ¶9). The pleading insufficiently states a claim for actionable fraud.
In Kentucky, a claim for fraudulent misrepresentation -- which Plaintiff 
attempts to assert requires the following elements:
1. The Defendant made a material representation to the Plaintiff;
2. The representation was false;
3. The Defendant knew the representation to be false or made it with 
reckless disregard for its truth or falsity;
4. The Defendant intended to induce the Plaintiff to act upon the 
misrepresentation;
5. The Plaintiff reasonably relied upon the misrepresentation;
6. The misrepresentation caused injury to the Plaintiff.
Bisig v. Time Warner Cable, 940 F.3d 205, 213 (2019) citing Giddings & Lewis 
Inc. v. Indus. Risk Insures. 348 S.W.3d 729, 747 (2011). See also Flegles, Inc. 
v. TruServ Corp. Ky., 289 S.W.3d 544 (2009).
Plaintiff does not allege that she has had any contact with Defendant 
Sewell. There is no suggestion that Plaintiff took any action or relied upon a 
statement made to her by Defendant Sewell. Instead, it is suggested that a
representation led a third party (her insurance company) to take action.

CONCLUSION
Pursuant to Kentucky Civil Rules 9.02 and 12.02 (f), Plaintiff has failed 
to state a claim against Defendant, Virginia Sewell, upon which relief can be 
granted. Plaintiff has failed to plead the time, place or benefit to her of the 
purported misrepresentation. Further, Plaintiff’s Complaint demonstrates that
there has been no contact between Plaintiff and defendant Sewell. In the 
absence of some misrepresentation by Defendant, to Plaintiff upon which 
Plaintiff relied to her detriment, the pleading fails to state a claim upon which 
relief may be granted pursuant to CR 12.02 (f) and fails as a matter of law. As 
such, Defendant respectfully requests that the Court enter the attached Order 
dismissing the claims asserted against her.
