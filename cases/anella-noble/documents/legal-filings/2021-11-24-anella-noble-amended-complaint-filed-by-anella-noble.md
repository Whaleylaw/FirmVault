NO. 21-CI-004985 JEFFERSON CIRCUIT COURT 
DIVISION: FIVE (5) 
JUDGE: MARY SHAW
Electronically filed 
ANELLA NOBLE PLAINTIFF 
v. 
VIRGINIA P. SEWELL 
STATE FARM MUTUAL AUTOMOBILE INSURANCE COMPANY
UNKNOWN DRIVER DEFENDANTS 
AMENDED COMPLAINT 
Comes the Plaintiff, Anella Noble, by counsel, and for her Amended Complaint, 
hereby states as follows: 
1. The Plaintiff adopts, reiterates, and incorporates by reference, as though fully set 
out herein, each and every allegation of her original Complaint including numerical 
paragraphs 1 – 18 of the Original Complaint. 
2. In addition to the allegations in the Original Complaint, Plaintiff further states that 
Defendant Sewell’s conduct as related in the police report was intended to deceive 
Plaintiff and conceal the identity of the negligent driver: 
‘Unit 1 Operator [unknown negligent driver] was not on scene when the 
police arrived. Officers located a photo of the suspected male on social 
media of a male described as the subject that fled. The subject was 
identified … as an individual known to Unit 1 vehicle owner [Virginia 
Sewell]. Officers found out that [Sewell] then made a stolen vehicle 
report for her vehicle while officers were on scene working the accident. 
Officers called [Sewell] and she stated she spoke to her boyfriend prior 

to filing the report for the stolen vehicle. 
There was no damage to the vehicle’s ignition and it being a push start 
would not allow the vehicle to start without a key. Officers spoke with 
[Sewell] and she stated she only had one set of keys. Then she changed 
her statement when confronted about the ignition. She then stated she 
had a spare but has not been able to locate that key for a while.’ 
3. In addition to the claims in the Original Complaint, at all relevant times, Virginia 
Sewell was the owner of the vehicle involved in the January 3, 2021 wreck that 
negligently collided with the vehicle occupied by Plaintiff. 
4. Defendant Sewell, negligently and carelessly entrusted said vehicle to the unknown 
driver defendant whom Defendant Sewell knew or should have known would 
operate the vehicle in a careless manner. 
5. Defendant Sewell’s negligent entrustment of the vehicle was a substantial factor in 
causing Plaintiff’s injuries as alleged in the Original Complaint. 
WHEREFORE, Plaintiff demands as follows: 
1. Judgement against the Defendants, jointly and individually, in an amount which 
will fairly and reasonably compensate the Plaintiff for her damages alleged 
herein; 
2. Trial by Jury; 
3. Plaintiff’s costs herein expended; and 
4. Any and all other relief which the Plaintiff may appear to be entitled, including 
the right to further amend this Complaint, if necessary.
