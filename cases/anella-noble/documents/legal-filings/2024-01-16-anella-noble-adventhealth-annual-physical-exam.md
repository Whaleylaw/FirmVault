This action comes before the Court on a motion to dismiss under CR 12.03 brought by Defendant Virginia P. Sewell as regards the claim of fraudulent misrepresentation made against her by Plaintiff Anella Noble.' After careful consideration of the record and the memoranda of the parties, as well as the applicable case, statutory, and procedural law and being otherwise sufficiently advised, the Court SUSTAINS the motion with further instruction.

FACTS’

This action arises out of an automobile accident wherein Ms. Noble alleges she sustained injury after the vehicle she occupied was rear-ended by a vehicle operated by an unknown male who fled the scene immediately after the collision. Ms. Sewell owned the vehicle driven by the unknown male. She spoke with law enforcement officers and gave statements Ms. Noble

contends were intended to prevent her from discovering the identity of the driver,

' Ms. Sewell filed her motion under CR 12.02(f), and Ms. Noble correctly notes that since she filed an answer the motion should be reviewed under the standard applicable to CR 12.03.

* The Court incorporates by reference the facts alleged in Ms. Noble’s complaint for the purpose of analyzing them under the applicable standard of review.




Approximately eight months after the incident, Ms. Noble filed an action against the Defendants, alleging fraudulent misrepresentation against Ms. Sewell.

Ms. Sewell filed the immediate motion arguing (1) the fact alleged in the complaint cannot amount to fraud because she never made any representations to Ms. Noble and (2) the allegation is deficient under the stringent requirements of CR 9.02. Ms. Noble responded that her complaint should not be dismissed as against Ms. Sewell because, at the time of her brief’s submission, there was a motion to file an amended complaint alleging negligent entrustment against Ms. Sewell and CR 9.02 requires only that a defendant be put on reasonable notice of the substance of her claim, which she claims it does. The issues have been fully briefed and the motion stands ready for a ruling.

ANALYSIS

CR 12.03 provides that “[a]fter the pleadings are closed but within such time as not to delay the trial, any party may move for judgment on the pleadings.” The Kentucky Supreme Court explained the standard of review as follows:

The purpose of the rule is to expedite the termination of a controversy where the

ultimate and controlling facts are not in dispute. It is designed to provide a

method of disposing of cases where the allegations of the pleadings are admitted

and only a question of law is to be decided. The procedure is not intended to delay

the trial in any respect, but is to be determined before the trial begins. The basis of

the motion is to test the legal sufficiency of a claim or defense in view of all the

adverse pleadings. When a party moves for a judgment on the pleadings, he

admits for the purposes of his motion not only the truth of all his adversary's well-

pleaded allegations of fact and fair inferences therefrom, but also the untruth of all

his own allegations which have been denied by his adversary. The judgment

should be granted if it appears beyond doubt that the nonmoving party cannot

prove any set of facts that would entitle him/her to relief.

City of Pioneer Village v. Bullitt County ex rel. Bullitt Fiscal Court, 104 S.W.3d 757, 759 (Ky.

2003).




The Kentucky Supreme Court summarizes the elements of fraudulent misrepresentation as follows:

Fraud through misrepresentation requires proof that: (1) the defendant made a

material representation to the plaintiff; (2) the representation was false; (3) the

defendant knew the representation to be false or made it with reckless disregard

for its truth or falsity; (4) the defendant intended to induce the plaintiff to act upon

the misrepresentation; (5) the plaintiff reasonably relied upon the

misrepresentation; and (6) the misrepresentation caused injury to the plaintiff.

Giddings & Lewis, Inc. v. Industrial Risk Insurers, 348 $.W.3d 729, 747 (Ky. 2011) (citing Flegles, Inc. v. TruServ Corp., 289 8.W.3d 544, 549 (Ky. 2009) and United Parcel Service Co. y. Rickert, 996 S.W.2d 464 (Ky. 1999). Here, Ms. Noble does not allege any misrepresentation Ms. Sewell made to her. Instead, she alleges Ms. Noble was not forthcoming when questioned about the driver by a law enforcement officer. Accordingly, Ms. Noble could not prevail on her claim of fraudulent misrepresentation even if she proved the facts as alleged in her complaint.* This claim must be dismissed with prejudice.

Despite this, the Court is disinclined to dismiss the complaint against Ms. Sewell at this juncture because Ms. Noble recently moved for leave to file an amended complaint that would add a claim for negligent entrustment. Ms. Noble failed to appear during motion hour on October 25, 2021, as noticed, but she may refile the motion by the end of the calendar year if she chooses. If Ms. Noble fails to move for leave to file an amended complaint with new claims

against Ms. Sewell by then or the Court overrules such a motion, then Ms. Sewell may file a

motion to dismiss consistent with this ruling.
