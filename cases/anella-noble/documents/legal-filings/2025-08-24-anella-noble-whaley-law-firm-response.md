KREKEKEKEKERER

Comes the Defendant, Virginia P. Sewell, by counsel, and submits her Reply in support of her Motion to Dismiss the claims asserted against her by Plaintiff, Anella Noble:

Plaintiff claims that Defendant Sewell’s motion should have been captioned as a Motion for Judgment on the Pleadings pursuant to CR 12.03, rather than as a Motion to Dismiss. While undersigned counsel disagrees with this position, it is a distinction without a difference. The fact remains that the claim set forth in the Complaint against defendant, Virginia Sewell, fails to state a claim upon which relief may be granted pursuant to CR 12.02(f) and should be dismissed.

The only claim asserted in the Complaint is one of “Fraud.” Kentucky Civil Rule 9.02 requires “In all averments of fraud or mistake, the

circumstances constituting fraud or mistake shall be stated with particularity.”

A claim for fraudulent misrepresentation -- which Plaintiff attempts to

assert requires the following elements:

1. The Defendant made a material representation to the Plaintiff; 2. The representation was false; 3. The Defendant knew the representation to be false or made it with

reckless disregard for its truth or falsity;

4. The Defendant intended to induce the Plaintiff to act upon the

misrepresentation; 5. The Plaintiff reasonably relied upon the misrepresentation; 6. The misrepresentation caused injury to the Plaintiff.

Bisig v. Time Warner Cable, 940 F.3d 205, 213 (2019) citing Giddings & Lewis

Inc. v. Indus. Risk Insures. 348 S.W.3d 729, 747 (2011). See also Flegles, Inc. v. TruServ Corp. Ky., 289 S.W.3d 544 (2009).

Plaintiff does not allege that she has had any contact with Defendant Sewell. There is no suggestion that Plaintiff took any action or relied upon any statement made to her by Defendant Sewell to her detriment. Instead, it is suggested that a representation led a third party (her insurance company) to take action. There is no legal authority from which to infer that this creates a cause of action for Plaintiff.

Plaintiff attaches to her Responsive Brief a copy of a police report (which is unequivocally inadmissible) which suggests that Ms. Sewell advised the questioning officer that she only had a single set of keys to the vehicle and then

later clarified that had previously been in possession of a second set which had

been lost. This does not cure the deficiency and create actionable fraud which can be asserted against Virginia Sewell by Anella Noble.

In the absence of some misrepresentation by Defendant, to Plaintiff upon which Plaintiff relied to her detriment, the pleading fails to state a claim upon which relief may be granted pursuant to CR 12.02 (f) and fails as a matter of law. No such representation/misrepresentation has been raised in either the Complaint or Plaintiff's responsive memorandum. As such, Defendant

respectfully requests that the Court dismiss the claims asserted against her.
