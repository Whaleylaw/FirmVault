Comes the Plaintiff, Anella Noble, by counsel, and moves the Court pursuant to CR 15.01 for leave to file her Amended Complaint. The Amended Complaint' adds additional detail and sets forth a common-law negligent entrustment claim against the defendant, Virginia Sewell, who owned the vehicle that was negligently driven by the unknown driver in this case. The wreck occurred on January 8, 2021 and suit was filed only months later, on August 30, 2021. Accordingly, there is no prejudice to any party as no discovery has been conducted and Plaintiff is well within the two-year limitations period applicable to automobile accident cases. Negligent

entrustment has been recognized in Kentucky since at least 1931. See Brady v. B&B Ice Co., 242

' Attached as Exhibit 1.




Ky. 138, 45 S.W.2d 1051 (Ky. 1931). The common-law theory of negligent entrustment is actionable against a vehicle owner who entrusts her vehicle to another whom she knows to be inexperienced, careless, or reckless, and the vehicle owner is liable for the natural and probable consequences of the entrustment. McGrew v. Stone, 998 S.W.2d 5 (Ky. 1999).

Electronically filed ANELLA NOBLE PLAINTIFF V. VIRGINIA SEWELL; STATE FARM MUTUAL AUTOMBILE INSURANCE COMPANY; UNKNOWN DRIVER DEFENDANTS AMENDED COMPLAINT

Comes the Plaintiff, Anella Noble, by counsel, and for her amended complaint hereby states as follows:

1. The Plaintiff adopts, reiterates, and incorporates by reference, as though fully set out herein, each and every allegation of her original Complaint including numerical paragraphs 1-18 of the Original Complaint.

2. In addition to the allegations in the Original Complaint, Plaintiff further states that Defendant Sewell’s conduct as related in the police report was intended to deceive Plaintiff and conceal the identity of the negligent driver:

Unit 1 operator [unknown negligent driver] was not on seen when police arrived. Officers located a photo of suspected male on social media of a male described as the subject that fled. The subject was identified ... as an individual known to Unit 1 vehicle owner [Virginia Sewell]. Officers found out that [Sewell] then made a stolen vehicle report for her vehicle while officers were on scene working the accident. Officers called [Sewell] and she stated she spoke with her

boyfriend prior to filing the report for the stolen vehicle.

There was no damage to the vehicle’s ignition and it being a push start would not allow the vehicle to start without a key. Officers spoke with [Sewell] and she stated she only has one set of keys. Then she changed her statement when confronted about the ignition. She then stated she had a spare but has not been able to locate that key for a while.




3. In addition to the claims in the Original Complaint, at all relevant times, Virginia Sewell was the owner of the vehicle involved in the January 3, 2021 wreck that negligently collided with the vehicle occupied by Plaintiff.

4. Defendant Sewell negligently and carelessly entrusted said vehicle to the unknown driver defendant whom Defendant Sewell knew or should have known would operate the vehicle in a careless manner.

5. Defendant Sewell’s negligent entrustment of the vehicle was a substantial factor in causing Plaintiff's injuries as alleged in the Original Complaint.

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff demand as follows:

1. Judgment against the Defendants, jointly and individually, in an amount which will fairly and reasonably compensate the Plaintiff for his damages alleged herein;

2. Trial by jury;

3. Plaintiff’s costs herein expended; and

4. Any and all other relief which the Plaintiff may appear to be entitled, including the right to further amend this Complaint, if necessary.
