AMENDED PERSONAL INJURY COMPLAINT 
NEGLIGENCE, FRAUD, NEGLIGENT ENTRUSTMENT 
Comes the Plaintiff, Anella Noble, by Counsel, and for their Complaint against the abovenamed Defendants, states as follows: 
COUNT ONE: NEGLIGENCE 
1. Plaintiff, Anella Noble, at all times herein referred to, were residents of Jefferson County, 
Louisville, Kentucky and involved in a motor vehicle collision in Jefferson County, 
Louisville, Kentucky. 
2. Defendant, Virginia Sewell, at all times herein referred to, was a resident of Jefferson County, 
Louisville, Kentucky and the registered owner of a vehicle that struck the vehicle in which Plaintiffs 
were occupying. 
3. Defendant, State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance Company, had an insurance policy with 
coverage in effect covering the vehicle which struck the vehicle in which the Plaintiffs were 

occupying. 
4. Defendant, Deshawn Ford, was operating the vehicle that struck the vehicle in which Plaintiffs were 
occupying, he/she fled from the scene immediately following the collision. 
5. On or about January 3, 2021 in Jefferson County, Kentucky, the Defendant, Deshawn Ford, 
operated a vehicle protected with a policy of insurance coverage issued by Defendant, Allstate 
Insurance Company, in a negligent manner causing the vehicle he was operating to strike the 
vehicle in which Plaintiff, Anella Noble was occupying. As a direct and proximate result of the 
negligence of the Defendants, the Plaintiff sustained serious injuries, medical damages; and 
was caused to suffer great pain, both mental and physical; damage in an amount which exceeds 
the jurisdictional minimum of this court. 
6. Pursuant to KRS 304.39-062(b), threshold requirements have been met by the Plaintiff in that 
Plaintiff have incurred over $1,000.00 in medical expenses and sustained serious injuries. 
COUNT TWO: FRAUD 
7. That Defendant, Virginia Sewell, engaged in fraud in an attempt to avoid insurance liability to 
Plaintiff. 
8. That Defendant, Virginia Sewell, knowingly provided false material representations regarding 
the whereabouts and driver of the vehicle in said collision. 
9. That said false material representations led her insurance company to wrongly deny 
coverage to Plaintiffs. 
10. That the Plaintiffs had no knowledge of the true identity and said fraud caused consequent and 
proximate injury to Plaintiffs. 
11. That Defendants should be subject to significant punitive damages over and above its 
insurance limits and the special damages of Plaintiffs for engaging in such behavior that was 
willful and reckless. 

COUNT THREE: NEGLIGENT ENTRUSTMENT 
12. Defendant, Virginia Sewell’s conduct as related to the police report filed at 19:41 on January 3, 2021, 
was intended to deceive the Plaintiffs and conceal the identity of the negligent driver. 
13. At all relevant times, Defendant Virginia Sewell was the owner of the vehicle involved in the January 
3, 2021 collision when her vehicle negligently collided with the vehicle occupied by the Plaintiff. 
14. Defendant, Virginia Sewell, negligently and carelessly entrusted said vehicle to the Defendant 
Deshawn Ford, whom Defendant, Virginia Sewell, knew or should have known would operate the 
vehicle in a careless manner. 
15. Defendant, Virginia Sewell’s negligent entrustment of the vehicle was a substantial factor in causing 
the Plaintiff’s injuries as alleged. 
WHEREFORE, Plaintiffs demand as follows: 
1. Judgment against the Defendants, jointly and individually, in an amount which 
will fairly and reasonably compensate the Plaintiff for her damages alleged herein; 
2. Trial by jury; 
3. Plaintiffs’ costs herein expended; and 
4. Any and all other relief which the Plaintiff may appear to be entitled, including 
the right to amend this Complaint, if necessary.
