THIRD AMENDED PERSONAL INJURY COMPLAINT 
Comes the Plaintiff, Anella Noble, by counsel, and for her Complaint against the abovenamed Defendants, states as follows: 
COUNT I: NEGLIGENCE 
 1. Plaintiff, Anella Noble, at all times herein referred to, was a resident of Louisville, 
Jefferson County, Kentucky, and was involved in a motor vehicle collision in Louisville, Jefferson 
County, Kentucky. 

2 
 2. Defendant, Virginia Sewell, at all times herein referred to, was a resident of 
Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky, and the registered owner of a vehicle that struck the 
vehicle which Plaintiff was occupying. 
 3. Defendant, Deshawn Ford, was operating the vehicle that struck the vehicle in 
which Plaintiff was occupying, and he fled from the scene immediately following the collision. 
 4. Subsequent to the filing of this action, Defendant Deshawn Ford passed away on 
June 4, 2023. Scott Barton, Jefferson County Public Administrator has been appointed as 
Administrator for the Estate of Deshawn Ford and is so identified above. 
 5. On or about January 3, 2021 in Jefferson County, Kentucky, the Defendant, 
Deshawn Ford, operated a vehicle protected with a policy of insurance coverage issued by the 
Defendant State Farm Automobile Insurance Company, in a negligent manner causing the vehicle 
he was operating to strike the vehicle which Plaintiff was occupying. 
 6. As a direct and proximate result of the negligence of the Defendant(s), Plaintiff 
suffered serious injuries, medical damages, and was caused to suffer great pain, both mental and 
physical, the damage in amount which exceeds the jurisdictional minimum of this Court. 
 7. Pursuant to KRS 304.39-062(b), the threshold requirements have been met by 
Plaintiff in that she has incurred over $1,000.00 in medical expenses and sustained serious injuries. 
COUNT II: NEGLIGENT ENTRUSTMENT 
 8. Defendant Virginia Sewell’s conduct as related to the police report filed at 19:41 
on January 3, 2021, was intended to deceive the Plaintiff and conceal the identity of the negligent 
driver. 
 9. At all relevant times, Defendant Virginia Sewell was the owner of the vehicle 
involved in the January 3, 2021 collision when her vehicle negligently collided with the vehicle 

3 
occupied by the Plaintiff. 
 10. Defendant Virginia Sewell negligently and carelessly entrusted said vehicle to 
Defendant Deshawn Ford, whom Defendant Virginia Sewell knew or should have known would 
operate the vehicle in a careless manner. 
 11. Defendant Virginia Sewell’s negligent entrustment of the vehicle was a 
substantial factor in causing the Plaintiff’s injuries as alleged herein. 
WHEREFORE, Plaintiff demand as follows: 
1. Judgment against the Defendants in an amount which will fairly and reasonably 
compensate the Plaintiff for her damages alleged herein; 
2. Trial by jury; 
3. Pre and post judgment interest; 
4. Plaintiff’s costs herein expended; and 
5. Any and all other relief which the Plaintiff may appear to be entitled, including 
the right to amend this Complaint, if necessary.
