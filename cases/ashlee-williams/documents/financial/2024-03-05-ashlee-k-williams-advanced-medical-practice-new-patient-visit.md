A PPN is a group of medical service providers who have joined a network and agreed to accept pre-arranged discounts for necessary medical services.

Explanation of Reporting Requirements

Section II] of the Medicare, Medicaid and SCHIP Extension Act of 2007 imposes on insurers mandatory reporting of Medicare beneficiaries who receive settlements, judgments, awards or other payment from liability insurance, no-fault insurance or workers compensation. The reporting process limits the number of instances when the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) must take action to enforce the Medicare lien. Providing the reporting information benefits all parties involved.

Medicare is a Secondary Payer of medical expenses incurred by Medicare beneficiaries under the Medicare program. The Medicare beneficiary, the beneficiary's attorney and the primary insurer responsible for making payment of medical expenses incurred by the beneficiary are all responsible for reporting claim and payment information to CMS. The reporting allows CMS to coordinate payments, so it does not pay medical expenses when it is an insurer's responsibility. If Medicare pays medical expenses covered by a primary insurer, this is a conditional payment that Medicare is entitled to recover from the beneficiary, the beneficiary's attorney and the primary insurer if the primary insurer does not protect Medicare's interest as the Secondary Payer.

The Medicare law imposes an automatic lien on all conditional payments. The Medicare lien applies to all settlements, judgments, awards or other payments made by insurers to claimants who are Medicare beneficiaries. The Medicare lien can be enforced against the Medicare beneficiary, the beneficiary's lawyer and the insurer.
