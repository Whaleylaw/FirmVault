Explanation of Benefits
The following explains benefits available to you.
Your Coverage Personal Injury Protection Coverage is intended to cover medical expenses 
for reasonable, necessary, and related medical treatment incurred as a result 
of injuries sustained in the accident. Additionally, you may also have 
purchased additional coverages, as outlined below.
Personal Injury 
Protection Benefits
Under the Personal Injury Protection (PIP) portion of this policy, you may be 
entitled to up to $10,000.00 in basic benefits for medical expenses incurred, 
work loss, and other benefits resulting from this accident.
You may be entitled to these benefits regardless of whether the vehicle that 
you were occupying was at fault or not at fault, or if you are a pedestrian.
If you elected an optional coverage at the time you purchased your policy, you
may be entitled up to an additional coverage of $10,000, $20,000, $30,000, or
$40,000 depending on the option selected.
If you incur medical expenses for injuries resulting from this accident, we will 
pay for those reasonably needed products, services, and accommodations, 
including those for medical care, physical rehabilitation, rehabilitative 
occupational training, and other remedial treatment and care. Certain nonmedical remedial treatment may also be covered.
Income Continuation 
Benefits
You may be eligible to receive compensation for time away from work due to 
injuries sustained from the accident. Specific limitations may apply.
Mileage Reimbursement You may be eligible for reimbursement of medical mileage expenses.
Replacement Services You may be eligible to receive compensation for replacement services that 
you are unable to perform because of injuries sustained from the accident. 
Specific limitations may apply.
Funeral Expense Benefits You may have coverage for funeral expenses available, subject to the policy 
limits.
Survivor’s Loss Benefits You may have additional coverage for accidental death as a result of the 
accident.
Additional Information In your state, and subject to your degree of liability for this accident, you may 
have the right to recover certain incurred Out-of-Pocket expenses directly 
from the responsible party’s insurance carrier. This relates to those expenses 
not normally covered by your policy. Please retain any receipts should you 
incur any Out-of-Pocket expense. If we identify a recovery opportunity, a 
Recovery team member will reach out with more information on how we can 
assist you in the process.

Additional Services Your Policy Provides: Preferred Provider Network
As part of our continuing effort to provide full service to our policyholders to assist you in finding the proper 
medical care, you are eligible for access to a Preferred Provider Network (PPN) of doctors and facilities 
under your medical coverage provided by your personal auto policy.
What is a PPN?
A PPN is a group of medical service providers who have joined a network and agreed to accept pre-arranged
discounts for necessary medical services.
What does this mean for you?
Our PPN network can offer assistance in locating a highly qualified physician in your area. The PPN network 
has contracted with a group of doctors and facilities (labs, x-ray facilities, specialists, etc.) that meet rigorous 
credentialing requirements and provide you simple and easy access to these services and professionals. If 
you presently have limited medical coverage on your policy, the use of PPN providers can extend the limit of 
that coverage.
For example:
Presently: If you have $1,000 of medical coverage under your policy and a non-PPN provider 
provides medical treatment that totals $1,000, your policy's medical benefit would then be exhausted.
Using a PPN doctor: If you receive medical treatment from a medical provider that had contracted for
a 20% discount below the charged amount of $1,000, $800 would be paid for the same treatment 
and $200 would remain available under the medical benefit.*
*This is only one example as each medical service provided and each medical provider's contract status will 
determine what reduction, if any, in medical cost will apply. Please keep in mind that there is no cost to you 
for this service and you are not required to use this service. 
Where is the nearest PPN provider that you could receive treatment with?
Please contact Coventry Integrated Network at 1-[PHONE-3] between 8 a.m. and 5 p.m., Monday through 
Friday and inform them you are a Liberty Mutual policyholder and the type of treatment you require and where 
you would like to be treated. They will provide you the names of local medical providers from which to choose. 
You may also visit www.coventryautosolutions.com. Select the "Locate a Provider" link (upper righthand 
corner) to search for a medical provider near you. When you arrive for your scheduled appointment, show the 
provider this notice, which includes important facts and telephone numbers. OR When accessing this site, click
on "Client Login" and, once in the Client Login window, enter Client ID "LMAUTO" to search for a conveniently 
located medical provider.
The next step is to schedule your visit with the provider you chose. Remember to take this notice with you
to all visits. Liberty Mutual Insurance reserves the right to refuse payment if coverage issues arise or if the 
policy was canceled before the accident occurred.
Your personal injury and medical benefits and the premium you pay on your policy will not change whether 
or not you select a medical provider from this voluntary program or one of your own choice or any 
combination of the above. There will be no penalties imposed if you chose a provider who is not included in 
the network.
Your Liberty Mutual Claims Representative will be available to answer any of your questions and will follow 
up with you regarding any further assistance you may need.

Explanation of Reporting Requirements
Section III of the Medicare, Medicaid and SCHIP Extension Act of 2007 imposes on insurers mandatory 
reporting of Medicare beneficiaries who receive settlements, judgments, awards or other payment from 
liability insurance, no-fault insurance or workers compensation. The reporting process limits the number 
of instances when the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) must take action to enforce 
the Medicare lien. Providing the reporting information benefits all parties involved.
Medicare is a Secondary Payer of medical expenses incurred by Medicare beneficiaries under the 
Medicare program. The Medicare beneficiary, the beneficiary's attorney and the primary insurer 
responsible for making payment of medical expenses incurred by the beneficiary are all responsible for 
reporting claim and payment information to CMS. The reporting allows CMS to coordinate payments, so it
does not pay medical expenses when it is an insurer's responsibility. If Medicare pays medical expenses 
covered by a primary insurer, this is a conditional payment that Medicare is entitled to recover from the 
beneficiary, the beneficiary’s attorney and the primary insurer if the primary insurer does not protect 
Medicare’s interest as the Secondary Payer.
The Medicare law imposes an automatic lien on all conditional payments. The Medicare lien applies to all 
settlements, judgments, awards or other payments made by insurers to claimants who are Medicare 
beneficiaries. The Medicare lien can be enforced against the Medicare beneficiary, the beneficiary's 
lawyer and the insurer.
