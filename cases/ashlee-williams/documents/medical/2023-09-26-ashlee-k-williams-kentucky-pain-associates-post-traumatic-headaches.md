PRACTITIONER INITIAL EVALUATION
HISTORY OF PRESENT INJURY: Patient is a 20-year-old female, who was the unrestrained 
front-seat passenger of a vehicle involved in a motor vehicle accident. The vehicle was hit on the 
passenger side and was totaled. The air bag deployed. Police were present at the scene. Patient 
was seen by the emergency room doctor at University of Louisville Hospital. She was taken to 
the ER by ambulance. Medical records regarding this accident have been ordered from the 
originating physician. She has taken Ibuprofen to treat her pain with no relief. She has been 
taking Naproxen to treat her pain with no relief. She has experienced severe right arm pain, rated 
as 9 out of 10, severe headache pain, rated as 8 out of 10, severe right upper leg pain, rated as 7 
out of 10, severe mid/upper back pain, rated as 8 out of 10, severe neck pain, rated as 8 out of 10, 
severe right shoulder pain, rated as 9 out of 10, right ribs pain, described as severe, rated as 8 out 
of 10. Patient did not have pain right before this accident happened.
Patient describes headaches as intermittent, pressure, vise-like lasting several hours and 
resolving on their own. She does have a history of anxiety and endorses an increased level of 
anxiety accompanied by intermittent memory loss and being easily irritated since the MVC. She 
endorses severe right shoulder pain with numbness and tingling in the right hand. She endorses 
mid/upper back pain without radicular symptoms. She endorses low back pain with radicular 
symptoms to the right lower extremity. She endorses mid sternal pain, right anterior chest wall 
pain, right lateral chest pain and right hip pain. States pain is worse with all range of motion, 
prolonged sitting, standing and transitioning form the sitting to the standing position. ImPACT 
test in 1 week. Patient informed and verbalized understanding.
The patient denies any previous headaches, neck pain, referred fight trapezius pain, right 
shoulder pain, mid sternum pain, right chest wall pain, right lateral chest wall pain, mid/upper 
back pain, low back pain, right hip pain, radicular symptoms, increased anxiety prior to the 
current MVC (deny any previous pain complaint for regions not injured in previous injury).
The patient was educated on and verbalized understanding of the purpose and goals of pain
management. Motor vehicle collision speed was greater than 17 MPH and experienced moderate 
or more damage. 
Patient suffered quick or violent movements to the neck and/or body. Patient suffered a blow to 
the head. Patient states she did lose consciousness. She was unconscious for approximately two 
minutes. Patient states loss of consciousness was witnessed.
