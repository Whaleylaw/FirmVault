This letter responds to questions that you may have regarding making direct or subrogatory payments to
Medicare Advantage plans, in situations where the entity is a “primary plan” under the Medicare
Secondary Payer Act (“MSP”) (42 U.S.C. § 1395y(b)).
Humana, as you know, is aMedicare Advantage plan that has entered into a contract with the
Department ofHealth and Human Services under Part C ofthe Medicare program, to arrange for the
provision ofMedicare covered services to Medicare beneficiaries who are enrolled in the plan. As you
may also know, the standards ofthe MSP Act apply to services provided to, or expenses incurred on
behalf of, a Medicare enrollee in aMedicare Advantage plan, in situations where those expenses are
found (or agreed) to be the responsibility ofa “primary plan,” which includesa liability or no-fault
insurer.
The Center for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) has authorized Medicare Advantage plans to
recover, and be directly paid for, any such expenses that they make on behalf oftheir enrollees to the
same extent that the Medicare program (i.e., the United States) could recover such expenses for
enrollees in Part A or Part B ofthe Medicare program. This authority is explained in 42 CFR.
§ 422.108(f). CMS adjusts the payments it makes to Medicare Advantage plans on the expectation that
Medicare Advantage plans will, in fact, make and be paid for such recoveries.
