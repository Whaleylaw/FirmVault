MOTION TO DISMISS ON BEHALF OF 
LOUISVILLE/JEFFERSON COUNTY METRO GOVERNMENT
Louisville/Jefferson County Metro Government (“Metro Government”), incorrectly 
named as Louisville Metro Government d/b/a The City of Louisville, is immune from suit 
and liability in this matter pursuant to the doctrine of sovereign immunity. Kentucky Revised 
Statute 67C.101(2)(e) states that: “A consolidated local government shall be accorded the 
same sovereign immunity granted counties, their agencies, officers, and employees.” K.R.S.
67C.101(2)(e); Meinhart, et al. v. Louisville Metro Government, et al., 627 S.W.3d 824 (Ky. 
2021); Lexington-Fayette Urban Cty. Gov’t v. Smolcic, 142 S.W.3d 128 (Ky. 2004); 
Louisville/Jefferson Cty. Metro Gov't v. Cowan, 508 S.W.3d 107 (Ky. Ct. App. 2016), review 
denied (Feb. 9, 2017). Therefore, plaintiff’s claims against Metro Government should be 
dismissed as a matter of law because Metro Government is entitled to sovereign immunity.
An appropriate Order is attached.
