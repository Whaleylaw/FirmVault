GENERAL OBJECTIONS 
To the extent that any instruction contained in Defendant’s Discovery Request exceeds the 
language or scope of the Kentucky Rules of Civil Procedure, it is objected to and will not be 
complied with. To the extent that any request calls for information which is protected under 
attorney client privilege or the work product doctrine, it is objected to and will not be answered. 
Nothing in these responses shall be deemed a waiver of attorney-client privilege or the work 
product doctrine. 
 These Answers and Responses reflect the current state of Plaintiff's knowledge concerning 
the documents requested and questions asked. These responses are given without prejudice to the 
Plaintiff’s right to use or rely at any time, including trial, on subsequently discovered information 
or documents not included in these responses. 
By providing information in response to Defendant’s Discovery Requests, Plaintiff does 
not concede that any information is relevant, material, or admissible. Plaintiff reserves all rights to
