SUMMARY OF INJURIES
 
 I see no need to fully detail for you here what the medical records will reveal. However, in 
an effort to assist your expeditious review in this regard, the following is a brief summary of the 
injuries our client sustained and the treatment she has been forced to endure as a result. 
Shortly following the collision, Okolona Fire/EMS responded to the scene and attended to 
Ms. Lang, who was experiencing significant pain in her head and neck. She was transported by 
EMS to Baptist Hospital for further evaluation and treatment. 
Upon arrival at the Baptist Emergency Room, Ms. Lang reported ongoing pain in her head 
and neck, accompanied by dizziness. To assess her condition, a CT scan of her head and cervical 
spine was performed. Fortunately, the imaging did not reveal any fractures or hemorrhage; 
however, an abnormality was noted at the C6-7 vertebrae level, prompting additional imaging for 
a more detailed evaluation. An MRI was subsequently conducted, which ruled out any acute or 
emergent injuries. Based on her symptoms and imaging results, Ms. Lang was diagnosed with a 
cervical strain/sprain. She was prescribed Acetaminophen for pain management and discharged 
with instructions to seek further medical attention if her symptoms worsened. 
On September 6, 2021, just three days after the collision, Ms. Lang’s condition took a 
troubling turn as she began to experience severe abdominal pain along with significant dizziness. 
This sudden onset of symptoms was particularly concerning as Ms. Lang had never experienced 
any issues of this kind prior to the collision, suggesting a possible link between the accident and 
her sudden health crisis. Alarmed by her escalating symptoms, medical providers conducted a 
series of imaging studies, including CT scans of her abdomen and pelvis, to determine the cause. 
The results identified two critical conditions: acute appendicitis and a pancreatic lesion. Given the 
urgency of her appendicitis diagnosis, Ms. Lang was scheduled for an emergency appendectomy, 
which she underwent the same day. While the surgery was successful, her medical team advised 
that she remain at Norton Hospital for further monitoring and evaluation, both to manage her 
recovery and to closely observe any ongoing or new symptoms that might arise as a result of her 
recent injuries. 

3 
On September 12, 2021, Ms. Lang experienced severe headache and dizziness, which 
prevented her from transporting herself to the hospital. She contacted Louisville Metro EMS, who 
transported her to the emergency room for further assessment. 
Upon arrival, Ms. Lang reported ongoing symptoms of headache and dizziness, as well as 
pain on the left side of her scalp and behind her left ear. To address her symptoms, she was 
administered an IV of iopamidol and prescribed Meclizine (Antivert) to help manage her dizziness. 
CT scans of Ms. Lang’s head and neck were performed, which revealed no evidence of acute 
infarction or aneurysm. However, the imaging did identify a disc osteophyte complex at the T7-
T8 level, anterolaterally to the right. Fortunately, there was no indication of vertebral artery 
dissection. As Ms. Lang’s condition was stable and there was no emergent need for hospital 
admission, she was referred to her primary care physician for further evaluation and management.
