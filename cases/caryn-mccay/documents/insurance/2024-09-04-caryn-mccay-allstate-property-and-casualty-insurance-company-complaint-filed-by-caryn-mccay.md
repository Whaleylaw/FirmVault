Comes the Plaintiff, Caryn McCay, by counsel, and for her cause of action against the 
Defendant, Allstate Property & Casualty Insurance Company (hereinafter “Allstate”) states as 
follows: 
1. The Plaintiff, Caryn McCay, at all times relevant to the events stated in this 
Complaint, was and remains a resident of Lexington, Fayette County, Kentucky. 
2. At all relevant times, Allstate was an insurance company with the DOI ID of 300593 
operating within the Commonwealth of Kentucky, writing policies of insurance, including the 
underinsured automobile policy issued to Caryn McCay at issue in this case. 
3. On or about July 30, 2023, the Defendant, Bradley Keene (hereinafter “Keene”) did 
so negligently and carelessly operate his motor vehicle, a certain 2011 Nissan Altima, Vin. No. 
1N4AL2EP2BC131077 (hereinafter “Keene’s vehicle”) on Man O War Boulevard at the 

2
intersection of Crosby Drive in Lexington, Kentucky, thereby causing it to collide with Plaintiff’s 
vehicle. 
4. At the time of the collision, Allstate had issued in favor of Plaintiff as its insured a 
policy of insurance, No. [POLICY-1], or such other policy or policies, which provided for the 
payment of underinsured motorist benefits in the amount of at least $25,000.00 (hereinafter the 
“Allstate UIM Policy”). 
5. Pursuant to the Allstate UIM Policy, Defendant Allstate agreed, promised to pay, and 
is liable to the Plaintiff for all sums or losses over and above the liability coverage limits on the 
policy of liability insurance issued by State Farm Mutual Automobile Insurance Company (“State 
Farm”) to and for the benefit of the tortfeasor Bradley Keene, up to the limits of the Allstate UIM 
Policy. 
6. State Farm, on behalf of Bradley Keene, previously notified Plaintiff of its 
willingness to offer $100,000.00 in settlement of Ms. McCay’s underlying liability claim, and 
Plaintiff has settled her claims with Bradley Keene as the tortfeasor and accepted State Farm’s 
offer following full and proper notice to State Farm’s pursuant of KRS 304.39-320(3). Under the 
KMVRA, Allstate chose to waive its subrogation interest and not to advance State Farm’s offer of 
$100,000.00 pursuant to its letter of September 4, 2024, which authorized a direct cause of action 
by the Plaintiff against Allstate as the contract insurer under the Allstate UIM Policy. 
7. Plaintiff’s damages exceed the policy limits recovered from State Farm and the 
tortfeasor and/or any applicable no fault funds, and Plaintiff has made due and proper demand and 
called upon Allstate to honor the Allstate UIM Policy, but Allstate has failed and/or refused to do 
so. 
8. The Plaintiff has performed each and every condition precedent to impose liability 

3
on the Defendant, Allstate, pursuant to the terms and provisions of the Allstate UIM Policy. 
9. The Defendant, Allstate, is liable to the Plaintiff for said damages to the extent of the 
applicable insurance coverage and for all damages exceeding the amount recovered against 
Bradley Keene and demand has been made and is made upon Allstate to pay and to account to 
plaintiff for such damages. 
WHEREFORE, Plaintiff, by counsel, demands as follows: 
1. Judgment against the Defendant, Allstate Insurance, in an amount which will fairly 
and reasonably compensate her for the damages, in contract, tort, statutory and otherwise, as 
alleged herein and as she sustained in the motor vehicle accident described aforesaid; 
2. Trial by jury; 
3. Prejudgment interest; 
4. Post judgment interest; 
5. Attorneys fees and court costs reasonably incurred or expended to the extent 
authorized under existing law; and, 
6. All just and proper relief to which she may appear entitled, including the right to 
amend this Complaint.
