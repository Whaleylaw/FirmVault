On [DOB-3] at 2144 hours I responded to the intersection of Man O War Boulevard and Crosby Drive in
reference to a dispatched vehicle collision with injuries. When I arrived on scene I made contact with both
involved units and their operators.
I observed that Unit 1 (F2M081) appeared to have been initially struck on the front passenger side, and
had then collided with a fixed electrical box beside the traffic signal support pole, destroying it. The operator
of Unit 1 appeared to have suffered an injury to his head, and had been pushed from the driver's seat to
the front passenger seat during the collision. The operator of Unit 1 was unable to provide a coherent
statement in reference to the collision. He stated that the last thing he remembered was driving down Clays
Mill Road.
The operator of Unit 2 stated that she had been driving on the inner loop of Man O War Blvd. approaching
the intersection with Crosby Drive. She stated that she proceeded through the intersection, and Unit 1
made a left hand turn from the outer loop of Man O War, into the intersection. She stated Unit 1 pulled out
in front of her too quickly for her to stop.
Both the operators were transported to UK Hospital by Lexington Fire Department ambulance. Both
vehicles sustained severe damage. Both vehicles were towed by Robert's Towing to their lot.
