The Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) is the federal agency that oversees the Medicare program.
Many Medicare beneficiaries have other insurance in addition to their Medicare benefits. Sometimes, Medicare is
Supposed to pay after the other insurance. However, if certain other insurance delays payment, Medicare may make
a “conditional payment” so as to not inconvenience the beneficiary, and then recover after the other insurance pays.
Section 111 of the Medicare, Medicaid and SCHIP Extension Act of 2007 (MMSEA), a federal law that became
effective January 1, 2009, requires that liability insurers (including self-insurers), no-fault insurers, and workers’
compensation plans report specific information about Medicare beneficiaries who have other insurance coverage.
This reporting is to assist CMS and other insurance plans to properly coordinate payment of benefits among plans
so that your claims are paid promptly and correctly.
