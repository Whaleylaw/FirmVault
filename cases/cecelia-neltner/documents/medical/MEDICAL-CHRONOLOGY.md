## II. THE ACCIDENT — April 1, 2023

**Date/Time:** April 1, 2023, approximately 8:49 PM
**Location:** Burlington Pike (KY 18), Florence, Boone County, Kentucky
**Conditions:** Raining, wet road, dark (highway lighted)
**Manner of Collision:** Rear-end
**Speed Limit:** 45 MPH

**Summary:** Cecelia Neltner was a restrained front-seat passenger in her father Greg Neltner's vehicle, traveling eastbound on KY 18. Defendant Trinity Cohara cut across multiple lanes of traffic and directly into their path, causing a collision. Airbags deployed. Police cited inattention, failure to keep proper lane, and failure to yield right-of-way as contributing factors for Cohara.

**Immediate Complaints:** Pain in arm, neck, and back. Did not go to the hospital. Noticed a rash developing on her forearm in the days/weeks following, which worsened over time with blistering and scabbing — consistent with chemical airbag burns.

## VIII. OBSERVATIONS & NOTES FOR COUNSEL

1. **Minimal medical treatment**: Only two documented post-accident medical visits directly related to the MVA (Bluegrass UC 4/12/23 and Cincinnati Children's 4/17/23). The 6/7/2023 St. Elizabeth visit addressed the rash among many other pre-existing complaints.

2. **Headache claim**: Cecelia disclosed headaches for the first time at her deposition. She has never sought treatment for them and has no medical documentation linking them to the accident. If headaches are to be pursued as a damage element, medical evaluation and documentation would be needed.

3. **Pre-existing psychiatric history**: Extensive history of anorexia nervosa, adjustment disorder, depression, anxiety, and self-harm. Defense may attempt to attribute some complaints to pre-existing conditions. However, the airbag burn/rash is clearly accident-related and well-documented.

4. **Low medical specials**: Total documented medical bills are approximately $200. The $5,000 demand is primarily based on pain and suffering from the airbag burns, scarring, and general damages.

5. **Defense subpoenas (3/7/2025)**: Defense obtained additional records from Christ Hospital Outpatient Center and TriHealth 1st Physicians — likely fishing for pre-existing conditions or inconsistencies.
