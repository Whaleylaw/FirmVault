Cervical Sprain
A cervical sprain is also called a neck sprain. It is a stretch or tear in one or more ligaments in the neck. Ligaments are tissues that
connect bones to each other.
Neck sprains can be mild, bad, or very bad. A very bad sprain inthe neck can cause the bones in the neck to be unstable. This can
damage the spinal cord. lt can also cause serious problems inthe brain, spinal cord, and nerves (nervous system).
Most neck sprains heal in 4—6 weeks. Itcan take more or less time depending on:
. What caused the injury.
. The amount of injury.
