Here are some helpful answers to FAQs

My claim has been referred to Subrogation. What's next?

When another party is primarily at fault for your damages, State Farm® may try to recover the amount of the claim paid by State Farm for your loss. This includes some or all of your deductible from the responsible party.

| paid a deductible. How is that handled?

Subrogation will include your deductible in the claim we present to the responsible party. Depending on state law requirements or your policy language, the returned deductible amount may be based on your share of liability in the accident or whether the recovery is equal to or more than your deductible amount.

How long does the process take?

While we work to recover your deductible as soon as possible, you should be aware subrogation can take several months or possibly years, depending on the complexity of the loss. For example, if there is disagreement over who is at fault, we may need to go through arbitration or litigation to resolve the dispute. You will be notified if your claim is advanced to arbitration or litigation.

Can | pursue my deductible on my own? You may choose to seek your deductible directly from the responsible party or that person's insurance company.

What is an out-of-pocket expense?

Your State Farm claim addresses damages covered by your State Farm policy. Any other damages you may have sustained which are not covered by the policy, State Farm cannot pursue on your behalf. This can include co-pays and upgrades for rental, diminished value or damaged personal items. Questions about coverage should be directed to your Claims team. If subrogation is successful, you may be able to present those damages to the other insurance carrier for reimbursement.

What if the other party doesn't have insurance?

Depending on the circumstances, State Farm may still pursue subrogation from the responsible party on your behalf through a collection vendor.
