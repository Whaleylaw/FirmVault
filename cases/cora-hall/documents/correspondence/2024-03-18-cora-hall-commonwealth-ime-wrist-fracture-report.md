Dear Dr. Bloemer: 
As you are aware, this office represents Ms. Cora Hall for injuries sustained in a work 
accident on December 5, 2022, while in the course and scope of her employment as an assistant 
manager with Sonic in Scottsville, KY. 
On December 5, 2022, Cora was walking into the door at Sonic and had almost made it 
inside when she slipped and fell on water that had been allowed to accumulate on the tile inside of 
the employee entrance. She fell backwards and immediately felt pain throughout her left upper 
extremity and neck. 
Immediately following the slip-and-fall, Ms. Hall reported the accident to her supervisors 
and presented to Medical Center Scottsville for x-rays. These images revealed a distal radius 
fracture. On December 7, 2022, Ms. Hall underwent an Open Reduction and Internal Fixation 
surgery on her left wrist. Ms. Hall diligently followed up with physical therapy, and while her pain 
moderately improved, it had not wholly resolved. Thus, Ms. Hall was referred to pain management, 
with some minimal improvements. 
At the request of the Defendant/Employer, Ms. Hall presented to Dr. Jeffrey Hazlewood 
for an Independent Medical Evaluation. Following his evaluation, Dr. Hazlewood assigned a 2% 
whole person impairment rating, based on Ms. Hall’s left wrist fracture. Dr. Hazlewood opined 
that Ms. Hall did not sustain any cervical spine injury and provided no associated impairment 
rating. A copy of this report, along with Ms. Hall’s medical records have been forwarded to your 
office.
