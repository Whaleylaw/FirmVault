CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS:  Ms. Hall has reached maximum medical improvement at the time of this examination for her left wrist.  I would place her at MMI on December 5, 2023.  As stated previously, I do not think she is at MMI for her neck or left shoulder condition as she has not had appropriate evaluation or treatment of these problems.

First, in terms of treatment for her continued pain in the cervical region, left shoulder, and left arm region, I would agree that her treating orthopedist that an MRI of the cervical spine is indicated.  I would start with this test as if it shows cervical pathology, it could explain her current complaints of pain, as well as numbness in the left arm.  While the EMG did not show any evidence of radiculopathy, this is not uncommon with cervical disc pathology.  

The modality for evaluation of choice is an MRI of the cervical spine and I would recommend that this be ordered.  Treatment to the cervical spine would then depend on the results of the MRI scan.  If the MRI of the cervical spine fails to show any significant pathology to account for her symptoms, then I would recommend doing an MRI scan of the left shoulder.  Again, her physical exam does show some evidence of pathology in the left shoulder, that being an impingement syndrome.  Treatment for the shoulder will depend on the results of the MRI scan.  

In my medical opinion, these two tests are necessary to completely evaluate Ms. Hall’s ongoing complaints of pain and numbness in her left arm.  It is certainly not the standard of care to simply ignore symptoms and to not evaluate symptoms with appropriate objective testing.

In terms of future medical treatment for her left wrist, she continues to have some pain and limited motion in the left wrist, which is not uncommon after a comminuted displaced intraarticular fracture despite appropriate surgery for ORIF.  She is more likely in the left injured wrist than the right to develop posttraumatic osteoarthritis.  This would require occasional followup with an orthopedic surgeon, occasional use of medication such as nonsteroidal anti-inflammatories or compounding cream.  

Occasionally, a cortisone injection could be done in the wrist if this develops.  In the future, if she does develop severe posttraumatic osteoarthritis in the left wrist, she may require further surgery for either a wrist fusion or total wrist arthroplasty.  Additionally, patients can develop a painful condition from the hardware.  Hardware can loosen or break, which would require surgical treatment to remove the surgical hardware, in this case, the plates and screws.
