In terms of a rating for her left shoulder, using the Range of Motion Method on Pages 475 through 479, Figures 16-38 through 16-46, she has a 2% impairment of the upper extremity due to loss of flexion, she has a 1% impairment of the upper extremity due to loss of extension, she has a 1% impairment of the upper extremity due to loss of abduction, and she has a 1% impairment of the upper extremity due to loss of internal rotation. This gives her a total of 5% impairment of the upper extremity due to her loss of motion.

Using Table 16-3 on Page 439, a 5% impairment of the upper extremity is equal to a 3% impairment of the whole person.

In my medical opinion, this entire rating is due to the work injury of December 5, 2022. There are no pre-existing conditions that affect this rating.

Combining the 3% whole person impairment for her left shoulder with her 7% whole person impairment for her wrist (as per my report of March 26, 2024) using the Combined Values Chart on Page 604 gives her a 10% whole person impairment due to the work injury to her left shoulder and left wrist on December 5, 2022.

In my medical opinion, she needs further evaluation and treatment for her cervical spine injury. She had an MRI scan of her neck and has had trigger point injections from pain management for treatment of this, but no other treatment has been given at present.

My recommendation for treatment would be to refer her to a spine surgeon who specializes in neck problems such as hers. She does have some mild spinal stenosis. I would leave the treatment recommendations up to the spine surgeon, but would recommend consideration of cervical epidural blocks, which can be done for both therapeutic and diagnostic purposes. If the epidural block gives her significant relief of symptoms for several days or weeks, these could be repeated a couple additional times to see if this does not resolve her symptoms. If it gives her relief but symptoms return, then consideration could be given to surgical intervention.

CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS: Ms. Payne has reached her maximum medical improvement at the time of this examination for her left wrist and left shoulder. As stated previously, I would place her at MMI for her left wrist on December 5, 2023. I would place her at MMI for her left shoulder on August 12, 2025, which is six months after her surgery.

As stated, it is my medical opinion that she is not at MMI for her cervical spine and needs further treatment as discussed above.

In my medical opinion, the treatment she has received to date has been necessary and appropriate for treatment of her December 5, 2022, work injury.
