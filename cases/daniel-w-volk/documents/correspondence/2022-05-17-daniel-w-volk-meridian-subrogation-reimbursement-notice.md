Anthem Health Plans of KY Inc retained Meridian Resource Company, LLC ("Meridian") to pursue
subrogation/reimbursement matters on behalf of the Blue Cross Blue Shield Service Benefit Plan (the
"Plan"). This letter serves as notice of our client's subrogation/reimbursement rights and claim for
reimbursement related to this matter.
Your client is/was enrolled in the Plan, one ofthe federal government's health benefits plans for federal
employees, retirees, and their dependents. The Plan is governed by the Federal Employees Health
Benefits Act ("FEHBA"), 5 U.S.C.§§ 8901-14; United States Office ofPersonnel Management (“OPM”)
regulations at 5 C.F.R.§ 890.106; and by the government contract entered into pursuant to FEHBA
between OPM and the Blue Cross Blue Shield Association on behalf ofBlue Cross and Blue Shield entities
nationwide. FEHBA’s express preemption provision at 5 U.S.C. §8902(m)(1) preempts state laws that
purport to limit the Plan’s reimbursement/subrogation rights. Coventry Health Care ofMo.., Inc. v. Nevils.
137 S. Ct. 1190 (2017); 5 C.F.R.§ 890.106(h).
Please note that the Plan is financed with funds held in the U.S. Treasury. 5 U.S.C. § 8909(a). Thus, the
conditional benefits the Plan has paid on your client’s behalf came from funds in the U.S. Treasury, and
the reimbursement amount to be collected will be credited back to the U.S. Treasury. 48 C.F.R. §
1631.201-70(a).
To date $2,452.30 in medical expenses has been paid by the Plan for your client. This amount may change
ifthere is additional treatment related to the above date ofloss. Please be advised that we may have also
placed the insurance carrier(s) on notice ofour claim in this matter.
