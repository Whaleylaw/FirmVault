Comes the Plaintiff, Daniel Volk, by counsel, and for his cause of action against the 
Defendant, Safeco Insurance Company of Illinois (hereinafter “Safeco”) states as follows: 
1. The Plaintiff, Daniel Volk, at all times relevant to the events stated in this Complaint, 
was and remains a resident of Crestwood, Oldham County, Kentucky. 
2. At all relevant times, Safeco is and remains an insurance company with the DOI ID 
of 301350 operating within the Commonwealth of Kentucky, writing policies of insurance, 
including the underinsured automobile policy issued to Daniel Volk at issue in this case. 
3. On or about September 24, 2021, Gary Pinkston did so negligently and carelessly 
operate his motor vehicle, a certain 2005 Chevrolet Suburban, Vin. No. 3GNFK16Z95G166429 
(hereinafter “Pinkston’s vehicle”) on Interstate 65 in Johnson, Franklin County, Indiana, thereby 
causing it to collide with Plaintiff’s vehicle. 
4. At the time of the collision, Safeco had issued in favor of Plaintiff as its insured a 

2
policy of insurance, No. K3226275, or such other policy or policies, which provided for the 
payment of underinsured motorist benefits in the amount of at least $250,000.00 (hereinafter the 
“Safeco UIM Policy”). 
5. Pursuant to the Safeco UIM Policy, Defendant Safeco agreed, promised to pay, and 
is liable to the Plaintiff for all sums or losses over and above the liability coverage limits on the 
policy of liability insurance issued by Grange Insurance Company to and for the benefit of the 
tortfeasor Gary Pinkston, up to the limits of the Safeco UIM Policy. 
6. Grange Insurance Company, on behalf of Gary Pinkston, previously notified Plaintiff 
of its willingness to offer $25,000.00 in settlement of Plaintiff’s underlying liability claim, and 
Plaintiff has settled her claims with Gary Pinkston as the tortfeasor and accepted Grange’s offer 
following full and proper notice to Safeco pursuant of KRS 304.39-320(3). Under the KMVRA, 
Safeco chose to waive its subrogation interest and not to advance Grange’s offer of $25,000.00 
pursuant to its correspondence of January 19, 2022, which authorized a direct cause of action by 
the Plaintiff against Safeco as the contract insurer under the Safeco UIM Policy. 
7. Plaintiff’s damages exceed the policy limits recovered from Grange and the tortfeasor 
and/or any applicable no-fault funds, and Plaintiff has made due and proper demand and called 
upon Safeco to honor the Safeco UIM Policy, but Safeco has failed and/or refused to do so. 
8. The Plaintiff has performed each and every condition precedent to impose liability 
on the Defendant, Safeco, pursuant to the terms and provisions of the Safeco UIM Policy. 
9. The Defendant, Safeco, is liable to the Plaintiff for said damages to the extent of the 
applicable insurance coverage and for all damages exceeding the amount recovered against Gary 
Pinkston and demand has been made and is made upon Safeco to pay and to account to plaintiff 
for such damages. 

3
WHEREFORE, Plaintiff, by counsel, demands as follows: 
1. Judgment against the Defendant, Safeco Insurance, in an amount which will fairly 
and reasonably compensate Plaintiff for the damages, in contract, tort, statutory and otherwise, as 
alleged herein and as he sustained in the motor vehicle accident described aforesaid; 
2. Trial by jury; 
3. Prejudgment interest; 
4. Post judgment interest; 
5. Attorneys fees and court costs reasonably incurred or expended to the extent 
authorized under existing law; and, 
6. All just and proper relief to which he may appear entitled, including the right to 
amend this Complaint.
