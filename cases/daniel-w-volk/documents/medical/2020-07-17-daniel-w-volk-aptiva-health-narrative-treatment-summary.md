Causation
The mechanism of injury is consistent with cervical spine strain/pain, left shoulder strain/pain with mild 
impingement symptoms, lumbosacral strain/pain and post-traumatic headaches. Mr. Volk states that 
he did not have a history of significant headaches, neck pain, left shoulder pain or low back pain prior 
to the [DOB-17] MVA. The records listed above were reviewed and I cannot find evidence that these 
symptoms pre-existed immediately prior to this MVA. The incidental finding of a brain lesion was 
appropriately treated at another facility with ultimate removal of the meningioma. Mr. Volk stated that 
his headaches had resolved following his brain surgery. While his meningioma was not directly related 
to the MVA, it was asymptomatic. The fact that his headaches resolved after removal supports that the 
MVA caused this pre-existing condition to become symptomatic and responsible for his post-MVA 
headaches.
