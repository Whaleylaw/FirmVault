Dear Atty Aaron Whaley:
Texas Roadhouse takes every customer's claim seriously and we complete a full investigation into every claim
made. As you can appreciate, we cannot pay every claim presented to Texas Roadhouse. But when our
investigation leads us to believe Texas Roadhouse is responsible, we pay what we owe. When our investigation
shows Texas Roadhouse is not responsible, we will advise you of the same. The results of our investigation into
your client's claim are discussed further in this letter.
Texas Roadhouse does not have a medical payments provision under our policy of insurance, and we do not pay
medical providers as a health insurer. When a guest makes a claim, responsibility is determined, and if we accept
responsibility, we gather all medical records and evaluate the claim before any offer of payment is made. The
offer is the entire amount we will pay for the claim, regardless of how many medical providers, or lien holders,
exist within the claim. The guest is responsible for satisfying any bills or liens that exist against them.
We have completed our investigation of your client's fall at our store. While it is confirmed that your client did fall
outside of our restaurant, we did not find any defect in our premises that caused or contributed to this incident.
While it is unfortunate that this occurred, we do not find any negligence on Texas Roadhouse or their staff and we
will not be making any payments on this claim.
If you or your client have additional information you feel would change our opinion in this matter, please provide
it for our review and we will take it into consideration.
