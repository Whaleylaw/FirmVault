CC The patient presents for evaluation of pain following a motor vehicle accident (MVA) that
occurred on [DOB-73].
HPI
The patient, a 46-year-old female, was involved in a rear-end collision on [DOB-74] as the
driver, wearing a seatbelt, when her vehicle was hit on the driver’s side, causing her head
to impact the window. The patient remained conscious but felt disoriented immediately after
the accident. Airbags did not deploy. The patient was able to self-extricate from the vehicle.
The patient was ambulatory immediately after the collision. The patient was not transported
to the hospital and did not seek immediate medical attention. The patient followed up with a
provider in the community and has been receiving chiropractic treatment for about 5 weeks,
reporting some relief but noting work activities hinder recovery. This provider organized
imaging recently (lumbar MRI which showed disc herniations in the lower back).
She reports pain in multiple areas, including upper back, right knee, and lower back,
described as moderate to severe with burning, stabbing, and shooting sensations, all
equally affected. The pain is exacerbated by work activities (washing, cooking, lifting
children, running, playing) and relieved partially by rest and ibuprofen (800 mg per dose).
The patient denies radiating pain. OTC medications have not been helpful recently.
She owns a small daycare, working 7:00 AM to 6:00 PM daily, and has been unable to take
time off, potentially exacerbating her pain.
The patient has a history of right knee arthritis, diagnosed by an orthopedist approximately
1 year ago, managed with exercise. The patient states her knee pain has worsened since
the MVC.
The patient denies any previous history of significant low back pain prior to the current
MVC.
The patient denies loss of consciousness, headaches, nausea, vomiting, light/noise
sensitivity, memory problems, concentration issues, or anxiety.
Acute Concussion Evaluation (ACE) Physician/Clinician Office Version was administered,
with a symptom score of 1/22 (disorientation reported at the time of accident).
