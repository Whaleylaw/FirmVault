Comes the Plaintiff, Debra Marshall, by counsel, and for her causes of action against the 
Defendant, states as follows: 
1. The Plaintiff, Debra Marshall, at all times relevant to the events stated herein, was a 
resident of Cave City, Barren County, Kentucky, residing at the address set forth above. 
2. Upon information and belief, at all relevant times, the Defendant, Jasmine Reed 
(hereinafter “Defendant Reed”), was a resident of Horse Cave, Hart County, Kentucky, residing 
at the address set forth above. 
3. On or about December 3, 2022, Defendant Reed did so negligently and carelessly 
operate her motor vehicle, a certain 2012 Chrysler 200, Vin No. 1C3CCBBB8CN191287 
(“Defendant Reed’s vehicle”) on Dixie Highway in Cave City, Kentucky thereby striking Plaintiff 
and/or Plaintiff’s vehicle. 
4. The negligence of Defendant Reed in the operation of her vehicle on the date and at 

2 
the place aforesaid was a substantial factor in causing Plaintiff serious and permanent injuries, 
including great pain of body and mind, both past and future. In addition, the Plaintiff has incurred 
medical expenses as a result of the collision and injuries she sustained and will incur future medical 
expenses for the treatment of her injuries. Further, Plaintiff lost time and money from her gainful 
employment, resulting in damages for past lost wages and has had her future ability to labor and 
earn money substantially impaired. 
5. The aforementioned damages incurred by Plaintiff are a sum in excess of the minimal 
amount necessary to confer jurisdiction upon this Court. 
6. Pursuant to KRS 304.39-062(b), threshold requirements have been met by the 
Plaintiff in that Plaintiff has incurred over $1,000.00 in medical expenses and sustained serious 
injuries. 
WHEREFORE, Plaintiff, by counsel, demands as follows: 
1. Judgment against the Defendant in such an amount which will fairly and reasonably 
compensate Plaintiff for the damages alleged herein; 
2. Prejudgment interest; 
 3. Post judgment interest; 
 4. Attorneys fees and court costs reasonably incurred or expended; 
 5. Trial by jury on all issues so triable; 
 6. Punitive damages to the extent a trier of fact determines the actions of the Defendant 
were malicious, oppressive, fraudulent, or reckless; and, 
 7. All just and proper relief to which he may appear entitled, including the right to 
amend this Complaint.
