MUTUAL INSURANCE COMPANY

v. ANSWER 
(Electronically Filed) 
DEBRA MARSHALL DEFENDANT 
* * * * * * * 
 Comes the Defendant, Debra Marshall, by and through counsel, and for her Answer to the 
Complaint herein states as follows: 
FIRST DEFENSE 
The Complaint fails, in whole or in part, to state a claim against this answering Defendant 
upon which relief can be granted and therefore, must be dismissed. 
SECOND DEFENSE 
Plaintiff’s claims are barred in whole or in part by the applicable statute of limitations. 
THIRD DEFENSE 
To the extent the damages, if any, of which the Plaintiff complains were caused and brought 
about by the carelessness and negligence of the Jasmine Reed, driver of the vehicle owned by Edie 
Renick and insured by Kentucky Farm Bureau Mutual Insurance Company, or persons not yet 
party to this suit, as may be determined by a Barren County jury, the claims of the Plaintiff as 

2
asserted against this answering Defendant must be denied or reduce by apportionment in an 
amount equal to the degree of fault that may be assessed against Plaintiff. 
FOURTH DEFENSE 
The Plaintiff’s claims are barred and/or limited in whole or in part to the extent that the 
Plaintiff failed to mitigate their damages. 
FIFTH DEFENSE 
The Defendant, Debra Marshall, may have defenses which are not presently known. 
Therefore, in order to preserve any such defenses, the Defendant incorporates by reference and 
adopts as part of its Answer herein, all of the appropriate defenses set forth in Rules 8 and 12 of 
the Kentucky Rules of Civil Procedure and reserves the right to amend this Answer to assert 
additional defenses and affirmative defenses and to file any Cross-claims, Counterclaims, or thirdparty claims which the Defendant, Debra Marshall, deems appropriate after discovery. 
SIXTH DEFENSE 
1. The Defendant, Debra Marshall, admits the allegations contained within 
Paragraphs One (1) and Two (2) of Plaintiff’s Complaint. 
2. With regard to the allegations contained within Paragraph Three (3) of the 
Plaintiff’s Complaint, the Defendant, Debra Marshall, admits a motor vehicle collision transpired 
on the date and in the location specified by the Plaintiff. The Defendant, Debra Marshall, denies 
the remaining allegations and averments contained within Paragraph Three (3) of the Plaintiff’s 
Complaint. 
3. The Defendant, Debra Marshall, is without information sufficient at the present 
time to form a belief as to the truth of the allegations contained in Paragraph Four (4) of the 

3
Plaintiff’s Complaint and must deny same. However, Defendant Marshall specifically denies any 
averments or allegations that there is any indebtedness owed by Defendant to the Plaintiff. 
4. The Defendant, Debra Marshall, specifically denies the allegations and/or 
averments contained in Paragraph Five (5) of Plaintiff’s Complaint and specifically denies any 
indebtedness by Defendant to the Plaintiff. 
5. Any allegation not specifically admitted by the Defendant is hereby denied. 
WHEREFORE, the Defendant, Debra Marshall, demands as follows: 
1. Dismissal of the Plaintiff’s Complaint against this answering Defendant in its entirety; 
2. For her costs and attorney fees herein expended; 
3. For a trial by jury on all issues so triable; and
4. Any and all other relief to which Defendant may appear entitled, including leave to file 
amended and supplemental pleadings as may be deemed appropriate by the evidence 
introduced prior to or at trial.
