On Saturday, [DOB-5] at 1742 hours, I was dispatched to a collision on N Dixie Hwy.
I arrived at 1744 hours, upon arrival I observed a person wearing camouflage clothing lying on the
roadway, a moped on roadway and a vehicle on the grass on the right side on the roadway.
I approached the person on the ground, Debra Marshall to check on her, Marshall advised she had injuries
to her head and on the side of her body, I advised her EMS was en route.
Officer G. Avery arrived at 1744 hours, same date, and approached the operator of Unit 2, Jasmine M
Reed. Avery advised she was fine and did not need medical attention but was shaken up.
Cave City Fire department arrived at 1746 hours, same date, and began first aid on Marshall.
Marshall advised she was traveling southbound on Dixie Hwy when she was struck by Unit 2.
Reed advised she was traveling south on Dixie Hwy and as she approached the Travel Inn Motel she
observed a moped coming out on the roadway and into her path. Reed advised she slammed on her
brakes and swerved to the right in an attempt to prevent from striking her. Reed advised she was unable to
move and struck Unit 1.
On Thursday, [DOB-4] at 1737 hours, I spoke with Christina Marshall, daughter of Debra Marshall.
Christina advised her mother was released from the hospital with in 24 hours and was back home.
At 2030 hours, I attempted to make contact with Debra at her residence, her husband advised she was
unable to come to the door at the time due to her being asleep, I advised him I would return next day to
speak to her
On Friday, 12/09/200 around 1800 hours, I made contact with Debra at her residence.
Debra advised she pulled out of the driveway and started to travel south on Dixie Hwy when she saw light
getting close to her then she felt the vehicle stick her. Debra advised she when she was struck she landed
on top of the hood of the vehicle then she was thrown to the ground and rolled for several feet. Debra
advised she felt something struck her foot as she attempted to move out of the road way. Debra advised a
female came to her and asked if she was OK. Debra advised she told the female to help her get out of the
road way but the person did not assist her so she began to use her legs as she dragged herself out of the
road way.
