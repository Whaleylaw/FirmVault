MOTION TO FOR SANCTIONS 
Come now the Plaintiffs, Destiny Adkins and Juanita Downs, by and through counsel, and 
pursuant to CR 37.02, et seq. hereby request that this Court enter an Order imposing some or all 
of the sanctions provided under CR 37.02(2) on Defendant Stephanie Nail for her failure to comply 
with this Court’s Order of August 21, 2023. In support of their motion, Plaintiffs state as follows: 
Plaintiffs filed their Complaint for personal injuries arising out of a motor vehicle collision 
on June 8, 2022. Defendant Stephanie Nail filed her Answer by counsel on October 24, 2022. The 
depositions of both Plaintiffs were taken at the request of Defendant Nail’s counsel on June 29, 
2023, and Defendant Nail’s deposition was scheduled to be taken shortly thereafter. 
After multiple unsuccessful attempts were made at taking Defendant Nail’s deposition, 
Plaintiffs filed a Motion to Compel. Upon no objection from Defendant Nail, this Court entered 

2 
an Order on August 21, 2023, compelling Defendant to submit to a deposition within thirty (30) 
days from entry.1
Extrajudicial efforts were again made to obtain Defendant Nail’s deposition which were 
again unsuccessful.2 Plaintiff’s counsel first attempted via telephone shortly after entry of the 
Order Compelling the deposition to obtain dates therefor. This call was not returned. Plaintiff’s 
counsel next reached out to Defendant Nail’s counsel via email on September 11, 2023, prior to 
the deadline identified by the Order to obtain dates, to which no response was made. On September 
26, 2023, Plaintiff’s counsel again wrote to Defendant’s counsel following the expiration of the 
September 22 deadline, advising of that if dates were not scheduled, the instant motion would 
follow. Again, Defendant’s counsel did not respond to this correspondence. To date, Defendant 
Nail has failed to submit to a deposition and her counsel has made no indication that she is or 
would be available to do so. In fact, her counsel has not offered any response to any attempted 
communications in this regard. 
If a defendant refuses to participate in the discovery after being directed to do so by the 
court in the district in which the deposition is being taken, the failure to comply with such an Order 
may be considered contempt of that court.3 The Civil Rules prescribe a practical pattern for the 
conduct of litigation and the effective administration of justice. To this end, reasonable compliance 
is necessary. The proper application and utilization of the Civil Rules should be left largely to the 
supervision of the trial judge. Discovery rules are designed to promote efficiency, order, and

expediency within the judicial system, and the sanction for their violation is well within the 
discretion of the trial court.4
In Greathouse v. American National Bank and Trust Co., the Court found that “willfulness” 
may be found where a party has the present ability to comply with discovery but fails to do so.5
Here, it is clear that Defendant Nail does not intend to comply with this Court’s Order compelling 
her deposition. There has been no communication from Defendant Nail or her attorney that she 
will submit to a deposition, nor has there been any indication she is unable to do so. To date, her 
avoidance of this litigation has been willful and in bad faith. As such, Plaintiffs request this Court 
enter an Order in accordance with CR 37.02(2)(c), rendering a default judgment against the 
Defendant Stephanie Nail. 
WHEREFORE, the Plaintiffs, by counsel, move the Court to enter their tendered Order.
