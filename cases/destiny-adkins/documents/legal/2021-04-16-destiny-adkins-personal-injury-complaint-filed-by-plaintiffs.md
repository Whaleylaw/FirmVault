PERSONAL INJURY COMPLAINT 
NEGLIGENCE, FRAUD, NEGLIGENT ENTRUSTMENT 
Comes the Plaintiffs, Destiny Adkins & Juanita Downs, by Counsel, and for their 
Complaint against the above- named Defendants, states as follows: 
COUNT ONE: NEGLIGENCE 
1. Plaintiffs, Destiny Adkins & Juanita Downs, at all times herein referred to, were residents of 
Jefferson County, Louisville, Kentucky and involved in a motor vehicle collision in Jefferson 
County, Louisville, Kentucky. 
2. Defendant, Stephanie M. Nail, at all times herein referred to, was a resident of Jefferson County, 

Louisville, Kentucky and the registered owner of a vehicle that struck the vehicle in which Plaintiffs 
were occupying. 
3. Defendant, Allstate Insurance Company, had an insurance policy with coverage in effect covering the 
vehicle which struck the vehicle in which the Plaintiffs were occupying. 
4. Defendant, Unknown Driver, was operating the vehicle that struck the vehicle in which Plaintiffs were 
occupying, he/she fled from the scene immediately following the collision. 
5. On or about April 16, 2021 in Jefferson County, Kentucky, the Defendant, Unknown Driver, 
operated a vehicle protected with a policy of insurance coverage issued by Defendant, Allstate 
Insurance Company, in a negligent manner causing the vehicle he was operating to strike the 
vehicle in which Plaintiffs, Destiny Adkins & Juanita Downs were occupying. As a direct and 
proximate result of the negligence of the Defendants, the Plaintiffs sustained serious injuries, 
medical damages; and was caused to suffer great pain, both mental and physical; damage in an 
amount which exceeds the jurisdictional minimum of this court. 
6. Pursuant to KRS 304.39-062(b), threshold requirements have been met by the Plaintiff in that 
Plaintiff have incurred over $1,000.00 in medical expenses and sustained serious injuries. 
COUNT TWO: FRAUD 
7. That Defendant, Stephanie M. Nail, engaged in fraud in an attempt to avoid insurance liability 
to Plaintiffs. 
8. That Defendant, Stephanie M. Nail, knowingly provided false material representations 
regarding the whereabouts and driver of the vehicle in said collision. 
9. That said false material representations led her insurance company to wrongly deny 
coverage to Plaintiffs. 
10. That the Plaintiffs had no knowledge of the true identity and said fraud caused consequent and 
proximate injury to Plaintiffs. 
11. That Defendants should be subject to significant punitive damages over and above its 

insurance limits and the special damages of Plaintiffs for engaging in such behavior that was 
willful and reckless. 
COUNT THREE: NEGLIGENT ENTRUSTMENT 
12. Defendant, Stephanie M. Nail’s conduct as related to the police report filed at 23:57 on April 16, 2021, 
was intended to deceive the Plaintiffs and conceal the identity of the negligent driver. 
13. At all relevant times, Defendant Stephanie M. Nail was the owner of the vehicle involved in the April 
16, 2021 collision when her vehicle negligently collided with the vehicle occupied by the Plaintiffs. 
14. Defendant, Stephanie M. Nail, negligently and carelessly entrusted said vehicle to the Defendant 
Unknown Driver, whom Defendant, Stephanie M. Nail, knew or should have known would operate the 
vehicle in a careless manner. 
15. Defendant, Stephanie M. Nail’s negligent entrustment of the vehicle was a substantial factor in causing 
the Plaintiffs’ injuries as alleged. 
WHEREFORE, Plaintiffs demand as follows: 
1. Judgment against the Defendants, jointly and individually, in an amount which 
will fairly and reasonably compensate the Plaintiff for his damages alleged herein; 
2. Trial by jury; 
3. Plaintiffs’ costs herein expended; and 
4. Any and all other relief which the Plaintiff may appear to be entitled, including 
the right to amend this Complaint, if necessary.
