Come the Plaintiffs, Destiny Adkins and Juanita Downs, by and through counsel, and for 
their Amended Complaint herein state as follows: 
COUNT I: NEGLIGENCE 
1. Plaintiffs restate and re-allege all of the allegations contained in their Amended 
Complaint, which is of record and is hereby incorporated by reference. 
 2. Plaintiffs, Destiny Adkins and Juanita Downs, at all times referred to herein, were 
residents of Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky, and involved in a motor vehicle collision in 
Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky. 

2 
 3. Defendant, Stephanie M. Nail, at all times referred to herein, was a resident of 
Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky, and the registered owner of a vehicle that struck the vehicle 
in which Plaintiffs were occupying. 
 4. Defendant, Allstate Insurance Company, had an insurance policy with coverage in 
effect covering the vehicle which struck the vehicle in with Plaintiffs were occupying. 
 5. Defendant, Demetrius Turner, was operating the vehicle that struck the vehicle in 
which Plaintiffs were occupying and fled from the scene immediately following the collision. 
 6. On or about April 16, 2021, in Jefferson County, Kentucky, the Defendant, Demetrius 
Turner, operated a vehicle protected with a policy of insurance coverage issued by Defendant Allstate 
Insurance Company, in a negligent manner causing the vehicle he was operating to strike the vehicle 
in which Plaintiffs were occupying. As a direct and proximate result of the negligence of the 
Defendants, Plaintiffs sustained serious injuries, medical damages, and were caused to suffer great 
pain, both mental and physical; damage in an amount which exceeds the jurisdictional minimum of 
this court. 
 7. Pursuant to KRS 304.69-062(b), threshold requirements have been met by the 
Plaintiffs in that they have incurred over $1,000.00 in medical expenses and sustained serious injuries. 
COUNT II: FRAUD 
 8. That Defendant, Stephanie M. Nail, engaged in fraud in an attempt to avoid insurance 
liability to Plaintiffs. 
 9. That Defendant, Stephanie M. Nail, knowingly provided false material 
representations regarding the whereabouts and driver of the vehicle in said collision. 
 10. That said false material representations led her insurance company to wrongly deny 
coverage to Plaintiffs. 

3 
 11. That the Plaintiffs had no knowledge of the true identity and said fraud caused 
consequent and proximate injury to Plaintiffs. 
 12. That Defendants should be subject to significant punitive damages over and above its
insurance limits and the special damages of Plaintiffs for engaging in such behavior that was willful 
and reckless. 
COUNT III: NEGLIGENT ENTRUSTMENT 
 13. Defendant, Stephanie M. Nail’s conduct as related to the police report filed at 23:57 
on April 16, 2021, was intended to deceive the Plaintiffs and conceal the identity of the negligent 
driver. 
 14. At all relevant times, Defendant Stephanie M. Nail was the owner of the vehicle 
involved in the April 16, 2021, collision when her vehicle negligently collided with the vehicle 
occupied by the Plaintiffs. 
 15. Defendant, Stephanie M. Nail, negligently and carelessly entrusted said vehicle to the 
Defendant Demetrius Turner, whom Defendant, Stephanie M. Nail, knew or should have known 
would operate the vehicle in a careless manner. 
 16. Defendant, Stephanie M. Nail’s negligent entrustment of the vehicle was a substantial 
factor in causing the Plaintiffs’ injuries as alleged. 
COUNT IV: BAD FAITH 
 17. The liability of Defendant Allstate’s insured in this instance is clear and the resulting 
medical treatment of Plaintiff, reasonable and necessary, and otherwise, Allstate is obligated to make 
a good faith effort to settle Plaintiff’s claim against its insured and has failed to do so. 
 18. That Defendant Allstate has effectively denied such claim by refusing to make a good 
faith attempt at compromising Plaintiff’s claim against its insured and such denial lacks a reasonable 

4 
basis in law or fact and was made with Allstate’s knowledge that no reasonable basis exists for such 
denial or was made with a reckless disregard for whether such a claim exists and otherwise, Allstate’s 
acts and omissions amount to a bad faith attempt at settling a valid claim on behalf of its insured. 
 19. The acts and omissions of Defendant Allstate in the handling of Plaintiff’s claim 
against its insured were intended to harass and deceive Plaintiff and its insured. 
 20. The acts and omissions of Defendant Allstate in the handling of Plaintiff’s claim 
against its insured was made with oppression, fraud, and malice and further, is conduct that amounts 
to a reckless indifference to its insured and others and is compensable under common law bad faith 
and as a violation of Kentucky statutes and specifically, The Unfair Claims Settlement Practices act, 
for which Allstate is liable to Plaintiff. 
WHEREFORE, Plaintiff, demands: 
 1. Judgment against the Defendants, jointly and individually, in a fair and reasonable amount 
sufficient to compensate Plaintiffs for their damage alleged herein; 
 2. Trial by jury on all issues so triable; 
 3. Plaintiffs’ costs herein incurred; 
 4. Punitive damages to the extent a trier of fact determines the actions of the Defendants to 
have been malicious, oppressive, fraudulent, or reckless; and, 
 4. Any and all other proper relief to which Plaintiffs may appear entitled including but not 
limited to the right to Amend their Complaint.
