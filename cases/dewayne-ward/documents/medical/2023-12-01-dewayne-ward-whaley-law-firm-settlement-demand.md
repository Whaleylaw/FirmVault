1. FACTS & LIABILITY

On August 29, 2023, Mr. Ward was the restrained driver of a vehicle (“Unit 2”) traveling southbound on Charlestown Road near its intersection with the Interstate Highway 265 entrance




ramp in New Albany, Floyd County, Indiana. At the same time, Ms. Robinson was the driver of a vehicle (“Unit 1’) traveling northbound on Charlestown Road, near the same intersection. As Mr. Ward lawfully turned left onto the Interstate Highway 265 entrance ramp on a green light, suddenly and without warning, Ms. Robinson failed to yield to the right of way, and the front of her vehicle violently collided with the front passenger side of Mr. Ward’s vehicle. The force of the impact caused Mr. Ward’s vehicle to spin around, and it came to a rest facing north on Charlestown Road. Upon impact, Mr. Ward’s airbags were immediately deployed, and his body was violently jolted around the interior of his vehicle. The collision resulted in Mr. Ward sustaining significant personal injuries and other damages.

The conditions for establishing liability, in this case, are clear. Ms. Robinson owed a duty of

care to the other drivers on the road. Pursuant to IN Code § 9-21-5-1(a), “[a] person may not drive a vehicle on a highway at a speed greater than is reasonable and prudent under the conditions, having regard to the actual and potential hazards then existing. Speed shall be restricted as necessary to avoid colliding with a person, vehicle, or other conveyance on, near, or entering a

On the date of loss, Ms. Robinson lacked attentiveness, failed to yield to the right of way to Mr. Ward, operated her vehicle at an excessive rate of speed for traffic conditions, failed to keep a proper lookout, and caused a collision with Mr. Ward’s vehicle. 3.4. Past and Future Pain and Suffering

Under Indiana law, historically, the most highly compensable area of damages, in general, is in the area of physical pain and suffering.

There is no question that the August 29, 2023, incident caused Mr. Ward a great deal of physical and emotional pain and suffering. As a result of the incident, Mr. Ward suffered serious injuries that affected his entire life. Mr. Ward experienced difficulty and pain when trying to complete

As a result of Mr. Ward’s physical pain, he experienced difficulty sleeping, which resulted in fatigue during the day. Mr. Ward’s profession as a mail processing clerk was impacted as he experienced pain while performing job-related tasks that required standing, walking, bending, and lifting. Due to these limitations, Mr. Ward was unable to work for a period of time following the incident. Additionally, Mr. Ward suffered from flashbacks of the incident and experienced mood swings, sadness, and feelings of isolation as a result of his injuries.

Simply put, Mr. Ward will be reminded of the August 29, 2023, incident every single day for the rest of his life when he awakens, drives in a car, goes to work, and tries to interact with friends and family or meet new people. The pain and impact of this ordeal will never leave Mr. Ward, and this should remain at the forefront of your consideration as you evaluate Mr. Ward’s significant noneconomic damages.
