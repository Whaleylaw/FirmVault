SUMMARY OF INJURIES
 
 There is no need to fully detail what the medical records reveal. However, in an effort to 
assist your review, the following is a brief summary of the injuries sustained by my client, as well 
as the treatment that she has been forced to endure as a result, to date: 
Shortly after the collision, New Albany Police and EMS arrived on scene. As the EMS 
records reveal, in the collision Ms. Williams sustained significant head trauma, causing her to lose 
consciousness and inciting a seizure. Ms. Williams was immediately transported to University of 
Louisville Hospital for evaluation and treatment. 
 
Upon arrival at the emergency department, Ms. Williams was noted to be dealing with 
significant pain in her head, neck, shoulder, and arm. Following physical examination, it was 
determined that Ashlee would need to undergo a series of imaging. Thankfully, CTs did not show 
any evidence of intracranial bleeding, and x-rays did not show any fractures. At this time, Ms. 
Williams was discharged from care with prescriptions for Skelaxin, Tylenol, and Ibuprofen, and 
instructed to follow up for further treatment in the coming days. 
Thus, the following day, Ms. Williams presented to Starlite Injury care for treatment. As 
these records provide, Ms. Williams had continued pain throughout her head, neck, back, and right 
extremities. Following examination, it was recommended at Ms. Williams undergo a series of 
physical therapy and chiropractic treatments in order to reduce her pain and stiffness and increase 
her function and mobility. 

3 
On September 26, 2023, Ms. Williams presented to Kentucky Pain Associates for treatment 
of her ongoing pain and related symptoms. During this visit, Ms. Williams was diagnosed with 
cervical, thoracic, and lumbar sprain/strains with radiculopathy, myofascial pain of the cervical 
and trapezius muscles, concussion with loss of consciousness, and acute post-traumatic headaches. 
For these conditions, a medication treatment plan was developed. This plan included medications 
such as Celecoxib, Tizanidine, Menthozen Cream, Propranolol, and Norco. 
Over the following weeks, Ms. Williams continued her medication and treatment 
programs. Ultimately, while Ms. Williams continues to suffer from residual pain and stiffness as 
a result of the collision, she follows her home exercise program and treats her pain as needed with 
over-the-counter medications.
