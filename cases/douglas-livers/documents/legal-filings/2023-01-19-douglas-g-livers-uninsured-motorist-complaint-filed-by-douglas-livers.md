COUNT I: NEGLIGENCE AGAINST DEFENDANT LEWIS 
4. On or about January 19, 2023, Defendant Lewis did so negligently and carelessly 
operate a certain 2012 Chevrolet Malibu, Vin No. 1G1ZG5E79CF277276, at the intersection of 
Fern Creek Road and Ferndale Road in Louisville, Kentucky thereby striking Plaintiff’s vehicle. 
5. The negligence of Defendant Lewis in the operation of her vehicle on the date and at 
the place aforesaid was a substantial factor in causing Plaintiff serious and permanent injuries, 
including great pain of body and mind, both past and future. In addition, the Plaintiff has incurred 
medical expenses as a result of the collision and injuries he sustained and will incur future medical 
expenses for the treatment of his injuries. Further, Plaintiff lost time and money from his gainful 
employment, resulting in damages for past lost wages and has had his future ability to labor and 
earn money substantially impaired. 
6. The negligence of Defendant Lewis in the operation of her vehicle on the date and 
place aforesaid was a substantial factor in causing Plaintiff to incur significant expense for property 
damage and for loss of use of his motor vehicle following the collision. 
7. The aforementioned damages incurred by Plaintiff are a sum in excess of the minimal 
amount necessary to confer jurisdiction upon this Court.
8. Upon information and belief obtained after due inquiry, Defendant Lewis was 
uninsured on the date of the collision described herein. 

3 
COUNT II: UNINSURED MOTORIST CLAIM AGAINST DEFENDANT ALLSTATE 
9. Plaintiff restates and realleges the averments in the paragraphs above as if set forth 
fully herein. 
10. Allstate is named as a Defendant herein because upon information and belief it issued 
a policy of insurance, number 830571369, covering or insuring a certain 2016 Chevrolet Silverado, 
VIN 3GCUKREC0GG300212, on behalf of the Plaintiff (“the Policy”) 
11. Upon information and belief, the aforementioned policy was in effect at the time of 
the motor vehicle collision described in this Complaint, and, pursuant to the terms of the policy 
the uninsured motorist protections afforded thereunder would inure to the benefit of the Plaintiff 
in connection with the collision identified herein. 
12. As alleged above, after due inquiry, Defendant Lewis appears to have been uninsured 
on the date of the collision at issue herein. 
13. Defendant Allstate has been notified of its potential obligation under its uninsured 
motorist policy, and Allstate is called upon to provide such uninsured motorist coverage to the 
extent that the damages sustained or recovered by the Plaintiff are not recoverable from Defendant 
Lewis.
