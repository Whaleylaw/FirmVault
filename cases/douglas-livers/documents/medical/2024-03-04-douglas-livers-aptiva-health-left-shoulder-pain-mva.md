This 58-year-old male is here for left shoulder pain and left foot pain. The patient was
involved in an MVA. This was on 1/19/23. Patient was the driver. He was restrained. No
airbag deployment. Patient stated that he was towing a trailer. Someone hit the back of
the trailer causing it to side-swipe the vehicle he was driving. He stated that he almost
rolled over, but, fortunately, he did not. Patient stated that after the incident, he talked with
his employer and he was told to follow up in a rehab facility. He saw a chiropractor. The
chiropractor told him that he only deals with the neck and the back. He stated that since
the accident, which was on 1/19/23, he has not had anybody look at his shoulder or foot.
Patient stated that his shoulder is very painful. He is not able to fully move or raise his arm
with the pain. He added that certain movements are worse than others. He added at times
his left foot feels like it is “on fire.” He denies any previous surgery to the shoulder or foot.
No problem with the shoulder or foot prior to the accident.
