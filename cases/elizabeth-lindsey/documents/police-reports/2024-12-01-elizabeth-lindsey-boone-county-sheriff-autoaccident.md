On December 1st, 2024 this Deputy responded to an injury accident involving two vehicles that had
collided head on.
Unit 1 driver stated he was in the fast lane on Mt. Zion Road and was going straight through the
intersection, and doesn't remember anything else other than right after the collision. Unit 1 driver reported
pain to his collar bone and right wrist. Unit 1 driver was driven to Children's Hospital in Cincinnati. Following
up, this Deputy was advised by Unit 1 driver's mother that he had a broken collar bone. Unit 1 passenger
reported pain to the chest, and was also driven by family to Children's Hospital in Cincinnati. Unit 1
passenger suffered bruised lungs, deep tissue bruising in various areas on his body, along with other minor
scrapes and bruises. Unit 1 vehicle sustained severe front end damage, and was towed by Rider's Towing.
Unit 2 driver stated that he had the green arrow to turn left from Mt. Zion Road into the frontage road where
Steak N' Shake was when a white SUV collided head on with Unit 1. Unit 1 driver sustained a minor
laceration to his right leg above the ankle, but refused transport. Unit 1 front passenger, reported chest pain
and road in the back of the ambulance with her two juvenile children. She is being checked out at the
University Hospital in Cincinnati, but as of this writing no injuries are reported. Unit 1 passenger (juvenile)
who was sitting directly behind the driver sustained lacerations to his spleen and liver, along with other
minor bruising. He was transported to Children's Hospital in Cincinnati by Florence EMS where he is being
treated. Unit 1 passenger (juvenile) who was on the driver side in the third row seat suffered minor bruising,
but complained of chest pain on scene. He was also transported to Children's Hospital in Cincinnati. Unit 2
vehicle sustained severe front end damage, and was towed by Complete Towing.
The witness, who was standing outside of Steak N' Shake reported that Unit 2 vehicle had the green
arrows to turn left, and the Unit 1 vehicle ran the red light.
