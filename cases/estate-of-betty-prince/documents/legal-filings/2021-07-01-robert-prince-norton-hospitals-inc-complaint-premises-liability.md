Comes now the Plaintiff, Robert Prince for the Estate of Betty Prince, by and through 
counsel, and for her cause of action against the Defendant, Norton Hospitals, Inc.,states as follows:
FACTUAL AND JURISDICTIONAL AVERMENTS TO ALL COUNTS
1. At the time of the injury on or about July 14, 2020 Betty Prince, and her husband Robert 
Prince, were residents of Trimble County, Kentucky.
2. At the time of the injury, Defendant was a business registered in this Commonwealth as 
Norton Hospitals, Inc. and was operating and conducting business in Jefferson County, 
Louisville, Kentucky.
3. The injury which is the subject of this Complaint occurred in Jefferson County, Louisville, 
Kentucky.
4. Plaintiff’s damages exceed the jurisdictional minimum limit of this Court.
COUNT I: PREMISES LIABILITY
5. Plaintiff herein re-alleges and incorporates paragraphs 1-4 verbatim by this specific 
reference.

6. On or about July 14, 2020, Defendant, Norton Hospitals, Inc., failed to maintain its 
premises in a reasonably safe condition, and negligently caused the Plaintiff injury.
7. Plaintiff sustained injuries when she fell on Defendant’s premises.
8. Defendant had knowledge of the premises which had been negligently offered to invitees.
9. Defendant had exclusive control and management of the subject premises.
10. Defendant breached their duty of care owed to Betty Prince as an invitee in and on their 
premises.
11. Defendant is liable for Betty Princes’ injuries under the doctrine of res ipsa loquitur.
12. As a direct and proximate result of the aforementioned negligence, Plaintiff was caused to 
sustain severe injuries including medical expenses, both past and future, physical and 
mental suffering, lost wages, impairment of her ability to labor and earn money, increased 
risk of future injury and harm, and loss of enjoyment of life.
COUNT 11: MEDICAL NEGLIGENCE
13. Plaintiff herein re-alleges and incorporates paragraphs 1-12 verbatim by this specific 
reference.
14. That at all times relevant hereto Betty Prince was a patient of the Defendant, Norton 
Hospitals, Inc.
15. That Defendant, Norton Hospitals, Inc., by and through its employees, agents, servants
and/or contractors was responsible for the care, treatment, security and safety of i’s patient, 
Betty Prince. Defendant, Norton Hospitals, Inc., is responsible for the actions and 
negligence of its employees, agents, servants and/or contractors, pursuant to the doctrines 
of master/servant, Respondeat Superior, agency, and all other applicable law.

16. That Defendant, Norton Hospitals, Inc., by and through it’s employees, agents, servants 
and/or contractors negligently, recklessly and/or grossly negligently caused and/or 
permitted Betty Prince to fall as a result of its failure to adequately care for Betty Prince. 
17. That Defendant, Norton Hospitals, Inc., knew or should have reasonably known Betty 
Prince was patient with a high risk of falling.
18. That as a direct and proximate result of the aforesaid negligence, recklessness, and/or gross 
negligence of Defendant, Norton Hospitals, In., by and through its employees, agents, 
servants and/or contractors and of its own accord, Defendant, Norton Hospitals, Inc., is 
responsible for payment of damages to Betty Prince’s personal injuries; past and future 
medical bills; past and future pain and suffering; loss of enjoyment of life, past and future 
lost wages; and impairment of ability to work and earn money, all in excess of the of the 
minimum jurisdictional requirements of this honorable court.
19. That as a result of the negligent, reckless, grossly negligent, and vicarious liability of itself 
and its employees, agents, servants and/or contractors, Plaintiff is entitled to punitive 
damages pursuant to Kentucky law against Defendant, Norton Hospitals, Inc.
