ARTICLE I. PURPOSE OF MEDICAL STAFF
SECTION 1.1 UNIFIED MEDICAL STAFF
The Medical Staff of Norton Healthcare Louisville Hospital System is a unified Medical Staff
comprised of all medical and osteopathic physicians, podiatrists, licensed clinical psychologists
and dentists privileged to attend patients at a Division Hospital within the Norton Healthcare 
Louisville Hospital System and who have been or will be appointed to the Medical Staff of the 
Hospital System. Each Division Hospital shall operate under these Bylaws and under their 
respective Division Rules and Regulations.
SECTION 1.2 PURPOSE
The purpose of this Medical Staff is to unite qualified physicians, podiatrists, licensed Clinical 
Psychologists (PhD, Ed.D., Psy.D.) and dentists who practice in the Norton Healthcare 
Louisville Hospital System and within its Hospital Divisions into a cohesive body to promote 
good care throughout the Hospital System, to provide the ethical and professional standards for 
its members, and to offer advice, recommendations, and input to the Chief Executive Officer and 
the Board of Trustees. The purpose of these Bylaws is to provide the organization of the 
Medical Staff and create a framework within which Medical Staff members, committees, clinical 
Services and other groups can act within a reasonable degree of freedom, confidence, and 
independence.
The Medical Staff is responsible to the Board of Trustees for the quality and safety of medical 
care, treatment and Services provided to patients. 
ARTICLE II. MEDICAL STAFF MEMBERSHIP
SECTION 2.1 NATURE OF MEDICAL STAFF MEMBERSHIP
Membership on the Medical Staff of Norton Healthcare Louisville Hospital System is a privilege 
which shall be extended only to professionally competent physicians, podiatrists, licensed 
Clinical Psychologists (PhD, Ed.D., Psy.D.) and dentists who continuously meet the 
qualifications, standards, and requirements set forth in these Bylaws, in Division Operating 
Rules, Rules and Regulations and associated policies of the Medical Staff and Norton Healthcare 
Louisville Hospital System. Nothing contained in these Bylaws, nor the fact of Medical Staff 
membership shall be deemed to create, nor does it create, any type of employment status or 
relationship between any member of the Medical Staff and the Hospital.
SECTION 2.2 QUALIFICATIONS FOR MEMBERSHIP
A. Initial Appointment. Applicants for initial appointment and renewal of Medical 
Staff membership must, at a minimum, demonstrate and document:
1. Current, valid, unconditional Kentucky license to practice medicine, 
podiatry, clinical psychology or dentistry.

SECTION 2.4 CREDENTIALING, APPOINTMENT, AND CLINICAL PRIVILEGES
A. Appointment Policies and Procedures. Upon the initial adoption and approval of 
these Bylaws, all current members in good standing of a Division Hospital
Medical Staff shall be appointed to the Medical Staff in the category of 
membership corresponding to their voting status on the Medical Staff within their 
Division Hospital and with such clinical privileges as they then enjoy within their 
respective Hospitals. Thereafter, initial appointments and reappointments to the 
Medical Staff and the grant of clinical privileges shall be made by the Board of 
Trustees in accordance with these Bylaws.
The Board of Trustees shall act on appointments, reappointments, and clinical 
privileges only after there has been a recommendation from the System MEC. 
Before making its recommendation, the System MEC shall have received and 
considered the recommendation of the System Credentials Committee. A 
pediatric credentials committee will make recommendations to the System 
Credentials Committee regarding appointment, reappointment and clinical 
privileges for those practitioners with pediatric privileges. 
B. Credentialing Criteria and Procedures. Except as otherwise provided herein or in 
Division Operating Rules, additional criteria and procedures pertaining to the 
credentialing process are contained in Articles VIII-XV of these Bylaws now in 
effect or as may be adopted in the future pursuant to these Bylaws.
Recommendations regarding the granting, renewal, or modification of clinical 
privileges shall be governed by these Bylaws.
C. Duration. Appointments to the Medical Staff and the granting of clinical 
privileges shall be for no more than twenty-four (24) calendar months and are 
subject to renewal in accordance with these Bylaws.

SECTION 2.5 STAFF DUES
A. Annual Dues. Annual Medical Staff dues shall be governed by the most recent 
action which has been recommended by the System MEC, after consultation with 
Division MECs, and adopted by majority vote of the active staff. The Medical 
Staff office shall notify each staff member in writing of any contemplated change 
in Medical Staff dues at least 21 days before a vote on such proposed changes 
takes place.
B. Emeritus. Emeritus staff members will not be required to pay dues.
C. Failure to Pay Dues. Dues shall be due and payable within 30 days of request. 
Failure to pay dues after three notices, (the third of which will be delivered via 
certified mail and may include a late fee) shall be construed as a voluntary 
resignation from the staff.
SECTION 2.6 RESPONSIBILITIES OF EACH MEMBER
A. Continuous Care. Each Medical Staff member must provide or arrange for the 
provision of appropriate, timely, and continuous care of his/her patients. She/he is 
not responsible for the actions of other practitioners, allied health professionals 
(unless under his/her supervision), or hospital system employees.
B. Performance Improvement. Each Medical Staff member must participate, if 
assigned, in relevant quality/performance improvement activities and in 
discharging other staff functions as may be required from time to time.
C. Hold Harmless. Each Medical Staff member agrees to waive any claim against, 
to release from liability, and to hold harmless all participants in peer review 
activities and actions involving the member.
D. On-Call Coverage. Each Medical Staff member must participate in the on-call 
coverage of the emergency Service and other coverage programs as required by 
the Operating Policies of the primary hospital in which the member renders 
professional Services.
E. Bylaws. Each Medical Staff member must abide by these Bylaws, rules, 
regulations, and policies of the Medical Staff, the Operating Rules of each 
Hospital Division in which the member renders professional Services, and the 
policies of the Hospital System which pertain to Medical Staff members to the 
extent such System policies are not in conflict with, superseded by, or restrictive 
of rights conferred by these Bylaws.
F. Code of Conduct. Each Medical Staff member will abide by the provisions of the 
Code of Conduct: Appropriate, Intimidating and Disruptive Behavior policy.

SECTION 2.7 RIGHTS OF EACH MEMBER
A. Written Notice. In the event a Medical Staff member is unable to resolve a 
difficulty working with a Division Medical Executive Committee, that member 
may, upon presentation of a written notice, meet with the System MEC to discuss 
the issue.
B. Recall Election. Any active Medical Staff member has the right to initiate a recall 
election of a Medical Staff officer by presenting to the System MEC a petition 
signed by at least 10% of the members of the active Medical Staff. Upon 
presentation of such valid petition, the System MEC will schedule a special 
general staff meeting for purposes of discussing the issue and, if appropriate, 
entertain a “no confidence” vote, and if appropriate, follow the removal from 
office process.
C. General Staff Meeting. Upon presentation of a petition signed by 10% of the 
active members of the Medical Staff, the System MEC will call a general staff 
meeting for the specific purpose addressed by the petitioners. No business other 
than that detailed in the petition may be transacted.
D. Petition. Any Medical Staff member may raise a challenge to any rule or policy 
established by the System MEC by submitting a petition signed by 10% of the 
active Medical Staff members. When such petition has been received, the System 
MEC will either: (1) provide the petitioners with information clarifying the intent 
of such rule, regulation or policy and, to the extent still necessary; or (2) schedule 
a meeting with the petitioners to discuss the issues.
E. Disciplinary Action. Sections A through D do not apply to issues involving 
disciplinary action, denial of requests for appointment to the Medical Staff, to 
clinical privileges, or to any other matter relating to individual membership, 
credentialing actions, or peer review procedures, recourse for which is the subject 
of Section F below.
F. Right to a Hearing. Any member, as well as an applicant for membership on the 
Medical Staff, has a right to a hearing and appeal pursuant to Article XXIII of 
these Bylaws.

but they shall not hold office or vote.

ARTICLE XIII. EMERGENCY / DISASTER PRIVILEGES
SECTION 13.1 EMERGENCY PRIVILEGES
A. Emergency Definition. An emergency is defined as a condition which would 
likely result in serious permanent damage to a patient or in which the life of the 
patient is in immediate danger and any delay in administering treatment would 
materially add to that danger.
In an emergency, practitioners registered and legally licensed to practice in the 
State of Kentucky shall, to the degree permitted by their license, and regardless of 
staff membership or privileges, be permitted to do everything possible to save the 
life of a patient, using every facility of the Hospital necessary, and including 
calling for assistance of any consultation necessary or desirable. When an 
emergency situation no longer exists, such practitioner must request temporary 
clinical privileges necessary to continue to treat the patient as provided herein or 
in the event such privileges are denied or he/she does not desire to request 
privileges, the patient shall be assigned to an appropriate member of the Active 
staff.
Emergency privileges granted under these circumstances will be specific to the 
particular patient’s treatment for the single admission and will not apply for any 
subsequent, routine admissions. Physicians who request emergency privileges for 
this purpose more frequently than three (3) times in a single twelve (12) month 
period will be required to apply for privileges at Norton Healthcare or to refer 
their patients to a physician with appropriate privileges.
SECTION 13.2 DISASTER PRIVILEGES
A. Emergency Plan. In the event of a disaster, any physician, oral surgeon or 
independent allied health professional who would otherwise be eligible to apply 
for clinical privileges under the guidelines established in the Medical Staff
Bylaws, may be granted disaster privileges by the Hospital Chief Administrative 
Officer, or the Medical Staff President, or their designees. A disaster is defined as 
a natural or man-made event that significantly disrupts the environment of care ( 
for example, damage to the organization's buildings and grounds due to severe 
winds, storms, or earthquakes); that significantly disrupts care and treatment (for 
example loss of utilities such as water, power or telephones, due to floods, civil 
disturbances, accidents in the organization or its community); or that results in 
sudden, significantly changed or increased demands to the organization's Services 
(for example, weapons of mass destruction attack, building collapse, or plane 
crash in the organization's community). The ability to issue disaster privileges 
would exist only in the event that one of the Division's emergency plans has been 
activated and the immediate patient care needs cannot be met with existing staff.

SECTION 16.1 GROUNDS FOR LEAVE OF ABSENCE 
A member of the Medical Staff may request a leave of absence from his/her responsibilities as a

by the practitioner for a qualified practitioner to assume responsibility for care of the member’s 
patients at the hospital and for staff call. Except in extraordinary circumstances and as approved 
by the President of the Division Hospital Medical Staff and the Chief Administrative Officer (or 
their respective designees), no more than one (1) leave of absence may be taken during any

SECTION 23.4 HEARING PROCEDURE
A. Discovery. There is no right to pre-hearing discovery in connection with the 
hearing, including access to peer review information concerning other 
practitioners on the Medical Staff. However, the Practitioner requesting the 
hearing shall be entitled, upon specific request, to the following, subject to a 
stipulation signed by the Practitioner that such documents shall be maintained as 
confidential and shall not be disclosed or used for any purpose outside the hearing 
and that the production of such documents shall not constitute a waiver of the 
protection afforded by state peer review statutes:
1. Copies of or reasonable access to all patient medical records referred to by 
any party in any pre-hearing statement or proceedings.
2. Reports of experts relied upon by the Medical Executive Committee or the 
Board of Trustees in taking the Adverse Action or making the 
recommendation that is the subject matter of the hearing.
3. Copies of redacted relevant committee or Service minutes (this provision 
does not constitute a waiver of the protection under state peer review 
statutes).
4. Copies of any other documents relied upon by the Medical Executive 
Committee or the Board of Trustees in taking the Adverse Action or 
making the recommendation that is the subject matter of the hearing.
All copies furnished shall be at the Practitioner’s expense. The extent of any 
other discovery shall be mutually agreed upon by the parties and approved and 
overseen by the Hearing Officer.
B. Pre-Hearing Proceedings. 
1. Pre-Hearing Conference. The Hearing Officer may require the parties to 
participate, through counsel, in a pre-hearing conference for purposes of 
resolving all procedural questions in advance of the hearing. In addition, 
the Hearing Officer shall establish deadlines by which each party is 
required to provide a list of the persons that party intends to call as 
witnesses at the hearing and to identify any patient records or other

hearing. The witness list of either party may, in the discretion of the 
hearing officer, be supplemented or amended at any time during the course 
of the hearing, provided that notice of the change is given to the other 
party. The hearing officer shall have the authority to limit the number of 
witnesses.
2. Postponement. Postponement of a hearing beyond the time set forth 
herein may be granted by the Hearing Officer upon the written request of 
either party, and only upon good cause shown.
C. Failure to Appear. The personal presence of the Practitioner is required. Failure, 
without good cause, of the Practitioner requesting the hearing to appear and 
proceed at a hearing shall be deemed to constitute a waiver of the Practitioner’s 
right to a hearing and of any other objection by the Practitioner and therefore shall 
constitute a voluntary acceptance of the recommendation or action pending.
D. Burden of Proceeding. The entity which took or recommended the Adverse 
Action which occasioned the hearing must come forward with evidence in support 
of its recommendation or action. Once this obligation is fulfilled, the Practitioner 
requesting the hearing must come forward with evidence to refute the 
recommendation or action taken which occasioned the hearing. 
The Hearing Panel shall uphold the action or recommendation which prompted 
the hearing unless it finds that the Practitioner has proven that the action or 
recommendation was not supported by substantial evidence from which 
reasonable persons could conclude it was intended to further quality healthcare. 
E. Rights At Hearing.

reasonable limits determined by the Hearing Officer and to waiver by the 
other party:

b. To call and examine witnesses to the extent available.
c. To present evidence considered relevant by the Hearing

to introduce exhibits.
d. To cross-examine any witness on any matter relevant to the 
issues and to rebut any evidence.

closed. The Hearing Officer shall establish a schedule for filing of written closing 
statements and/or proposed findings by the parties within a reasonable time after 
the completion of the transcript of the hearing. Upon the filing of such written 
closing statements and/or such proposed findings, the hearing shall be closed. The 
Hearing Panel shall thereupon, at a time convenient to itself, conduct its 
deliberations outside the presence of the parties. Upon the conclusion of its 
deliberations, the hearing shall be declared finally adjourned.

SECTION 23.6 APPELLATE REVIEW
A. Request for Appellate Review. Within fourteen (14) days after receiving notice as 
set forth in section 23.5 B hereof, either party may request an appellate review. The 
request must be in writing, to the Chief Administrative Officer, and sent by certified 
mail, return receipt requested. The request must contain a brief statement of the 
reasons for the appellate review. If appellate review is not requested within the 
fourteen (14) day period, the right to appellate review shall be deemed to have been 
waived and the parties shall be deemed to have accepted the recommendation of 
the Hearing Panel.
B. Grounds for Appeal. The following shall be the only grounds for an appellate 
review panel to reverse a Hearing Panel report in support of an Adverse Action:
1. Substantial failure to comply with the System Medical Staff Bylaws in 
conducting the hearing and related proceedings.

grounds for appeal. The appellate review panel shall not act as a hearing 
panel and rehear evidentiary presentations. 
2. Conduct of Review. Each party shall have the right to present a written 
statement in support of its position on appeal which must be submitted to 
the appellate review panel at least ten (10) days prior to the appellate 
review. The written statement must be served on the opposing party. 
Parties may submit a response to the initial written statement of an 
opposing party at least five (5) business days prior to the appellate review. 
The appellate review panel, at its sole discretion, may allow a 
representative of each party to appear personally and make an oral 
presentation at the appellate review. New or additional information not 
raised during the hearing, in the Hearing Panel report, or otherwise 
reflected in the record, may not be introduced during the appellate review,

ARTICLE XXIV. HISTORY AND PHYSICAL EXAMINATION
A medical history and physical examination (“H&P”) is completed and documented for each 
patient no more than 30 days before or 24 hours after admission or registration, but prior to 
surgery or a procedure requiring anesthesia Services. An updated examination of the patient, 
including any changes in the patients’ condition, must be completed and documented within 24 
hours after admission or registration when the history and physical examination are completed 
within 30 days before admission or registration. 
There must be a complete history and physical examination, and an update, if applicable, in the 
medical record of the patient prior to surgery or a procedure requiring anesthesia Services, 
except in emergencies. 
A physician, dentist, podiatrist, oral surgeon, or nurse practitioner (APRN/CRNA) may complete 
an H&P as privileges or job descriptions allow. Physician assistants (PA), and /or residents may 
also complete an H&P co-signed by an attending physician. Nurses, Medical Students and other 
appropriately credentialed individuals acting within the scope of their practice may assist in 
collecting and documenting components of the H&P co-signed by the attending physician.

A prenatal record may be utilized as the H&P provided it is updated as outlined above. A 
consult report may also be used as an H&P so long as it meets the content and timeframe 
requirements and the attending practitioner confirms the information and findings, updates 
results as defined above, and signs and dates the consult indicating that it serves as the H&P.
A history and physical completed by a practitioner not holding privileges in the facility may be 
used provided it was performed within 30 days. In this situation a practitioner who is

with updated or revised information as an attestation that it is current.

SECTION 26.2 AMENDMENT
A. Proposed Amendments. Proposed amendments to these Bylaws may be submitted 
to the System MEC for its consideration by the President of the Medical Staff, any 
Division President, any Division MEC, or by any standing or special committee 
appointed for that purpose. The voting members of the Medical Staff may propose 
to adopt a rule, regulation or policy, or an amendment thereto. Ideally the Medical 
Staff will first communicate the proposal to the System MEC. 
After consideration of a proposed amendment, the System MEC may decide: (1) 
whether to reject the proposal; (2) to submit it to the Medical Staff for a vote; or, 
(3) to adopt it by the procedure described in Subsection B below.
If the System MEC rejects the proposed rule, regulation or amendment, and it was 
submitted by the voting members of the Medical Staff, the Medical Staff may 
propose the rule, regulation, policy or amendment directly to the Board of 
Trustees. In this situation, or if the proposal was not initially communicated to the 
System MEC, the Medical Staff must notify the System MEC of its intent to 
submit directly to the Board of Trustees, so that the System MEC will have the 
opportunity for comment. If indicated by the situation a joint conference 
committee, as specified in Article VI, may be convened to attempt resolution of 
the issues. 
B. Procedure for System MEC Adoption on Behalf of Medical Staff. Proposed 
amendments to these Bylaws will be reviewed and may be adopted by a majority 
vote of the System MEC provided the following procedures have been followed:
1. At least twenty-one (21) days before the System MEC’s vote, the 
proposed amendment shall be distributed to all active Medical Staff
members or posted in conspicuous places throughout the Division 
Hospitals where they may be reviewed by the members.
2. If fewer than ten percent (10%) of the active Medical Staff members 
submit a written objection to the proposed amendment to the President of 
the Medical Staff, the amendment shall be deemed to be adopted by the 
System MEC on behalf of the Medical Staff.
3. If ten percent (10%) or more of the active Medical Staff members submit 
to the President of the Medical Staff a written objection to the proposed 
amendment, a special meeting of the Medical Staff shall be called to 
review and vote on the proposed amendment, or a vote by written ballot
may be taken, as provided in Section 8.5 of these Bylaws. The vote of the 
Medical Staff shall be conclusive.
