INTERROGATORY NO. 3: State in specific detail each and every fact which Plaintiff and 
Plaintiff’s counsel claim constitutes evidence of negligence by Norton Hospitals, Inc., in this 
action, including also the name of the specific agent, servant or employee of Norton Hospitals, Inc. 
involved and the date, time and place of each alleged incident or occurrence of such negligence as 
well as each specific standard of care which each such person allegedly violated including the 
source of each standard. 
ANSWER: 
Objection, this interrogatory calls for a legal conclusion or conclusions. 
Notwithstanding said objection please refer to Plaintiff’s Complaint. Without 
waiving said objection, Plaintiff asserts generally that the Defendant was 
negligent and breached its duty of care to Mrs. Prince by failing to recognize 
when a patient is at high risk for falls. 
The standard of care requires any professional working in a health care 
facility to recognize, be aware of and take into consideration all risks a patient 
demonstrates that would increase their risk of falling. The Norton Hospital 
Inc. staff failed to meet the standard of care by failing to implement proper 
fall precautions for Mrs. Prince, up to and including failing to notify a health 
care professional to aid with her mobility. The standard of care requires ‘any’ 
professional working in a health care facility to ensure fall precautions are in 
place for all patients in the facility, particularly for those at high risk for falls 
including the elderly, who have demonstrated unsteadiness and weakness. The 
standard of care requires a professional in ‘any’ area of a health care facility 
to notify a health care professional to aid with patients who are at risk of falls. 
Allowing the health care professional to provide contact guard assist and/or 
stand by assist or the provision of a wheelchair when transferring an elderly 
patient at risk for falls and/or who are unfamiliar with the location. 
For further information please refer to the Affidavit of Donna S. Jones, RN.
