 DIVISION ONE (1)
 JUDGE ERIC HANER

The Estate of BETTY PRINCE PLAINTIFF
v. RESPONSES TO PLAINTIFF’S SECOND SET OF INTERROGATORIES
AND REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION OF DOCUMENTS
NORTON HOSPITALS, INC. DEFENDANT
 * * * * * * * * 
Comes the Defendant, Norton Hospitals, Inc. (“Norton”), by counsel, and for its Responses 
to Plaintiff’s Second Set of Interrogatories and Requests for Production of Documents, states as 
follows: 
INTERROGATORIES
INTERROGATORY NO. 1: Please identify the individual(s) answering these Interrogatories by 
name, title, and address. 
ANSWER: 
Jessica Leasor
Director of Care Coordination, Patient Safety Officer
Norton Hospital 
200 E. Chestnut St. 
Louisville, KY 40202 
These answers were prepared with the assistance of undersigned counsel.
INTERROGATORY NO. 2: Please identify any and all falls resulting from injury of any form 
which occurred in the second-floor outpatient registration area of Norton Hospital downtown (as 
identified in Lucretia Vinson’s deposition pages 21-26) in the five (5) years preceding the fall at 
issue herein. 
ANSWER: Objection. The Defendant objects to this Interrogatory on the grounds that it is
overly broad, unduly burdensome, and not reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of 

2
admissible evidence. The Defendant further objects to this Interrogatory to the extent it seeks 
Protected Health Information prohibited from disclosure under HIPAA. 45 C.F.R. § 164.502
INTERROGATORY NO. 3: Please state whether Norton Hospitals maintains surveillance 
footage of the second-floor outpatient registration area of Norton Hospital downtown (as identified 
in Lucretia Vinson’s deposition pages 21-26) and/or the area where the incident at issue in this 
litigation occurred. 
ANSWER: Objection. The Defendant objects to this Interrogatory on the grounds that it is
overly broad, unduly burdensome, and not reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of 
admissible evidence. Without waiving objection, security cameras exist at the top of the 
entrance-way escalators, near second-floor registration. There are no security cameras 
behind the registration door, where the fall is alleged to have occurred.
INTERROGATORY NO. 4: If your Answer to Interrogatory No. 3 is in the affirmative, please 
describe the standard procedures and protocols employed by Norton Hospitals for the storage, 
retention, and preservation of surveillance footage captured by the cameras in the second-floor 
outpatient registration area of Norton Hospital downtown (as identified in Lucretia Vinson’s 
deposition pages 21-26). 
ANSWER: Objection. The Defendant objects to this Interrogatory on the grounds that it is
overly broad, unduly burdensome, and not reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of 
admissible evidence. Without waiving objection, please refer to Interrogatory Answers Nos. 
3, 5, 6, and 7. 
INTERROGATORY NO. 5: Please state whether any surveillance footage related to incident at 
issue in this litigation was captured by the cameras in the second-floor outpatient registration area 
of Norton Hospital downtown (as identified in Lucretia Vinson’s deposition pages 21-26) on July 
14, 2020. If so, please specify the exact date and time range of the footage and indicate whether 
it has been preserved or retained in any form. 
ANSWER: The alleged fall occurred outside of surveillance camera view, and thus no 
footage was captured. 

3
INTERROGATORY NO. 6: If any surveillance footage existed but is no longer available, please 
provide a detailed explanation as to the reason(s) for its unavailability, including but not limited 
to any procedures for automatic deletion or overwriting of footage, any incidents of equipment 
malfunction or failure, and intentional deletion or alteration of footage, or any other circumstances 
resulting in its absence. 
ANSWER: Objection. The Defendant objects to this Interrogatory on the grounds that it is
overly broad, unduly burdensome, and not reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of 
admissible evidence. The Defendant further objects to the extent this Interrogatory asserts 
or implies any wrongdoing on the part of Norton regarding surveillance footage storage. 
Without waiving objection, no footage of the subject incident ever existed.
INTERROGATORY NO. 7: If any efforts were made to locate or retrieve the surveillance 
footage following the incident at issue in this litigation, please describe in detail the steps taken 
and the results of those efforts. 
ANSWER: Objection. The Defendant objects to this Interrogatory on the grounds that it is
overly broad, unduly burdensome, and not reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of 
admissible evidence. Without waiving objection, not applicable. 
INTERROGATORY NO. 8: Please identify any and all individuals who were in a position 
and/or had the authority to identify and/or label Plaintiff as a fall risk on the date of the incident at 
issue in this litigation.
ANSWER: Objection. The Defendant objects to this Interrogatory on the grounds that it is
overly broad, unduly burdensome, and not reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of 
admissible evidence. The Defendant further objects to this Interrogatory as vague. Without 
waiving objection, the Plaintiff was in the process of registration at the time of the fall, and 
thus she had not been evaluated by any healthcare provider regarding her fall risk.
