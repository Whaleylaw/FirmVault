Dear Mr. Nafziger, 
After reviewing your client’s recent discovery responses, I have noticed a number of 
deficiencies and/or objections for which I will need some clarification: 
- Interrogatory No. 2: Interrogatory No. 2 asks for information regarding any other falls 
in the specific registration area within the last five years. In response, your client stated 
that the request is overly broad and unduly burdensome and seeks health information 
protected by HIPAA. Please advise what section and/or portion of HIPAA would be 
violated by disclosure of other instances of individuals falling in this specific area within 
the last five years. 
- Document Request No. 2: Document Request No. 2 asks for specific compliance or inservice training documentation related to Ms. Vinson’s role in the registration department. 
In response, you objected that the request was overly broad and there were no documents 
identified on page 17 of her deposition. However, this request is for specific training for 
Ms. Vinson’s specific role at Norton. Respectfully, Ms. Vinson testified in her deposition 
on page 17 that she undergoes compliance training yearly, and such training is different 
depending upon one’s role within Norton. Please provide such compliance or in-service 
training documentation at your earliest possible convenience. 
- Document Request No. 4: Document Request No. 4 asks for policies specific to the 
registration department and its staff as identified on page 40 of Lucretia Vinson’s 
deposition. In response, you objected on the grounds that the requested documentation is 
overly broad and unduly burdensome. Specifically, Ms. Vinson stated that “There are
