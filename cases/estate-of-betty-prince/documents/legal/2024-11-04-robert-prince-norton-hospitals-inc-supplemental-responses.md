 * * * * * * * * 
Comes the Defendant, Norton Hospitals, Inc. (“Norton”), by counsel, and for its
Supplemental Responses to Plaintiff’s Second Set of Interrogatories and Requests for Production 
of Documents, states as follows:
INTERROGATORIES
INTERROGATORY NO. 2: Please identify any and all falls resulting from injury of any form 
which occurred in the second-floor outpatient registration area of Norton Hospital downtown (as 
identified in Lucretia Vinson’s deposition pages 21-26) in the five (5) years preceding the fall at 
issue herein. 
ANSWER: Objection. The Defendant objects to this Interrogatory on the grounds that it is 
overly broad, unduly burdensome, and not reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of 
2 admissible evidence. The Defendant further objects to this Interrogatory to the extent it 
seeks Protected Health Information prohibited from disclosure under HIPAA. 45 C.F.R. § 
164.502. 
SUPPLEMENTAL ANSWER: Subject to the preceding objection, and pursuant to the 
Court’s October 4, 2024 Discovery Order, Norton is aware of and identifies two falls as 
responsive to this Interrogatory. The first occurred on November 29, 2018. The second 
occurred on December 11, 2019. 
INTERROGATORY NO. 6: If any surveillance footage existed but is no longer available, please 
provide a detailed explanation as to the reason(s) for its unavailability, including but not limited 
to any procedures for automatic deletion or overwriting of footage, any incidents of equipment 

2

resulting in its absence. 
ANSWER: Objection. The Defendant objects to this Interrogatory on the grounds that it is 
overly broad, unduly burdensome, and not reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of 
admissible evidence. The Defendant further objects to the extent this Interrogatory asserts 
or implies any wrongdoing on the part of Norton regarding surveillance footage storage. 
Without waiving objection, no footage of the subject incident ever existed. 
SUPPLEMENTAL ANSWER: As the Defendant has previously indicated, the Defendant is 
not in possession of any video footage of the Plaintiff at the hospital on the day of the fall. 
REQUEST NO. 2: Please produce any and all compliance, in-service, or other continuing training
