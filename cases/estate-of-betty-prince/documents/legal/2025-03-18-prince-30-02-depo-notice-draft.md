<u>**NOTICE **</u><u>**TO TAKE CR 30.02(6) DEPOSITION OF**</u>

<u>**CORPORATE REPRESENTATIVE**</u>

*** *** ***

Please take notice that on Tuesday, April 22, 2025, at 11:00 a.m., pursuant to CR 30.02(6), the Plaintiff, by counsel, will take the deposition of Defendant Norton Hospitals, Inc. by and through said Defendant’s authorized designee. Unless otherwise agreed as to location or means, said deposition will be held at Defendant’s counsel’s law office, located at 716 West Main Street, Suite 300, Louisville, KY 40202.

Pursuant to CR 30.02(6), Defendant Norton Hospitals, Inc. is required to designate and fully prepare one or more officers, directors, managing agents, or other persons who consent to testify on behalf of Defendant and whom Defendant will fully prepare to testify regarding the following designated matters and as to such information that is known or reasonably available to Defendant’s organization.

The deposition will be taken pursuant to Rule 30.02 of the Kentucky Rules of Civil Procedure before an officer duly authorized to administer oaths, by stenographic and/or video means, and will be taken for purposes of discovery, cross-examination, preservation of evidence, impeachment, for use at trial and for all other permissible purposes. The oral examination will continue until completed.  

Examination is requested on the following matters from the designated Defense officer, representative or managing agent, or other designee with the most knowledge as to each of the following areas of inquiry, after and following due inquiry into each area and full preparation for testimony in this matter consistent with the applicable rules.

1. The identity of any and all individuals with knowledge of the facts leading up to, concerning, or surrounding Betty Prince’s fall on July 14, 2020, in the registration area of Norton Hospital, located at 200 E. Chestnut Street, Louisville, KY 40202. The deponent should be prepared to identify all individuals with knowledge and describe what knowledge they have pertaining to the incident. This includes but is not limited to the identity of any individuals present or identified in any documentation produced in the course of discovery in the instant case. 
2. Any and all policies, procedures, instructions, guidelines, or training materials in effect at the time of the incident at issue in this litigation addressing the identification, assessment, and prevention of patient falls, including but not limited to compliance with state/federal regulations and accreditation standards (whether formally documented or informally known to anyone, including employees). 
3. The precise location which the Defendant believes the Plaintiff’s fall occurred on or about July 14, 2020. 
4. Specific tools or criteria Norton Hospitals, Inc. uses (if any) to identify patients who may be at a higher risk for falls during intake and/or registration, and the training provided to staff regarding these methods. 
5. Procedures for conveying a patient’s fall-risk status among departments and staff members (e.g., from registration to nursing), including any documentation or electronic record alerts. 
6. Protocols for providing mobility aids, wheelchairs, or staff assistance to patients – particularly those exhibiting difficulty ambulating or who have known fall-risk factors – during the intake and registration process. 
7. Information, verbal or written, provided to patients about safely navigating the premises, including any warnings, signage, or instructions on requesting assistance. 
8. Processes and procedures followed immediately after a patient’s fall, including assessments, documentation, reporting, investigations, any root cause analyses, and the roles of individuals or committees involved in reviewing such incidents. 
9. The work of internal committees, safety councils, or patient safety officers in developing, updating, or monitoring fall-prevention strategies, including any reports, recommendations, or evaluations made before or after the incident. 
10. Identification of personnel present in the intake or registration area at the time of the fall, their responsibilities, their training related to fall-risk identification, and how staff communicated patient safety concerns. 
11. Documentation of staff training sessions, frequency of refresher courses, and any internal or external audits evaluating compliance with patient safety and fall-prevention protocols. 
12. Maintenance, inspection, and safety measures in the intake and registration area, including lighting, flooring, signage, handrails or mobility aids, and any known hazards at or around the time of the fall. 
13. Hospital protocols for creating, updating, and retaining paper and electronic records related to patient intake, fall-risk assessments, or other indications of mobility challenges, including chain-of-custody and retention policies. 
14. Placement and maintenance of surveillance equipment in the hospital, as well as policies for recording, storing, and retaining video footage, procedures for addressing equipment malfunctions, and any alternative methods of documenting patient movement. 
15. Records or data regarding comparable patient falls or injuries during intake or registration within a reasonable timeframe before or after the subject incident, as well as any remedial measures considered or implemented in response to those occurrences. 
16. Policies for communicating with the patient and/or family after a fall, documenting the event, notifying risk management or legal counsel, and outlining any required follow-up steps.  

If not already produced, pursuant to CR 34, the Plaintiff further requests the Deponent produce any documents reviewed or relied upon by your CR 30.02(6) designated representative in preparation for this deposition as outlined in the instructions above. 

Said deposition will be taken pursuant to and for all purposes provided by the Kentucky Rules of Civil Procedure.
