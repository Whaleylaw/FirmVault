This matter is before the Court upon Plaintiff's Motion to Compel pursuant to CR 37.01, and the
Court having considered the response filed by the Defendant Norton Hospitals, Inc., (“Norton”) and
Plaintiff's reply, and being otherwise sufficiently advised:
IT IS HEREBY ORDERED:
Interrogatory No. 2: The Court does notfind merit in Norton's objections. Plaintiff's Complaint
alleges premises liability and medical negligence, not just medical negligence as suggested in Norton's
response. The Court believes that the narrowly tailored request for disclosure of “any and all falls resulting
in injury” in the identified “second-floor outpatient registration area’ of the “Norton Hospital Downtown” may
lead to the discovery of admissible evidence, and therefore is discoverable. And since the request does not
ask for the name of any individuals, nor any information regarding their medical treatment, the Court fails to
see how the mere disclosure of the fact of an occurrence of a fall could be a violation of HIPAA under the
“protected health information” provisions cited by Norton. Accordingly, Norton's objections are
OVERRULED.
Document Request No. 2: The Court does not find merit in Norton’s objections. The fact that
Norton employee Lucretia Vinson may not have specifically identified a particular policy or policies in her
deposition does not alleviate Norton's responsibility to respond to Plaintiff's request in good faith. Ms.
