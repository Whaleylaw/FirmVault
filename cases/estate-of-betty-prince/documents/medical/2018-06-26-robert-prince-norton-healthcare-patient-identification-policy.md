Definitions:
Electronic Medical Record (EMR): An electronic (digital) collection of medical information about a 
person, including information about a patient’s health history, such as diagnoses, medicines, tests, 
allergies, immunizations, and treatment plans as well as demographic information.
EPIC: NHC’s current proprietary electronic medical record software application to collect and record 
data throughout our healthcare system.
Purpose:
To establish guidelines to ensure Norton Healthcare’s commitment to comply with federal regulations and correctly 
identify patients during the scheduling and registration process.
Policy:
The patient name entered into the electronic Medical Record system (EMR) will be defined as the patient's legal name. A 
standardized process of identification will promote safe patient practices. Instances of patient misidentification can 
result in: patient misdiagnosis, incorrect procedures performed, improper medication administration, and in some cases 
even death. The practices set forth are meant to contribute to the efforts of patient safety and reduce the risks of 
patient error.
Patients will be identified by his/her legal name during the scheduling and registration process at all Norton Healthcare 
facilities and physician practices. Documentation verifying the patient's legal name will be requested from the patient 
during the registration process.
