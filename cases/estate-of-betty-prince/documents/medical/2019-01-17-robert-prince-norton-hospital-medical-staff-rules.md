GENERAL RULES AND REGULATIONS
OF
THE NORTON HOSPITAL
MEDICAL STAFF
Admission, Treatment and and Discharge
a) Each member of the Medical Staff shall provide continuous coverage of his/her patients in the 
Hospital. 
b) The management of each patient’s care is the responsibility of a qualified licensed independent 
practitioner with appropriate clinical privileges. Members of the Medical Staff are required to confine 
their work to areas within their delineation of privileges. Consultation between the various services is 
encouraged, but duties shall not be assumed by such a consultant unless they are a definite 
responsibility of the service to which he is assigned. 
c) An associate or assistant to a member of the Medical Staff may not treat a patient of the latter unless 
such associate or assistant is a member of the Medical Staff or the Dependent Allied Health Staff.
d) Physicians requesting admission for private patients shall provide the Hospital with a provisional 
diagnosis and necessary information as requested by the Admission Clerk. Physicians admitting 
medical patients who may be a source of danger to themselves or others must state this information 
before admission.
e) Emergency patients may be admitted without the above data required, but the attending physician will 
be expected to furnish such data within twenty-four (24) hours after admission of the patient. (For the 
purpose of these Rules and Regulations, the term "emergency" may be applied to any patient whose 
condition is such that any delay occasioned by compliance with any of these Rules and Regulations 
might prejudice the physical welfare of the patient.)
f) Patients who do not have a private physician and need to be admitted through the Emergency 
Department shall be assigned to the attending physician on call at that time.
g) When a patient is admitted as an emergency, whether at the physician's instigation or via the 
Emergency Department, the physician will be expected to see the patient and initiate treatment as soon 
as feasible--during the first twelve (12) hours and not longer than twenty-four (24) hours in any case. 
h) The physician or his/her designee is expected to see all his/her hospitalized patients regularly; 
ordinarily once daily is the practice. 
i) Patients will be admitted on the basis of the following order of priorities:
a) Emergency Admissions - within twenty-four (24) hours following an emergency admission, 
the attending practitioner shall place on the chart written documentation of the need of this 
admission.
b) Transfers out of or into special care units.

c) Patients transferred from other hospitals will take priority over routine admissions.
d) Routine Admissions - this will include elective admissions involving all services.
j) To provide a specialized environment for the optimal care of critically ill patients, post-anesthesia, 
intensive care, coronary care and other specialized nursing care units may be established. Welltrained, highly motivated nursing personnel, capable of recognizing and instituting treatment of 
emergencies which arise will function in these areas. In the absence of the attending physician, the 
nursing personnel will institute emergency resuscitative procedures including, but not limited to, 
defibrillation unless expressly contraindicated by the physician's written order.
k) If any question as to the validity of the admission to or discharge from the special care units should 
arise, that decision is to be made in consultation with the appropriate Medical Director of the special 
nursing care unit. In the event the Medical Director is unavailable, then the appropriate service 
chairman or committee chairman shall be consulted.
l) Patients shall be discharged only by order of the attending physician or his/her designee. At the time 
of discharge, the attending physician shall make every effort to complete all (both inpatient and 
outpatient) medical records. A maximum of thirty (30) days is allowed for completion of the medical 
records.
m) If, during the course of a hospitalization, a patient or designated representative disagrees with the 
direction of therapy and wishes to change attending physician:
a) The patient must notify attending physician of the desire to request another physician;
b) The patient, with the assistance of the attending physician or chairman of respective service, 
selects another physician, if requested; and
c) Upon agreement of selected physician to accept the medical care of the hospitalized patient, the 
attending physician shall place an order on the chart transferring primary care to the selected 
physician.
Orders and Medication Use
1. All diagnostic and therapeutic orders shall be in accordance with the system Medical Orders policy 
2. All medications prescribed and administered by the hospital must be in accordance with the formulary 
medications policy approved by the System Pharmacy and Therapeutics Committee.
3. All medications used in the hospital are procured by the pharmacy service. If patients bring their own 
drugs to the Hospital, these drugs will not be administered unless identified by a pharmacist according 
to hospital policy and the attending physician has written an order for their administration. 

4. Self administration of medication by patients must be authorized by an order from the attending 
physician. If self-administration is allowed it must be in accordance with the provisions of the selfadministration of medications policy
5. If the patient is to continue receiving home medication, the physician should order it in the medical 
record and identify it by name, prescription number and source, and include specific instructions as to 
dosage and administration. A blanket order to resume home medications is not acceptable. This 
should be done no later than twenty-four (24) hours following admission of the patient.
6. The System Pharmacy and Therapeutics Committee has authorized the pharmacy service to perform 
therapeutic interchange for certain medications and to change doses based on approved protocols.
7. Medication Reconciliation will be performed upon the patient’s admission/entry to the organization 
and on discharge home or to another facility, in accordance with the medication reconciliation policy.
Consultation
1. Any specialist with privileges on the Medical Staff may serve as a consultant.
2. A request for consultation should be sought in order to obtain the consultant's recommended course of 
treatment rather than for permission for particular treatment. If a difference of opinion is manifested, 
further consultation should be obtained.
3. Consultation shall be required on all critically ill patients where the diagnosis is obscure, and/or where 
there is doubt as to the best treatment, and/or when the illness is not within the treating physician's 
delineated clinical privileges. A signed record made by an acceptable consultant shall be a part of the 
patient's record.
4. All patients admitted to the critical care unit will have a consultation by a member of the approved 
Critical Care Panel.
5. The Service Chairman shall have the authority to call a consultant to attend the patients of a Medical 
Staff member who is absent from the community and has not made adequate provisions for the care of 
his/her patients, or when an emergency condition prevails.
Undergraduate Medical Education
Medical students may assist in collecting and documenting components of the history and physical 
cosigned by the attending physician.
Graduate Medical Education
1. Resident physicians rotating through the clinical services of the Hospital are under the direct 
supervision of the attending physician to whom they are assigned. When taking night, weekend or 
holiday call, the resident will report to the attending physician for whose patient he or she has been 

called; and, in the event the attending physician (or designee) cannot be contacted, the resident will 
contact the appointed residency program director or appropriate service chairman of the hospital.
2. Attending physicians are responsible for the supervision and monitoring of resident house staff 
assigned to them. Evidence for this oversight shall be by countersigning of resident physicians’ chart 
entries or a note indicating that the treatment and evaluation of the patient met with the approval of the 
attending physician. This will include H&Ps, consultations, operative notes, discharge summaries, 
and progress notes. Co-signing of the residents’ entries by the attending physician shall not be 
necessary for completion of the medical record if the attending physician has attested to approval of 
the residents’ entries in the medical record.

General 
1. Every member of the Medical Staff shall be actively interested in securing autopsies whenever 
possible and pursuant to the criteria contained in the Pathology Service’s rules and regulations. 
No autopsy shall be performed without written consent of the next of kin. All autopsies shall be 
performed by the Hospital Pathologist or by his/her physician designee.
2. Orders for “Do Not Resuscitate” will be in accordance with system policy.
All attending physicians will follow the policy and procedures for advanced directives (including the 
living will and healthcare surrogate) as formulated by the Ethics Committee, approved by the Ethics 
Council. 
3. As an entity of Norton Healthcare, the Norton Hospital campus is a smoke-free environment. 
4. The use of restraint or seclusion within the Hospital is limited to those situations with appropriate 
clinical justification based on the individually assessed behaviors and needs of the patient and 
must be consistent with the Restraint and Seclusion Policy 

5. Materials submitted by a physician as part of the application process for medical staff privileges 
shall be available to them for inspection. Documents generated by the hospital in the course of 
processing an application and documents arising from the peer review process are considered to be 
privileged information and as such are not available to the physician. 
6. The Chief Administrative Officer of the hospital and the President and Officers of the Norton 
Healthcare System shall be eligible to attend all medical staff meetings. The administrator or 
his/her representative shall attend Norton Hospital Division Executive Committee Meetings.
.
Review and Amendment
Any part of these Rules and Regulations which is in conflict with the other parts of the Operating Rules of 
the Medical Staff or the System Bylaws will immediately become null and void on discovery.
These Rules and Regulations shall be reviewed at least every three (3) years by the Norton Hospital 
Division Executive Committee (or its designee) of the Medical Staff, which shall report to the Medical 
Staff as to its recommendations for changes, if any.
These Rules and Regulations may be amended or repealed, as set out in the Medical Staff Manual.
Approved Board of Trustees January 17, 2019
