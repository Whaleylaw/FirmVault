ORGANIZATION AND FUNCTIONS MANUAL
OF
THE NORTON HOSPITAL
MEDICAL STAFF
This Organization and Functions Manual has been created pursuant to and under the authority of the 
System Medical Staff Bylaws. The purpose of this Manual is to describe the current organization of the Medical 
Staff and to define the mechanisms that the Medical Staff will use to accomplish its functions. 
ARTICLE I. CURRENT ORGANIZATION
The Norton Hospital Medical Staff shall be organized into the following services:
1. Anesthesiology
2. Emergency Medicine
3. Medicine
4. Obstetrics and Gynecology
5. Pathology
6. Psychiatry
7. Radiology
8. Surgery
ARTICLE II. COMMITTEES
The Norton Hospital Medical Staff shall have standing committees and shall participate on Hospital 
committees as required and appropriate. Each committee shall meet as often as is necessary to discharge its 
assigned duties. Additional information regarding each committee is outlined in the committee minute books and 
will be reviewed annually by the committees. Suggested revisions shall be submitted to the Operating Rules Review 
Committee. 
A. The Medical Staff standing committees are:
 
 1. The Norton Hospital Division Medical Executive Committee shall have the functions as stated in 
the System Medical Staff Bylaws. The Committee shall meet monthly.
2. The Operating Rules Review Committee The Operating Rules Review Committee shall review the 
Rules, regulations and policies to consider any changes. Proposed amendments to the Operating 
Rules or policies shall be acted upon by the Division Medical Executive Committee on behalf of 
the Medical Staff, and shall be forwarded to the System Medical Executive Committee for 
recommendation and then to the Governing Body before becoming effective. The Committee shall 
meet as needed.
3. The Critical Care Appraisal Committee shall be responsible for policy formulation and evaluation 
of the quality of care for patients in the critical care units, coronary care units, transitional care 
units, telemetry units, and the cardiac catheterization laboratory. The Committee shall advise 
management and the Medical Staff regarding facilities and equipment needed for the diagnosis 
and treatment for cardiology patients. The Committee shall also make recommendations to the 
Credentials Committee as to the granting of privileges (and reprivileging) concerning cardiology. 
The Committee shall meet quarterly.

2
4. The Medical Care Appraisal Committee shall review selected cases of medical deaths and/or 
additional case records chosen to critically analyze the quality of the medical care rendered by the 
Medicine Service within the Hospital. The Committee will monitor and evaluate the quality of 
patient care and the clinical performance of all individuals with delineated clinical privileges. The 
Committee shall be responsible for reviewing and overseeing utilization of resources used to 
render patient care. The Committee has the authority to review the medical record of any patient 
and to discuss the case with the patient's attending physician. Cases which warrant further 
consideration shall be reported in writing with recommendations to the Norton Hospital Division 
Executive Committee. The Committee shall meet monthly.
 5. The OB/ Neonatal Review Committee
a. Composition. The OB and Neonatal Review Committee shall consist of at least five (5) Medical 
Staff members with representation from Obstetrics, Pediatrics, and Anesthesiology specialties, and 
representatives from Nursing, Quality and Administration.
b. Duties. The duties of the OB/Neonatal Review Committee shall be to:
i. conducts case review of obstetrical, gynecologic, and pediatric cases, including 
review of the anesthesia aspects of care rendered, risk management, the 
important aspects of care and continuous quality improvement activities for the 
Departments of OB/Neonatal and Anesthesia;
ii. review and evaluates referrals from other committees;
iii. assures the plan, design, and execution of a continuous quality improvement 
process using a methodology that measures, assesses, compares the medical care 
received by patients with objective clinically valid criteria assigned to identify 
and improve important processes in patient care;
iv. Ensures that priorities for improvement activities are based on those issues that 
are high volume, high risk, and/or problem prone, are related to processes within 
the functions important to the outcomes of patient care, and are in accordance 
with the organization’s mission, vision, values and goals; 
v. utilizes the systematic continuous quality improvement processes in place to 
collect data needed to design and assess new processes, assess dimensions of 
performance relevant to functions, processes and outcomes; measures the level 
of performance and stability of important existing processes; identifies areas for 
possible improvement in existing processes and determines whether changes 
improved the process;
vi. monitors and seeks to improve publicly reported data;
vii. recommends topics/programs to the CME department based on findings of 
continuous quality improvement activities;
viii. uses results of quality assessment and improvement in the credentialing process;
ix. Recommends and acts on recommendations of Physician Advisory Committee 
of OB/Neonatal.
c. Meetings. The Committee shall meet at least bi-monthly. The Committee will maintain a 
permanent record of its proceedings and report quarterly its conclusions, recommendations, 
actions taken and evaluation of actions taken to the Obstetrics, Neonatal and Anesthesia 
Services, and the Division Medical Executive Committee. 
8. The Physician Wellness Committee shall assist physicians about whom there are significant 
concerns regarding physical or emotional well-being. The Committee will also provide 
consultation to the Norton Hospital Division Executive Committee in cases of suspected 
impairment of physical or emotional health of Medical Staff members. The Committee shall meet 

3
as needed. An incident of apparent impairment will be referred to the Committee and, if the 
impairment is or may be substance abuse, the input of the Kentucky Physician Health Foundation 
Impaired Physicians Program will be obtained immediately. The System Impaired Provider Policy 
shall apply to all actions taken relating to impaired or disruptive physicians.
B. The Hospital committees with Medical Staff members are:
1. The Ethics Committee shall advise, consult, and educate the Medical Staff, health care workers, 
Administration, and patients and their families regarding ethical issues relating to medical 
treatment. The Ethics Committee is not a decision-making body and does not replace the 
patient/family/physician relationship as the decision-making triad in treatment decisions. The 
Committee shall meet bi-monthly and as needed.
2. The Infection Control Committee shall be responsible for formulating, implementing, and 
reviewing the Infection Control policies and procedures of Norton Hospital. This includes the 
review and analysis of nosocomial infections, infection potential and the promotion of preventive 
and corrective programs designed to minimize infection hazards. The Committee serves as a point 
of communication for regulatory agency(ies) regarding Infection Control issues. The Committee 
also serves as a resource to the tenants of Norton Healthcare facilities and affiliated facilities as it 
relates to appropriate Infection Control practices. The Committee shall meet monthly.
3. The Medical Library Committee shall be a multi-disciplinary committee of the Norton Hospital 
and Norton Children's Hospital. The Committee shall serve as a liaison between the library and its 
users; advise on user needs and services; and shall monitor the effectiveness of the library in 
meeting the informational and educational resource needs of the medical staffs and Hospital 
personnel. The Committee shall meet three times annually.
4. The Operating Room Operations Council shall make recommendations to the Norton Hospital 
Division Executive Committee concerning the operation of the operating room and report to the 
Surgery Service. The Committee shall review and evaluate all surgery performed in the Hospital 
on the basis of approved criteria for surgical case review. The Committee shall monitor and 
evaluate the quality of surgical care within the Hospital and the clinical performance of all 
individuals with delineated surgical clinical privileges. The Committee shall be responsible for 
reviewing and overseeing utilization of resources used to render patient care. The Committee has 
the authority to review the medical record of any patient and to discuss the case with the patient’s 
attending physician. Cases which warrant further consideration shall be reported in writing to the 
Norton Hospital Division Executive Committee. The Committee shall meet monthly.
