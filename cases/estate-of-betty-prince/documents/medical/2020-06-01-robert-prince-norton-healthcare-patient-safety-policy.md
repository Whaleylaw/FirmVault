Definitions:
Patient safety event: an event where the delivery of patient care created a situation that resulted, or could result, in 
harm to a patient.
Serious safety event: a patient safety event that reaches the patient and results in moderate to severe harm or death. (A 
Patient Safety Event that reaches a patient and results in no or minimal harm is a precursor safety event.)
Sentinel event: a patient safety event (not primarily related to the natural course of the patient’s illness or underlying 
condition) that reaches the patient and results in severe temporary harm, permanent harm or death. Certain patient 
safety events are considered sentinel events regardless of the harm level to the patient. (See Policy on Sentinel Events.)
Near miss or good catch: a patient safety event that occurred but did not reach the patient as a result of an individual 
using an error prevention strategy, a safety barrier (such as bar code scanning), or by chance.
Unsafe condition: any circumstance that increases the probability of a patient safety event.
Harm (healthcare associated): any physical or psychological injury or damage to a person arising from the delivery of 
healthcare; includes both temporary and permanent injury and damage.
Patient Safety Report (PSR): the information entered into the Patient Safety Reporting System (PSRS) by the initial 
reporter at (or near) the time the unsafe condition, near miss or patient safety event was discovered.
Patient Safety Reporting System (PSRS): an electronic system available to staff and health care providers at Norton 
Healthcare for communicating information about unsafe conditions, near misses and patient safety events. The 
information entered into the PSRS is collected within the Norton Healthcare Patient Safety Evaluation System (PSES) for 
reporting to a Patient Safety Organization (PSO) and is deemed confidential and privileged Patient Safety Work Product 
(PSWP) under the Patient Safety and Quality Improvement Act.
Patient Safety and Quality Improvement Act (Patient Safety Act): Congress passed the Patient Safety Act in 2005 to 
improve patient safety and the quality of care nationwide. The Patient Safety Act creates a voluntary program through 
which health care providers can share information about patient safety events with Patient Safety Organizations in a 
confidential, secure environment. Norton Healthcare began compliance with the Patient Safety Act in 2009, and has 
continuously reported information to a Patient Safety Organization since 2009.
The following is a summary of terms that are defined by the Patient Safety Act and its implementing regulations:
 Patient Safety Organization (PSO): a separate entity listed by the Secretary of the U.S. Department of Health and 
Human Services as having met the PSO certification requirements under federal law.
 Patient Safety Evaluation System (PSES): the process for collecting, managing and analyzing information related 
to patient safety activities for submission to a PSO. Information within the PSES is confidential and privileged 
unless removed from the PSES by, or at the direction of, authorized individuals in the Norton Healthcare Risk 
Management or Legal departments.
 Patient Safety Work Product (PSWP): a designation for any data, reports, records, memoranda, analyses (e.g., 
cause analyses), or written and oral statements that may be used to improve patient safety, healthcare quality,

Purpose:
Norton Healthcare is committed to continuously improve the quality and safety of patient care in an effort to eliminate 
preventable healthcare harm. Patient safety events are identified, reported and reviewed to identify possible process 
improvements that will enhance the delivery of patient care and improve patient safety by preventing future harm.
Policy:
Norton Healthcare is committed to continually evaluating and improving the quality and safety of patient 
care. Improving patient safety depends upon identifying and communicating conditions and events that result in, or 
could result in, harm to a patient. When these events are promptly identified and communicated internally, post-event 
patient safety activities may be conducted to analyze care delivery systems and processes so that actions may be taken 
to prevent future occurrences or harm. Patient safety activities, including identifying, communicating and managing 
patient safety events, are conducted within Norton Healthcare’s Patient Safety Evaluation System for reporting patient 
safety information to a Patient Safety Organization.
Norton Healthcare employees, medical staff and other ancillary providers are encouraged to participate in the detection 
and reporting of unsafe conditions, near miss events and patient safety events.
Identifying unsafe conditions, near miss events or patient safety events:
 Staff and providers take appropriate action to correct the condition and contain the risk to patients and others.
 If a patient safety event results in harm to a patient, staff focus first on meeting the needs of the patient, 
including notifying the attending physician. When a serious safety event involving severe harm or death or a 
sentinel event occurs, staff promptly notify their supervisor or manager, and the supervisor or manager then 
notifies hospital administration and Risk Management.

Reporting unsafe conditions, near miss events or patient safety events:
 The individual who discovered or witnessed the condition or event, or an individual with personal knowledge of 
what happened, enters a Patient Safety Report (PSR) into the electronic Patient Safety Reporting System (PSRS) 
within 24 hours.
 After a PSR is entered, the information is reviewed by the responsible manager(s) and others, as indicated, to 
determine whether additional patient safety activities such as a causal analysis or peer review, if any, will be 
conducted in response to the event report. Managers and/or directors are expected to complete post-event 
reviews for PSRs involving their areas of responsibility within seven (7) days after a PSR is entered. (See the 
Sentinel Event policy for additional information about the management of sentinel events and serious safety 
events.)
Managing unsafe conditions, near miss events or patient safety events:
Post-event reviews, cause analyses, and other patient safety activities are conducted within the Norton Healthcare PSES 
and may be documented as appropriate within the PSRS.
The information entered into the PSRS is collected within the Norton Healthcare Patient Safety Evaluation System (PSES) 
for reporting to a Patient Safety Organization (PSO) and is deemed confidential and privileged Patient Safety Work 
Product (PSWP) under the Patient Safety and Quality Improvement Act. As a voluntary program, the Patient Safety Act 
allows Norton Healthcare discretion in choosing the patient safety information it reports to a PSO. If Norton Healthcare 
elects not to report patient safety information to a PSO an appropriate person from Risk Management or Legal 
Department may direct that it be removed from the PSES. Information submitted to the PSRS is presumed to be 
confidential and privileged Patient Safety Work Product (PSWP). The only exceptions are: 
1) if Risk Management or the Legal Department have removed the patient safety information from the PSES prior 
to being reported to a PSO and the removal is documented 
2) if Risk Management or the Legal Department have reviewed the patient safety information and determined that 
it is an original patient or provider record which may not be designated as PSWP according to the Patient Safety 
Act.
With respect to any particular patient safety information, Norton Healthcare’s Legal Department will make the 
determination whether such information may be designated as PSWP according to the Patient Safety Act.
Employees are reminded that, as a general rule, PSWP is confidential and privileged and employees are prohibited from 
disclosing PSWP to a third-party. To comply with this prohibition, employees will consider any person a “third-party” if 
he/she is not employed by or does not maintain privileges as a provider with Norton Healthcare. Any exceptions or 
questions regarding the disclosure of PSWP is addressed to Legal Department.
Patient Safety Reports are used for quality improvement purposes, not punishment. Norton Healthcare presumes that 
healthcare providers are doing their very best and patient safety events or errors are not the result of incompetence or 
misconduct. The manager may discuss the event and appropriate strategies to reduce the risk of reoccurrence with an 
employee. This discussion is not considered disciplinary, but is an essential component of accountability, ongoing quality 
management and process improvement. Employees are subject to disciplinary action in the following circumstances:
 The employee consistently fails to participate in the detection and reporting of events or system based 
processes designed to prevent errors. Example: Employee consistently makes the same or similar mistake after 
the process has been evaluated and proven sound and effective and the error and ways to reduce the error have 
been discussed with the employee.
 Actions or behavior involving a reckless disregard for patient safety.
