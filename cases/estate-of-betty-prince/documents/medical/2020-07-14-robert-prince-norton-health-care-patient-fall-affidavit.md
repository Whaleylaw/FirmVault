-7-
Betty M. Prince, a 79-year-old female with numerous co-morbidities including 
anemia, cancer, diabetes, gout, stage three kidney disease, mini stroke and thyroid 
disease was to be admitted to Norton Health Care for the insertion of a venous shunt into 
her right arm. Upon arrival to Norton Health Care, she was apparently signing in at 
registration and fell. Ms. Prince was transferred to the Emergency Department where she 
was examined by Sarah E Chandler, PA who noted; "Pt was upstairs at registration for 
fistula placement by Dr. Dwivedi when her feet got tangled and she fell striking her head." 
The physical exam showed a large left parietal hematoma with 3-inch laceration with 
minimal active bleeding. It was noted she reported soreness where she hit her head, but 
denied a headache, vision changes, loss of consciousness, weakness, paresthesia or 
dizziness. 
-8-
A CAT scan without contrast was performed on Ms. Prince at 17:07 on July 14, 
2020 and interpreted by John Berger, MD. The scan indicated Ms. Prince had a stable 
small left parietal subdural hemorrhage. The decision was to admit her to the ICU for 
observation where she remained until July 15, 2020 at which time she was transferred to 
the floor and a repeat CAT scan of her head was performed. The scan read by Durrett 
2 

Craddock, MD indicated Ms. Prince had a; "stable tiny left parietal subdural hematoma." 
-9-
Upon admission, nursing and physical therapy documentation indicated Ms. Prince 
used a walker rollator and occasionally required assistance from her husband for gait 
balance. Documentation also indicated she had experienced two falls within the previous 
six months, required contact guard assistance with mobility, shuffled with a wide stance 
and was unsteady. 
-10-
Ms. Prince was discharged home on July 16, 2020 with referrals for home health. 
-11-
According to Robert Prince, Ms. Prince's husband, he indicated upon arrival to 
Norton Healthcare, she required assistance walking into the facility with him at her side 
for stability and direction. Mr. Prince also noted Ms. Prince appeared weak and her 
endurance had lessened and her breathing was shallow and labored during the walk into 
the waiting room of Norton Health. He noted after a short interval upon arrival in the 
waiting room, he assisted her to the door of the registration office where he was informed 
he could not enter due to Covid. Mr. Prince also confirmed despite Ms. Prince being weak, 
there was no offer of a wheel chair, walker or even direct personal assistance to offer aid 
to her. 
-12-
After Ms. Prince's registration, Mr. Prince indicated no assistance was offered to 
her to ensure her safety when she returned to the waiting room. He noted; "as Betty 
stepped into the waiting-room she fell backwards hitting her head on the floor which 
resulted in a large cut in the back of the head." He reported after her fall, no one offered 
assistance to address her injury or monitor her vital signs; and, he had to ask for a towel 
to put pressure on the area of her head that was bleeding. He further noted it took 
approximately ten minutes before staff arrived to transport her to the emergency 
department. 
-13-
3 

I have extensive professional knowledge, skill, education, training and experience 
regarding the acceptable standard of care applicable to all professionals working in a 
health care facility, similar to those providing care and support for Ms. Prince. Based 
upon my review of the above described records and reliable principles and methods of 
nursing care to a reasonable degree of probability and certainty, and which are consistent 
with and are supported by my extensive education, professional knowledge, skill, training 
and experience, the conduct of the staff at the Registration office at Norton Healthcare 
deviated from the standard of care, while under same or similar conditions and like 
circumstances employed by any professional working in a health care facility. Specifically, 
failed to meet the standard of care by: 
i) Failed to meet the standard of care by failing to recognize and know when a 
patient is at high risk for falls. Ms. Prince, was a high risk for falls due to her 
age, and according to her husband required his assistance for mobility. The 
standard of care requires any professional working in a health care facility to 
recognize, be aware of and take into consideration all risks a patient 
demonstrates that would increase her risk of falling. 
ii) Failed to meet the standard of care by failing to implement proper fall 
precautions for Ms. Betty Prince including notifying a health care professional 
to provide assistance with her mobility. According to Mr. Prince, the registrar 
staff allowed Ms. Prince to walk unassisted and/or without a wheel chair when 
leaving the registrar's office. Even though those who work in a registrar's office 
may not be allowed or required to provide mobility assistance to patients, the 
standard of care requires any professional working in a health care facility to 
ensure fall precautions are in place for all patients in the facility, particularly for 
those at high risk for falls including the elderly; who, according to Ms. Prince's 
husband, have demonstrated unsteadiness and weakness. The standard of 
care requires professionals working in any area of a health care facility to notify 
a health care worker to provide assistance for patients who are at risk of falls 
which would allow the health care professional to provide contact guard assist 
4 

(CGA) and/or stand by assist (SBA) or the provision of a wheel chair when 
transferring an elderly patient at risk for falls, who are in an unfamiliar location, 
and, and according to Robert Price, her husband, required assistance 
previously for mobility.
