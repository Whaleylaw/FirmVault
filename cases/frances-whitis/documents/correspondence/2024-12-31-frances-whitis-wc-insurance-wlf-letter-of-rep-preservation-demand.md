To Whom It May Concern: 
Please be advised that our office has been retained to represent Ms. Frances Whitis for injuries sustained in a workrelated accident that took place during the scope of her employment for your company. Ms. Whitis was injured when she was 
involved in a motor vehicle accident on September 6, 2024 while driving a vehicle owned & maintained by Bluegrass Taxi. 
 On October 15, 2024 we sent our initial letter of representation via certified mail which was signed for by Rose Mauser 
on October 30, 2024. On October 30, 2024, Rose Mauser contacted our office and advised that Bluegrass Taxi does not carry 
worker compensation insurance and that her attorney had advised her that she was not required to. Our office requested Rose 
to contact her attorney and provide our contact information for further investigation into this matter. 
As of December 31, 2024, our office has not heard from any attorney on behalf of Rose Mauser and/or Bluegrass Taxi. 
At this time, our office has filed an Application for Resolution of a claim – Injury, with the Kentucky Department of 
Workers’ Compensation. Please forward this letter to your attorney as soon as possible for their response and to avoid any 
further delays in this matter. 
We further understand that Mrs. Mauser is under the impression that Mrs. Whitis would be considered an independent 
contractor, unfortunately, under the criteria outlined by the Kentucky Department of Workers' Compensation, Mrs. Whitis is 
in fact an ‘employee’ of Bluegrass Taxi as Mrs. Whitis does not control her hours, does not provide her own equipment, does 
not set her own charges/pricing, nor does Mrs. Whitis have the opportunity to pass along any cost associated with the 
Bluegrass Taxi service to the customers. Please see the KY DWC Website for additional information at 
https://elc.ky.gov/Workers-Compensation/Pages/Employee-Independent-Contractor-Guide.aspx.
