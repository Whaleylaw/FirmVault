Re: Frances Whitis v. Bluegrass Taxi 
 Claim No. 2024-01285 
Counsel: 
 We have reviewed your client’s responses to Plaintiff’s Requests for Production and find 
that many of the responses either fail to address the requests as written or offer minimal disclosures 
that are not reasonably responsive. For the sake of clarity and to allow for prompt supplementation, 
we outline below our concerns with each response. 
Request No. 1 
 The production of a 1099 form does not satisfy this request. The request sought documents 
identifying Ms. Whitis’s role or title, such as job descriptions, organizational charts, or 
employment records. A tax form merely reflects how Bluegrass Taxi chose to classify her and does 
not describe the nature or scope of her responsibilities. 
 Request No. 2 
 This request sought documents supporting the basis for your classification of Ms. Whitis 
as an independent contractor. A 1099 form alone does not explain the reasoning or support behind 
the classification or reflect any mutual understanding between the parties. If no other supporting 
documentation exists, that fact should be clearly stated. 
 Request No. 3 
 The response references a videotape but does not include the footage, describe its contents, 
or explain its relevance. If Bluegrass Taxi is in possession of any such footage believed to support 
the allegation of voluntary intoxication, a copy should be produced as soon as possible so that it 
may be evaluated in full.

 Request No. 4 
 This request seeks all materials relating to the intoxication allegation, including 
photographs, video, audio recordings, or other documentation. Incorporating the same reference 
to unproduced video footage does not resolve the request. If that footage is the only such material 
in Bluegrass Taxi’s possession, that should be confirmed, and the footage itself should be provided 
without delay. 
 Request No. 5 
 The 1099 provided does not satisfy this request, which sought documents reflecting how 
Ms. Whitis’s compensation was determined and paid. Records such as invoices, payroll entries, 
checks, or other payment documentation are necessary to evaluate the nature of the compensation 
arrangement. If no such records exist, that should be stated directly. 
 Request No. 6 
 The response “N/A” does not confirm whether any onboarding or training materials were 
ever provided to Ms. Whitis or maintained by Bluegrass Taxi. This request specifically sought 
documents such as training manuals, job descriptions, standard operating procedures, and 
orientation materials. It seems unlikely that a business, particularly one operating as a taxi service, 
would have no such materials at all. If none exist, that should be stated clearly so the record is 
complete. 
 Request No. 7 
 Providing the W9 of a single individual does not fulfill the request, which sought 
identification of all individuals performing services or working on the date of the incident. If Mr. 
Cunningham was the only individual associated with Bluegrass Taxi on that date, that should be 
explicitly stated. 
 Request No. 8 
 The response indicates that documents will be provided but offers no indication as to timing 
or the scope of the anticipated production. If there are communications between Ms. Whitis and 
Bluegrass Taxi relating to her work or the incident at issue, those should be gathered and produced. 
If no such communications exist, that should be confirmed in writing. 
 Request No. 9 
 The materials provided include an automobile insurance policy but do not address the 
existence or absence of any workers’ compensation coverage. The request was not limited to auto 
policies. If no workers’ compensation or other applicable coverage exists, that should be clearly 
confirmed. If additional policies are in place, those should be produced. 

3 
 Request No. 10 
 The reference to a “verbal agreement” does not provide sufficient information to determine 
its scope or relevance. If a verbal agreement was made, details should be provided, including the 
individuals involved, the date or general timeframe, and the substance of any agreed-upon terms. 
If there are communications or records referencing the agreement, those should also be included. 
 Request No. 11 
 This request sought all records of trips and services conducted by Bluegrass Taxi on the 
date of the incident, not just those related to Ms. Whitis. Referring to materials allegedly retained 
by the claimant does not satisfy the company’s obligation to produce documents within its own 
possession, custody, or control. If Bluegrass Taxi does not possess any responsive records, that 
fact should be clearly stated. 
 Request No. 12 
 This request specifically sought records relating to trips and services conducted by Ms. 
Whitis on the date of the incident. Referencing a prior response regarding Plaintiff’s possession of 
certain records does not resolve the request and avoids addressing its substance. To the extent 
Bluegrass Taxi possesses any records reflecting Ms. Whitis’s trips, services, or vehicle activity 
that day, those should be produced. If no such records exist, that should be clearly confirmed. 
 
 Please review the above and provide supplemental responses at your earliest convenience.
