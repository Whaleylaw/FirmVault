Comes now the Plaintiff, Frances Whitis, by and through counsel, and requests the Defendant/Employer, Bluegrass Taxi, to produce the following documents and/or things within thirty (30) days of service hereof in accordance with the Kentucky Rules of Civil Procedure or any other applicable rule or regulation.

Knowledge of the attorneys for the Defendant or any other agent of the defendant, or of any employee of the Defendant, shall be deemed to be the knowledge of the Defendant. Anything coming to your knowledge after filing Responses to these Requests for Production which would change your original responses should be contained in an Amended Response and served upon Plaintiff’s attorneys immediately after same is brought to your attention. 

<u>**DEFINITIONS**</u>

A.The words "and" and "or" shall be construed conjunctively or disjunctively as necessary to make the request inclusive rather than exclusive.

B.The use of the words "include(s)" and "including" shall be construed to mean "without limitation."

C.“Document” as used herein means anything created or reduced to writing whether produced on paper or by electronic mean including but not limited to letters, faxes, emails, telephone messages, texts, tweets, memoranda, data stored on any computer hardware or software, in the cloud, on an electronic system or otherwise.

D.Plural shall include the singular and the singular shall include the plural.

E.The term "present” used herein to refer to a point in time shall mean the date upon which Responses to the Requests are being made.

F.“You” or “Your” or “Defendant” as used herein means the Defendant Bluegrass Taxi and any and all of the Defendant’s employees, agents, or third persons who are under the supervision or control of the Defendant.   

I. The term “collision” as used in these Interrogatories refers to the series of events leading up to and following the fall Plaintiff incurred while attempting to board the TANK bus on July 30, 2024, as alleged in the Complaint.   

<u>**INSTRUCTIONS**</u>

A.It is expected that before responding to any Document Request, a good faith and complete inquiry by the Defendant, referring to all documents, correspondence, memoranda, records, books or other papers in the Defendant’s possession, custody or control will be done so as to enable the Defendant to make full and truthful responses. 

B.All documents provided in response to these Requests shall be appropriately numbered or marked to the specific number of each Request.

C.If any document or thing requested is withheld on the grounds of privilege or work product, state for each such document, in accordance with CR 26:

1.  The nature of the privilege claimed;

2.  The name of the author and recipient of the document;

3.  The general subject matter of the document;

4.  The date of the document; and 

5.  Identify the custodian of such document.

D.In the event that any documents conforming to these Requests no longer exist or are not in the custody or possession of Defendant or counsel, Defendant shall so state under oath and in writing within the time prescribed for their production. In the case of documents which no longer exist, Defendant shall further state when and by whom the documents were disposed of or destroyed. In the case of documents which are not in the custody or possession of the Defendant or its counsel, Defendant shall further state the name and address of the person presently having custody or possession of them.  

E.If part but not all of a document Request is objected to, any part of the document Request to which no objection is made should be fully complied with, and the parts of document Request should be considered severable for this purpose.

   F.       If a document Request is objected to, the nature of the objection and the nature of the information with respect to which the objection is made shall be fully set forth.
