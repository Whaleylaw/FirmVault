On Friday, September 6TH, 2024 I was dispatched to a single vehicle injury accident on the Thornhill
Bypass (Wilkinson BLVD, US 421) in the City of Frankfort, Franklin County. The caller advised the driver
was out of the vehicle and it was sitting sideways, blocking the road. Upon my arrival, I observed a tan van
sitting sideways off the right hand shoulder (looking down the hill, north bound side), with very severe front
end/left (driver) side front corner damage.
The driver was loaded up and transported to Frankfort Regional Medical Center by Frankfort City Fire/EMS
and was I was not able to talk to her on scene. However, officers did locate a witness that reported the
following.
Kristin Johnson advised both her and the van were in the right lane. She advised the van served into the
guard rail and she believed it was due to a large black object that was laying in the roadway as she got
closer to the vehicle.
On scene, there was a large black object laying on the right hand shoulder of the roadway that had been
cleared from the roadway prior to my arrival. However, that large box was determined to be apart of the
guard rail system that the van had struck and was knocked into the roadway as a result of unit 1 hitting the
guardrail end.
Based upon observations on scene, it was determined that the van was in the fast (left) hand lane going
north bound, when the struck the guard rail end protecting the concrete support of the pedestrian walking
bridge going over US 421 to Elkhorn Elementary School and then spun sideways coming to rest on the
shoulder.
Unit 1 vehicle, a 2016 Kia Sedona had very severe front end / left (driver) side front corner damage with the
front and side air bags all deployed and the left front driver wheel almost torn completely off. It was towed
from the scene by Carey's Towing of Frankfort.
Upon arriving at FRMC and speaking with the operator, she reported the following.
Unit 1 operator, Frances Whitis advised she was cab driver for Bluegrass Taxi and looked down to answer
her phone when she struck the guardrail. She advised her chest and left shoulder were hurting. She also
advised she was wearing her seat belt. She reported she was going between 45 to 50 MPH but may have
been going a little faster. She was being treated by FRMC Staff while I was talking to her.
