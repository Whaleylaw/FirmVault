BRIEF SUMMARY OF FACTS SUPPORTING LIABILITY 
AGAINST TRINITY COHARA 
As you are aware, this collision occurred on April 1, 2023, on Burlington Pike (KY 18) in 
Florence, Kentucky. Cecelia Neltner was a restrained passenger in her father’s vehicle, travelling 
eastbound on KY 18 when your client, suddenly and without warning, cut across multiple lanes of 
traffic and directly into their path, causing a severe collision. The police report noted inattention, 
failing to keep proper lane, and failing to yield right of way on behalf of your client as contributing 
factors in the collision. This collision caused Cecelia painful injuries which required medical 
treatment. 
 It is clear that the fault for this collision lies with your insured. As I see no legitimate issues 
as to liability which need to be addressed further, the remainder of this demand will focus on my 
client’s damages.

SUMMARY OF INJURIES
 I see no need to fully detail for you here what the medical records will reveal. However, in 
an effort to assist your expeditious review in this regard, the following is a brief summary of the 
injuries our client sustained and the treatment she has been forced to endure as a result. 
 Immediately following the collision, Cecelia complained of pain in her arm, neck, and 
back, but thought she would ultimately be okay. Unfortunately, however, over the following 
weeks, the airbag burns were not healing, and Cecelia was forced to seek treatment. Thus, on April 
12, 2023, Cecelia presented to Bluegrass Urgent Care for evaluation. Following physical 
examination, Cecelia was diagnosed with pruritus and a rash, which was attributable to the 
collision. For this ailment, Cecelia was prescribed Silvadene topical cream and instructed to follow 
up should her condition worsen. 
 Over the following weeks, young Cecelia continued her prescription medication program. 
Cecelia ultimately recovered with only mild residual scarring and no emergent injuries.
