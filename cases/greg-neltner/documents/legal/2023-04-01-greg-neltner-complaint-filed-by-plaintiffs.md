Come the Plaintiffs, Greg Neltner and Cecelia Neltner, by counsel, and for their cause of 
action against the Defendant, states as follows: 
1. The Plaintiffs, Greg Neltner and Cecelia Neltner, at all times relevant to the events 
stated herein, were residents of Newport, Campbell County, Kentucky, residing at the address set 
forth above. 
2. -Upon information and belief, at all relevant times, the Defendant, Trinity Cohara 
(hereinafter “Defendant Cohara”), was a resident of Washington, Gallatin County, Kentucky, 
residing at the address set forth above. 

2 
3. On or about April 1, 2023, Defendant Cohara did so negligently and carelessly 
operate her motor vehicle, a certain 2010 Ford Fusion, Vin No. 3FAHP0HA4AR220163 on 
Burlington Pike (KY-18) in Florence, Boone County, Kentucky thereby colliding Plaintiff’s 
vehicle. 
4. The negligence of Defendant Cohara in the operation of their vehicle on the date and 
at the place aforesaid was a substantial factor in causing Plaintiffs serious and permanent injuries, 
including great pain of body and mind, both past and future. In addition, the Plaintiffs have 
incurred medical expenses as a result of the collision and injuries they sustained and will incur 
future medical expenses for the treatment of their injuries. Further, Plaintiffs lost time and money 
from their gainful employment, resulting in damages for past lost wages and has had their future 
ability to labor and earn money substantially impaired. 
5. The aforementioned damages incurred by Plaintiffs are a sum in excess of the 
minimal amount necessary to confer jurisdiction upon this Court. 
6. Pursuant to KRS 304.39-062(b), threshold requirements have been met by the 
Plaintiffs in that Plaintiffs have incurred over $1,000.00 in medical expenses and sustained serious 
injuries. 
WHEREFORE, Plaintiffs, by counsel, demand as follows: 
1. Judgment against the Defendant in such an amount which will fairly and reasonably 
compensate Plaintiffs for the damages alleged herein; 
2. Prejudgment interest; 
 3. Post judgment interest; 
 4. Attorneys fees and court costs reasonably incurred or expended; 
 5. Trial by jury on all issues so triable;

 6. Punitive damages to the extent a trier of fact determines the actions of the Defendants

 7. All just and proper relief to which she may appear entitled, including the right to
