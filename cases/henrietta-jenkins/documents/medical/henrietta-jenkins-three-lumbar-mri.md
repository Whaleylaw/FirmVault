None.
TECHNIQUE:
Magnetic resonance imaging of the lumbar spine was performed WITHOUT injected contrast
using standard department protocols.
FINDINGS:
The alignment is anatomic. Mild spondylosis and disc desiccation is seen at L3-5. Remainder of
the intervertebral discs are well-maintained and well-hydrated. Bone marrow signal intensity is
unremarkable. The visualized abdominal and pelvic organs and paravertebral musculature are
unremarkable. There is no evidence of signal abnormality in the conus medullaris and cauda
equina. There is no evidence of signal abnormality in the exiting or traversing nerve roots. The
central cord terminates at T12-L1.
L1-2: Intervertebral disc space is adequately maintained. There is no evidence of canal stenosis
or neural foraminal narrowing.
L2-3: Intervertebral disc space is adequately maintained. There is no evidence of canal stenosis
or neural foraminal narrowing.
L3-4: 2 to 3 mm left paracentral disc herniation effaces the ventral surface of the thecal sac
resulting in mild-to-moderate left neural foraminal encroachment left exiting L3 nerve
impingement. The right neural foramen is adequately patent. The central canal is adequately
patent.
L4-5: Posterior annular tear is seen within the intervertebral disc. 1 to 2 mm broad-based disc
herniation effaces the ventral surface of the thecal sac resulting in moderate bilateral neural
foraminal encroachment and bilateral exiting L4 nerve impingement in conjunction with facet
arthrosis. The central canal is adequately patent.
