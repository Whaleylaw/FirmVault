Upon arrival to the scene, Traffic Control Officer (TCO) T. Baker observed that Unit 1, Unit 2 and Unit 3
remained in the roadway on the right side at the location of the collision. The operator of Unit 1 and the
operator and passenger of Unit 3 indicated that they did not have injuries requiring EMS to be called to the
scene. The operator of Unit 2 indicated that she would seek medical treatment by private means.
Unit 1, Unit 2 and Unit 3 were traveling south in the right southbound lane of Newburg Road at the listed
location. The operator of Unit 1 indicated that when he recognized that Unit 2 had stopped behind Unit 1,
he was unable to brake sufficiently to avoid the impact.
The operator of Unit 2 indicated that she observed Unit 3 come to a stop and she had stopped behind her
when Unit 1 impacted her from the rear, causing her vehicle to move forward and impact Unit 3.
The operator of Unit 3 indicated that she had come to a stop for traffic when she felt a single impact from
the rear.
The front of Unit 1 impacted the rear of Unit 2, causing the front of Unit 2 to impact the rear of Unit 3.
