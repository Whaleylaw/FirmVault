Comes the Plaintiff, Hope Padgett, by counsel, and for her cause of action against the 
Defendant, Progressive Casualty Insurance Company (hereinafter “Progressive”) states as follows: 
1. The Plaintiff, Hope Padgett, at all times relevant to the events stated in this 
Complaint, was and remains a resident of Mount Sterling, Montgomery County, Kentucky. 
2. At all relevant times, Progressive was an insurance company with the DOI ID of 
300696 operating within the Commonwealth of Kentucky, writing policies of insurance, including 
the underinsured automobile policy issued to Hope Padgett at issue in this case. 
3. On or about January 16, 2023, one Paul Barnes (hereinafter “Barnes”) did so 
negligently and carelessly operate his motor vehicle, a certain 2005 Honda Pilot, Vin. No. 
2HKYF18465H503007 (hereinafter “Barnes’ vehicle”) on College Avenue in Stanton, Powell 
County, Kentucky, thereby causing it to collide with Plaintiff’s vehicle. 
4. At the time of the collision, Progressive had issued in favor of Plaintiff as its insured 

2
a policy of insurance, No. [POLICY-1], or such other policy or policies, which provided for the 
payment of underinsured motorist benefits in the amount of at least $50,000.00 (hereinafter the 
“Progressive UIM Policy”). 
5. Pursuant to the Progressive UIM Policy, Defendant Progressive agreed, promised to 
pay, and is liable to the Plaintiff for all sums or losses over and above the liability coverage limits 
on the policy of liability insurance issued by Kentucky Farm Bureau Insurance Company 
(“Kentucky Farm Bureau”) to and for the benefit of the tortfeasor Paul Barnes, up to the limits of 
the Progressive UIM Policy. 
6. Kentucky Farm Bureau, on behalf of Paul Barnes, previously notified Plaintiff of its 
willingness to offer $25,000.00 in settlement of Ms. Padgett’s underlying liability claim, and 
Plaintiff has settled her claims with Paul Barnes as the tortfeasor and accepted Kentucky Farm 
Bureau’s offer following full and proper notice to Progressive pursuant to KRS 304.39-320(3). 
Under the KMVRA, Progressive chose to waive its subrogation interest and not to advance 
Kentucky Farm Bureau’s offer of $25,000.00 pursuant to its correspondence of December 22, 
2023, which authorized a direct cause of action by the Plaintiff against Progressive as the contract 
insurer under the Progressive UIM Policy. 
7. Plaintiff’s damages exceed the policy limits recovered from Kentucky Farm Bureau 
and the tortfeasor and/or any applicable no-fault funds, and Plaintiff has made due and proper 
demand and called upon Progressive to honor the Progressive UIM Policy, but Progressive has 
failed and/or refused to do so. 
8. The Plaintiff has performed each and every condition precedent to impose liability 
on the Defendant, Progressive, pursuant to the terms and provisions of the Progressive UIM Policy. 
9. The Defendant, Progressive, is liable to the Plaintiff for said damages to the extent of 

3
the applicable insurance coverage and for all damages exceeding the amount recovered against 
Paul Barnes and demand has been made and is made upon Progressive to pay and to account to 
Plaintiff for such damages. 
WHEREFORE, Plaintiff, by counsel, demands as follows: 
1. Judgment against the Defendant, Progressive Insurance, in an amount which will 
fairly and reasonably compensate her for the damages, in contract, tort, statutory and otherwise, as 
alleged herein and as she sustained in the motor vehicle accident described aforesaid; 
2. Trial by jury; 
3. Prejudgment interest; 
4. Post judgment interest; 
5. Attorneys fees and court costs reasonably incurred or expended to the extent 
authorized under existing law; and, 
6. All just and proper relief to which she may appear entitled, including the right to 
amend this Complaint.
