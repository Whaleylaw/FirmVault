INVESTIGATING AGENCY STANTON POLICE DEPARTMENT AGENCY ORI 0990100 LOCAL CODE 2023-0162

On the above date and time | was dispatched to a collision with injuries at 54 West College ave. Upon arrival | observed Unit 1 a silver Kia Soul on the side walk with damage to the driver door and driver fender. The Kia was being operated by Hope Padgett who was at that time trapped inside the vehicle. Stanton Fire department was able to remove her from the vehicle by prying open the door. Padgett was cleared by EMS on scene and declined medical treatment. Unit 2 was sitting in the west bound lane with damage to the front bumper and fender. Unit 2 was operated by Paul Barnes.

Unit 1 stated that she was traveling east on West College when she was struck by Unit 2. Unit 1 stated she did not see Unit 2.

Unit 2 stated that he was traveling east on West College and was struck by Unit 1 when he attempted to make a left hand turn onto Sipple Street. Unit 2 stated he did not see Unit 1 coming because she did not have her headlights illuminated.

Both Unit 1 and Unit 2 where both removed from the scene by Banks Towing.

After reviewing security footage | observed Unit 1 operating her vehicle on the roadway with her head lights not illuminated.

Cervical Sprain

A cervical sprain is a stretch or tear in one or more of the ligaments in the neck. Ligaments are the tissues that connect bones. Cervical sprains can range from mild to severe. Severe cervical sprains can cause the spinal bones (vertebrae) in the neck to be unstable. This can result in spinal cord damage and in serious nervous system problems.

The time that it takes for a cervical sprain to heal depends on the cause and extent of the injury. Most cervical sprains heal in 4—6 weeks.

What are the causes? Cervical sprains may be caused by trauma, such as an injury from a motor vehicle accident, a fall, or a sudden forward and backward whipping movement of the head and neck (whiplash injury), Mild cervical sprains may be caused by wear and tear over time.
