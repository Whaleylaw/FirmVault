Cervical Sprain
A cervical sprain is a stretch or tear in one or more ofthe ligaments in the neck. Ligaments are the tissues that
connect bones. Cervical sprains can range from mild to severe. Severe cervical sprains can cause the spinal
bones (vertebrae) in the neck to be unstable. This can result in spinal cord damage and in serious nervous
system problems.
The time that it takes for a cervical sprain to heal depends on the cause and extent ofthe injury. Most cervical
sprains heal in 4—6 weeks.
What are the causes?
Cervical sprains maybe caused by trauma, such as an injury from a motor vehicle accident, a fall, or a sudden
forward and backward whipping movement ofthe head and neck (whiplash injury), Mild cervical sprains may
becaused by wear and tear over time.
What increases the risk?
The following factors may make you more likely to develop this condition:
« Participating in activities that have a high risk oftrauma to the neck. These include contact sports, auto
racing, gymnastics, and diving.
« Taking risks when driving or riding in a motor vehicle.
© Osteoarthritis of the spine.
© Poor strength and flexibility of the neck.
