On the above date and time I was dispatched to a collision with injuries at 54 West College ave. Upon
arrival I observed Unit 1 a silver Kia Soul on the side walk with damage to the driver door and driver fender.
The Kia was being operated by Hope Padgett who was at that time trapped inside the vehicle. Stanton Fire
department was able to remove her from the vehicle by prying open the door. Padgett was cleared by EMS
on scene and declined medical treatment. Unit 2 was sitting in the west bound lane with damage to the
front bumper and fender. Unit 2 was operated by Paul Barnes.
Unit 1 stated that she was traveling east on West College when she was struck by Unit 2. Unit 1 stated she
did not see Unit 2.
Unit 2 stated that he was traveling east on West College and was struck by Unit 1 when he attempted to
make a left hand turn onto Sipple Street. Unit 2 stated he did not see Unit 1 coming because she did not
have her headlights illuminated.
Both Unit 1 and Unit 2 where both removed from the scene by Banks Towing.
After reviewing security footage I observed Unit 1 operating her vehicle on the roadway with her head lights
not illuminated.
