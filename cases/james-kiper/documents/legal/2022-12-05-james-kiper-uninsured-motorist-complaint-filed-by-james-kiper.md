 Comes the Plaintiff, James Kiper, by and through counsel, and for his cause of action 
against the Defendants, Musimbi Amani and Travelers Insurance Company, states as follows: 
1. Plaintiff is an individual resident of Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky, 
residing at the address set forth above. 

2 
2. Upon information and belief, at all times relevant herein, Defendant Musimbi 
Amani (hereinafter “Defendant Amani”) was and remains an individual resident of Louisville, 
Jefferson County, Kentucky, residing at the address set forth above. 
3. Upon information and belief, Defendant The Standard Fire Insurance Company 
d/b/a Travelers Insurance Company (hereinafter “Defendant Travelers”) is a national insurance 
company authorized by the Kentucky Department of Insurance, DOI ID 301068 with its principal 
place of business at the address set forth above. 
COUNT I: COMMON LAW NEGLIGENCE AGAINST DEFENDANT AMANI 
4. On or about December 5, 2022, Defendant Amani did so negligently and carelessly 
operate his motor vehicle, a certain 2010 Lincoln MKS, VIN No. 1LNHL9DR5AG607801 
(“Defendant Amani’s vehicle) on Preston Highway, in Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky, 
thereby causing a collision with Plaintiff’s vehicle. 
 5. The negligence of Defendant Amani in the operation of his vehicle on the date and 
at the place aforesaid was a substantial factor in causing Plaintiff serious and permanent injuries, 
including great pain of body and mind, both past and future. In addition, the Plaintiff has incurred 
medical expenses associated with the collision and the injuries that they sustained, will incur future 
medical expenses for the treatment of their injuries. Further, Plaintiff lost time and money from 
his gainful employment, resulting in damages for past lost wages and has had his ability to labor 
and earn money substantially impaired. 
 7. The aforementioned damages incurred by Plaintiff are a sum in excess of the 
minimal amount necessary to confer jurisdiction upon this Court. 

3 
COUNT II: UNINSURED MOTORIST CLAIM AGAINST DEFENDANT 
TRAVELERS INSURANCE COMPANY 
 8. Plaintiff restates and realleges the counts set forth above. 
9. Travelers is named as a Defendant herein because upon information and belief it 
issued a policy, number 607600818 203 1, covering or insuring a certain 2006 Scion XB, VIN 
JTLKT324364092017, on behalf of the Plaintiff (“the Policy”). 
10. Upon information and belief, the Travelers policy aforesaid was in effect at the time 
of the motor vehicle collision described in this Complaint, and, pursuant to the terms of the policy 
the uninsured motorist protections afforded thereunder would inure to the Plaintiff in connection 
with the collision described in this Complaint. 
11. Upon information and belief, on or about December 5, 2022, Defendant Amani did 
not hold the required motor vehicle insurance coverage in violation of KRS 304.99-060. 
12. Defendant Travelers has been notified of its potential obligation under its uninsured 
motorist policy, and Travelers is called upon to provide such uninsured motorist coverage to the 
extent that the damages sustained or recovered by the Plaintiff are not recoverable from Defendant 
Amani. 
WHEREFORE, Plaintiff, by counsel, demands as follows: 
1. Judgment against the Defendant, Musimbi Amani, in an amount which will fairly and 
reasonably compensate Plaintiff for the damages alleged herein; 
2. Judgment against the Defendant, Travelers Insurance Company, in such amounts 
which will fairly and reasonably compensate Plaintiff for the damages as alleged herein; 
2. Trial by jury; 
3. Pre-judgment interest; 
4. Post-judgment interest; 
5. Attorney fees and court costs reasonably incurred or expended; 

4 
6. Punitive damages to the extent a trier of fact determines the actions of the Defendant 
to have been malicious, oppressive, fraudulent, or reckless; and, 
7. All just and proper relief to which Plaintiff may appear entitled, including the right 
to amend this Complaint.
