## XII. OBJECTIONS TO ANTICIPATE



### Common Objections You Should Make



1. **Attorney-Client Privilege** - Any questions about conversations with you
 - Legal advice you've given
 - Case strategy discussions
 - **Instruct client not to answer**

2. **Work Product** - Questions about how you prepared the case
 - Documents you've created
 - Investigation you've conducted

3. **Vague/Ambiguous** - Poorly worded questions
 - Questions with undefined terms

4. **Compound Questions** - Multiple questions in one
 - Ask them to break it down

5. **Calls for Speculation** - "What do you think the defendant was thinking?"
 - "Why do you think...?"

6. **Beyond Scope of Knowledge** - Medical opinions (what caused injury)
 - Legal conclusions

**Note:** Even if you object, client usually still must answer unless you instruct otherwise.
