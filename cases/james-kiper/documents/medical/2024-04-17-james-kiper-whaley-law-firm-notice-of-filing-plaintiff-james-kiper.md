RDL and Single Leg RDL (Romanian Deadlift)

This is a great hamstring Strengthener. Holding a weight lean forward at the hips by sending the hips backward. The back should remain straight throughout the movement to protect the lumbar spine. It may be progressed by performing the movement while standing on only one leg.

It is normal to feel significant pulling in the hamstrings at first, however this will improve with practice. In addition there is often a strong pulling sensation behind the knees and into the calves which is neural tension. This too will improve with practice, but it should not be severe.

Jefferson Hamstring Curl ATG

Standing on slant board, toes pointed up the board, begin to round head, neck, shoulders and back forward, reaching hands towards the ground in front of the slant board. Maintain extension at knees by maintaining quadriceps and dorsiflexor contractions throughout entire movement. Pause at bottom for a sustained hold if stretch desired or a short pause then controlled lift back to full upright standing. Ensure use of glutes, hamstrings, and back extensors in synchronicity to assist standing back into upright- avoid only engaging back muscles here! This is a compound movement.

Goal is to get wrists below ankles with knees fully extended. If you are being impeded by the ground, cautiously elevate siantboard on riser/step/curb.




ATG modified elephant walk/stretch

Hinging forward at the hips with buttock pointed up in air and slight knee flexion bilaterally, rest hands in comfortable position to support self in position of gentle stretch in hamstrings. One leg at a time, flex the quadriceps until knee joint is fully straightened.

Pause and return it to slight flexed/slack position before doing the opposite side. Go back and forth, contracting quad then relaxing back to flexed position. If stretch begins to decrease, you may try to walk hands down to

lower resting position, thus increasing the tension felt in the hamstrings.
