He explains that this strict standard makes proving fault in rear-end collisions crucial for obtaining compensation in Alabama.

Lanier states that he can prove he provided enough time and space to responsibly stop in a manner to remain safe and not collide with the car in front of him. Hence, he contends, they were clearly negligent. They were traveling at about 50 mph. If the driver behind had provided five car lengths of distance between his car and theirs, then they could have successfully avoided hitting Lanier and his family from behind.

Lanier presents what he calls indisputable facts: the driver’s speed, lack of distance between their car and his, and lack of good judgement left the driver negligent for not doing everything possible to successfully avoid hitting him and his family from behind. He also states another indisputable fact: if the driver had used good judgement, left enough time and space, and done everything possible to avoid a collision, there would have been zero chance of the driver ever hitting him.
