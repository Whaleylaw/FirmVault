Comes the Plaintiff, James Sadler, by counsel, and for his cause of action against the 
Defendant, states as follows: 
1. The Plaintiff, James Sadler, at all times relevant to the events stated herein, was a 
resident of Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky, residing at the address set forth above. 
2. Upon information and belief, Defendant Eyob Tafesse (hereinafter “Defendant 
Tafesse”) was a resident of Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky, residing at the address set 
forth above. 
3. On or about April 7, 2023, Defendant Tafesse did so negligently and carelessly 
operate his motor vehicle, a certain 2009 Hyundai Santa Fe, Vin No. 5NMSH73E09H307375, at 
the intersection of Hikes Lane and Breckenridge Lane in Louisville, Kentucky thereby striking 
Plaintiff’s vehicle. 
4. The negligence of Defendant Tafesse in the operation of his vehicle on the date and 

2 
at the place aforesaid was a substantial factor in causing Plaintiff serious and permanent injuries, 
including great pain of body and mind, both past and future. In addition, the Plaintiff has incurred 
medical expenses as a result of the collision and injuries he sustained and will incur future medical 
expenses for the treatment of his injuries. Further, Plaintiff lost time and money from his gainful 
employment, resulting in damages for past lost wages and has had his future ability to labor and 
earn money substantially impaired. 
5. The aforementioned damages incurred by Plaintiff are a sum in excess of the minimal 
amount necessary to confer jurisdiction upon this Court. 
6. Pursuant to KRS 304.39-062(b), threshold requirements have been met by the 
Plaintiff in that Plaintiff has incurred over $1,000.00 in medical expenses and sustained serious 
injuries. 
WHEREFORE, Plaintiff, by counsel, demands as follows: 
1. Judgment against the Defendant in such an amount which will fairly and reasonably 
compensate Plaintiff for the damages alleged herein; 
2. Prejudgment interest; 
 3. Post judgment interest; 
 4. Attorneys fees and court costs reasonably incurred or expended; 
 5. Trial by jury on all issues so triable; 
 6. Punitive damages to the extent a trier of fact determines the actions of the Defendant 
were malicious, oppressive, fraudulent, or reckless; and, 
 7. All just and proper relief to which he may appear entitled, including the right to 
amend this Complaint.
