1. FACTS & LIABILITY

On April 7, 2023, Mr. Sadler was the restrained driver of a vehicle traveling northbound on Breckenridge Lane approaching the intersection with Hikes Lane in Louisville, Jefferson County,




Kentucky. At the same time, Mr. Tafesse was the driver of a vehicle traveling eastbound on Hikes Lane approaching the intersection with Breckenridge Lane and a vehicle driven by Clay Faison (“Mr. Faison”) was traveling southbound on Breckenridge Lane approaching the intersection with Hikes Lane. Suddenly and without warning, Mr. Tafesse disregarded a red traffic signal, entered the intersection, and collided with Mr. Faison’s vehicle. The force of the collision caused Mr. Tafesse’s vehicle to spin 180 degrees and he collided with Mr. Sadler’s vehicle. The collision resulted in Mr. Sadler sustaining significant personal injuries and other damages.

As exposed in the Kentucky Uniform Police Traffic Collision Report,! Officer Thomas Baker of the Louisville Metro Police Department investigated the collision and determined that Mr. Tafesse was the sole party responsible for the collision due to his disregard for a red traffic signal and his failure to yield to the right of way.

The conditions for establishing liability are clear. Mr. Tafesse owed a duty of care to the other drivers on the road. Pursuant to Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 189.338(3)(a), “[v]ehicular traffic facing a circular red signal alone shall stop at a clearly marked stop line but, if none, then before entering the crosswalk on the near side of the intersection or, if none, then before entering the intersection and shall remain standing until a green indication is shown...” Further, Ky. Rev. Stat. Ann. § 189.290(1), “[t]he operator of any vehicle upon a highway shall operate the vehicle in a careful manner, with regard for the safety and convenience of pedestrians and other vehicles upon the highway.” On the date of loss, Mr. Tafesse lacked attentiveness, disregarded a red traffic signal, failed to yield to the right of way, lacked all due care, and caused a collision with Mr. Sadler’s vehicle. As a result of Mr. Tafesse’s acts and/or omissions on April 7, 2023, Mr. Sadler suffered significant personal injuries and damages for which Mr. Tafesse is undoubtedly liable.

The value of lost household services, which includes losses for tasks the injured claimant provided around the home, are cognizable damages in personal injury cases in Kentucky where proof of such loss is substantiated by a preponderance of the evidence. Primal Vantage Co., Inc. v. O’Bryan, No. 2018-CA-0000106-MR, 2019 WL 6044870, at *2 (Ky. Ct. App. Nov. 15, 2019), aff'd in part, rev'd in part and remanded, No. 2020-SC-0247-DG, 2022 WL 3641122 (Ky. Aug. 18, 2022). Recovery for the loss of household services should be limited to the period of recuperation, and, if there is a permanent impairment of a person’s ability to perform household tasks, that item of damage would be included within the claim for the permanent impairment of earning capacity. Schulz v. Chadwell, 558 S.W.2d 183, 189 (Ky. Ct. App. 1977).
