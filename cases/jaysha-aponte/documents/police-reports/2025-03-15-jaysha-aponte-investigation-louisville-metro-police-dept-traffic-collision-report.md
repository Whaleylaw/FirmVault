Officers were dispatched to the location for what was originally reported as a non-injury collision. Officers
were notified of a vehicle being in a ditch so Officers then decided to respond code 3 to the scene. Officer
arrived on scene and noted Unit 1 disabled within the intersection and Unit 2 being partially submerged in a
water filled ditch northeast of the intersection. Officer located the witnesses of the accident and Unit 2s
operator north of the intersection.
Officer performed a trauma assessment on Unit 2 and noted she was alert but not oriented and was
reporting a painful headache. Unit 2 was not able to state how the accident occurred or any other
information outside who she is. Unit 2 also appeared to have short term memory loss ad confusion. Officer
requested EMS to respond code 3 for patient care and transport.
Officer acquired statements from Unit 1 and witnesses. Statements noted below.
Unit 1 Stated:
They were operating eastbound on E Market ST approaching the Roy Wilkins Ave intersection when they
stated they proceeded through the intersection. Unit 1 stated they had a green traffic light. Unit 1 stated
that they came into contact with Unit 2 within the W Market ST / Roy Wilkins Ave intersection.
Unit 1 did not report any injuries on scene. Unit 1 sustained severe damage to their 11-1 o'clock area. Unit
1 was towed by a private tow called by Unit 1s operator.
 Unit 1 requires a Spanish translator.
Unit 2:
Unit 2 suffered a suspected severe head injury from the collision and was unable to give a statement or
provide insurance at the time of the collision.
Unit 2s operator was transported with the use lights and sirens to University of Louisville hospital by EMS
due to suspected serious injuries. Unknown exactly how severe injuries are at the time of the report.
Unit 2 sustained severe damage to their 10-12 o'clock areas. Unit 2 was towed by Suburban towing and
recovery.
Witnesses Stated:
They were operating their vehicle behind Unit 2 going northbound on Roy Wilkins Ave. Witnesses stated
that they were approaching the W Market ST intersection when they noticed that they had a green traffic
light; furthermore, the Witnesses stated that they saw Unit 2 proceed through the intersection with the
green traffic light. Witnesses then stated that they saw Unit 1 disregard their red traffic light and proceed
through the intersection striking Unit 2.
Witnesses stated that Unit 1 disregarded their red traffic light and caused the accident.
Louisville Metro Police Department's Real Time Crime Center was contacted and they stated that
their is no camera footage of the incident. Please see axon evidence for scene photos.
Louisville Fire and Rescue Engine 2 (or LFR/FE02) responded to the scene for chemical/ debris
clean up.
Louisville Metro Emergency Medical Service's Medic 106A (or EMS/106A) responded to the scene
and transported Unit 2.
