MOTION FOR LEAVE TO FILE AMENDED COMPLAINT 
Comes the Plaintiff, Jerome Hedinger, by and through counsel, and pursuant to C.R. 15.01 
respectfully moves the Court for leave to file her First Amended Complaint, attached hereto, to 
add Jessica Burden as a Defendant to this action. 
Upon newly obtained information and belief, Jessica Burden was the individual renter of 
the home located at 562 Lilly Avenue, Louisville, Kentucky 40217 where the events in Plaintiff’s 
Complaint occurred. Prior to the Answer and Crossclaim filed by Defendant 432 Atwood, LLC, 
Plaintiff and his counsel were unaware that the property at issue was not rented to Defendant 
Brown, but rather his sister, Jessica Burden. 
In support, Plaintiff, by counsel, states that C.R. 15.01 provides that once an Answer or 
other responsive pleading has been filed a party may amend their Complaint by leave of Court 

which shall be freely given when justice so requires. 
The trial Court is to exercise its discretion to grant leave to amend under C.R. 15.01 freely 
and liberally. Caldwwell v. Bethlehem Mines Corp., 455 S.W.2d 67 (Ky. 1970). Leave should 
always be granted where the Defendant is not prejudiced by the delay, or a color of excuse for 
delay exists, and where there is no suggestion of bad faith on the part of the Plaintiff. Ashland Law 
& Ref. Co. v. Phillips, 404 S.W. 2d 449 (Ky. 1996). This Amendment is permitted under C.R. 
15.01 and is in the interest of justice. Further, no prejudice will result from this amendment. 
Plaintiff respectfully requests that Summons not be issued for Defendants Dahlyn Brown 
and 432 Atwood LLC, who have already been properly served, and that they shall be deemed 
served with the Amended Complaint upon filing. 
WHEREFORE, Plaintiff moves the Court for leave to file his Amended Complaint to add 
a party as a defendant in this action and for an Order directing the Court Clerk to issue process, 
such amendment to related back to the original filing date of the Complaint.

Comes the Plaintiff, Jerome Hedinger, by and through counsel, and for his Amended 
Complaint herein states as follows: 
1. Plaintiff Jerome Hedinger, at all times relevant to the events stated in this 
Complaint, was and remains a resident of Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky. 
2. At all relevant times herein, Defendant Dahlyn Brown (hereinafter “Defendant 
Brown”) was an individual resident of Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky. 
3. At all relevant times herein, Defendant Jessica Burden (hereinafter “Defendant 
Burden”) was an individual resident of Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky. 

2 
4. At all relevant times herein, Defendant 432 Atwood, LLC (hereinafter “Defendant 
Atwood”) was a Kentucky Limited Liability Company, with its principal office and registered 
agent identified above and permitted to conduct business in the Commonwealth, being assigned 
Organization Number 0811570 by the Kentucky Secretary of State. 
5. Defendant Atwood is the owner of the home and property located at 562 Lilly 
Avenue, Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky, and was renting said property to Defendant 
Brown. 
6. Upon information and belief, at all relevant times herein, Defendant Jessica Burden 
rented or was otherwise residing at the property located at 562 Lilly Avenue, Louisville, Kentucky, 
40217. 
7. Upon information and belief, Defendant Jessica Burden is the sister of Defendant 
Dahlyn Brown and allowed Defendant Brown and his dog to enter and/or remain on the property 
identified above. 
COUNT I: COMMON LAW NEGLIGENCE AGAINST DEFENDANTS 
 8. On October 24, 2023, Defendants Brown, Burden, and Atwood, together or 
separately, were the owner and/or keeper of a canine named “Chance” in Louisville, Jefferson 
County, Kentucky. 
 6. On October 24, 2023, the dog attacked, bit, and damaged the body of Plaintiff 
Jerome Hedinger and his dog while unattended, unrestrained, and roaming freely. 
 7. Upon information and belief, the incident identified herein was not an isolated 
incident. Rather and to the contrary, Defendants regularly allowed the dog to roam free and 
unrestrained without concern as to the potential harms or consequences of their actions, and as 
such, Defendants acted knowingly, maliciously, or recklessly on the date of the incident alleged in 

3 
this Complaint. 
 8. At the time of the attack alleged herein, Plaintiff Jerome Hedinger was lawfully 
walking down the sidewalk immediately outside of 562 Lilly Avenue, Louisville, Jefferson 
County, Kentucky, with his own dog, not committing trespass or other tort, and was not tormenting 
or abusing the dog identified above. 
 9. The negligence of the Defendants, together or separately, in failing to properly 
restrain or maintain control over the dog on the date aforesaid was a substantial factor in causing 
Plaintiff serious and permanent injuries and scarring, including great pain of body and mind, both 
past and future, as well as loss of enjoyment of life and impairment to labor and earn money. In 
addition, the Plaintiff has incurred medical expenses as a result of the attack and injuries he 
sustained and will incur future medical expenses for the treatment of his injuries. 
 10. The aforementioned damages incurred by Plaintiff are a sum in excess of the 
minimal amount necessary to confer jurisdiction upon this Court. 
COUNT II: STRICT LIABILITY AND/OR NEGLIGENCE PER SE 
AGAINST DEFENDANTS 
 11. In addition to the conduct described above, Defendants (together or separately) 
violated Kentucky statutory law on the date of the aforementioned incident, specifically including 
KRS 258.235, which makes a dog owner strictly liable for any damages (including injury to a 
person) caused by a dog. 
 12. Defendants (together or separately) further violated KRS 258.265 (failure to 
exercise proper care or control over a dog), as well as multiple Louisville Metro Ordinances, and/or 
other statutes or laws which may be applicable. 
 13. The Defendants’ statutory violations of law constitute negligence per se which 

4 
resulted in injury to the Plaintiff, all as specified above, and such conduct is therefore actionable 
pursuant to KRS 446.070. 
 14. The Defendants’ conduct described herein was a substantial factor in causing 
Plaintiff serious and permanent injuries and scarring, including great pain of body and mind, both 
past and future, as well as loss of enjoyment of life and impairment to labor and earn money. In 
addition, the Plaintiff has incurred medical expenses as a result of the attack and injuries he 
sustained and will incur future medical expenses for the treatment of his injuries. 
 15. The aforementioned damages incurred by Plaintiff are a sum in excess of the 
minimal amount necessary to confer jurisdiction upon this Court. 
 16. The Plaintiff is entitled to recover from the Defendants (together or separately) for 
all harms and damages specified above or as may otherwise be proven in evidence.
