Comes the Plaintiff, Jerome Hedinger, by and through counsel, and requests the Defendant, 
432 Atwood, LLC (hereinafter “432 Atwood” or “Defendant Atwood”), to answer the following 
Interrogatories within thirty (30) days of service hereof, in accordance with all applicable Rules of 
Civil Procedure. 
 These Interrogatories shall be deemed continuing. Knowledge of the attorneys for the 
Defendants or the claims agent or any other agent of the Defendants, or of any employee of the 
Defendants, shall be deemed to be the knowledge of the Defendants. 
Anything coming to your knowledge after filing an Answer to the following Interrogatories 
which would change your original answers or responses should be contained in an Amended 
Answer or an Amended Response and served upon Plaintiff’s attorneys immediately after the same 
is brought to your attention. 
DEFINITIONS 
 A. The words "and" and "or" shall be construed conjunctively or disjunctively 
as necessary to make the request inclusive rather than exclusive. 

2
B. The use of the words "include(s)" and "including" shall be construed to mean 
"without limitation." 
C. The words "related to" and "relating" mean relating to, referring to, reflecting, 
pertaining to, evidencing, or any way relevant to any issue, claim, defense, or question posed by 
the interrogatory or request within the meaning of CR 26 or any other applicable Kentucky Rule 
of Civil Procedure or Evidence. 
D. “Document” as used herein means anything created or reduced to writing whether 
produced on paper or by electronic mean including but not limited to letters, faxes, emails, 
telephone messages, texts, tweets, memoranda, data stored on any computer hardware or software, 
in the cloud, on an electronic system or otherwise. 
E. Plural shall include the singular and the singular shall include the plural. 
F. The term "present” used herein to refer to a point in time shall mean the date upon 
which Responses to the Requests are being made. 
G. “You” or “Your” or “Defendant” as used herein means the Defendant 432 Atwood, 
LLC and any and all of the Defendant’s employees, agents, or third persons who are under the 
supervision or control of the Defendant. 
H. “Identify” or “explain” or “state” as used in these Interrogatories with respect to 
any person or thing means to state fully all who or what the thing or person is and if relating to a 
person to give the person’s full name, address, and other contact information to the extent known 
or available to the Defendant or other person contributing to the answers and responses and for 
that person to state and explain fully all of the information that person or persons has in connection 
with the subject matter of the request herein. 

3
I. The term “incident” as used in these Interrogatories refers to the series of events 
leading up to and following the dog bite and injuries Plaintiff incurred while walking outside of 
562 Lilly Avenue, Louisville, Kentucky, 40217, on October 24, 2023, as alleged in the Complaint. 
 INSTRUCTIONS 
 A. It is expected that before answering the Interrogatories and responding to any 
Document Request, a good faith and complete inquiry by the Defendant, referring to all 
documents, correspondence, memoranda, records, books or other papers in the Defendant’s 
possession, custody or control will be done so as to enable the Defendant to make full and truthful 
answers to the following. 
B. All documents provided in response to these Requests shall be appropriately 
numbered or marked to the specific number of each Request. 
C. If any document or thing requested is withheld on the grounds of privilege or work 
product, state for each such document, in accordance with CR 26: 
 1. The nature of the privilege claimed; 
 2. The name of the author and recipient of the document; 
 3. The general subject matter of the document; 
 4. The date of the document; and 
 5. Identify the custodian of such document. 
D. In the event that any documents conforming to these Requests no longer exist or 
are not in the custody or possession of Defendant or counsel, Defendant shall so state under oath 
and in writing within the time prescribed for their production. In the case of documents which no 
longer exist, Defendant shall further state when and by whom the documents were disposed of or 
destroyed. In the case of documents which are not in the custody or possession of the Defendant 

4
or its counsel, Defendant shall further state the name and address of the person presently having 
custody or possession of them. 
E. If part but not all of a document Request is objected to, any part of the document 
Request to which no objection is made should be fully complied with, and the parts of document 
Request should be considered severable for this purpose. 
F. If a document Request is objected to, the nature of the objection and the nature of 
the information with respect to which the objection is made shall be fully set forth.
