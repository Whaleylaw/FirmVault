Animal Bite, Adult
Animal bites can be mild or serious. Small bites from house pets normally are mild. Bites from cats, strays, or wild
animals can be serious. If a stray or wild animal bites you, you need to get medical help right away. You may also
need a shot to prevent rabies infection. What increases the risk?
· Being near pets you do not know. · Being near animals that are eating, sleeping, or caring for their babies. · Being outside where small, wild animals move freely. What are the signs or symptoms?
· Pain. · Bleeding. · Swelling. · Bruising.
How is this treated?
Treatment may include: · Cleaning your wound. · Rinsing out (flushing) your wound. This uses saline solution, which is made of salt and water. · Putting a bandage on your wound. · Closing your wound with stitches (sutures), staples, skin glue, or skin tape (adhesive strips). · Antibiotic medicine. You may be given pills, cream, gel, or fluid through an IV. · A tetanus shot. · Rabies treatment, if the animal could have rabies. · Surgery, if there is infection or damage that needs to be fixed. Follow these instructions at home:
Medicines
· Take or apply over-the-counter and prescription medicines only as told by your doctor. · If you were prescribed an antibiotic medicine, take or apply it as told by your doctor. Do not stop using it even if
your wound gets better. Wound care
