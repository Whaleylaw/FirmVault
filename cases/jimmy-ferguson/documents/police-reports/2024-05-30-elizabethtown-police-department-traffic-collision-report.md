In Summary: As Unit #2 Operator Jace CHYTLA travelled southbound on N. Dixie Ave, he struck Unit #1
as Unit #1 crossed N. Dixie Ave on Walmart Dr. CHYTLA was driving Hardin County EMS Ambulance #6,
transporting patients from a crash on N. Dixie Ave./Pear Orchard NW, and had emergency equipment
activated as he approached the intersection. CHYTLA stated he saw Unit #2 enter the intersection from
the area of Walmart Dr, slow as if to stop, then continue across N. Dixie Ave.
Unit #1 Operator Jimmy FERGUSON stated that he left the Walmart parking lot under a green light onto N.
Dixie Ave. He heard the sirens but did not see the ambulance from his position. FERGUSON stated the
vehicle in front of him had continued a left turn onto southbound N. Dixie and he continued into the
intersection. FERGUSON stated he never saw the ambulance.
I observed crash damage across the passenger side of Unit #2's front bumper, and crash damage to Unit
#1 behind the rear passenger side wheel, which also dislodged the rear bumper. After the collision Unit #1
came to final rest facing the opposite direction on Town Mall Access (same street as Walmart Dr, divided by
N. Dixie Ave).
Witness Johnnelle KNIGHT stated that she was stopped on N. Dixie Ave at the traffic light at Walmart Dr
facing northbound. She saw and heard the ambulance's emergency equipment and ambulance
approaching, then saw Unit #1 enter the intersection.
Witness Crystal MILLER stated that she was stopped in the left turn lane on N. Dixie to turn into the Towne
Mall parking lot. MILLER stated she saw Unit #2 approaching the intersection "running code" MILLER
stated she saw Unit #1 enter the intersection under a green light where it was struck by the ambulance.
MILLER and KNIGHT provided written statements.
Assignment of unit numbers does not necessarily indicate an at-fault unit in this collision.
