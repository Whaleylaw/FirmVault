TeamCare’s plan language explicitly provides TeamCare with a right of first

(d) No Covered Individual (including his or her attorneys and other 
representatives) is authorized to act on behalf of the Fund with respect to the 
Fund’s Subrogation Rights, or to receive any payment or reimbursement on 
behalf of the Fund or to release or impair the Fund’s Subrogation Rights to 
any extent. The Fund is entitled to receive payment and reimbursement in 
the full amount of the Fund’s Subrogation Rights before the Covered 
Individual receives any settlement proceeds or other proceeds (collectively
“Proceeds”) in full or partial satisfaction of his or her Loss Recovery Rights. 
If the Fund is vested with Subrogation Rights pursuant to this Section 11.14, 
then, before the Covered Individual receives any Proceeds, the Covered 
Individual, and every person and entity that provides any recovery of 
Proceeds to or on behalf of a Covered Individual, are obligated to cause all 
such Proceeds to be paid primarily and directly to the Fund until the Fund 
has received full payment and reimbursement of the Fund’s Subrogation 
Rights. Plan Document, § 11.14(d) (emphasis added).
By reason of TeamCare’s status under federal law, ERISA would preempt any state law that is 
contrary to TeamCare’s subrogation provisions. See FMC Corp. v. Holliday, 498 U.S. 52 (1990); 
Land v. Chicago Truck Drivers Union, 25 F.3d 509 (7th Cir. 1994); Cutting v. Jerome Foods, 
Inc., 993 F.2d 1293 (7th Cir. 1993); cert. denied, 114 S. Ct. 308 (1993); Walter v. Hormel Foods 
Corporation, 120 F.3d 138 (8th Cir. 1997).
In accordance with TeamCare’s subrogation/reimbursement rules, we request that in the 
event any insurer makes any payment in connection with the August 29, 2023 claim, TeamCare
be included as a payee on any draft settlement issued to Julmonzhae L Moore and/or her
attorney, Aaron G. Whaley.
TeamCare’s coordination of benefits (“COB”) provisions specify that, with respect to 
injuries resulting from accidents, no-fault and personal injury protection (“PIP”) benefits, as well 
as third party medical reimbursement coverage, are prime and must be paid to their policy limits 
before TeamCare is obligated to provide benefits. However, if for any reason a primary insurer 
fails to promptly extend coverage, TeamCare may without waiver of any right, provide benefits 
and then seek reimbursement or contribution from responsible parties. For the reasons stated, we 
request that you confirm in writing the existence and extent of coverage that may be applicable 
to the captioned claim and advise us of any payment you may have made to or on behalf of our 
member or any other covered individual.
