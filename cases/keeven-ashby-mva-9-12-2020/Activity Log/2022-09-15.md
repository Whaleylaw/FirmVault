               I have been discussing this case with State Farm. We have issues with several factors here, as I’m sure you are aware. Mr. Ashby’s recollection of his alcohol consumption from that night has changed since the recorded statement he provided to State Farm. However, he admits in both instances to having consumed alcohol in addition to his prescription pain medication prior to driving that night. He was also not wearing his seatbelt. These things alone would be problems, even if liability was not at issues. But here, the liability is a big stumbling block. Mr. Ashby rear-ended another vehicle. He testified that he was looking to his right so he could change lanes when the collision occurred. The only basis he has for saying that the other vehicle was without lights is a witness whom he cannot identify.
              Also, the driver of the other vehicle lost consciousness and was injured as a result of being hit so hard. State Farm paid out its limits to her.
              Additionally, the claimed medicals are mostly related to his blood clots which are arguable not related to this accident, where he suffered a sprained ankle and broken ribs. Those clots did not develop until months later and he testified that his injuries were resolved within a few weeks from the accident.
              Given all of the above, it will probably come as no surprise that State Farm is not willing to pay much on this case. I did get authority to offer $1,000.00 in lieu of trial. Let me know if you want to discuss this further or if you think we should just get a trial date. Thanks!
