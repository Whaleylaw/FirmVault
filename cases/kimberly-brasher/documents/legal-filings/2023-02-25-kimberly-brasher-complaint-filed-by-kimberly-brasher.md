 Comes the Plaintiff, Kimberly Brasher, by counsel, and for her Complaint herein states as 
follows: 
1. The Plaintiff, Kimberly Brasher, was at all times relevant to the events stated in this 
Complaint, a resident of Hanson, Hopkins County, Kentucky, residing at the address set forth above. 
2. Upon information and belief, Defendant Networks USA XIV (hereinafter “Networks 
USA”), is and was at all times relevant to the events stated in this Complaint a foreign corporation 

2 
with its principle office located at the address set forth above. 
3. Upon information and belief, Defendant Reit Property Management (hereinafter 
“Reit”), is and was at all times relevant to the events stated in this Complaint a foreign corporation 
with its principle office located at the address set forth above. 
4. Upon information and belief obtained via due inquiry, and according to the McLean 
County Property Valuation Administration, Defendants, together or separately, were at all relevant 
times the owners, operators, or were otherwise in control of the property located at 115 KY 81 N, 
Calhoun, Kentucky, 42327. 
5. On or about February 25, 2023, Plaintiff was involved in a very minor motor vehicle 
collision on KY 81 N, following which the involved vehicles turned into the parking lot of the 
property identified above and located at 115 KY 81 N, Calhoun, Kentucky, 42327. 
6. On that date, as she was exiting her vehicle to discuss with the other parties involved 
in the incident, Plaintiff tripped or otherwise fell over a crack, lip, bump, or other hazard in the parking 
lot of the property identified above. As a result of her fall, Plaintiff sustained serious injuries, including 
but not limited to fractured hip. 
7. At all times referred to herein, Defendants acted by and through their agents, servants, 
and/or employees. 
8. Defendants, together or separately, owed a duty of care toward the Plaintiff to exercise 
ordinary care to keep its property and parking lot in a reasonably safe condition and to discover the 
existence of or avoid the creation of dangerous conditions on the premises and/or either correct or 
warn of them. This duty extended to the Plaintiff and the public generally. 
9. The Defendants, together or separately, through their authorized agents, servants, 
and/or employees created the dangerous condition mentioned hereinabove, or knew of, or by the 

3 
exercise of reasonable diligence and care should have known of the existence of the dangerous 
condition on the premises and failed to remediate the condition or warn Plaintiff of it and/or failed to 
correct the condition, thereby breaching its of care duty owed to Plaintiff. 
10. The negligence of the Defendants, together or separately, by and through their 
authorized employees, servants, agents, or others acting on its behalf was a substantial factor in 
causing Plaintiff serious and permanent injuries, great pain of body and mind in the past, present, and 
future, and to incur medical expenses in the past and future for treatment of her injuries, all to her 
damage in a sum in excess of the minimal amount necessary to confer jurisdiction upon this Court. 
WHEREFORE, Plaintiff, demands: 
 1. Judgment against the Defendants in a fair and reasonable amount sufficient to compensate 
her for the damages alleged herein; 
 2. Trial by jury on all issues so triable; 
 3. Her costs herein incurred; 
 4. Punitive damages to the extent a trier of fact determines the actions of the Defendants to 
have been malicious, oppressive, fraudulent, or reckless; and, 
 4. Any and all other proper relief to which Plaintiff may appear entitled including but not 
limited to the right to Amend her Complaint.
