Case No. 24-CI-00012
Dear Counselors:
Thank you for your compliment in agreeing to my services as a mediator. This letter will
confirm the agreement to mediate the above-styled matter as follows:
1. A mediation session will be held Tuesday, February 25, 2025, at 1:30 p.m. via
ZOOM video conferencing. This mediation has been scheduled for a half day, or
a minimum of 2 hours. Judge Knopf will send an email prior to mediation with
the link. 
2. The mediator will be Thomas J. Knopf and his fee will be $400 an hour which will
be divided equally between the parties to the mediation, unless otherwise agreed.
3. The parties and/or someone with settlement authority will attend the mediation
session with counsel. If, for whatever reason, the decision-maker/person with
financial authority cannot be physically present, I expect that person to be on speaker
phone for the opening session and every subsequent break out session when the
mediator is in the room with that party’s counsel. In addition, that person must be
available by telephone throughout the mediation. The mediation will proceed with
a general session attended by the mediator and all parties and counsel following
which the mediator will meet independently and on an alternating basis with each
party and their counsel until the matter is resolved or becomes apparent to the
mediator that resolution is not possible.

4. In that our schedule is fortunately tight, there will be a $200.00 per half-day
cancellation fee if not noticed seven (7) calendar days or more prior to the scheduled
mediation date, unless we are able to fill your time slot. Reluctantly, we point out
that our agreement is with you, the attorneys, and not with your client. If these
conditions are not acceptable, please advise forthwith.
5. The Mediation Agreement and Release, a copy of which is attached to this letter, will
be signed by all parties on the day of the mediation before the beginning of the
mediation session.
Experience hasshown that the greatest chance of success occurs when, in addition to counsel,
the plaintiff(s) and defendant(s) and the decision-makers on settlement are physically present at the
mediation–not just “available by telephone.” If, because of time-distance expenses, considering the
amount at issue, the actual decision-maker cannot be present, please bring an appropriate designee
of that decision-maker or you have authority for telephone conversations when necessary. Also, I
strongly suggest that all counsel be aware of applicable insurance coverage limits (if any)–for
obvious reasons.
Generally, I prefer a maximum five-page statement of counsel with whatever significant
documentary evidence you think necessary for my advance information. I will leave up to the
counsel involved the decision as to whether or not they want to exchange copies of pre-mediation
statements or keep them confidential. Please mail or deliver all mediation statements to my home
address: 7402 Beech Spring Farm Blvd., Louisville, Kentucky, 40241; via telefacsimile to
888/389-3559; or via email at [EMAIL-10].
If there is anything in this letter, or in the Mediation Agreement and Release, which I require
to be signed before conducting a mediation session, with which you do not agree, please advise
immediately.
I look forward to meeting with you and your clients for the purpose of resolving the dispute
between them.

MEDIATION AGREEMENT AND RELEASE
RE: Kimberly Brasher v. Walgreen Co.
McLean Circuit Court, Div. 1
Hon. Brian Wiggins
Case No. 24-CI-00012
The Parties to this mediation, and their counsel, agree as follows:
1. They will keep confidential the discussions of the parties and other information disclosed in
the mediation session.
2. They will not try to introduce, in any subsequent proceedings, the discussions and other
information disclosed in the mediation session, except as permitted by the Court.
3. They will not subpoena the mediator to testify concerning the mediation session.
4. They acknowledge that Thomas J. Knopf does not provide legal, financial, counseling, or
other professional advice.
5. That the $400 per hour cost of the mediation will be divided equally between the parties
signing this agreement. The attorneys signing this agreement agree that it obligates both
them and the party they are representing for their share of the costs of this mediation.
6. They will not hold the mediator liable for the results of the mediation, whether or not they
resolve their disputes.
7. They understand that the mediation process requires good faith efforts to be successful. They
will pursue settlement of their dispute through the mediation process in good faith and use
their best efforts in attempting to settle their disputes.
