drill and insertion of the head and neck component was placed. Once this was
appropriate and in place, we tightened the locking screw. Once both of these
were checked on AP and lateral radiographs and found to be in appropriate
position, we then used the guide on static and, with a small stab incision,
made our interlocking screw distally. We checked this on AP and lateral
radiographs as well. Copious irrigation with normal saline then followed. We
removed the insertion hardware. We made sure that our last set of AP and
lateral radiographs showed good hardware placement. We then copiously
irrigated with normal saline once again, closed the fascia with 2-0 Vicryl in
interrupted deep buried fashion and closed the skin. The dressings included
coverlets, and the patient was then taken to the recovery room.
