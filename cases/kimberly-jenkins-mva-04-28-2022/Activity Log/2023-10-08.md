Mr. Deitrich, I appreciate you taking a look at this and providing an
offer. I am going to reject the offer and renew my demand that Auto Owners
tender its policy limit of $300,000. So we are clear, I am not going to
come off of this number.

Your justification for questioning the relatedness of the C5-C7 surgery is
not strong. Your claim that Ms. Jenkins did not have complaints of neck
pain for "even a few weeks after the accident" is not true. Ms. Jenkins
presented to Chambers Medical for her first date of treatment on May 18,
2022. This is 20 days after the accident. Ms. Jenkins did not wake up on
May 18, 2022 with spontaneous neck pain and then decide to go to Chambers
Medical.

05/18/2022 Chambers Medical: "She has since been referred to us for
evaluation and recommendation, complaining of neck pain..." The physical
exam revealed "tightness and tenderness of the paracervical muscles" and
"rather tense in the supraspinous areas, more so on the right side." Her
very first visit has reports of neck pain and physical manifestation of
neck pain more so on the right." It should be noted that the referral that
is referenced is from Workers Comp.

 She then began a conservative course of physical therapy.

06/08/2022: Cervical MRI: C5-C6 "Broad-based disc/osseous protrusion
predominates in the right paracentral direction. Moderate spinal stenosis
and mild cord effacement. C6-C7: Broad based disc protrusion. Slender
upware extrusion. Mild to moderate spinal stenosis and slight cord
flattening.

MRI demonstrates the reason for her complaints. This directly correlates
the examination finding "rather tense in the supraspinous areas, more so on
the right side." C5-C6 "Broad-based disc/osseous protrusion predominates in
the right paracentral direction."

PT continues, she gets an injection and is then referred to Bluegrass
Ortho. They ordered a repeat MRI on 03/03/2023.

030//2023 Bluegrass Orthopedics: Dr. Hunt confirms the new MRI "again
demonstrates previously seen right-sided disc protrusion C5-C6 contacts the
exiting C6 nerve root."

Kimberly Jenkins has no history of neck pain. She was in an accident on
April 28, 2022 and shortly thereafter began having neck pain. She waited to
see if the symptoms would resolve. Every defense Dr. I have ever deposed in
a car accident says that the majority of cervical sprains will resolve
within a few weeks and that most people should just wait for the symptoms
to naturally resolve. Ms. Jenkins did that and the symptoms did not
resolve. She then underwent conservative treatment which failed. She has
objective physical findings in her first visit to the Dr. only 20 days post
accident. She has diagnostic imaging that directly correlates with her
complaints and physical findings. These same diagnostic findings are
confirmed 9 months later. She then has surgery.

This is about as clear a chain of causation as one gets in a case like
this. Additionally,  workers comp approved all of the treatment and surgery
as related to the accident. While that fact is not admissible to a jury it
should tell you something about the strength of the facts.

I am happy to discuss this or provide you with any additional
documentation. However, if you are completely set that you are not going to
tender the limits just tell me and I will go ahead and file suit. I see no
need to have anymore unnecessary delays.

Thank you.
