Narrative
On 10/31/25, unit M320 was dispatched code 1 but upgraded to code 3 by M225 to pt Deshields, a 23 year old male who was involved in a motor vehicle accident. Pt
was found sitting upright in the passenger seat of a non-involved vehicle, alert and oriented x 4. Pt reported that he had been the restrained passenger of the
involved vehicle. Pt reported that he had been asleep when his vehicle collided with another vehicle, awakening him to then experience a secondary impact when his
vehicle collided with a tree. Pt reported that he did his head on the passenger side window but did not lose consciousness. Pt reported that he was experiencing
bilateral arm pain, left sided thorax pain with increased pain on inhalation. Pt reported that he had generalized abdominal pain. Pt denied shortness of air, headache,
dizziness, blurred vision, nausea, and vomiting. 
Pt did not present with any obvious injuries of the head and his pupils were equal, round, and reactive to light. Pt did not present with any obvious deformities or
step off to the cervical spine with no pain on palpation. Pt did complain of pain on range of motion of the neck so was placed in a cervical collar. Pt then complained
of increased difficulty breathing upon placement of the cervical collar so the collar was removed. Pt did not present with any obvious injuries to his upper chest or to
the lateral sides of the chest and his lung sounds were clear and equal bilaterally. Pt presented with mild guarding to the abdomen with increased pain on palpation
but was otherwise soft and non-tender. Pt did not present with any obvious injuries to the back. Pt presented with minor, superficial abrasions to bilateral posterior
arms. Pt did not present with any obvious injuries to the legs. Pt had good pulse and motor sensory at all distal sites. 
Pt's vehicle presented with moderate front and right sided damage. Front and side airbags deployed. No starring to the dashboard, windshield, or passenger window
was noted. 
Pt's vitals were as listed. Pt's airway was patent and his breathing was normal in rate, rhythm, and effort. Pt's pulse was strong and regular, and his skin was normal in
condition, color, and temperature. 
Pt was transported code 1 to UofL hospital where he was triaged and transferred without change or incident.
