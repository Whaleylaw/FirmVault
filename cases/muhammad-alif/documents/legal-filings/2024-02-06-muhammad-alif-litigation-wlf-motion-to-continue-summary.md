DEFENDANT'S RESPONSE TO PLAINTIFF'S MOTION TO CONTINUE TRIAL
Case: Muhammad Alif v. Jermaine Powe
Case No.: 2023-L-010945
Court: Circuit Court of Cook County, County Department, Law Division
Filed: February 6, 2024
Attorney: Thomas C. Hinthorn (Pretzel & Stouffer, Chartered)

================================================================================
DEFENDANT'S POSITION
================================================================================
OBJECTS to continuance and requests trial proceed as scheduled on February 26, 2024.

================================================================================
KEY ARGUMENTS
================================================================================

1. RULE 213(f)(3) NON-COMPLIANCE
   - Plaintiff failed to provide treating physician's narrative report
   - Only provided physician name and address (Dr. Ather)
   - No narrative opinions disclosed as required

2. TIMELINE OF EVENTS
   - January 22, 2024: Plaintiff's 213(f) disclosure (incomplete)
   - January 25, 2024: Defendant served subpoena on Dr. Ather for 2/15/24 deposition
   - January 26, 2024: Plaintiff's attorney objected to deposition
   - January 29, 2024: Plaintiff moved to continue trial

3. PLAINTIFF'S OBJECTION TO DEPOSITION
   - Claimed deposition would interfere with trial preparation
   - Stated plaintiff needed to depose Dr. Ather first
   - Defendant argues this is plaintiff's own creation

4. SELF-CREATED EMERGENCY
   - Plaintiff had ample time to provide required disclosures
   - Plaintiff had ample time to depose treating physician
   - Continuance request is result of plaintiff's own delay

================================================================================
RELIEF REQUESTED
================================================================================
1. Deny plaintiff's motion to continue trial
2. Proceed with trial on February 26, 2024
3. Award defendant costs and attorney fees incurred in responding to motion

================================================================================
LEGAL AUTHORITY CITED
================================================================================
- Illinois Supreme Court Rule 213(f)(3) - requirement for narrative reports from treating physicians

================================================================================
STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS
================================================================================
- Defendant is ready for trial and wants to proceed
- Highlights plaintiff's procedural failures
- Positions any continuance as rewarding plaintiff's non-compliance
- Creates record for potential sanctions or fee awards
