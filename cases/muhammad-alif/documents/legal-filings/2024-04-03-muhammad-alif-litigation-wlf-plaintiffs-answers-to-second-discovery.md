Comes the Plaintiff, Muhammad Alif, by counsel, and hereby provides his Answers to the 
Interrogatories, Requests for Production of Documents, and Request for Admissions propounded 
by the defendants. 
GENERAL OBJECTIONS 
To the extent that any instruction contained in Defendant’s Discovery Request exceeds 
the language or scope of the Kentucky Rules of Civil Procedure, it is objected to and will not be 
complied with. To the extent that any request calls for information which is protected under 
attorney client privilege or the work product doctrine, it is objected to and will not be answered. 
Nothing in these responses shall be deemed a waiver of attorney-client privilege or the work 
product doctrine. 
These Answers and Responses reflect the current state of Plaintiff's knowledge 
concerning the documents requested and questions asked. These responses are given without 
prejudice to the Plaintiff’s right to use or rely at any time, including trial, on subsequently 
discovered information or documents not included in these responses. By providing information 

in response to Defendant’s Discovery Requests, Plaintiff does not concede that any information 
is relevant, material, or admissible. Plaintiff reserves all rights to object to the admissibility of 
any evidence. Plaintiff reserves all rights to supplement, change, or amend any response, 
description, calculation, or amount contained in any response herein. 
INTERROGATORIES 
INTERROGATORY NO. 1: Regarding the date of the accident and currently, please identify: 
(a) The model of your cell phone; 
(b) Your cell phone service provider and/or carrier; 
(c) Cell phone number; 
(d) The name of the account holder under which each listed phone number is/was operated; 
(e) Whether you were using your cell phone at the time of the accident. 
ANSWER: Plaintiff states that he has an iPhone 13 Pro Max, with service provided by 
Verizon. Plaintiff’s cell phone number is [PHONE-1]. The account is under 
Plaintiff’s name, Muhammad Alif. Plaintiff was not using his cell phone in any 
capacity at the time of the collision. 
INTERROGATORY NO. 2: State whether you were engaged in any of the following activities 
at the time of the collision: 
(a) Smoking; 
(b) Drinking a beverage of any sort; 
(c) Eating; 
(d) Talking on the phone or using the phone in any other manner; 
(e) Texting; 
(f) Listening to music; 
(g) Talking to passengers in the vehicle. 
ANSWER: Plaintiff was not engaged in any of the activities identified above, and he was 
alone in his vehicle at the time of the collision. 

REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION OF DOCUMENTS 
REQUEST NO. 1: Please produce all text messages send and/or received on the date of the 
collision on any Cellular phone, tablet, iPad, or other similar electronic devices in the Plaintiff’s 
possession at the incident that is the basis of this lawsuit. 
RESPONSE: Objection. This request is overly broad, unduly burdensome, and not 
reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of admissible information. This 
request is also a violation of Plaintiff’s right to privacy. Further, this 
information is equally available to the defense via their subpoena power. 
REQUEST NO. 2: Please produce any messages sent and/or received by the Plaintiff on 
Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, Messenger, or any other social networking sites on the date of the 
subject incident that is the basis of the lawsuit. 
RESPONSE: Objection. This request is overly broad, unduly burdensome, and not 
reasonably calculated to lead to the discovery of admissible information. This 
request is also a violation of Plaintiff’s right to privacy. Further, this 
information is equally available to the defense via their subpoena power. 
REQUEST FOR ADMISSION
