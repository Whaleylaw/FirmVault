Come the Defendants, Jacquelyn B. Wickliffe and Harbor House of Louisville 
(“Defendants”), by and through counsel, and hereby respectfully moves the Court for a reasonable 
continuance for the deadlines and trial in this matter currently set to begin June 3, 2025. 
As for grounds for this motion, Defendants state that by Order dated August 14, 2024, this 
Court set a number of pretrial deadlines for the parties in this case, including a deadline of February 
3, 2025 for experts disclosures by the Plaintiff, April 4, 2025 for expert disclosures by Defendants, 
and a trial date starting on June 3, 2025. Plaintiff filed a Motion to extend their deadline for expert 
disclosures and an Order was entered granting Plaintiff until March 3, 2025 for their expert 
disclosures. Defendants have scheduled an IME of Plaintiff with Edelson Forensics to occur on 
March 28, 2025. Upon receipt of Plaintiff’s Expert Disclosures on March 3, 2025, Defendants 
wish to have Plaintiff attend an additional IME with Dr. Chris Stephens to address his spinal injury 
claims. The undersigned counsel made diligent efforts to schedule Plaintiff’s IME with Dr. 
Stephens, but the first available date to complete the examination is not until June 3, 2025, a date 
falling outside of the deadlines set by this Court and the beginning date of the scheduled trial. At 
the most recent hearing before this Court when Plaintiff’s Motion was granted, it was indicated 
that Defendants would be granted a reciprocal extension upon request, which would put their 

expert disclosure deadline very close to the current scheduled trial date with significant activity to 
occur in a short period of time, which further necessitates the need to continue the current trial 
date. 
As of the date of the filing of this Motion, upon information and belief, all parties have in 
good faith made diligent efforts to comply with this Court’s August 14, 2024 Order; however, 
Defendants’ expert Dr. Chris Stephens’ unavailability and conflicts related to the same have 
complicated undersigned counsel’s ability to complete the expert discovery by the deadlines set. 
Because of the foregoing considerations and circumstances, in the interest of justice and fairness, 
Defendants respectfully request extension of the pretrial discovery deadlines set by this Court in 
its August 14, 2024 Order and to continue the trial. If pretrial discovery deadlines in this case are 
not extended, Defendants will be unjustifiably and unjustly prejudiced in their pretrial discovery 
efforts solely because of scheduling conflicts that could not be avoided. 
Additionally, due to the extension of deadlines that are necessary the trial date should also 
be continued. If the June date for the examination is used, then Defendants would only need a 
couple of months to complete its expert discovery and then would be prepared to try this matter. 
In Deleo v. Deleo, 533 S.W.3d 211 (Ky. App. 2017), the Kentucky Court of Appeals stated: 
. . . [T]he decision to grant or deny a continuance must be made within a legal 
framework so that there can be some meaningful appellate review. In Snodgrass v. 
Commonwealth, 814 S.W.2d 579, 581 (Ky. 1991), overruled on other grounds by 
Lawson v. Commonwealth, 53 S.W.3d 531 (Ky. 2001) our Supreme Court provided 
that framework setting forth he factors to be considered: (1) length of delay; (2) 
previous continuances; (3) inconveniences to litigants, witnesses, counsel, and the 
court; (4) whether the delay is purposeful or caused by the accused; (5) availability 
of other competent counsel; (6) complexity of the case; and (7) whether denying 
the continuance will lead to identifiable prejudice. 
Id. at 217.

In this matter, the length of the delay would be only a matter of months and no prior 
continuances have been requested or granted. This delay is not enough to establish a significant 
inconvenience for the parties and therefore continuance should be granted. See Eldred v. 
Commonwealth, 906 S.W.2d 694, 700 (Ky. 1994). The delay has not been purposeful or caused 
by Defendants as it just comes down to an unavoidable scheduling issue. Additionally, this is a 
somewhat complex case as there are issues regarding the severity of Plaintiff’s injuries and 
whether fault can be apportioned to the Plaintiff. It should be noted that Defendants are requesting 
only a reasonable extension of time in light of the circumstances.
WHEREFORE, Defendants, Jacquelyn B. Wickliffe and Harbor House of Louisville, by 
and through counsel, hereby move this honorable Court, for an extension of pretrial deadlines set 
in the Court’s August 14, 2024 Order, and continue the trial.
