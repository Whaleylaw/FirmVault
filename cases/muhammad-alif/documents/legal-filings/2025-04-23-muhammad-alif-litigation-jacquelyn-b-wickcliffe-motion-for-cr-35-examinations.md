Come the Defendants, Jacquelyn B. Wickliffe and Harbor House of Louisville
(“Defendants”), by and through counsel, and for their Motion for CR 35 examinations of Plaintiff,
Muhammad Alif, hereby states as follows:
In this case, the Plaintiff has alleged that he has suffered physical injuries for which she
has incurred medical expenses, lost wages, loss of earning capacity and pain and suffering.
Therefore, the physical condition of the Plaintiff is of central importance and the Defendants are
entitled to physical examinations of the Plaintiff pursuant to CR 35. An examination conducted
pursuant to CR 35 maybe ordered by the Court for good cause shown, when the mental orphysical
condition of a party 1s in controversy. See Ky. R. Civ. P. 35.01. The conditions of a CR 35
examination are within the sound discretion of the Court. See Metropolitan Property & Casualty
Insurance Co. v. Overstreet, 103 S.W.3d 31, 35-36 (Ky. 2003). “A plaintiffin a negligence action
who asserts mental or physical injury places that mental or physical injury clearly in controversy
and provides the defendant with good cause for an examination to determine the existence and
extent of such asserted injury.” /d. at 35 (citing Taylor v. Morris, 62 S.W.3d 377, 380 (Ky. 2001)).
Plaintiff's claimed injuries in this matter are clearly in controversy, and Defendants have good
cause to have Plaintiff submit to a CR 35 examination.
