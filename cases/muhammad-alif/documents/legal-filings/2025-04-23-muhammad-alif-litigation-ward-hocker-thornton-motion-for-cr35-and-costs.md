Come the Defendants, Jacquelyn B. Wickliffe and Harbor House of Louisville 
(“Defendants”), by and through counsel, and for their Motion for CR 35 examinations of Plaintiff, 
Muhammad Alif, hereby states as follows:
In this case, the Plaintiff has alleged that he has suffered physical injuries for which she 
has incurred medical expenses, lost wages, loss of earning capacity and pain and suffering. 
Therefore, the physical condition of the Plaintiff is of central importance and the Defendants are
entitled to physical examinations of the Plaintiff pursuant to CR 35. An examination conducted 
pursuant to CR 35 may be ordered by the Court for good cause shown, when the mental or physical 
condition of a party is in controversy. See Ky. R. Civ. P. 35.01. The conditions of a CR 35 
examination are within the sound discretion of the Court. See Metropolitan Property & Casualty 
Insurance Co. v. Overstreet, 103 S.W.3d 31, 35-36 (Ky. 2003). “A plaintiff in a negligence action 
who asserts mental or physical injury places that mental or physical injury clearly in controversy 
and provides the defendant with good cause for an examination to determine the existence and 
extent of such asserted injury.” Id. at 35 (citing Taylor v. Morris, 62 S.W.3d 377, 380 (Ky. 2001)). 
Plaintiff’s claimed injuries in this matter are clearly in controversy, and Defendants have good 
cause to have Plaintiff submit to a CR 35 examination.

Counsel for the parties have discussed Plaintiff submitting to IMEs with Dr. Richard 
Edelson and Dr. Chris Stephens. Defendants originally scheduled Plaintiff for an IME with Dr.

in their Motion to Continue Trial and Pretrial Deadlines filed with this Court on March 3, 2025. 
The undersigned was notified on March 27, 2025 that Plaintiff would not be in attendance at the 
scheduled IME after the undersigned office’s attempt to confirm same. See Exhibit 1. The 
undersigned sent new potential dates on April 1, 2025 to Plaintiff’s counsel for IMEs with Dr.

attempts to confirm Plaintiff’s attendance at the IMEs on the given dates. 
Additionally, a cancellation fee applied for the late cancellation of the March 28, 2025 IME

before the scheduled exam. See Exhibit 3. The undersigned informed Plaintiff’s counsel that a 
cancellation fee may be applied if forced to reschedule and advised the invoice for same would be 
forwarded for their payment due to the late notice. The invoice was forwarded to Plaintiff’s counsel 
along with the available dates to reschedule and no response was received regarding same. See 
Exhibit 2. Therefore, Defendants request this honorable Court enter an Order awarding fees and 
costs pursuant to CR 37, which will be supplemented by an Affidavit of Counsel regarding Fees 
and Costs associated with the examination and this Motion.
Further, upon receipt of Plaintiff’s Expert Witness Disclosures on March 4, 2025, 
Defendants requested dates to take the discovery depositions of Plaintiff’s identified experts: Dr. 
William Hwang, Dr. Robert Van Boven and Dr. Claude Fortin. See Exhibit 4. To date, no available
