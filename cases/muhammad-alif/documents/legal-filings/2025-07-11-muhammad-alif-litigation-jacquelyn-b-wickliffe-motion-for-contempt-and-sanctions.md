Come the Defendants, Jacquelyn B. Wickliffe and Harbor House of Louisville 
(“Defendants”), and pursuant to CR 37.02, and respectfully moves this Court to find the Plaintiff 
in contempt and impose sanctions against the Plaintiff for violating the Court’s April 29, 2025 
order compelling the Plaintiff to attend a CR 25 Examination by Dr. Richard Edelson on July 11, 
2025. In support of said Motion, Defendant states as follows:
A trial court has broad discretion in addressing violations of its orders regarding discovery. 
Turner v. Andrew, 413 S.W.3d 272, 279 (Ky. 2013). CR 37.02 provides, in part, that “If a party . 
. . fails to obey an order to provide or permit discovery . . .” the court may impose a number of 
sanctions, including but not limited to the exclusion of evidence, the striking of pleadings, and 
dismissal of the action. CR 37.02(2); Nowicke v. Cent. Bank & Tr. Co., 551 S.W.2d 809, 810 (Ky. 
App. 1977). Additionally, CR 37.02(3) provides that “the court shall require the party failing to 
obey the order or the attorney advising him or both to pay the reasonable expenses, including 
attorney’s fees, caused by the failure, unless the court finds that the failure was substantially 
justified or that other circumstances make an award of expenses unjust.” Id.
In this case, the Plaintiff has alleged that he has suffered physical injuries for which he has 
incurred medical expenses, lost wages, loss of earning capacity and pain and suffering. Therefore,
