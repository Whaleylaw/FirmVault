Come the Plaintiff, Muhammad Alif, by and through counsel, and hereby respectfully moves the Court for a reasonable continuance for the deadlines and trial in this matter currently set to begin April 14, 2026, and in support thereof states as follows:

At the time the trial date was scheduled for April 14, 2026, it was represented to the Court that Plaintiff had completed his medical treatment related to the injuries sustained in the motor vehicle accident that is the subject of this litigation. Based on that representation, the Court set the trial date with the understanding that Plaintiff's medical condition had stabilized and no further treatment was anticipated.

However, since the trial date was scheduled, Plaintiff's medical condition has deteriorated, necessitating additional medical treatment that was not anticipated at the time of scheduling. Plaintiff is currently undergoing additional treatment for his back injuries, which includes ongoing conservative treatment and evaluation, a scheduled epidural steroid injection in his lumbar spine, and potential surgical intervention depending on the outcome of the injection.

Plaintiff recently served Supplemental Responses to Discovery on Defense Counsel, disclosing this additional medical treatment and the treating providers. Plaintiff is scheduled to receive an epidural injection in his back, and pending the outcome of that injection, could be scheduled for surgery. Plaintiff's treating physician has indicated that surgical intervention may be necessary if the injection does not provide adequate relief. This additional treatment is directly related to the injuries sustained in the subject motor vehicle accident and is material to Plaintiff's damages claim.

As of the date of the filing of this Motion, Plaintiff has in good faith made diligent efforts to complete his medical treatment; however, Plaintiff's medical condition has worsened, requiring additional treatment that could not have been anticipated when the trial date was set. Because of the foregoing considerations and circumstances, in the interest of justice and fairness, Plaintiff respectfully requests extension of the pretrial discovery deadlines and to continue the trial. If pretrial discovery deadlines in this case are not extended, Plaintiff will be unjustifiably and unjustly prejudiced in his ability to present complete evidence of his injuries and damages solely because of medical circumstances that could not be avoided.

Proceeding to trial at this time would prevent Plaintiff from presenting complete evidence of his injuries and damages, require speculation about future medical needs rather than presenting actual treatment received, and potentially undervalue Plaintiff's claim by excluding significant medical treatment and expenses. Additionally, the additional treatment was only recently disclosed through supplemental discovery responses. Defense counsel is entitled to adequate time to review the new medical records and bills, opportunity to depose the new treating providers if desired, time to retain expert witnesses to review the additional treatment, and opportunity to conduct an independent medical examination if requested.

Additionally, due to the ongoing medical treatment, the trial date should also be continued. If Plaintiff completes his treatment, then Plaintiff would only need a reasonable period of time to finalize his damages evidence and then would be prepared to try this matter.

In Deleo v. Deleo, 533 S.W.3d 211 (Ky. App. 2017), the Kentucky Court of Appeals stated:

. . . [T]he decision to grant or deny a continuance must be made within a legal framework so that there can be some meaningful appellate review. In Snodgrass v. Commonwealth, 814 S.W.2d 579, 581 (Ky. 1991), overruled on other grounds by Lawson v. Commonwealth, 53 S.W.3d 531 (Ky. 2001) our Supreme Court provided that framework setting forth he factors to be considered: (1) length of delay; (2) previous continuances; (3) inconveniences to litigants, witnesses, counsel, and the court; (4) whether the delay is purposeful or caused by the accused; (5) availability of other competent counsel; (6) complexity of the case; and (7) whether denying the continuance will lead to identifiable prejudice.

*Id. at 217.*

In this matter, the length of the delay would be only a matter of months and no prior continuances have been requested or granted by Plaintiff. This delay is not enough to establish a significant inconvenience for the parties and therefore continuance should be granted. See Eldred v. Commonwealth, 906 S.W.2d 694, 700 (Ky. 1994). The delay has not been purposeful or caused by Plaintiff as it arises from unforeseen medical complications requiring additional treatment. Additionally, this is a complex case involving significant spinal injuries requiring ongoing medical intervention. It should be noted that Plaintiff is requesting only a reasonable extension of time in light of the circumstances and to allow for completion of necessary medical treatment.

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff, Muhammad Alif, by and through counsel, hereby moves this honorable Court, for an extension of pretrial deadlines and to continue the trial currently scheduled for April 14, 2026.
