PERSONAL INJURY COMPLAINT 
NEGLIENCE & NEGLIGENT ENTRUSTMENT 
Comes the Plaintiff, Muhammad Alif, by Counsel, and for their Complaint against 
the above-named Defendant, states as follows: 
COUNT ONE: NEGLIGENCE 
1. Plaintiff, Muhammad Alif, was at all times herein referred to, as residents of 
Jefferson County, Louisville, Kentucky. 
2. Defendant, Jacquelyn B. Wickcliffe, was at all times herein referred to, a resident 
of Jefferson County, Louisville, Kentucky. 
3. Defendant, Jacquelyn B. Wickcliffe, was at the time of the wreck, negligently 
operating a vehicle in Jefferson County, Louisville, Kentucky. 

4. On or about November 8, 2022 in Jefferson County, Kentucky, the Defendant, 
Jacquelyn B. Wickcliffe, operated a motor vehicle in such a careless and negligent manner 
as to cause a collision with the vehicle driven by Muhammad Alif. 
5. As a direct and proximate result of the negligence of the Defendant, the Plaintiff 
sustained serious injuries, medical damages; and was caused to suffer great pain, both 
mental and physical; damage in an amount which exceeds the jurisdictional minimum of 
this court. 
6. Pursuant to KRS 304.39-062(b), threshold requirements have been met by the 
Plaintiff in that Plaintiff have incurred over $1,000.00 in medical expenses and sustained 
serious injuries. 
COUNT TWO: NEGLIGENT ENTRUSTMENT 
1. At all relevant times, Defendant Harbor House of Louisville was the employer of the 
Defendant Wickcliffe, who was working within the scope of her employment when the 
accident took place. 
2. Defendant, Harbor House of Louisville, negligently and carelessly entrusted Defendant 
Wickcliffe, whom Defendant, Harbor House of Louisville, knew or should have known would 
operate the vehicle in a careless manner. 
3. Defendant, Harbor House of Louisville’s negligent entrustment was a substantial factor in 
causing the Plaintiff’s injuries as alleged. 
WHEREFORE, Plaintiff demand as follows: 
1. Judgment against the Defendant in an amount which will fairly and 
reasonably compensate the Plaintiff for his damages alleged herein; 
2. Trial by jury; 
3. Plaintiff’s costs herein expended; and 
4. Any and all other relief which the Plaintiff may appear to be entitled, 

including the right to amend this Complaint, if necessary.
