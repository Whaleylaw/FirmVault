TRIAL ORDER
MUHAMMAD ALIF PLAINTIFF
vs.
JACQUELYN B. WICKCLIFFE, et al. DEFENDANTS
 
*** *** ***
On August 12, 2024, this matter came before the Court during motion hour. Parties 
appeared requesting a date for a trial.
Wherefore, IT IS HEREBY ORDERED, that:
1. This matter is set for a trial on June 3, 2025, at 10:00 a.m. The Court
has allotted three days for the trial. A final pretrial is scheduled on May 14, 2025, at 
1:30 p.m.
2. No continuance shall be granted, even by agreement of the parties, without prior 
approval of the Court. Any request for a continuance shall comply with CR 43.03.
3. At least 45 days before the final pretrial, the parties shall attempt to resolve their 
differences through mediation.
4. The following time frames may be changed by written agreement of counsel without 
notice to or approval of the Court.
5. At least 120 days before trial, Plaintiff shall identify each person whom he expects to 
call as expert witnesses at trial and comply with CR 26.02(4)(a)(I) regardless of

6. At least 60 days before trial, Defendant shall identify each person whom they expect

7. At least 75 days before trial, any motions to dismiss, motions for judgment on the

8. At least 30 days before trial:
A. The parties shall exchange lists of the names and addresses of all persons 
who may testify at the trial and file them of record.
B. Each party shall make available for inspection and copying all documentary 
evidence and exhibits of any kind to be presented at trial.
C. Each party claiming any damages shall submit an itemization of those 
damages to opposing counsel and fully comply with CR 8.01(2) and CR 
26.05.
9. At least 14 days before final pretrial, unless otherwise agreed by counsel, the taking 
of depositions, including expert witnesses, shall be completed.
10. At least seven days before final pretrial:
A. Counsel shall file of record proposed jury instructions;
B. All motions in limine shall be submitted with appropriate authority cited with a 
tendered order listing grant/denied/or reserve; and
C. Any objections to portions of any depositions shall be filed of record.
