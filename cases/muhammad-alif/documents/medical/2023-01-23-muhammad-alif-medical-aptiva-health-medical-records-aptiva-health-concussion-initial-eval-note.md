CC & Description of
Injury
The patient is a 61-year-old male who presented today for an initial evaluation of an
injury that occurred on 11/8/22, when he was the restrained driver of a vehicle that was
in a collision with a van that was trying to merge into his lane. He believes that he lost
consciousness for approximately 20 seconds. He presented to the ED approximately 2-3
weeks post-injury for exacerbation of dizziness, where he was reportedly diagnosed with
a concussion. He then reportedly traveled to Dallas to see a neurologist who confirmed a
diagnosis of concussion.
He was seen for an initial evaluation with Dr. Patton at Norton Community Medical
Associates on 12/6/22, at which time he complained of dizziness, memory difficulties, and
chest pain. He was referred to PT and neurology. He is currently participating in vestibular
therapy through court PT. He has an appointment with neurology for March 2023. He has
also been following with Dr. Mark Lynn (neuro-optometry). He is also continuing to see
the neurologist in Dallas Texas who is treating his concussion by "testing with lights and
goggles."
Neuroimaging No neuroimaging available for review. Through conversation, it sounds as if the patient had
a negative CT and MRI. He stated that they "showed a concussion TBI." When asked if
imaging indicated any bleeding or fracture, he stated no.
Current Symptoms The patient feels that overall his symptoms have been worsening in the 2 months since the
accident. Specifically, he reported that dizziness and shortness of breath are the symptoms
that are worsening.
Physical: Headaches are denied. Slow/weighty dizziness and balance issues. Discomfort
in busy environments is denied. Blurred vision provoked by testing (e.g. with Dr. Lynn) but

Cognitive: Memory difficulties, word finding pauses
Emotional: Increased anxiety especially surrounding driving. When asked if he is feeling
