RightEye Oculomotor Testing
Patient Name: MUHAMMAD ALIF
DOB: [DOB-1]
DOI: [DOB-33]
Date of Testing: [DOB-34]
Interpreting Physician: Claude Fortin MD
History:
MUHAMMAD ALIF is a 63-year-old Male who presented for oculometric testing with the
RightEye system to evaluate injuries resulting from an MVC suffered.
Technique:
Dynamic Vision was used to measure oculomotor behavior with eye tracking. Eye tracking helps
determine horizontal and vertical saccades as well as other oculo-metric parameters as a
measure of damage to neural circuits from neurological injuries.
Oculomotor tracking captures 30-250 pictures of eye movements per second and provides
quantitative data to assess how the eyes are following the movement of objects. The eye
movements are graphed and documented, and deviations from normal patterns commonly
occur as a result of traumatic brain injury.
Findings:
Pursuits score: Smooth pursuit is the ability to maintain a gaze on an object. The test
examines the error in keeping a gaze on the target and provides metrics related to movement of
eye in relation to an object. The test is performed with gaze fixation on a target and provides a
report on Circular Smooth Pursuit, Horizontal Smooth Pursuit and Vertical Smooth Pursuit.
Patient Score: 39 which indicates Moderate dysfunction.
Saccades score: Saccades are fast eye movement mechanisms that allow a gaze shift from
one target to the next. Saccades represent the spatial accuracy of targeting and retinotopic
maps. The test provide metrics related to quick movement of the eye to relocate foveal vision.
Patient Score: 88 which is within the normal range.
Fixations score: This provides metrics related to the stopping point or the ability to maintain
fixation of the eye on a target. Patient Score: 64 which indicates Mild dysfunction.
Dysfunctional scale (EyeQ): This is a metric that describes where the patient falls in relation to
age matched normative population data. Patient Score: 63 which indicates Mild dysfunction.
Interpretation:
Smooth pursuits are below the normal functional range. This is indicative of dysfunction at the
cerebellum and parietal areas.

Saccadic movements are within the normal functional range.
Fixations are below the normal functional range. This is indicative of dysfunction in the neural
integrator and brainstem.
Dysfunctional Scale is 63.
Impression:
While these findings can be indicative of a variety of pathologies, it is within a reasonable
degree of medical certainty and more probable than not that they are present as a result of a
traumatic brain injury sustained on [DOB-35].
Recommendations:
● Correlate with clinical status, neurologic exam, and videonystagmography/video
oculography.
● Consider oculomotor rehab for rehabilitating and improving eye tracking functions.
● May consider a repeat oculomotor study to evaluate for improvement with treatment.
