History:
MUHAMMAD ALIF is a 63-year-old Male who presented for computerized dynamic
posturography testing with BTrackS system due to injuries sustained as a direct result of a
MVC.
Technique:
The modified Clinical Test of Sensory Integration and Balance (CTSIB) evaluates sensory
contributions to postural control. The first trial is the "Standard" condition where balance is
tested with eyes open and two feet on the BTrackS Balance Plate's firm surface. The second
(i.e. eyes closed on a firm surface), third (eyes open on a "perturbed", foam surface), and fourth
(eyes closed on a foam surface) trials to give an indication of how proprioception, vision and
vestibular information are respectively used for balance.
Computerized dynamic posturography tests balance in both stable and unstable conditions
utilizing a computerized force plate. This assessment can also help differentiate the cause of
postural instability and determine if injury to the brain is contributing to a patient's balance
problems.
Findings:
1. Standard score (Eyes Open, Firm) was in the 43rd percentile.
2. Proprioception score (Eyes Closed, Firm) was in the 60th percentile.
3. Visual score (Eyes Open, Foam) was in the 98th percentile.
4. Vestibular score (Eyes Closed, Foam) was in the 100th percentile.
5. When compared to healthy adults of the same sex, scores reveal bottom quartile
performance in all four conditions.
Impression:
This is an abnormal study indicating either central etiology related to deficits in sensory
integration or concurrent vestibular and somatosensory dysfunction. While these findings can be
indicative of a variety of pathologies, it is within a reasonable degree of medical certainty and
more probable than not that they are present as a result of a traumatic brain injury sustained on
[DOB-3].
Recommendations:
1. Consider further correlation with the brain MRI, VNGNOG testing and Oculometric
testing as clinically warranted.

2. May consider repeating posturography to evaluate improvement in parameters.
