an effort to assist your expeditious review in this regard, the following is a brief summary of the 
injuries our client sustained and the treatment she has been forced to endure as a result, to date: 
Following the collision on July 13, 2024, Ms. Adadevoh was evaluated at Jewish Hospital 
for multiple complaints, including pain in her neck, lower back, left thigh, and left knee. She 
reported being a passenger on a party bus that was struck by another vehicle, during which multiple 
people fell onto her. She denied any loss of consciousness or head trauma. On examination, she 
described midline neck pain, lower back discomfort, and localized pain in the left thigh and knee. 
Imaging studies, including CT scans of the cervical and lumbar spine and X-rays of the left femur 
and knee, showed no acute fractures or subluxations. Her injuries were diagnosed as blunt trauma 
with associated cervical and lumbar strain. She was prescribed Robaxin, a muscle relaxant, and 
discharged with instructions to follow up as needed. 
On July 29, 2024, Ms. Adadevoh followed up with Advocate Medical Group in Chicago. 
At that time, she reported general improvement in her condition and presented primarily for a 
check-up and continued symptom monitoring. 
In total, Ms. Adadevoh’s post-accident treatment included emergency medical evaluation, 
diagnostic imaging, medication, and follow-up care. Her symptoms, though gradually improving, 
resulted in significant discomfort and required medical intervention. The records reflect a clear 
temporal link between the collision and her reported injuries, supporting her claim for 
compensation under the applicable uninsured motorist coverage.
