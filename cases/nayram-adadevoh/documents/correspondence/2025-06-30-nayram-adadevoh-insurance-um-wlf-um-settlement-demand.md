I see no need to fully detail for you here what the medical records will reveal. However, in 
an effort to assist your expeditious review in this regard, the following is a brief summary of the 
injuries our client sustained and the treatment he has been forced to endure as a result, to date: 
Following the collision on July 13, 2024, Abby Sitgraves presented to Jewish Hospital with 
complaints of right knee and ankle pain. She was diagnosed with a knee contusion and ankle sprain. 
Imaging of the foot, fibula, and femur revealed no acute fractures, though a 7mm radiopaque 
density was noted within the soft tissue of the medial calf. Ms. Sitgraves was discharged with 
conservative care instructions, including a knee immobilizer, ACE wrap, crutches, and nonweightbearing precautions. She was instructed to follow up with her primary care provider. 
On August 6, 2024, she was evaluated at University of Louisville Orthopedics for persistent 
lower extremity symptoms. Her providers noted ongoing anterior knee pain and swelling, 
aggravated in part by the fact that, during the collision, a relative weighing approximately 300 
pounds fell onto her right knee. While she had resumed most routine activities and retained full 
range of motion, her pain remained intermittent. These findings were consistent with the earlier 
diagnoses and supported continuation of conservative management. 
In total, Ms. Sitgraves’ treatment spanned multiple providers over the course of several 
weeks, including emergency evaluation, diagnostic imaging, orthopedic follow-up, and continued 
conservative care. Despite gradual improvement, she experienced prolonged pain, swelling, and 
mobility restrictions that disrupted her daily routine and required assistive devices. Her 
documented complaints and objective findings establish a clear pattern of injury and recovery 
directly tied to the July 13, 2024, collision, warranting full consideration in evaluating the value 
of her claim.
