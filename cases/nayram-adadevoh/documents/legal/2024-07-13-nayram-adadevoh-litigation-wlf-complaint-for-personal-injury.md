Come the Plaintiffs, Abby Sitgraves and Nayram Adadevoh, by counsel, and for their cause 
of action against the Defendants, state as follows: 
1. The Plaintiff, Abby Sitgraves, at all times relevant to the events stated herein, was a 
resident of Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky, residing at the address set forth above. 
2. The Plaintiff, Nayram Adadevoh, at all times relevant to the events stated herein, was 
a resident of Hazel Crest, Cook County, Illinois, residing at the address set forth above. 
3. Upon information and belief, Defendant CAAL Worldwide, Inc. (hereinafter 
“Defendant CAAL”) at all times relevant to the events stated in this Complaint, was and remains 
a Kentucky corporation in the business of transportation services, licensed to do business in the 
Commonwealth of Kentucky and granted Organization Number 0791478 by the Kentucky 
Secretary of State. 
4. Upon information and belief, Defendant Frederick Moore is the owner and/or 
proprietor of Defendant CAAL, and at all times relevant to the events stated herein, was a resident 
of Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky, residing at the address set forth above. 
5. Upon information and belief, Defendants Frederick Moore and CAAL Worldwide, 
Inc., together or separately, are the owner(s) of the vehicle involved with Plaintiff’s injuries. 
6. Upon information and belief, Defendant Unknown Driver is a driver employed by 
Defendant CAAL who was acting within the course and scope of his employment and/or agency, 
as the operator of the CAAL vehicle during all relevant times herein. Despite due inquiry, Plaintiffs 
have been unable to ascertain the name of the individual driving the vehicle at issue herein. 
7. Upon information and belief, at the time of the incident described herein, Defendant 
Unknown Driver was an employee working as a driver for Defendant CAAL who was acting 
within the course and scope of his employment, and in furtherance of Defendant CAAL’s business. 

3 
8. On or about July 13, 2024, Plaintiffs and other individuals rented or otherwise 
commissioned a “party bus” to be driven by an individual employee of Defendant CAAL. 
9. On or about July 13, 2024, Defendant Unknown Driver did so negligently and 
carelessly operate the vehicle on Cane Run Road in Louisville, Kentucky, suddenly stopping 
without warning or notice, thereby causing Plaintiffs and others in the vehicle to fall. 
10. The negligence of Defendant Unknown Driver in the operation of his vehicle on the 
date and at the place aforesaid was a substantial factor in causing Plaintiffs serious and permanent 
injuries, including great pain of body and mind, both past and future. In addition, the Plaintiffs 
have incurred medical expenses as a result of the incident and injuries they sustained and will incur 
future medical expenses for the treatment of their injuries. Moreover, Plaintiffs have had their 
future ability to labor and earn money substantially impaired; all to their damage in a sum in excess 
of the minimal amount necessary to confer jurisdiction upon this Court. 
11. Pursuant to KRS 304.39-062(b), threshold requirements have been met by the 
Plaintiffs in that Plaintiffs have incurred over $1,000.00 in medical expenses and sustained serious 
injuries. 
12. Additionally, although Plaintiffs continue to investigate the facts surrounding the 
incident at issue, Plaintiffs state that to the extent the discovery or proof developed in this case 
indicates that Defendant Unknown Driver was operating his vehicle in a grossly negligent or 
reckless manner, or otherwise in violation of KRS 411.184 and/or KRS 411.186, Plaintiffs are 
entitled to pursue, and do hereby assert, a claim for punitive damages against all defendants herein 
should the proof developed establish that said Defendant authorized, ratified, or should have 
anticipated the negligent and/or unlawful conduct alleged herein.
