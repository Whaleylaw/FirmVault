Re: Adadevoh, et al. v. CAAL Worldwide, et al. 
 Jefferson Circuit Court, Case No. 25-CI-00133 
Counsel: 
 I am writing to follow up on Defendant CAAL Wordwise’s responses to our recent 
discovery requests. We have reviewed the responses in detail and respectfully request 
supplementation on several points where the answers provided are incomplete or insufficient: 
Interrogatory No. 2
 Defendant identifies Teresa Lee and Michelle Andrews as potential witnesses but omits 
their addresses. The interrogatory specifically requests this information and it must be provided. 
Please supplement with the last known addresses for both individuals. 
Interrogatory No. 10
 Defendant states it lacks “specialized knowledge” to answer, yet the interrogatory only 
seeks standard vehicle details such as transmission type, brake system, and mileage. This 
information is plainly accessible to a commercial carrier. If CAAL does not know the answers, it 
should say so clearly and explain what efforts, if any, have been made to obtain this routine data. 
Interrogatory No. 16
 The objection offered in response to this interrogatory is vague and inadequate. Defendant 
must confirm whether the subject vehicle was equipped with a Black Box and whether any effort 
has been made to obtain or preserve data. If data is unavailable or was not collected, that too should 
be stated clearly and directly. 
 
WHALEY

2 
 Interrogatory No. 20
 Defendant’s objection that it cannot respond because it “is an entity and not an individual” 
is incorrect and evasive. CAAL, like any corporation, acts through its representatives, and here, 
the individual identified as having participated in the preparation of responses is Frederick Moore, 
its owner. The refusal to provide even a basic narrative of the incident is indefensible. If Defendant 
wants to take the position that it cannot describe how it believes the incident occurred or who was 
at fault, that will speak for itself, but the failure to provide a substantive answer here is not 
acceptable. 
Request for Production No. 2 
 Defendant’s response states that photographs were produced, but none were attached. 
Moreover, the request specifically includes video footage, and no reference is made to whether 
any video exists or was reviewed. Please clarify whether any photographs or video exist and ensure 
all responsive materials are provided. 
Request for Production No. 3
 This request specifically seeks repair orders, estimates, and salvage documents related to 
the subject vehicle. The response references only “maintenance records,” which are not responsive 
to the request. Please supplement accordingly or confirm that no responsive documents exist. 
Request for Production No. 6
 As with Interrogatory No. 16, Defendant fails to confirm whether a Black Box is present 
in the vehicle or whether data has been retrieved. Please provide a direct response to whether the 
vehicle contains such a system and whether data exists or was accessed 
 We respectfully request that Defendant supplement its responses within ten (10) days of 
the date of this letter. Should these issues remain unresolved, we will be prepared to seek relief 
through a motion to compel and request appropriate costs and fees. Please let me know if you 
would like to discuss any of the above.
