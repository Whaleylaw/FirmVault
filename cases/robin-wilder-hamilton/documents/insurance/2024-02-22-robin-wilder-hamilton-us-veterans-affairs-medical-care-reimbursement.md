Understanding VA Bill Ledger
Please be advised that the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) Notice of Claim and VA Bill Ledger may contain
charges for care provided by a non-VA physician/medical facility in which theVAreimbursed a non-VA
provider/facility for the care via either the VA Fee/Purchased Care Program and/or the VA Choice Program.
In some cases, the amount billed by community care providers, the amount paid bytheVA for those services, and
the amount reflected on theVA bill ledger can differ. Please note that the VA bill ledger reflects the accurate VA
claim amount, as authorized by 38 C.F.R. §17.100.
Ifyou have knowledge of non-VA care that the VA paid for, provide evidence of thesame to this office.
Additionally, if you have knowledge of a difference between a VA bill amount and theamount that the VA
reimbursed a non-VA provider/facility, please identify each such bill and provide evidence of the difference. The
VA will take this information into consideration when determining its final claim amount and/or in response to a
request for compromise.
VA Billing:
38 C.F.R. § 17.101 establishes reasonable charges based on thetype of care being billed (note that per the
regulation, several types of care maybe billed for a single encounter). The type of care is a question determined
by medical providers under VA policy. Reasonable charges for each of the twelve types of care are determined by
specific type-of-care formulas using national base rates that are adjusted for the geographic area of the care
provided (with the single exception of prescription drugs which are billed at the cost toVA). VA publishes the
national base rates for each type of care in theFederal Register and at:
https://www.va.gov/communitycare/revenue_ops/payer_rates.asp.
The national base rates usually change annually on January 1st (outpatient/ professional) and October Ist
(inpatient). For billing format purposes, VA policy groups the twelve types of care into two types: Facility (billed
on Form UB-04) and Professional (billed on Form CMS 1500) charges.
VA’s “reasonable charges” are set by methodology and published in theFederal Register as required by 38 C_F.R.
§17.101(a)(2). Once published, VA billing rates are presumed to have been duly issued, prescribed, and
promulgated, 44 U.S.C. §1507.

| RESPONSIBILITIES OF INJURED PARTIES AND THEIR COUNSEL
By requesting and receiving evidence of the cost of care provided by the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA), injured
parties with care provided or paid for byVA are on notice of VA's claim and that it now must be satisfied. Should VA's
claim remain unsatisfied, the third party’s duty to pay VA and the injured parties’ duties to cooperate in the prosecution of
VA's claim will remain outstanding.
For assistance regarding VA's claim please contact the case manager identified on the notice of claim. Please visit
www.va.gov/oac/collections.asp for further information.
Issues concerning the relatedness of treatment on the attached ledger must be resolved with VA's Office of General
Counsel Collections National Practice Group prior to the injured party relying on the cost of said treatment to support
his/her damage claim. Stated differently, all costs for health care provided or paid-for by VA that are presented and relied
on to support a damage claim are deemed “related.” VA will not negotiate the relatedness of care after the costs of the
care have been presented and relied on in support of a damage claim. Timely written documentation of unrelated bills will
be reviewed by VA for possible cancelation prior to final resolution.
Injured parties and their counsel retain control over the handling of their case when asserting the cost of treatment
provided or paid for by VA as part of an injured party's claim for damages. If legal action has not yet been instituted,
include the following type of allegation in the injured party’s complaint. If legal action has been instituted, amend the
injured party’s complaint to include the following type ofallegation. Provide a copy ofall such documents to this office.
As a result of said injuries, the plaintiff has received and in the future will continue to receive medical and
hospital care and treatment furnished by the United States of America. The plaintiff, for the sole use ofand
benefit of the United States of America, under the provisions of42 U.S.C. § 2651 and 38 U.S.C. § 1729, and with
its expressed consent, asserts a claim for the reasonable value of said past and future care and treatment.
VA expects payment infull by check made to Department of Veterans Affairs and sent to VA’s Office of General Counsel
Collections National Practice Group. That same office will equitably resolve VA claims by taking into consideration
factors such as the costs of litigation when there are not sufficient settlement/judgment funds for all parties due to
insurance policy limits, litigation risk, comparative fault or other considerations. 42 U.S.C. § 2652. It is noted that factors
such as insufficient policy limits, litigation risk and comparative fault are assumed equally. Please also note that 5 U.S.C.
§ 3106 precludes VA from paying attorney’s fees.
In accordance with 28 C.F.R. § 43.2, among other duties, injured parties must notify VA’s Office ofGeneralCounsel
Collections National Practice Group of any settlement negotiations or mediation. In order forVA to properly assess the
value of its claim and provide any reasonably necessary assistance notice must be a minimum of7 days in advance.
To the extent not already provided or in the event of changes or updates to information previously provided, please
furnish and continue to furnish this office with all requested information in accordance with an injured party's duties.
Requested information includes: responsible third parties contact information and insurance information including
policy numbers, limits and any other details for responsible third parties including any counsel contact information;
injured party’s insurance carrier contact information; notice of payments for medical care from injured party’s
insurance - VA demands immediate payment therefrom; notice of non-VA medical treatment related to
accident/injury; copies of any complaint, any other legal filings, and police report; notice of withdrawal at the time of

of any settlement agreement prior to settlement to include particulars of proposed disbursement.

VA is entitled to reimbursement of the same under 38 U.S.C. §1729. This law authorizes VA to recover
reasonable charges as determined by the Secretary of VA from a third party, such as a health insurer. The
Secretary of VA has laid out the methodologies and formulas for creating such reasonable charges in 38
C.F.R. §17.101. The above-indicated billing was produced in compliance with this regulation. As per this
regulation, charges will vary depending on the geographic area inwhich treatment is rendered and may also
vary depending on the condition and health concerns ofthe patient. While this regulation allows for some
breakdown in charges such as standard or Intensive Care Unit rates for acute inpatient care, we are not
aware of anything in 38 C.F.R. §17.101 allowing for further itemization ofthe above-indicated billing that
the form in which billing is being submitted to the HEALTH INSURER.
Federal law prohibits health plans from discriminating against VA. Refusing to make payment to VA because
VA is not authorized to bill in the manner in which HEALTH INSURER would prefer constitutes just such
discrimination. 38 U.S.C. §1729(f) states “No provision of any contract or other agreement shall operate to
prevent recovery or collection by the United States.” Federal law does not allow for refusal to reimburse VA
on the basis of a provision that operates with the practical effect of preventing VA from recovering their
costs in situations whereaprivate facility would be entitled to recover. “Express exclusions or limitations in
third-party payer plans that are inconsistent with 38 U.S.C. §1729 are inoperative.” 38 C.F.R. §17.106(f}(2)(i).
See U.S. v. State of Ohio, 957 F.2d 231 (6th Cir. 1992); U.S. v. State Farm Ins. Co., 599 F.Supp. 441 (E.D.Mich.
