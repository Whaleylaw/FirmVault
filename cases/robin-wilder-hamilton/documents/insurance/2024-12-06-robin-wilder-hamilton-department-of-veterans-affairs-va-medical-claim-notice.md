VA’S FEDERAL MEDICAL CARE RECOVERY PROGRAM
This program ensures the responsible party pays for injury-related medical care provided by VA instead of the
American taxpayer. Money recovered supplements funds appropriated by Congress to help each VA medical
facility provide the excellent medical care and services that each Veteran deserves. VA’s medical facilities
together form one of the largest health care systems in the world. In addition to medical care for Veterans, VA
provides training to a majority of America’s health professionals & operates medical research programs
benefiting society at large. Advancements include: the nicotine patch (1984), benefits ofone aspirin a day
(1994), movement of paralyzed limbs (1991), sense of touch (2007) and electronic power (2014) to prosthetics,
continued leadership in research on PTSD & traumatic brain injury (2008, 2013).
NOTICE OF CLAIM FOR REIMBURSEMENT FOR THE COST OF MEDICAL CARE
VA submits the attached ledger ofcharges and will continue to update its claim and forward revised ledgers in
accordance with the creation of billing for any additional VA care related to this matter. If you know of related
VA care which is not included on the attached ledger, notify the Case Manager of the date(s) of service.
If there is an objection to a particular service’s relevancy to injuries incurred, timely written documentation
supporting that position must be sent directly to the Case Manager.
The VA Bill Ledger will contain charges for care provided by a non-VA physician/medical facility which was paid
for byVA under VA’s Fee/Purchased Care Program and/or Choice Program. Normally, once VA has made
payment to a non-VA provider, that provider is required to accept VA’s payment as payment in full and may not
bill the Veteran for the care. The amount non-VA providers billed VA, the amount paid by VA, and the amount
on the VA Bill Ledger can differ. The VA bill ledger reflects the accurate VA claim amount for such treatment, as
authorized by 38 C.F.R. §17.100. If you have knowledge of non-VA care that VA paid for, provide evidence of the
same including evidence of amounts paid byVA to the Case Manager. VA will take this information into
consideration when determining its final claim amount and/or in response to a request for compromise.
VA is entitled to reimbursement of reasonable charges for injury-related treatment it provided or paid for, or
will provide or paid for, from a tortfeasor and any applicable insurer under 42 U.S.C. § 2651, and from a VA
beneficiary’s insurance policy for medical payments (i.e. uninsured or underinsured motorist coverage}, a
workers compensation plan, and an auto reparation plan, among others, under 38 U.S.C. § 1729. VA’s
reasonable charges are set by methodology based on type of care and geographic area. The type of care is

RESPONSIBILITIES OF INJURED PARTIES AND THEIR COUNSEL
By requesting and receiving evidence of the cost of care provided by the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA), injured
parties with care provided or paid for by VA are on notice of VA's claim and that it now must be satisfied. Should VA's
claim remain unsatisfied, the third party’s duty to pay VA and the injured parties’ duties to cooperate in the prosecution of
VA's claim will remain outstanding.
For assistance regarding VA's claim please contact the case manager identified on the notice of claim. Please visit
www.va.gov/ogc/collections.asp for further information.
Issues concerning the relatedness of treatment on the attached ledger must be resolved with VA's Office of General
Counsel Collections National Practice Group prior to the injured party relying on the cost of said treatment to support
his/her damage claim. Stated differently, all costs for health care provided or paid-for byVA that are presented and relied
on to support a damage claim are deemed “related.” VA will not negotiate the relatedness of care after the costs of the
care have been presented and relied on in support of a damage claim. Timely written documentation of unrelated bills will
be reviewed by VA for possible cancelation prior to final resolution.
Injured parties and their counsel retain control over the handling of their case when asserting the cost of treatment
provided or paid for byVA as part of an injured party’s claim for damages. If legal action has not yet been instituted,
include the following type of allegation in the injured party’s complaint. If legal action has been instituted, amend the
injured party’s complaint to include the following type ofallegation. Provide a copy of all such documents to this office.
As a result of said injuries, the plaintiff has received and in the future will continue to receive medical and
hospital care and treatment furnished by the United States of America. The plaintiff, for the sole use ofand
benefit oftheUnited States of America, under the provisions of 42 U.S.C. § 2651 and 38 U.S.C. § 1729, and with
its expressed consent, asserts a claim for the reasonable value of said past and future care and treatment.
VA expects payment in full by check made to Department of Veterans Affairs and sent to VA’s Office ofGeneral Counsel
Collections National Practice Group. That same office will equitably resolve VA claims by taking into consideration
factors such as the costs of litigation when there are not sufficient settlement/judgment funds for all parties due to
insurance policy limits, litigation risk, comparative fault or other considerations. 42 U.S.C. § 2652. It is noted that factors
such as insufficient policy limits, litigation risk and comparative fault are assumed equally. Please also note that 5 U.S.C.
§ 3106 precludes VA from paying attorney’s fees.
In accordance with 28 C.F.R. § 43.2, among other duties, injured parties must notify VA’s Office ofGeneral Counsel
Collections National Practice Group of any settlement negotiations or mediation. In order for VA to properly assess the
value of its claim and provide any reasonably necessary assistance notice must be a minimum of7 days in advance.
To the extent not already provided or in the event of changes or updates to information previously provided, please
furnish and continue to furnish this office with all requested information in accordance with an injured party’s duties.
Requested information includes: responsible third parties contact information and insurance information including
policy numbers, limits and any other details for responsible third parties including any counsel contact information;
injured party’s insurance carrier contact information; notice of payments for medical care from injured party’s
insurance - VA demands immediate payment therefrom; notice of non-VA medical treatment related to
accident/injury; copies ofanycomplaint, any other legal filings, and police report; notice of withdrawal at the time of
an attorney’s withdrawal from a case or transfer to another attorney with new attorney contact information; and, notice
of any settlement agreement prior to settlement to include particulars ofproposed disbursement.

U.S.C. 1729 and 42 U.S.C. 2651, VA has
the right to recover or collect from a
third party or parties the reasonable
charges for certain medical care and
treatment furnished, to be furnished,
paid for or to be paid for by VA. Under
38 CFR 2.6(e)(3) and (e)(9), the authority

waive any claim and execute the release

or against a third person;
(b) Notify VA of a settlement with or
any offer of settlement from athird

(c) Cooperate in the prosecution of all
claims and actions by the United States
against such third person.
These duties necessitate affirmative
verification, or e-verification, to identify
all Veterans, or Veterans’ beneficiaries,
who may exist within any member class
including class action lawsuits, multidistrict litigation or other similar mass
tort litigation.
Under 38 CFR 17.47(g)(2), VA
