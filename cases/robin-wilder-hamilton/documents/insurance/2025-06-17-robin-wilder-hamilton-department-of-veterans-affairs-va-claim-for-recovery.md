This Notice of Claim for Recovery of Reasonable Charges and VA’s Ledger of Billed Charges are selfauthenticating documents, Federal Rule of Evidence 902. The below signed hereby attests and certifies the
documents and billed charges therein were created and are asserted within VA’s routine business processes
in accordance with federal law and regulation. 38 U.S.C. § 1729, 42 U.S.C. § 2651, 38 C.F.R. § 17.101.
NOTICE OF CLAIM FOR RECOVERY OF REASONABLE CHARGES
VA’s Federal Medical Care Recovery Program ensures the responsible party pays for an injured Veteran's
medical care, rather than the American taxpayers. Money recovered by this program supplements funds
appropriated by Congress to help VA medical facilities provide the excellent medical care and services each
Veteran deserves. VA is entitled to reimbursement of reasonable charges for injury-related treatment
VA provided or paid for, or will provide or pay for, from a tortfeasor and any applicable insurer
under 42 U.S.C. § 2651, and from a VA beneficiary’s insurance policy for medical payments (i.e.
uninsured or underinsured motorist coverage), a workers’ compensation plan, and an auto reparation
plan, among others, under 38 U.S.C. § 1729.
VA’s reasonable charges are set by methodologies set forth in 38 C.F.R. § 17.101. Data for calculating
charge amounts based on the methodologies therein is published in a notice in the Federal Register and/or
at: https://www.va.gov/communitycare/revenue-ops/payer-rates.asp. VA’s billing rates shall be
judicially noticed. 44 U.S.C. § 1507. State courts are subject to and bound byjudicial notice of federal
laws and regulations. The Federal Government is not required to litigate reasonableness of
administratively fixed rates as compared to prevailing rates at non-governmental facilities. VA is not subject
to state law conditions (i.e., time limits, fee schedules) where in conflict with federal law.
PAYMENT
VA's claim is not extinguished by a payment to or a release from the Veteran. VA’s claim must be paid at the
time of distribution. Contact VA Case Manager before settlement to confirm final VA claim amount. Payment
of less than the total amount of VA’s claim must be approved by VA's Office of General Counsel
Revenue Law Group. VA’s depositing of payment does not constitute an approval of a reduction or
compromise of VA’s claim.
Executive Order 14247 directs all federal agencies to process payments electronically. Payment in satisfaction
of this claim must be made via PAY.GOV (https://www.pay.gov/public/form/start/[PHONE-15]). See VA’s
Ledger of Billed Charges for case specific information required to complete payment.
For further program information, please visit www.va.gov/oge/collections.asp

RESPONSIBILITIES OF INJURED PARTIES AND THEIR COUNSEL
By requesting and receiving evidence of the cost of care provided by the Department of Veterans Affairs (VA), injured
parties with care provided or paid for byVA are on notice of VA’s claim and that it now must be satisfied. Should VA’s
claim remain unsatisfied, the third party's duty to pay VA and the injured parties’ duties to cooperate inthe prosecution
of VA's claim will remain outstanding.
For assistance regarding VA’s claim, please contact thecase manager identified onthe notice of claim.
Issues concerning the relatedness of treatment on the attached ledger must be resolved with VA’s Office of General
Counsel Collections National Practice Group prior to the injured party relying on the cost of said treatment to support
his/her damage claim. Stated differently, all costs for health care provided or paid-for byVA that are presented and relied
on to support a damage claim are deemed “related.” VA will not negotiate the relatedness of care after the costs of the
care have been presented and relied on in support of a damage claim. Timely written documentation of unrelated bills
will bereviewed byVA for possible cancelation prior to final resolution.
Injured parties and their counsel retain control over the handling of their case when asserting the cost of treatment
provided or paid for byVA as part of an injured party's claim for damages. If legal action has not yet been instituted,
include the following type of allegation in the injured party’s complaint. If legal action has been instituted, amend the
injured party’s complaint to include the following type of allegation. Provide a copy of all such documents to this office.
Asa result of said injuries, the plaintiff has received and inthe future will continue to receive medical and hospital care
and treatment furnished bythe United States of America. The plaintiff, forthe sole use of and benefit oftheUnited States
of America, under the provisions of 42 U.S.C. § 2651 and38 U.S.C. § 1729, and with its expressed consent, asserts a claim
for the reasonable value of said past and future care and treatment.
VA expects payment in full by check made to Department of Veterans Affairs and sent to VA’s Office of General Counsel
Collections National Practice Group. That same office will equitably resolve VA claims bytaking into consideration factors
such as the costs of litigation when there are not sufficient settlement/judgment funds for all parties due to insurance
policy limits, litigation risk, comparative fault or other considerations. 42 U.S.C. § 2652. It is noted that factors such as
insufficient policy limits, litigation risk and comparative fault are assumed equally. Please also note that 5 U.S.C. § 3106
precludes VA from paying attorney’s fees.
In accordance with 28 C.F.R. § 43.2, among other duties, injured parties must notify VA’s Office of General Counsel
Collections National Practice Group of any settlement negotiations or mediation. In order for VA to properly assess the
value of its claim and provide any reasonably necessary assistance notice must be a minimum of 7days in advance.
To the extent not already provided or in the event of changes or updates to information previously provided, please
furnish and continue to furnish this office with all requested information in accordance with an injured party's duties.
Requested information includes: responsible third parties contact information and insurance information including policy
numbers, limits and any other details for responsible third parties including any counsel contact information; injured
party’s insurance carrier contact information; notice of payments for medical care from injured party's insurance — VA
demands immediate payment therefrom; notice of non-VA medical treatment related to accident/injury; copies of any
complaint, any other legal filings, and police report; notice of withdrawal atthe time of an attorney's withdrawal from
a case or transfer to another attorney with new attorney contact information; and, notice of any settlement agreement
prior to settlement to include particulars of proposed disbursement.
