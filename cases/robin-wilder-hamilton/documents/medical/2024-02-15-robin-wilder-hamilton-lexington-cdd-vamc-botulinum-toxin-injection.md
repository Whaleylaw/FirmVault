6. Reason for the treatment (diagnosis, condition, or indication):
Muscle pain, spasms, and/or poor muscle control.
7. Treatment/procedure: This procedure involves injecting botulinum toxin into
your muscles that are having chronic repeated pain and/or spasms. Botulinum
toxin is a purified, low-concentration form of a toxin. It is thought to block
certain chemicals from reaching nerve endings. Muscle injections of the toxin
have shown to improve function and reduce pain.
Your skin will be cleaned and/or may be sprayed with a numbing medication. A
needle attached to a syringe containing the toxin will be inserted into the
targeted area. After the toxin is injected, the needle will be removed. You will
receive several injections around the affected muscles. It may take a week to
start feeling the effects of the injection. The effects may last up to three
months. You may see a better response with repeated doses over time.
8. No, neither anesthesia nor moderate sedation will be used in this
treatment/procedure.
9. Consent to Blood Products (if applicable):
It is not expected that blood products will be used in this
treatment/procedure.
