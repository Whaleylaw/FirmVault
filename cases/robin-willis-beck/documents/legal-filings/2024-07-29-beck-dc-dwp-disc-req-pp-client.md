of the Incident, which forms the basis of and is at issue in this lawsuit, and which is the subject of your Complaint.

9.The term "**communication**" and  “**communicate(d)**”means each manner or means of disclosure or exchange of information (in the form of facts, ideas, inquiries, or otherwise), regardless of means utilized, whether oral, electronic, by document or otherwise, and whether in an in-person meeting, by telephone, facsimile, e-mail (desktop or mobile device), text message, MMS or SMS message, regular mail, telexes, releases, or otherwise.

10."**Communication with**,'' "**communications from**," and "**communications  between**" means any communication involving the related parties, regardless of whether other persons were involved in the communication, and includes, but is not limited to, communications where one party is cc'd or bcc'd, both parties are cc'd or bcc'd, or some combination thereof.

11.“**Document**” means any document, electronically stored information, writings, recordings, photographs, drawings, graphs, charts, sound recordings, images, data, or data compilations stored in any medium from which information can be obtained, as set forth in Rule 34 of the Kentucky Rules of Civil Procedure and Rule 1001(1-4) of the Kentucky Rules of Evidence.

For purposes of the production of “**documents**,” the term shall include copies of all documents being produced; to the extent the copies are not identical to the original, thus requiring the production of copies that contain any markings, additions or deletions that make them different in any way from the original.

“**Documents**” also includes those within your possession, custody, or control, or the possession, custody, or control of any of your agents, heirs, assigns, and/or representatives, regardless of where located, as well as those “**documents**” you know exist, but are not now in your possession, custody, or control.  In the event that a document has been lost or destroyed, identify that last person known to be in possession of the document and describe its contents.

12.“**Identify**” means to set forth with sufficient information within the meaning of Rule 26.02(1) of the Kentucky Rules of Civil Procedure, the identity and location of the all people who know of any discoverable matter, such as name, residential address, business address, residential telephone number, business telephone number, business e-mail address, and personal e-mail address, as to enable this Defendant to issue an appropriate notice and/or subpoena for such person’s deposition and/or records. 

13.“**Person(s)**” means natural persons or legal entities recognized by law. 

14.Where an answer or response requires a “date,” the exact day, month, and year shall be provided; if such information is not reasonably ascertainable, then provide the best estimate.

14.The term “**relating to**” as used herein includes pertaining to, referring to, or having as a subject matter, directly or indirectly, expressly or impliedly, the subject matter of the specific request.

15.Where appropriate, the singular form of a word shall be construed to include the plural, and the plural form shall be construed to include the singular.

16.Where appropriate, “**and**” as well as “**or**” shall be construed either conjunctively or disjunctively, as necessary, to bring under the scope of these Interrogatories and Requests information which might otherwise be construed to be outside of their scope.

17.Where appropriate, “**each**” shall be construed to include “**every**” and “**every**” shall be construed to include “**each**”.

18.Where appropriate, “**any**” shall be construed to include “**all**” and “**all**” shall be construed to include “**any**”.

19.An Interrogatory asking you to “**state in detail**,” “**explain in detail**,” or “**state specifically**” seeks disclosure of each fact, circumstance, condition, and thing known to you about the subject of the Interrogatory containing the phrase, as of the date the Interrogatory is answered.

20.“**Inspection**” means the physical observation of any tangible matter.

21.“**Concerning**" means, directly or indirectly, in whole or in part, relating to, referring to, describing, evidencing or constituting.

22.When required to “**describe**” or “**identify**” a document, it is intended that your answer shall identify with respect to each document:

(a)title;

(b)date;

(c)author(s);

(d)addressee(s);

(e)recipient(s) of original or copy;

(f)file number or other identifying mark or code;

(g)nature and subject matter of the document; and

(h)location by room, building, and address, or if lost or no longer in 

existence, the last known location and custodian and the circumstances of the loss or destruction.

In lieu of identifying any document, a copy of such document may be attached to your answers.

23.In answering each Interrogatory, your answer should identify each document which refers to, supports, or otherwise evidences the subject matter of the Interrogatory; is relied upon to form the basis of the answer given; or in any way corroborates the answer or the substance of the answer given. Every document to be so identified may be produced for inspection in lieu of such identification.

24.In the event any document referred to in any Interrogatory or Request is not in your possession, custody, or control, specify what disposition was made of said document and identify the person currently having possession, custody, or control of the document.

25.To the extent that you identify and withhold any responsive documents from production, on the basis of any privilege or otherwise, please including in your Answer or Response, for each document, a written statement evidencing (a) the nature, date, and location of the document; (b) the author, sender, and recipient of the document; and (c) the nature of the privilege asserted and a brief description of the contents sufficient to allow the Court to rule on a motion to compel.

26.Please Bates label all documents produced. The Bates label should identify the party producing the document and should be placed in a location on each document that does not interfere with, obscure, affect, or otherwise disturb the existing text or markings.

27.These Interrogatories and Requests are continuing in nature so as to require supplemental responses in the event that you later obtain any additional information responsive to any of these Interrogatories or Requests, and such information has not been previously disclosed by the Estate of Trinity Jackson to Defendant or the additional information makes a previous disclosure more accurate.
