 Comes the Plaintiff, Robin Willis Beck, by counsel, and for her Complaint herein states as 
follows: 
 1. The Plaintiff, Robin Willis Beck (hereinafter “Plaintiff”), at all times relevant to the 
events stated in this Complaint, was and remains a resident of Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky, 
residing at the address set forth above. 
 2. Upon information and belief, the Defendant, Danny Wimmer Presents, LLC 
(hereinafter “Defendant DWP”), is and was at all times relevant to the events stated in this 
Complaint a Foreign Limited Liability Company with its principal office located at the address set 
forth above and authorized to conduct business in the Commonwealth. Defendant DWP promotes, 
organizes, and presents music festivals nationwide. 
 3. Upon information and belief, Defendant DWP organized, planned, constructed, 
produced, or otherwise maintained the Louder Than Life Festival, held in Louisville, Jefferson 
County, Kentucky at the Kentucky Exposition Center in September 2023. 
4. Upon information and belief, Defendant Kentucky State Fair Board is a corporate 
body established by KRS Chapter 247 and has usual corporate powers, including but not limited 
to the power to sue and be sued, and is subject to the jurisdiction of this Court and to service of 
process upon it through its managing agent, president, and CEO, David S. Beck. 
 5. Upon information and belief, Defendant Kentucky State Fair Board was the owner, 

3 
operator, and/or property manager or was otherwise in operation and/or control of the Kentucky 
Exposition Center and adjacent premises located at 937 Phillips Lane, Louisville, Kentucky, 
40209, (“The Exposition Center”). 
6. Upon information and belief, at all times relevant hereto, Defendant Kevin McCoy 
was the Executive Director of Operations for the Kentucky State Fair Board. 
 7. Upon information and belief, at all times relevant hereto, Defendant Shawn Hensler 
was the Executive Director of Safety and Security of the Kentucky State Fair Board. 
 8. Upon information and belief, at all times relevant hereto, Defendants McCoy and 
Hensler were employed and/or acting as agents, actual, ostensible, and/or otherwise, of Defendant 
Kentucky State Fair Board, holding the titles described above, and each being subject to the 
jurisdiction of this Court. 
 9. Upon information and belief, at all times relevant hereto, Defendants McCoy and 
Hensler were acting within the course and scope of their employment with Defendant Kentucky 
State Fair Board, and the claims in this Complaint are brought against these Defendants in their 
official capacity, and, separately, in their individual capacity for ministerial negligence in 
operating, managing, maintaining, and/or controlling a safe premises during the Louder Than Life 
Festival at the Kentucky Exposition Center on or about September 21, 2023. 
 10. Upon information and belief, Defendant Kentucky State Fair Board rented, leased, 
or otherwise authorized the use of the Exposition Center by Defendants Danny Wimmer and 
Southern Hospitality for the Louder Than Life music festival in September 2023. 
 11. On or about September 21, 2023, the Plaintiff was a customer and business invitee of 
Defendants and The Exposition Center and was attending the Louder Than Life music festival. 
 12. On or about September 21, 2023, the Plaintiff was walking from the “Food and Drink 

4 
Area” toward the center of the venue, when she stepped off of an unlit grass lip and fell onto concrete. 
The area at issue was poorly lit and the height difference was unable to be seen at night. 
 13. The unlit area and ledge as described above created a dangerous condition at The 
Exposition Center and Louder Than Life. 
 14. The dangerous condition described above was not marked with any warning or 
caution signs, and the Defendants, together or separately, knew or reasonably should have known of 
the dangerous condition and failed to warn The Exposition Center and Louder Than Life guests, 
including individuals who could have been injured by the condition, including the Plaintiff. 
 15. The Defendants, together or separately, had a duty toward the Plaintiff as their 
customer and/or business invitee to exercise ordinary care to keep The Exposition Center and/or 
Louder Than Life in a reasonably safe condition and to discover the existence of dangerous 
conditions existing in The Exposition Center and/or Louder Than Life and either correct them or 
warn of them. 
 16. The Defendants, together or separately, through their authorized agents, servants, or 
employees knew or should have known of the existence of the dangerous condition in the 
aforementioned area and failed to warn Plaintiff of it and/or correct it thereby breaching their duty 
owed to the Plaintiff. 
 17. The failure of the Defendants, together or separately, to discover, eliminate, 
remediate, or warn of the unreasonably dangerous condition in The Exposition Center and/or 
Louder Than Life or to otherwise make it safe constitutes negligence. 
 18. The negligence of the Defendants as alleged above was a substantial factor in 
causing the Plaintiff serious and permanent injuries, great pain of body and mind, and in causing 
the Plaintiff to incur medical expenses in the past and future for treatment of her injuries, all to her 

5 
damage in a sum in excess of the minimal amount necessary to confer jurisdiction upon this Court. 
 19. If the Defendants or their agents or employees, collectively or individually, had preexisting knowledge predating the Plaintiff’s injuries that others had been injured by the ledge in 
The Exposition Center and/or Louder Than Life or that it had existed for a long period of time and 
failed to warn or remediate the condition, the Defendants acted with gross negligence, or 
maliciously, oppressively, fraudulently and/or recklessly, entitling the Plaintiff to an award of 
punitive damages. 
 20. As discovery is ongoing, there may be other individuals or companies that may 
have been obligated by law, contract, ordinance, or otherwise to maintain the condition of The 
Exposition Center and/or Louder Than Life. As such, the Plaintiff reserves the right to amend this 
Complaint to assert additional claims against such other parties as information from discovery may 
reveal. 
 WHEREFORE, Plaintiff, demands as follows: 
 1. Judgment against the Defendants, together or separately, in a fair and reasonable 
amount sufficient to compensate Plaintiff for her damages alleged hereinabove; 
 2. Prejudgment interest; 
 3. Post judgment interest; 
 4. Trial by jury on all issues so triable; 
 5. Her costs herein incurred, including applicable attorney’s fees; 
 6. Punitive damages to the extent a trier of fact determines the actions of the Defendant to 
have been malicious, oppressive, fraudulent, or reckless; and, 
 7. Any and all other proper relief to which Plaintiff may appear entitled, including the right 
to amend this Complaint.
