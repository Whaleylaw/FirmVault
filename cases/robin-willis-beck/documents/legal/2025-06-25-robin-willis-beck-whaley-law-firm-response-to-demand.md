From the above picture, you’ll notice the presence of the food trucks (white truck and yellow truck) that 
are present in the photograph your client provided and confirmed in her deposition as the area of her fall. While 
your client could not testify as to what time she fell,4this picture indicates the general lighting of the area
sundown. As illustrated, the asphalt walkway and concrete risers would have been clearly visible to festivalgoers 
walking around that area.
Put frankly, and as the above picture indicates, our position is ultimately that DWP provided adequate 
lighting for festival goers ambulating through the premises. Given these photographs, we believe that a Kentucky 
jury will agree with that position. 
That said, while we do not believe DWP shares any culpability in relation to Mrs. Beck’s fall, we regret 
her injury on premises and recognize that this lawsuit entails further time and expense for all parties. As such, 
we are willing to settle this matter for $10,000 and the following conditions:
1. Entry of Dismissal of all Claims, with Prejudice; 
2. All parties to bear own costs of litigation to include attorney’s fees and court costs;
3. Full and final settlement agreement to include: 
a. No interest on settlement; 
b. Reasonable time to provide disbursement of settlement funds (45 days); 
c. Release of all defendants, assigns, officers, subsidiaries, attorney, and insurers, if any; 
d. Agreement by Plaintiff to be responsible for all liens;
e. Indemnity and Hold Harmless agreement by Plaintiff for any and all liens owed; 
f. Confidentiality provision; 
g. Acknowledgement of no Tax Advice by defendant(s) or Defendant’s Counsel; 
4. Counsel to provide W-9 and manner in which payment is to be made upon settlement; 
This settlement represents an amount greater than the charged medical expenses of Mrs. Beck. Please 
take this offer to your client and let us know any thoughts or concerns you have in relation to the above.
