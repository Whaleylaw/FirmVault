CIVIL ACTION NO. 23-CI-006039 JEFFERSON CIRCUIT COURT 
DIVISION SEVEN (7)
SCHRICE THOMAS as next of kin
on behalf of SARIYA MASON, a minor PLAINTIFF
v. RESPONSES TO REQUESTS FOR ADMISSIONS
YANELYS CASTILLO, et al. DEFENDANTS
* * * * *
Comes now Defendant, Surelis Castillo, by counsel, and for her Response to the Requests 
for Admission propounded upon her by Plaintiff respectfully states as follows:
GENERAL OBJECTION
Defendant objects to the “Instructions” and “Definitions” sections of Plaintiffs’ discovery 
requests on the ground that they are oppressive, unduly burdensome, confusing, vague, ambiguous, 
and impossible to coordinate with answers to questions propounded; moreover, the terms and 
apparent conditions of the “Instructions” and “Definitions” sections may seek information 
protected by the work product doctrine and/or the attorney client privilege. Without waiving said 
objections Defendant respectfully states as follows:
REQUEST NO. 1:
Please admit or deny that you were the owner of the dog Friday which was involved in an 
altercation with Plaintiff. 
RESPONSE: Defendant admits that she was the owner of the dog, but is without 
knowledge or information sufficient to form a belief as to the truth of the matter regarding whether 
the dog was “involved in an altercation with Plaintiff,” and accordingly denies same at this time.
REQUEST NO. 2:
Please admit or deny that on or about August 19, 2022, your dog Friday was involved in 
an altercation with Plaintiff.

2
RESPONSE: Defendant was out of the country on August 19, 2022, and, therefore, is 
without knowledge or information sufficient to form a belief as to whether her dog was “involved 
in an altercation with Plaintiff” on that day, and, therefore, accordingly denies same at this time. 
REQUEST NO. 3:
Please admit or deny that on or about August 19, 2022, immediately prior to the Incident 
one of the Defendants allowed the dog, Friday, to leave the property located at 2326 Quinn Drive, 
Louisville, Kentucky unrestrained. 
RESPONSE: Defendant was out of the country on August 19, 2022, and, therefore, is 
without knowledge or information sufficient to form a belief as to whether the dog was 
“allowed…to leave the property…unrestrained,” and, therefore, accordingly denies same at this 
time.
REQUEST NO. 4:
Please admit or deny that August 19, 2022, was not the first time that the dog Friday had 
left the property located at 2326 Quinn Drive, Louisville, Kentucky unrestrained.
RESPONSE: Defendant was out of the country on August 19, 2022, and, therefore, is 
without knowledge or information sufficient to form a belief as to whether the dog “left…the 
property…unrestrained” and, therefore, accordingly denies same at this time.
