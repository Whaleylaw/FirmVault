Comes the Plaintiff, Shrice Thomas as next kin on behalf of Sariya Mason, by and through 
counsel, and for her First Amended Complaint herein states as follows: 
1. Plaintiff restates and re-alleges all of the allegations contained in her initial 
Complaint, which is of record and hereby incorporated by reference. 
2. Plaintiff Sariya Mason, at all times relevant to the events stated in this complaint, 
was and remains a minor child and resident of Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky. 
3. Plaintiff Shrice Thomas, as mother and guardian of Plaintiff Sariya Mason, at all 
times relevant to the events stated in this complaint, was and remains a resident of Louisville, 
Jefferson County, Kentucky. 
4. At all relevant times herein, Defendant Surelis Castillo was an individual resident

of Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky. 
5. At all relevant times herein, Defendant Esther Ricardo Vilches was an individual 
resident of Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky, residing at the address set forth above. 
COUNT I: COMMON LAW NEGLIGENCE AGAINST DEFENDANTS 
 6. On August 19, 2022, Defendants Surelis Castillo and Esther Ricardo Vilches, 
together or separately, were the owner and/or keeper of a canine named “Friday” in Louisville, 
Jefferson County, Kentucky. 
 7. On August 19, 2022, the dog attacked, bit, and damaged the body of Plaintiff Sariya 
Mason while unattended, unrestrained, and roaming freely. 
 8. Upon information and belief, the incident identified herein was not an isolated 
incident. Rather and to the contrary, Defendants regularly allowed their dog to roam free and 
unrestrained without concern as to the potential harms or consequences of their actions, and as 
such, Defendants acted knowingly, maliciously, or recklessly on the date of the incident alleged in 
this Complaint. 
 9. At the time of the attack alleged herein, Plaintiff Sariya Mason was lawfully on the 
property of her own residence, not committing trespass or other tort, and was not tormenting or 
abusing the dog identified above. 
 9. The negligence of the Defendants, together or separately, in failing to properly 
restrain or maintain control over their dog on the date aforesaid was a substantial factor in causing 
Plaintiff serious and permanent injuries and scarring, including great pain of body and mind, both 
past and future, as well as loss of enjoyment of life and impairment to labor and earn money. In 
addition, the Plaintiff has incurred medical expenses as a result of the attack and injuries she 
sustained and will incur future medical expenses for the treatment of her injuries. 

3 
 10. The aforementioned damages incurred by Plaintiff are a sum in excess of the 
minimal amount necessary to confer jurisdiction upon this Court. 
COUNT II: STRICT LIABILITY AND/OR NEGLIGENCE PER SE 
AGAINST DEFENDANTS 
 11. In addition to the conduct described above, Defendants (together or separately) 
violated Kentucky statutory law on the date of the aforementioned incident, specifically including 
KRS 258.235, which makes a dog owner strictly liable for any damages (including injury to a 
person) caused by a dog. 
 12. Defendants further violated KRS 258.265 (failure to exercise proper care or control 
over a dog), as well as multiple Louisville Metro Ordinances, and/or other statutes or laws which 
may be applicable. 
 13. The Defendants’ statutory violations of law constitute negligence per se which 
resulted in injury to the Plaintiff, all as specified above, and such conduct is therefore actionable 
pursuant to KRS 446.070. 
 14. The Defendants’ conduct described herein was a substantial factor in causing 
Plaintiff serious and permanent injuries and scarring, including great pain of body and mind, both 
past and future, as well as loss of enjoyment of life and impairment to labor and earn money. In 
addition, the Plaintiff has incurred medical expenses as a result of the attack and injuries she 
sustained and will incur future medical expenses for the treatment of her injuries. 
 15. The aforementioned damages incurred by Plaintiff are a sum in excess of the 
minimal amount necessary to confer jurisdiction upon this Court. 
 16. The Plaintiff is entitled to recover from the Defendants for all harms and damages 
specified above or as may otherwise be proven in evidence.
