Comes the Plaintiff, by counsel, and gives notice that she will, on Monday, September 23,

MOTION TO COMPEL DISCOVERY RESPONSES 
Comes now the Plaintiff, Shrice Thomas as next of kin on behalf of Sariya Mason, a minor, 
by and through counsel, and pursuant to all applicable Civil Rules and/or Local Rules hereby move 
the Court for an Order compelling the Defendant Esther Vilches to respond fully to Plaintiff’s 
outstanding written discovery requests. In support thereof, Plaintiff states as follows: 
On July 9, 2024, Plaintiff served her First Set of Interrogatories, Requests for Production 
of Documents, and Requests for Admission upon Defendant Esther Vilches via email to her 
counsel of record, accompanied by a Notice of Service filed with this Court. 
On August 19, 2024, Defendant served her Responses to Requests for Admission, but did 
not respond to Plaintiff’s Interrogatories or Requests for Production of Documents. Thus, on 
September 3, 2024, Plaintiff’s counsel attempted to follow up on these outstanding via email to 
defense counsel. Exhibit 1. After receiving no response, Plaintiff’s counsel again reached out to 

2 
defense counsel via email. Again, Plaintiff’s counsel received no response. Despite good faith 
attempts by the undersigned to resolve this discovery dispute and obtain responses to Plaintiff’s 
discovery from the Defendant, they have not been obtained to date. 
Plaintiffs’ counsel affirmatively states that he has attempted to confer in good faith with 
Defendant’s counsel in an effort to obtain the responses identified herein, as detailed above, and 
believes that only a Court Order will result a due and sufficient response by the Defendant. An 
Order is appropriate under these circumstances pursuant to CR 37.01. 
WHEREFORE, the Plaintiff, by counsel, moved the Court to enter her tendered Order.
