CIVIL ACTION NO. 23-CI-006039 JEFFERSON CIRCUIT COURT 
DIVISION SEVEN (7)
SCHRICE THOMAS as next of kin
on behalf of SARIYA MASON, a minor PLAINTIFF
v. RESPONSES TO REQUESTS FOR ADMISSIONS
YANELYS CASTILLO, et al. DEFENDANTS
* * * * *
Comes now Defendant, Esther Ricardo Vilches, by counsel, and for her Response to the 
Requests for Admission propounded upon her by Plaintiff respectfully states as follows:
GENERAL OBJECTION
Defendant Vilches objects to the “Instructions” and “Definitions” sections of Plaintiffs’ 
discovery requests on the ground that they are oppressive, unduly burdensome, confusing, vague, 
ambiguous, and impossible to coordinate with answers to questions propounded; moreover, the 
terms and apparent conditions of the “Instructions” and “Definitions” sections may seek 
information protected by the work product doctrine and/or the attorney client privilege. Without 
waiving said objections Defendant respectfully states as follows:
REQUEST NO. 1:
Please admit or deny that you were the owner of the dog Friday which was involved in an 
altercation with Plaintiff. 
RESPONSE: Objection insofar as this Request may seek a conclusion of law regarding 
legal ownership which Defendant Vilches is not qualified to provide. Subject to, and without 
waiving said Objection, the dog, Friday, was owned by Defendant Vilches’s daughter, Surelis 
Castillo, who was out of the country at the time of the alleged incident. Defendant Vilches is 
presently without knowledge or information sufficient to form a belief as to the truth of the 
assertion that the dog was “involved in an altercation with Plaintiff,” and same is denied 

accordingly at this time.
REQUEST NO. 2:
Please admit or deny that on or about August 19, 2022, the dog Friday was involved 
in an altercation with Plaintiff.
RESPONSE: Objection insofar as this Request seeks a conclusion regarding a material 
issue which is the province of the jury. Subject to, and without waiving said Objection, Defendant 
Vilches states that she is presently without knowledge or information sufficient to form a belief as 
to the truth of the assertion that the dog was “involved in an altercation with Plaintiff,” and same 
is denied accordingly at this time.
REQUEST NO. 3:
Please admit or deny that on or about August 19, 2022, immediately prior to the 
Incident one of the Defendants allowed the dog, Friday, to leave the property located at 2326 Quinn 
Drive, Louisville, Kentucky unrestrained. 
RESPONSE: Denied.
REQUEST NO. 4:
Please admit or deny that August 19, 2022, was not the first time that the dog Friday 
had left the property located at 2326 Quinn Drive, Louisville, Kentucky unrestrained.
RESPONSE: Objection insofar as “left the property” is not defined. Defendant Vilches is 
without knowledge or information sufficient to respond conclusively to this Request. Therefore, 
denied as written. 

REQUEST NO. 5:
Please admit or deny that August 19, 2022, you had sole control and/or responsibility of 
the dog Friday at issue herein.
RESPONSE: Objection insofar as this Request may seek a conclusion of law regarding 
legal ownership which Defendant Vilches is not qualified to provide. Subject to, and without 
waiving said Objection, the dog, Friday, was owned by Defendant Vilches’s daughter, Surelis 
Castillo, who was out of the country at the time of the alleged incident. Therefore, denied as 
written.
