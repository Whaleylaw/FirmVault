Comes the Plaintiff, Shrice Thomas as next kin on behalf of Sariya Mason, by and through counsel, and requests the Defendant, Surelis Castillo (hereinafter “Castillo” or “Defendant Castillo”), to answer under oath the following Supplemental Interrogatories and Requests for Admissions, and to produce the following documents within thirty (30) days of service hereof in accordance with the Kentucky Rules of Civil Procedure. 

These Interrogatories and Requests for Production of Documents shall be deemed continuing. Knowledge of the attorneys for the Defendant or the claims agent or any other agent of the Defendant, or of any employee or agent of the Defendant, shall be deemed to be the knowledge of the Defendant. Anything coming to your knowledge after filing Answers to these Interrogatories and Responses to these Requests for Production which would change your original answer or responses should be contained in an Amended Answer or an Amended Response and served upon Plaintiff’s attorneys immediately after the same is brought to your attention.

<u>**DEFINITIONS**</u>

A.The words "and" and "or" shall be construed conjunctively or disjunctively as necessary to make the request inclusive rather than exclusive.

B.The use of the words "include(s)" and "including" shall be construed to mean "without limitation."

C.The words "related to" and "relating" mean relating to, referring to, reflecting, pertaining to, evidencing, or any way relevant to any issue, claim, defense, or question posed by the interrogatory or request within the meaning of CR 26.02(1).

D.“Document” as used herein means anything created or reduced to writing whether produced on paper or by electronic mean including but not limited to letters, faxes, emails, telephone messages, texts, tweets, memoranda, data stored on any computer hardware or software, in the cloud, on an electronic system or otherwise.

E.Plural shall include the singular and the singular shall include the plural.

F.The term "present” used herein to refer to a point in time shall mean the date upon which Responses to the Requests are being made.

G.“You” or “Your” or “Defendant” as used herein means the Defendant Surelis Castillo and any all of the Defendant’s employees, agents, or third persons who are under the supervision or control of the Defendant.

H.“Identify” or “explain” or “state” as used in these Interrogatories with respect to any person or thing means to state fully all who or what the thing or person is and if relating to a person to give the person’s full name, address, and other contact information to the extent known or available to the Defendant or other person contributing to the answers and responses and for that person to state and explain fully all of the information that person or persons has in connection with the subject matter of the request herein.

I.The term “incident” as used in these Interrogatories refers to the dog bite and associated series of events which occurred on August 19, 2022, as alleged in Plaintiff’s Complaint.

   <u>**INSTRUCTIONS**</u>A.It is expected that before answering the Interrogatories and responding to any Document Request, a good faith and complete inquiry by the Defendant, referring to all documents, correspondence, memoranda, records, books or other papers in the Defendant’s possession, custody or control will be done so as to enable the Defendant to make full and truthful answers to the following. 

B.All documents provided in response to these Requests shall be appropriately numbered or marked to the specific number of each Request.

C.If any document or thing requested is withheld on the grounds of privilege or work product, state for each such document:

1.  The nature of the privilege claimed;

2.  The name of the author and recipient of the document;

3.  The general subject matter of the document;

4.  The date of the document; and 

5.  Identify the custodian of such document.

D.In the event that any documents conforming to these Requests no longer exist or are not in the custody or possession of Defendant or counsel, Defendant shall so state under oath and in writing within the time prescribed for their production. In the case of documents which no longer exist, Defendant shall further state when and by whom the documents were disposed of or destroyed. In the case of documents which are not in the custody or possession of the Defendant or its counsel, Defendant shall further state the name and address of the person presently having custody or possession of them.  

E.If part but not all of a document Request is objected to, any part of the document Request to which no objection is made should be fully complied with, and the parts of document Request should be considered severable for this purpose.

   F.       If a document Request is objected to, the nature of the objection and the nature of the information with respect to which the objection is made shall be fully set forth.
