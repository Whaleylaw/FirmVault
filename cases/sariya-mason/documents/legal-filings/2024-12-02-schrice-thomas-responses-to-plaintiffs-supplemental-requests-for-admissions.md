Comes now Defendant, Surelis Castillo, by counsel, and for her Responses to Plaintiff’s
Supplemental Requests for Admissions propounded upon her by Plaintiff respectfully states as 
follows:
GENERAL OBJECTION
Defendant objects to the “Instructions” and “Definitions” sections of Plaintiffs’ discovery 
requests on the ground that they are oppressive, unduly burdensome, confusing, vague, ambiguous, 
and impossible to coordinate with answers to questions propounded; moreover, the terms and 
apparent conditions of the “Instructions” and “Definitions” sections may seek information 
protected by the work product doctrine and/or the attorney client privilege. Without waiving said 
objections Defendant respectfully states as follows:
REQUEST NO. 5: Please admit that Louisville Metro Animal Services was dispatched to 
your residence on Juny 19, 2019, in response to a complaint that your dog was loose.
RESPONSE: Defendant is without knowledge or information sufficient to form a belief 
as to the truth of the allegations, and therefore denies same at this time pending additional 
discovery.
