MOTION TO COMPEL DISCOVERY RESPONSES 
Comes now the Plaintiff, Shrice Thomas as next of kin on behalf of Sariya Mason, a minor, 
by and through counsel, and pursuant to all applicable Civil Rules and/or Local Rules hereby move 
the Court for an Order compelling the Defendant Surelis Castillo to respond fully to Plaintiff’s 
outstanding written discovery requests. In support thereof, Plaintiff states as follows: 
On October 18, 2024, Plaintiff served Supplemental Interrogatories, Requests for 
Production of Documents, and Requests for Admission upon Defendant Surelis Castillo via email 
to her counsel of record, accompanied by a Notice of Service filed with this Court. 
After receiving no response to these requests, on December 2, 2024, Plaintiff’s counsel 
reached out to defense counsel for an update on the outstanding responses. On that date, Defendant 
served her Responses to Requests for Admission, but did not respond to Plaintiff’s Interrogatories 
or Requests for Production of Documents. Thus, on December 4, 2024, Plaintiff’s counsel 

2 
attempted to follow up on these outstanding via email to defense counsel. Plaintiff’s counsel 
received no response. Despite good faith attempts by the undersigned to resolve this discovery 
dispute and obtain responses to Plaintiff’s discovery from the Defendant, they have not been 
obtained to date. 
Plaintiffs’ counsel affirmatively states that he has attempted to confer in good faith with 
Defendant’s counsel in an effort to obtain the responses identified herein, as detailed above, and 
believes that only a Court Order will result a due and sufficient response by the Defendant. An 
Order is appropriate under these circumstances pursuant to CR 37.01. 
WHEREFORE, the Plaintiff, by counsel, moved the Court to enter her tendered Order.
