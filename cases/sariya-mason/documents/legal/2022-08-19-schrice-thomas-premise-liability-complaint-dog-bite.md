PREMISE LIABILITY COMPLAINT: 
DOG BITE 
 
Comes the Plaintiff, by counsel, and for their cause of action states: 
1. The Plaintiff, Schrice Thomas, as next of kin, is at all times relevant hereto was a resident 
of Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky. 
2. The Plaintiff, Sariya Mason, a minor, is at all times relevant hereto was a resident of 
Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky. 
3. The Defendant, Yanelys Castillo, at all times relevant hereto was a resident of Louisville, 
Jefferson County, Kentucky. 
4. The Defendant, Surelis Castillo, at all times relevant hereto was a resident of Louisville, 
Jefferson County, Kentucky. 

5. On August 19, 2022, the Defendants Yanelys & Surelis Castillo, were the owner and/or 
keeper of a canine of unknown breed, in Louisville, Jefferson County, Kentucky. 
6. On the said date the dog did attack, bite and damage the body of Plaintiff, Sariya Mason, 
while left unattended, unrestrained and roaming freely. 
7. The Plaintiff, Sariya Mason, at the time the dog attacked and bit her was lawfully on the 
property of her own residence, not committing a trespass, or other tort, and was not 
teasing, tormenting, or abusing the dog. 
8. The Defendants knew or should have known by the exercise of reasonable care that the 
dog was dangerous. 
9. As a result of the attack, the Defendants are strictly liable to the Plaintiff, Sariya Mason, 
for all resulting damages pursuant to KRS 258.990 and KRS 258.095. 
10. Defendants, were negligent in their handling, supervision and/or maintenance of said dog 
causing Saryia Mason damages. 
11. Defendants failed to adequately warn the Plaintiff, Sariya Mason, a minor neighbor, of 
the dangerous nature of the dog. 
12. As a result of the attack, the Plaintiff, Sariya Mason, was seriously injured resulting in 
medical expenses, both past and future, pain and suffering both past and future, loss of 
the enjoyment of life, permanent scaring and loss wages both past and future of which 
result in damages in an amount in excess of the minimum dollar amount necessary to 
establish the jurisdiction of this court. 
WHEREFORE, the Plaintiff, Saryia Mason, demands against the Defendants, Yanelys & Surelis 
Castillo, as follows: 

1. For an amount which will fairly and adequately compensate that for pain and suffering, 
both mental and physical, past and future medical bills, mental anguish, loss of 
enjoyment of life and permanent scarring; 
2. For his/her costs herein expended; 
3. For prejudgment interest as allowed by law; 
4. For a trial by jury on all counts so triable; 
5. For any and all relief to which he may appear entitled.
