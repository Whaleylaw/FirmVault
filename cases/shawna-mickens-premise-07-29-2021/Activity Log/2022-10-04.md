Interesting. Well, I will say if Metro doesn’t respond to BS’s motion, and you and BS stipulate to dismissal based on the arguments on pages 16-18 of BS’s MTD—which you should do at the latest possible moment, like at the hearing on the MTD—then there can’t be apportionment against BS as a matter of law. See, e.g. Morris v. Boerste, 641 S.W.3d 688, 694 (Ky. Ct. App. 2022)(apportionment “require[s] proof at trial of a party's negligence or lack of reasonable care that caused the injury.”) If BS is dismissed out based no duty, superseding cause, etc, then no apportionment line for BS.

If they get dismissed for the pre-injury release or arbitration, that’s a different story. Those aren’t fault-based dismissals.

Thanks Aaron.

They’re arguing that the FAA applies (the doc selects the FAA as the governing law for the arb provisions, and CA law for all else). The FAA preempts state law, including Ally Cat. Our SC held that Ally Cat only applies to matters that aren’t governed by the FAA. "Ally Cat has no applicability to an arbitration agreement governed exclusively by the Federal Arbitration Act.” Ernst & Young, LLP v. Clark, 323 S.W.3d 682, 687 (Ky. 2010).

The pre-injury waiver is also problem. See attached case which recently became final involving the Thirsty Pedaler. I think you’re right about maybe getting past the MTD to at least getting discovery on whether BS violated any statute or ordinance, whether their conduct was willful/wanton (another end-around pre-injury waivers), whether the release comports with Hargis v. Baize, etc.  Ideally though, if you have that information, it’s better to amend the complaint and allege statutory violations, willful or wanton conduct, etc. On that note: what is it that BS did here?  

The main problem is arbitration though. Even if you get past the MTD/MTC, BS — like the Metro Defendants—can take an interlocutory appeal. The Metro Defendants get that right through Breathitt Co. Bd of Ed. v. Prater. BS gets that right by statute: KRS 417.220(1)(a). Unfortunately, this case features the two main exceptions to final-judgment appealability: immunity and arbitration.

Extension on this one to Oct 31 too?
