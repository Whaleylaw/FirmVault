NO. 23-CI-8019 JEFFERSON CIRCUIT COURT 
 DIVISION TWELVE (12) 
 JUDGE SUSAN GIBSON 
WAYNE WEBER PLAINTIFF 
vs. DEFENDANT HEYMAN’S ANSWER
Electronically Filed
JAMES FENWICK DEFENDANT 
 * * * * * * * * * 
 Comes the Defendant, James Fenwick, by counsel, and for his Answer to the Plaintiff’s 
Complaint, filed herein, states as follows: 
FIRST DEFENSE
 The Complaint fails, in whole or in part, to state claims against this Defendant for which 
relief can be granted. 
SECOND DEFENSE
 The accident, injuries and damages, if any, of which Plaintiff complains were caused and 
brought about, in whole or in part, by the carelessness and negligence of the Plaintiff, or persons 
not yet a party to this action, and if said negligence or carelessness on the part of the Plaintiff, or 
persons not yet a party to this litigation were proven at trial, same would act as a bar or limitation 
on Plaintiff’s claims against him herein. 
THIRD DEFENSE
 Defendant’s investigation of this claim is not complete at this time and Defendant may 
have defenses which are not presently known. Therefore, in order to preserve any such defenses, 
the Defendant incorporates by reference and adopts as part of his Answer herein, all defenses as 
set forth in Rules 8 et. seq. and 12 et. seq. of the Kentucky Rules of Civil Procedure and reserves 

the right to amend his Answer hereto to assert additional defenses and affirmative defenses and 
to file any Crossclaims, Counterclaims or Third Party Claims which he deems appropriate after 
discovery. 
FOURTH DEFENSE
 Plaintiff’s claims herein are governed by the Kentucky Motor Vehicle Reparations Act, 
and Defendant cites each and every defense available thereunder. 
FIFTH DEFENSE
1. Defendant assumes the truth of the allegations regarding residency as contained in 
paragraph 1 of the Complaint, subject to proof of the contrary. 
2. Defendant admits the allegations regarding residency as contained paragraph 2 
of the Complaint. 
3. As to the allegations in paragraphs 3 and 4 of the Complaint, Defendant admits only 
the date and location of a motor vehicle accident involving Plaintiff Wayne Weber, 
Defendant, James Fenwick. Defendant specifically denies any and all allegations of 
negligence, carelessness, and recklessness on the part of this Defendant. 
4. With regard to the allegations contained in paragraphs 5 and 6 of the Complaint, 
Defendant admits the location of the subject accident and that Plaintiff has alleged 
damages in excess of the jurisdictional requirements of this Court. 
5. Any allegation contained in Plaintiff’s Complaint not specifically admitted is hereby 
denied. 
 WHEREFORE, Defendant, James Fenwick, demands as follows: 
 1. Dismissal of Plaintiff’s Complaint against Defendant in its entirety; 
 2. Apportionment of fault as may be deemed appropriate by the evidence introduced 

at trial; 
3. For his costs herein expended; 
4. For a trial by jury on all issues so triable; 
5. All other relief to which Defendant may be entitled, including leave to file 
amended and supplemental pleadings as may be deemed appropriate by the 
evidence introduced at trial.
