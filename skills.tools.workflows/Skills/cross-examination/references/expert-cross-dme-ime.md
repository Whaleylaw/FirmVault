# Cross-Examining a DME / IME Doctor

## When this applies

The defense has designated a medical expert — typically an IME ("independent" medical examiner) or DME (defense medical exam) doctor — and you need to cross them at deposition or trial. The goal is not to win a medical debate; it is to show the jury the opinion is bought, rushed, or based on a selective record.

---

## Pre-deposition background research

Use `legal-research` for web background and `courtlistener-legal-research` for prior testimony. Target:

1. Publication history and what it actually says
2. Prior testimony history and which side they usually work for
3. Estimated income from defense work
4. Professional background and board certifications
5. Any disciplinary history
6. Cases where they've been excluded or criticized by a court

Key numbers to nail down before the depo:
- Approximate annual income from IME/DME work
- Number of exams per year
- Percentage of work for defense vs. plaintiff
- How many times this same law firm has retained them

---

## The five cross-examination areas

### 1. Bias and financial interest
- Annual litigation-work income
- Percentage defense vs. plaintiff
- Relationship with defense firms and insurers
- Referral sources
- Number of exams per year

### 2. Limited examination
- Time spent with the patient (compare to treating physician's hours)
- Tests not performed
- Records not reviewed
- History not obtained

### 3. Cherry-picking the records
- Records reviewed vs. records available
- Findings mentioned in the report vs. findings in the underlying records
- Reliance on incomplete or one-sided data
- Contradictory evidence ignored

### 4. Methodology challenges
- Basis for opinions
- Acceptance of the methodology in the medical community
- Deviation from standards of practice
- Absence of objective testing

### 5. Contradictions
- Inconsistent with the expert's own writings
- Inconsistent with their prior testimony
- Inconsistent with medical literature
- Inconsistent with the treating doctors

## Sample question blocks

### Financial bias
```
Q. You earn income from performing IMEs, correct?
Q. Approximately how much did you earn from IMEs last year?
Q. The majority of your IME work is for defendants, correct?
Q. You've performed IMEs for this law firm before?
Q. If you found in favor of plaintiffs more often, you'd receive fewer referrals, wouldn't you?
```

### Limited exam
```
Q. You spent approximately 30 minutes with [Client], correct?
Q. Her treating doctor has seen her more than 20 times?
Q. You did not perform [specific test], did you?
Q. You did not review [specific record], correct?
```

### Cherry-picking
```
Q. You noted the MRI showed [finding], correct?
Q. But you didn't mention [other finding] in your report?
Q. Dr. [Treating] found [positive finding], didn't he?
Q. That's not in your report, is it?
```

## Preparation checklist

- [ ] Background research memo completed and saved to `cases/<slug>/documents/research/<expert-slug>-background.md`
- [ ] Every prior testimony transcript you can find reviewed
- [ ] Expert's report compared against the treating records, with omissions flagged
- [ ] Contradictory literature identified
- [ ] Financial-bias numbers calculated
- [ ] Topic-by-topic outline drafted
- [ ] Impeachment exhibits pre-marked

## Outputs

- Background research memo
- Cross-examination outline (topic-based)
- Impeachment exhibit list with the point each one makes
- List of key admissions to obtain
