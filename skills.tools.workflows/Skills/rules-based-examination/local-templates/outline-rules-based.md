## 1. Background (brief — 10 min max)

- Name, role, training, tenure
- Scope of responsibility on the day in question
- No fact-of-the-case questions yet

## 2. Rules establishment (rules in the abstract — BEFORE the case facts)

For each rule in the inventory:

### R-1: <rule text>

```
Q. A <role> must <rule>, correct?
Q. That's because <reason tied to safety/harm>?
Q. Violating that rule would be <careless>?
Q. … <negligent>?
Q. … <reckless or dangerous>, depending on the circumstances?
Q. You were trained on this rule, right?
```

## 3. Policies and training (defendant's own materials)

- Walk through the employee handbook / training slide deck / SOP
- Confirm the witness received and acknowledged each relevant section
- Lock agreement with the written rule *as written*

## 4. Application to the case facts

Only now introduce the facts of this incident.

- What happened on <date> at <location>?
- Which of the rules from §2 applied in that moment?
- Did the witness follow each one? Walk through one by one.
- Use prior statements / records / video to pin answers that drift.

## 5. Prior incidents and notice

- Any prior complaints, near-misses, or similar incidents?
- When was the rule last reviewed or retrained?
- Any changes to the rule since the incident?

## 6. Closing gambit

```
Q. Looking back at this incident, were any mistakes made?

[If YES:]
Q. What mistakes?
Q. Why did they happen?
Q. What has been done to prevent this from happening again?

[If NO:]
Q. So your conduct was exactly as trained and expected?
Q. If you had to do it over again, you would do nothing different?
Q. The procedures were followed exactly as designed?
Q. And this injury still occurred?
```
