# Adjuster File Closure Threats

> Insurance adjusters — both PIP and BI — will threaten to close their claim file when the firm fails to respond to repeated outreach attempts. This is distinct from the adjuster-unreturned-calls pattern (where the adjuster is trying to settle); here the adjuster is explicitly warning that inaction will result in lost benefits or a closed claim. File closure threats are most dangerous on PIP claims where benefits may be forfeited.

## Pattern

### Escalation Sequence
1. **Initial Contact**: Adjuster calls or emails requesting information (PIP demand, medical release, records)
2. **First Follow-Up** (2-4 weeks later): Adjuster calls again, references prior attempt
3. **Second Follow-Up** (2-4 weeks later): Adjuster becomes more explicit about consequences
4. **Closure Warning**: Adjuster states "if I do not hear back from anyone from your firm, I am going to go ahead and assume to close this file"
5. **File Closure**: Adjuster closes the claim, requiring formal reopening if the firm later wants to pursue it

### PIP File Closure (Most Dangerous)
When a PIP adjuster closes the file, the client may lose:
- Remaining PIP benefits (up to $10K in Kentucky)
- PIP coverage for future medical bills
- The ability to submit a PIP demand for reimbursement

### BI File Closure (Less Immediate Risk)
When a BI adjuster closes their file:
- The claim is not extinguished (SOL governs that)
- But the adjuster's settlement authority may be withdrawn
- Reopening requires re-review and potentially new adjuster assignment
- The carrier's willingness to negotiate may decrease

### Root Causes of Non-Response
- Case is not actively being managed (between case managers)
- Firm is focused on a different track of the same case (e.g., litigating BI while ignoring PIP)
- Staff member handling the case left and no handoff occurred
- Automated missed-call logging does not create follow-up tasks

## Evidence

- In a multi-track MVA case (DOI 1/2023, KY), the PIP adjuster (Shelter Insurance) left 5+ voicemails over 6 months. The voicemails escalated from "just to see if you guys are going to be sending in a PIP demand" to "if I do not hear back from anyone from your firm, I am going to go ahead and assume to close this file." The adjuster also sent emails that bounced (staff email address was incorrect). The firm had been focused on the BI/Medicare demand track and had not engaged with PIP at all.
- In the same case, the adjuster identified a specific issue blocking progress: "we have a Medicare demand in the file, but it has related charges on it. Unrelated charges that we need an updated Medicare lien. We can only get this if we get the doctor's release from your firm." This shows the adjuster had identified a concrete next step but could not proceed without firm cooperation.
- In a second case, the adjuster's voicemail pattern showed clear escalation: first call was informational ("calling to discuss"), second was follow-up ("following up on my voicemail from 2 weeks ago"), third was urgent ("trying to get a status of the return settlement release"), and fourth included a warning about consequences for non-response.
- In 3 additional cases, BI adjusters followed up on unreturned releases or demand acknowledgments. While not explicitly threatening closure, the pattern of escalating follow-ups (3-5 attempts) suggests that non-response beyond 4-6 weeks puts the claim relationship at risk.

## Agent Guidance

1. Treat adjuster voicemails with file-closure language as URGENT — respond within 24 hours
2. Even if the case is not ready for a PIP demand or settlement, call the adjuster to provide a status update: "We are still treating / collecting records / waiting for [X]. We will be in touch by [date]."
3. Never let a PIP adjuster close a file without attorney review — PIP benefits may be permanently lost
4. When an adjuster references a specific document need (medical release, updated lien, records), fulfill the request within 1 week or provide a timeline
5. Create a follow-up task for EVERY adjuster voicemail — do not rely on activity log entries alone
6. When staff email addresses change, update carrier contacts immediately to prevent bounced communications

## Pitfalls

- Ignoring adjuster closure warnings because the firm is focused on a different claim track
- Assuming the adjuster will not actually close the file (they will)
- Not having a process to escalate adjuster closure threats to the attorney
- Staff email changes causing adjuster emails to bounce without anyone knowing
- Treating PIP adjuster follow-ups as low priority (PIP benefits are time-sensitive)
