# Case Lifecycle Duration Patterns

> PI cases vary dramatically in duration based on case type, complexity, and outcome pathway. Analysis of 228 archived cases with complete lifecycles reveals that the median case lasts approximately 11 months (330 days), with MVA cases clustering around this median and specialty case types (premises liability, slip-and-fall, dual claims) trending significantly longer. Understanding these benchmarks is essential for client expectation management and resource allocation.

## Pattern

### Overall Duration Benchmarks
- **Median duration**: 330 days (~11 months)
- **Average duration**: 351 days (~11.7 months)
- **Range**: 1 day (same-day decline) to 835 days (~2.3 years)
- **Based on**: 228 cases with documented date ranges from first to last activity log

### Duration by Case Type
| Case Type | Count | Avg Days | Median Days | Min | Max |
|-----------|-------|----------|-------------|-----|-----|
| MVA | 191 | 352 | 330 | 5 | 835 |
| Workers Comp | 15 | 272 | 263 | 37 | 519 |
| Premises Liability | 4 | 438 | 588 | 37 | 615 |
| Slip and Fall | 3 | 732 | 763 | 644 | 788 |
| Medical Malpractice | 2 | 55 | 76 | 34 | 76 |
| Dog Bite | 1 | 779 | 779 | 779 | 779 |
| Dual WC/MVA | 1 | 628 | 628 | 628 | 628 |
| Dual WC/PL | 1 | 811 | 811 | 811 | 811 |

### Key Duration Insights
1. **MVA cases are the baseline**: 191 cases averaging ~352 days, with remarkable consistency (median 330)
2. **Workers comp resolves faster**: Average 272 days, but includes both simple claims and complex dual cases
3. **Premises liability/slip-and-fall cases take longest**: Averaging 438-732 days due to litigation tendency
4. **Medical malpractice cases resolve fastest or are declined quickly**: Average 55 days suggests rapid decline
5. **Dog bite and dual cases are outliers**: Small sample sizes but consistently long (600-800+ days)

### Same-Day or Rapid Decline Pattern
- Some cases (min 1-5 days) are opened and closed immediately when coverage gaps or client fault are discovered at intake
- These rapid declines pull down the average but represent an important workflow pattern

## Evidence

- In 228 archived cases with complete lifecycles (first log to last log), the median duration was 330 days. MVA cases (n=191) had an average of 352 days (median 330, range 5-835). Workers comp cases (n=15) averaged 272 days. Premises liability cases (n=4) averaged 438 days, and slip-and-fall cases (n=3) averaged 732 days.
- Medical malpractice cases (n=2) had the shortest average at 55 days, reflecting early case evaluation and decline rather than case development.
- Dual claim cases (WC/MVA, WC/PL, Premise/MVA) consistently exceeded 400 days, reflecting the complexity of managing two parallel claim tracks.
- The longest observed case duration was 835 days (~2.3 years) for an MVA case, consistent with cases that proceed through litigation before settlement.

## Agent Guidance

1. Set client expectations based on case type: MVA ~11 months, PL/slip-and-fall 1.5-2+ years, WC ~9 months
2. If a case approaches 18 months (540 days), flag for attorney review — it may be stalled
3. Rapid decline cases (< 30 days) should be identified within the first week based on coverage verification
4. Use these benchmarks to identify outlier cases that may need intervention
5. Dual claim cases should be staffed with expectation of 18+ month timelines

## Pitfalls

- Quoting average duration to clients (includes both rapid declines and multi-year cases) — use median instead
- Not accounting for case type when setting timeline expectations
- Treating same-day declines as "completed cases" in performance metrics
