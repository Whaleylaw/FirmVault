# Case Outcome Distribution Patterns

> Analysis of 230 archived cases with complete lifecycles reveals that PI cases follow a funnel: approximately 56% reach settlement, 51% are declined or closed without settlement, and 19% involve litigation at some point. These numbers overlap — cases can show both settlement and decline activity, and litigation often leads to settlement rather than trial. Understanding this distribution is critical for firm resource allocation and intake decision-making.

## Pattern

### Outcome Funnel (230 archived cases)
| Outcome Signal | Cases | Percentage |
|---------------|-------|------------|
| Settlement activity | 128 | 56% |
| Decline/closing activity | 117 | 51% |
| Negotiation activity | 93 | 40% |
| Litigation activity | 43 | 19% |
| Demand submitted | 51 | 22% |
| Disbursement documented | 5 | 2% |

### Settlement Pathways
- **Negotiation-only settlement**: 68 cases (53% of settlements) — settled without litigation
- **Litigation-then-settlement**: 32 cases (25% of settlements) — filed suit then settled
- **With lien resolution**: 94 cases (73% of settlements) had concurrent lien activity

### Decline/Closing Triggers
- **Unreachable client co-factor**: 64 of 117 decline cases (55%) — the most common co-factor
- **No-coverage finding**: 23 of 117 decline cases (20%) — explicit coverage gaps
- **KAC involvement**: 60 cases total had KAC (Kentucky Assigned Claims), indicating uninsured at-fault drivers

### Case Type Distribution
| Case Type | Count | % of Total |
|-----------|-------|------------|
| MVA | 191 | 83% |
| Workers Comp | 16 | 7% |
| Unknown/Other | 9 | 4% |
| Premises Liability | 4 | 2% |
| Slip and Fall | 3 | 1% |
| Medical Malpractice | 2 | 1% |
| Dog Bite | 1 | 0.4% |
| Dual Claims | 3 | 1.3% |
| General PI | 1 | 0.4% |

### Key Ratios
- **Settlement rate**: ~56% of all cases
- **Litigation rate**: ~19% of all cases
- **Litigation-to-settlement conversion**: 74% of litigated cases also showed settlement (32/43)
- **Demand-to-settlement conversion**: High — 51 demands submitted, 93 cases with negotiation activity
- **Records-to-demand conversion**: 45% — only 51 of 114 cases with records activity progressed to demand

## Evidence

- In 230 archived cases, 128 (56%) showed settlement activity, 117 (51%) showed decline/closing activity, 93 (40%) had negotiation activity, and 43 (19%) involved litigation.
- The settlement and decline percentages exceed 100% combined because some cases show both patterns (partial settlement on one claim, decline on another, or settlement after initial decline consideration).
- 68 cases settled through negotiation alone; 32 required litigation before settlement — confirming that most PI settlements are pre-litigation.
- Only 5 cases had explicit disbursement documentation, suggesting the disbursement step is under-documented in activity logs or occurs outside the log system.
- MVA cases dominated at 83% of the portfolio. Workers comp (7%), premises liability (2%), and slip-and-fall (1%) were the next most common case types.
- Negotiation intensity averaged 2.9 mentions per case (median 2, max 10), suggesting most negotiations involve 2-3 documented rounds.

## Agent Guidance

1. At intake, understand that approximately half of all retained cases will ultimately be declined — focus early coverage verification to identify declines sooner
2. Cases that reach demand submission have a high probability of settlement — invest in demand quality
3. Litigation should be viewed as a settlement tool, not a separate pathway: 74% of litigated cases settle
4. Resource allocation should reflect the 83% MVA case type dominance
5. Track the records-to-demand conversion rate (45%) as a bottleneck metric

## Pitfalls

- Assuming all retained cases will settle — approximately half will be declined
- Over-investing in cases that will ultimately be declined due to coverage gaps discoverable at intake
- Under-resourcing lien management — 73% of settlements require lien resolution
- Treating litigation and settlement as mutually exclusive outcomes
