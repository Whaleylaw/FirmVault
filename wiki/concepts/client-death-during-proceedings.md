# Client Death During Active Proceedings

> In rare cases, a client may die while their case is in active litigation. This creates immediate procedural requirements: the firm must notify opposing counsel, cancel scheduled proceedings (depositions, IMEs), and determine whether the claim survives the client's death (which depends on case type and jurisdiction). The firm typically learns of the death through a family member contact.

## Pattern

### Discovery of Death
- A family member (parent, spouse, child) contacts the firm, often via voicemail to the assigned attorney
- The caller may reach the firm's general line or an attorney who doesn't primarily handle the case
- The family member may reference the client's next scheduled event (e.g., "He was supposed to be there for a deposition tomorrow")

### Immediate Actions Required
1. Verify the information with the family contact
2. Notify defense counsel immediately (same day, via email)
3. Cancel all scheduled proceedings (depositions, IMEs, hearings)
4. Notify the court if applicable
5. Consult with the attorney on claim survivability

### Claim Survivability
- **Workers' compensation**: WC death benefits may be available to dependents; the claim may convert from a personal injury WC claim to a death benefit claim
- **Personal injury (PI)**: In Kentucky, wrongful death and survival actions may apply if the death is related to the injury; the estate must be substituted as plaintiff
- **Unrelated death**: If the death is unrelated to the case injury, the personal injury claim may still survive through the estate under survival statute provisions

### Estate Substitution
- An estate representative (executor/administrator) must be identified
- Motion to substitute the estate as plaintiff may be required
- Probate proceedings may need to be initiated if no estate exists

## Evidence

- In a WC case (workplace injury at automotive plant, DOI 5/2023), the client passed away on 11/2/2025. The client's mother left a voicemail the day before the scheduled deposition (11/14/2025): "My son was supposed to be there for a deposition tomorrow. He has passed away..." The assigned attorney sent an email to defense counsel that same evening notifying them of the death and requesting a call to discuss next steps. The most recent substantive filing before the death was a document filed via LMS. The case remained open as of the last activity log, indicating procedural resolution was still pending.

## Agent Guidance

1. If a family member calls reporting a client's death, treat as highest priority — route to the assigned attorney immediately
2. Document the caller's name, relationship, contact information, and date of death
3. Do NOT discuss case details with the family member until attorney authorization
4. Cancel all scheduled proceedings within 24 hours
5. Send written notification to all opposing counsel
6. Flag the case for attorney review regarding claim survivability
7. If an estate needs to be opened, coordinate with the family and the attorney on probate requirements

## Pitfalls

- Delaying notification to opposing counsel — this can create ethical issues
- Discussing settlement or case value with the grieving family member
- Assuming the claim dies with the client — many claims survive through the estate
- Failing to cancel scheduled proceedings (depositions, IMEs, hearings)
- Not documenting the notification chain (who was told, when, how)
