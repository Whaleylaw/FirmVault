# Demand Sent by Prior Firm

> When a client transfers from another firm that already sent a demand letter, the new firm inherits a negotiation position they did not create. This complicates strategy because the new firm must understand the demand's terms, the adjuster's response, and any existing offers before proceeding.

## Pattern

This edge case creates several challenges:

1. The new firm may not know the demand amount or its basis without the prior firm's file
2. An offer may already exist (the client may have reported it during intake)
3. The new firm cannot simply send a new demand — the adjuster already has one
4. The case may be in "negotiation" phase from day one, skipping the firm's normal demand preparation workflow
5. The attorney may need to "wrap up" the case rather than run a full demand cycle

## Evidence

- In Case A, the client reported an existing offer (described as approximately $18K) from the prior firm's negotiation. When the new firm's attorney reviewed the file, he noted "Demand was sent by [prior firm]" and "It is in negotiation but I don't see a demand." This created confusion about whether a formal demand existed or just an offer. The attorney then asked the paralegal to "wrap this one up."

## Agent Guidance

1. During intake for transfer cases, ask the client: "Has your prior attorney sent a demand? Have you received any offers?"
2. When the prior firm's file arrives, locate the demand letter and any offer correspondence immediately
3. Report to the attorney: demand amount, offer amount, adjuster's stated reasons for the offer level
4. The attorney decides whether to renegotiate from the existing position or take other action
5. Do not send a new demand without attorney approval — this could reset negotiations unfavorably

## Pitfalls

- Not asking about prior demand/offers during intake
- Attempting to start the demand process from scratch when a demand already exists
- Not understanding the pre-existing negotiation dynamics
- Moving the case to "negotiation" phase without a demand in the new firm's file (confuses internal tracking)
