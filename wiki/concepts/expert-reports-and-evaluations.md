# Expert Reports and Evaluations

> Expert reports — including Functional Capacity Evaluations (FCE), Independent Medical Examinations (IME), and vocational/economic analyses — are critical for establishing damages in PI cases, especially in litigation. They provide objective documentation of physical limitations, treatment outcomes, and economic impact of injuries. These evaluations require significant scheduling coordination and expense management.

## Pattern

### Functional Capacity Evaluation (FCE)
- Ordered when objective measurement of physical limitations is needed
- Performed by specialized providers (e.g., Biokinetics through Commonwealth IME)
- Client must travel to the evaluation site (may be in a different city)
- Cost: ~$550+ per evaluation
- Client may reschedule on their own, which delays the case
- Report typically received within 2-3 weeks of evaluation

### Independent Medical Examination (IME)
- Expert physician reviews records and/or examines the client
- May need amendments as new treatment occurs (e.g., shoulder reaches MMI)
- Attorney reviews IME findings and may direct additional imaging or treatment based on results
- Can be arranged through IME coordination services (e.g., Commonwealth IME)

### Vocational/Economic Expert
- Retained when the injury affects earning capacity
- Expert (e.g., vocational economics firm) needs: medical records, employment history, tax returns
- Client may not have worked due to the accident — need to document pre-accident earning capacity
- Coordination involves sending expert the necessary documents and following up on report timeline

### Scheduling Challenges
- Expert evaluations are frequently rescheduled (provider unavailability, client conflicts)
- Client must be notified promptly of schedule changes via email and USPS
- Follow up on reports when they are overdue

## Evidence

- In a premises liability case, the following expert work was coordinated:
  - FCE scheduled with Biokinetics through Commonwealth IME; originally scheduled for 6/27/2022, rescheduled by provider to 5/9/2022 (physician out of town), then rescheduled again by client to 7/25/2022 (client wanted to wait for additional testing). FCE eventually completed, report received ~2.5 weeks later.
  - IME by Dr. Nazar required amendment when the client's shoulder reached MMI; new visit summary sent to Commonwealth IME for amendment.
  - Attorney directed neurosurgery consultation and new MRI imaging based on IME findings — client scheduled 3 scans.
  - Economist report requested from vocational economics firm; required tax documentation for 2016, 2018, and 2020 — client did not work in 2020 due to the accident.
  - Expert invoices tracked through FreshBooks (e.g., Biokinetics invoice for FCE).
- In a WC estate case (DOI 12/2020), Commonwealth IME was used for 3 separate IME evaluations totaling $1,750+ in invoices. IME scheduling was coordinated via email between the firm, defense counsel, and Commonwealth IME. Invoices were tracked through FreshBooks. This confirms Commonwealth IME as the standard vendor for both WC and PI expert evaluations.
- In a wrongful death case (DOI 2/2022), expert work focused on vocational economics ($3.2K) and accident reconstruction ($2K) rather than medical evaluations — since the decedent had no treatment. Investigation services ($20K+) were also retained. This demonstrates that wrongful death cases shift expert focus from medical to economic/reconstruction analysis.

## Agent Guidance

1. When the attorney orders an expert evaluation, confirm the provider, date, location, and cost immediately
2. Send the client notification via email AND USPS mail (especially for rural clients without reliable email)
3. If the expert provider reschedules, immediately update the client and internal calendar
4. Instruct clients NOT to reschedule expert evaluations on their own without contacting the firm first
5. After the evaluation, set a 14-day follow-up task for the report
6. When the report is received, route to the attorney for review
7. Track all expert expenses on the case expense log with due dates for payment
8. For vocational/economic experts, prepare a package of: medical records, employment history, tax returns, and a summary of the client's work limitations

## Pitfalls

- Client rescheduling expert evaluations without firm knowledge (delays case by months)
- Not following up on overdue expert reports
- Sending the wrong tax year or incomplete documents to experts
- Not notifying the client of schedule changes via multiple channels (email alone may not reach rural clients)
- Losing track of expert invoices and payment deadlines
- Not requesting IME amendments when new treatment occurs
