# Gross Negligence as Policy Limit Demand Trigger

> When evidence shows the at-fault driver engaged in grossly negligent behavior (texting while driving, DUI, running a red light at high speed), the firm may pursue a policy limit demand regardless of the medical specials amount. Gross negligence shifts the demand strategy from treatment-value-based to liability-based, where the strength of the liability facts alone justifies demanding the full policy limits.

## Pattern

1. **Evidence Identification**: Police report or witness statements reveal grossly negligent behavior
2. **Attorney Directive**: Attorney reviews the liability facts and directs a policy limit demand
3. **Demand Preparation**: Demand sent for full policy limits with liability narrative emphasizing gross negligence
4. **Carrier Response**: Carrier evaluates the punitive exposure risk and may tender limits more quickly

### Types of Gross Negligence Observed

- **Texting and driving**: At-fault driver admitted to texting at time of collision
- **Running red lights**: At-fault driver ran a red light at high speed
- **Multiple violations**: At-fault driver committed multiple traffic violations in the incident

### Key Indicator

The attorney's note "Need Policy Limit Demand regardless of Medicals upon receipt of PR, if admission documented" establishes the decision framework: gross negligence documented in the police report triggers policy limit demand independent of medical specials.

## Evidence

- In a KY MVA case (DOI 10/2021), the at-fault driver admitted to texting and driving. The firm noted: "Per Client @F admitted to texting & Driving - Gross Negligence - Need Policy Limit Demand regardless of Medicals upon receipt of PR, if admission documented." This establishes the pattern: documented gross negligence triggers policy limit demand independent of medical value.
- In a KY MVA case (DOI 7/2023), the at-fault driver's behavior was characterized as grossly negligent, prompting early liability-focused demand strategy.
- In a KY MVA case (DOI 4/2022), the police report documented multiple violations by the at-fault driver, supporting a policy limit demand.
- In a KY MVA case (DOI 6/2023), the incident facts suggested extreme negligence, with the attorney evaluating liability strength for demand strategy.

## Agent Guidance

1. During file setup, review the police report for indicators of gross negligence (DUI, texting, excessive speed, fleeing)
2. If gross negligence indicators are found, flag the case immediately for attorney review
3. The attorney may direct a policy limit demand "regardless of medicals" — document this directive
4. Obtain the police report urgently in potential gross negligence cases (the liability narrative is the key to the demand)
5. If the at-fault driver's admission is documented in the police report, this significantly strengthens the demand

## Pitfalls

- Not identifying gross negligence indicators in the police report during file setup
- Waiting for treatment to complete before sending the demand (in gross negligence cases, the demand can go out based on liability alone)
- Not documenting the attorney's directive for policy limit demand
- Sending a medical-specials-based demand when a policy-limit demand is warranted
