# Incarcerated Client Complications

> Clients who become incarcerated during active PI cases create a distinct set of complications separate from standard unreachability. Incarcerated clients cannot receive normal phone calls, may not have access to email or text, cannot sign documents electronically, and cannot attend depositions, mediations, or settlement conferences. This pattern can emerge at any phase but is most impactful during negotiation and settlement when client involvement is required.

## Pattern

### Discovery
- Incarceration is typically discovered during a routine client contact attempt
- The paralegal logs "client is currently incarcerated" or "client is in [facility name]"
- The discovery may occur weeks or months after the incarceration begins

### Impact on Case Flow
1. **Communication**: Standard phone/text/email channels are unavailable or severely limited
2. **Document signing**: VineSign and electronic signatures may not be accessible from jail
3. **Settlement authorization**: Client cannot easily authorize or reject settlement offers
4. **Deposition attendance**: Cannot attend in-person or Zoom depositions without court coordination
5. **PIP exhaustion risk**: Medical bills continue accruing while the client cannot coordinate with providers

### Workarounds
- Facility phone calls during designated hours
- Written correspondence via jail mail system
- Contact through family members who can relay messages
- Attorney visits to the facility for critical decisions

## Evidence

- In a case (DOI mid-2025, KY, negotiation phase), the client was discovered to be incarcerated in county jail during active negotiation. The paralegal logged multiple notes: "client is currently incarcerated" and later "client is in [county] jail." The firm continued PIP ledger requests and adjuster correspondence during incarceration but the client's involvement in settlement decisions was blocked. A separate note indicated PIP benefits may have been exhausted, adding financial urgency.
- In a previously observed case (different batch), an incarcerated client with pre-settlement funding obligations had limited ability to communicate about case status, mirroring the pattern seen here.

## Agent Guidance

1. When a client is discovered to be incarcerated, immediately document the facility name and any available contact procedures
2. Determine whether the client can receive calls, mail, or visits
3. Identify a family member or designated contact who can relay non-privileged information
4. Continue all case work (adjuster correspondence, records collection, PIP management) — the case does not pause
5. For settlement authorization, coordinate with the attorney about whether a facility visit is needed
6. Monitor PIP benefits closely — incarcerated clients cannot manage bill-related issues

## Pitfalls

- Allowing the case to stall simply because the client is unreachable — continue all non-client-dependent work
- Not identifying the specific facility and contact procedures
- Attempting to discuss privileged case details with family members without client authorization
- Missing SOL deadlines because settlement authorization cannot be obtained
- Not checking whether PIP benefits are exhausted during client absence
