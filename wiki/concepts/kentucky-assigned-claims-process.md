# Kentucky Assigned Claims (KAC) Process

> When the at-fault driver in a Kentucky MVA has no valid insurance coverage (due to coverage denial, policy lapse, or being uninsured), the plaintiff can file a claim through the Kentucky Assigned Claims Plan (KAC). KAC assigns the claim to a carrier (e.g., Nationwide) who acts as the PIP carrier for medical bill payments. The KAC process differs from standard PIP in its documentation requirements, bill submission rules, and the potential for disputes about which bills are covered.

## Pattern

The KAC claim lifecycle:

1. **Identification**: During file setup, when BI coverage is denied or the at-fault driver is uninsured, identify the need for a KAC claim
2. **Filing**: Submit a KAC application with the required documentation (accident report, proof of no coverage, client information)
3. **Assignment**: KAC assigns the claim to a carrier. In observed cases, Nationwide was the assigned carrier.
4. **Adjuster Assignment**: The assigned carrier appoints an adjuster who manages the claim similarly to standard PIP but with additional requirements
5. **Bill Submission**: Medical bills are submitted to the KAC carrier for payment, but the carrier may dispute which bills are covered
6. **Lien Coordination**: KAC payments interact with health insurance liens (e.g., Medicaid/Conduent) — the firm must track what KAC pays versus what the health insurer paid
7. **Coverage Follow-Up**: The KAC carrier will periodically follow up on coverage status — if BI coverage is eventually accepted, the KAC carrier may seek reimbursement

### Key Differences from Standard PIP
- KAC requires the client to have no available PIP coverage of their own OR the at-fault driver to have no coverage
- The assigned carrier performs its own investigation into coverage status
- Bill submission may require proof that the treatment is accident-related (similar to PIP relatedness disputes)
- The attorney may negotiate which bills are submitted to KAC (e.g., agreeing during an EUO that certain bills will not be sent to KAC)
- KAC coverage may have different limits than standard PIP

  - A "Follow up on coverage" task was set repeatedly (every 1-2 weeks) tracking whether the KAC carrier had confirmed coverage
  - The KAC carrier investigated coverage status in parallel with the BI carrier's coverage investigation
- Across 14 additional KAC cases in batch 2/3, the KAC process was confirmed as highly standardized:
  - State Farm was the most frequently assigned KAC carrier (assigned in 8+ of the 14 cases), confirming State Farm's dominant role in KY's assigned claims pool
  - The KAC application emails follow a standard format: "KACP: Your claim application KY[number] for control [number] has been assigned to [Carrier]"
  - In companion cases (e.g., siblings from the same accident), each companion required a separate KAC application with a distinct control number, even when assigned to the same carrier
  - KAC claims were typically set up within the first 2 weeks of file setup, immediately after determining that no standard PIP was available
  - In multiple cases, the KAC carrier (State Farm) was also the PIP carrier assigned to handle medical bill payments — the firm tracked these as standard PIP claims once assigned
  - KAC cases frequently involved Conduent/Medicaid liens running in parallel, creating a three-way coordination between KAC payments, Medicaid liens, and eventual BI settlement

## Agent Guidance

1. When BI coverage is denied or the at-fault driver is uninsured, immediately identify whether a KAC claim is needed
2. File the KAC application promptly — do not wait for the BI coverage dispute to resolve
3. Track the KAC claim separately with its own claim number, adjuster, and follow-up schedule
4. Submit medical bills to the KAC carrier with documentation of accident-relatedness
5. Coordinate with the attorney before agreeing to exclude any bills from KAC submission
6. Track the interaction between KAC payments and Medicaid/health insurance liens — who paid what
7. Set 2-week follow-up tasks to confirm coverage status with the KAC carrier
8. If BI coverage is eventually accepted, be prepared for the KAC carrier to seek reimbursement
9. Maintain clear records of which bills were submitted to KAC and which were excluded

## Pitfalls

- Not identifying the need for a KAC claim early (delays medical bill payments)
- Submitting bills to KAC without proper documentation of accident-relatedness
- Not coordinating with the attorney about which bills to submit (the attorney may agree to exclude certain bills)
- Losing track of the interaction between KAC payments and Medicaid liens
- Assuming KAC coverage is automatic — the assigned carrier still investigates
- Not following up regularly on coverage confirmation
