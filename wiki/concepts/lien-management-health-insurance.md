# Health Insurance Lien Management

> When a PI client has health insurance that pays accident-related medical bills, the health insurer asserts a subrogation lien — a right to be reimbursed from any settlement. Managing these liens involves verifying their accuracy, removing unrelated charges, and ultimately negotiating a reduction. Liens directly reduce the client's net recovery.

## Pattern

The health insurance lien lifecycle:

1. **Identification**: The health insurer sends a "notice of subrogation claim" or "subrogation interest" letter once they learn of the accident
2. **Itemization Request**: Request a detailed itemization of the lien showing each date of service and amount paid
3. **Accuracy Audit**: Compare the lien itemization against the case's actual accident-related treatment
4. **Dispute Unrelated Charges**: If the lien includes charges for treatment unrelated to the accident (pre-existing conditions, unrelated medical visits), request removal with supporting documentation
5. **Updated Ledger**: Once disputed items are removed, obtain an updated lien ledger
6. **Settlement Integration**: Factor the adjusted lien into the settlement math
7. **Negotiation**: After settlement, negotiate a reduction of the lien (typically firms negotiate a percentage reduction)

## Evidence

- In Case C, the health insurer (Anthem) asserted a subrogation lien that included a 5/16/2024 date of service for an angiogram. The attorney identified that this procedure was scheduled prior to the accident (on 4/10/2024) for a pre-existing cardiac condition and was unrelated to the MVA. The paralegal emailed the lien handler (Carelon) requesting removal of the unrelated dates of service. An updated lien ledger with the charges removed was received the same day.
- In Case D (chain-reaction MVA with 20+ providers), the case accumulated 6+ liens including health insurance (Optum at $1,707), provider liens (Cutting Edge Orthopedics at $25,763.75), and a hospital funding lien (Plano Surgical Hospital Funding LLC at $2,553.70). Multiple "Unknown" lien holders were also identified, illustrating that lien tracking becomes exponentially harder with more providers. Provider liens (as distinct from health insurance liens) may come from providers who treated on a lien basis.
- In Case F (auto accident, dental injury), Anthem Blue Cross Blue Shield and Anthem Medicaid both asserted liens of $425.41 on the same case. The firm used ZipLiens to track the health insurance lien but initially sent the request to the wrong entity. Delta Dental of Kentucky also asserted a lien (amount unconfirmed). This demonstrates that dental injury cases can involve dental insurance liens in addition to health insurance liens — a distinct lien type not seen in standard MVA cases.
- In Case G (auto accident, UIM litigation), Humana asserted a health insurance subrogation lien of $11,809. The case also involved pre-settlement funding ($31,200 from Legal Funding Partners), illustrating how the combination of health insurance liens plus funding can dramatically reduce client net recovery.
- In Case H (auto accident, Medicaid), a Conduent lien of $20,669.08 was identified. Conduent is the third-party administrator for Kentucky Medicaid subrogation. The firm sent a first lien letter to Conduent at intake and later provided the Conduent lien information to the KAC adjuster during the claims process. Conduent called the firm to follow up on the case status, demonstrating that Medicaid subrogation administrators actively monitor cases.
- In Case M (MVA, multi-plaintiff), multiple health insurance liens were identified early: Anthem BCBS, a managed care provider, and a pain management provider lien. The lien request was faxed to the health insurer on the day of file setup. Additionally, multiple "Unknown" lien holders appeared in the lien tracker, demonstrating that in cases with 6+ medical providers, identifying all subrogation interests takes extended effort. The case's policy limits (25/50 split among 4 plaintiffs) made lien resolution particularly critical since the per-plaintiff recovery was limited.
- In Case N (premises liability estate, DOI 7/2020), a health insurance lien was identified from Humana at $5,887.97 (amount confirmed). Three additional "Unknown" lien holders were also listed. The Humana lien was identified early in the case via the case summary. The confirmed lien amount combined with the estate nature of the case (deceased client) means lien resolution will impact the surviving spouse's net recovery. This case further confirms the pattern that health insurance liens must be identified at intake and tracked throughout the case — even in litigation that spans 5+ years.
- In a wrongful death MVA case (DOI 8/2023, KY), a union benefit trust (TeamCare) asserted a health insurance lien of $89,212.31. A second lien from a BCBS plan was also identified. The TeamCare lien required calling the trust's main line (800 number) and escalating when the member's status was flagged as "not active." The firm spoke with trust representatives multiple times before the status was being reinstated. The lien verification service Zipliens was used to confirm amounts. This case illustrates that union/multi-employer trust benefit plan liens can be among the largest health insurance liens observed, and that trust plan administrative issues (inactive member status) can create delays in lien confirmation.
- In an auto accident case (DOI 1/2024, KY), multiple liens were identified including a hospital lien from a Medicaid managed care plan ($607.41 confirmed), plus two "Unknown" lien holders and a health insurance lien from a third-party administrator. The multiplicity of unknown liens confirms the pattern that in cases with Medicaid managed care, multiple subrogation entities may be involved (the MCO itself, the state Medicaid program, and third-party administrators).
- In an auto accident case (DOI 9/2021, KY), a Medicaid lien of $2,452.30 was confirmed from a state Medicaid managed care plan. An additional "Unknown" health insurance lien was also present. Despite extensive treatment ($176K+ in medical bills), the Medicaid lien was relatively modest — confirming that Medicaid reimbursement rates result in smaller liens relative to total billing.

## Agent Guidance

1. When a subrogation notice arrives, log it immediately and request a detailed itemization
2. Compare every date of service on the lien against the client's accident-related treatment dates
3. Flag any dates of service that fall outside the treatment window or relate to unrelated conditions
4. Draft a dispute letter to the lien handler citing the specific dates and reasons for removal
5. Obtain pre-accident medical records if needed to prove a condition was pre-existing
6. Keep the updated lien ledger in the case file for settlement calculations
7. Alert the attorney whenever the lien amount changes materially

## Pitfalls

- Accepting the lien amount at face value without auditing it
- Not knowing the client's pre-existing medical history (makes it impossible to identify unrelated charges)
- Delaying the lien dispute — the longer you wait, the harder it is to resolve before settlement
- Confusing the health insurance lien with PIP payments (they are separate obligations)
- Not getting the updated lien ledger in writing after charges are removed

### Government vs. Private Liens

Note: Government entity liens (VA, Medicare, Medicaid) follow fundamentally different processes than private health insurance liens. See [[va-lien-management]] for the VA-specific lien process. Key differences include federal legal authority, longer timelines, and less flexibility in negotiation.

- In 5+ cases, ZipLiens was used as the standard lien verification and resolution service. ZipLiens processed Medicaid liens, Anthem/Carelon liens, and sent automated notifications when liens were processed. The firm relied on ZipLiens for lien identification, amount verification, and negotiation.
- In a case (DOI 2/2023, KY), after settlement the Anthem lien payment of $4,664.54 was mailed by check from the firm. The lien was tracked by Anthem file number.
- In a case (DOI 4/2023, KY), the Medicaid lien payment of $2,526.69 was mailed to Carelon (Anthem's subrogation entity). Carelon subsequently reached out for a "status update on the distribution of the settlement funds" when the payment had not been received, and the firm's ZipLiens file remained open.
- In a case (DOI 5/2022, KY), Optum called asking for an update on lien payment after PIP was exhausted. The firm asked the analyst to send the updated lien with reduction — confirming that lien amounts are negotiated downward post-settlement.
- In a settled case (DOI 11/2023, KY), med pay from the at-fault carrier's policy was used specifically to cover the health insurance lien: "Settled at $15,000. Need to use Med Pay from policy to cover health lien." This confirms med pay as a strategic tool for lien resolution.
- In a multi-track case, the BI carrier specified "settled inclusive of all liens" vs "exclusive of PIP" — these settlement terms directly affect how liens are allocated in the disbursement.

### Batch 5 Evidence (5 supplementary cases)

- In a Medicaid case (DOI 6/2021, KY, minor child), Discovery Health Partners (MDwise/Medicaid managed care) asserted a lien. The firm faxed a "Final Health Lien Request" to Discovery Health Partners and followed up repeatedly over months (Nov 2022 through Nov 2022+). The Conduent claim number was tracked. The inability to obtain the final lien amount from Conduent/Medicaid over multiple months is a persistent pattern — in this case, repeated tasks were set to follow up with "Have we received the Final lien from Conduent? If not follow up." This demonstrates that Medicaid lien confirmation can stall settlement disbursement for months.
- In a MVA case (DOI 2/2022, KY, litigation->settlement), Conduent (Kentucky Medicaid subrogation administrator) proactively contacted the firm multiple times requesting lien status: first via email in Aug 2025 ("We sent you a notice of our lien... kindly advise us of the status"), then by phone in Jan 2026. The lien remained unresolved 8+ months after the case moved to settlement phase. This confirms that Conduent actively monitors and pursues lien recovery on settled cases, and prolonged non-response creates compliance risk.
