# Lost Wages Verification Workflow

> When a PI client has missed work due to their injuries, lost wages become a component of the demand. The firm must obtain a signed salary and wage verification form from the client's employer to document the lost income. This process requires the client to obtain their employer's signature and return the form — creating a separate collection track parallel to medical records collection.

## Pattern

Lost wages documentation follows a distinct workflow:

1. **Identification**: During intake or treatment phase, the firm identifies that the client has missed work
2. **Form Distribution**: The firm emails a salary and wage verification form to the client
3. **Client Action Required**: The client must get the form signed by their employer — the firm cannot contact the employer directly for this information
4. **Follow-Up**: Clients often need reminders to complete and return the form
5. **Demand Integration**: Once received, lost wages are included in the demand calculation alongside medical specials

This process is distinct from medical records collection because it requires client action (not just provider authorization) and employer cooperation.

## Evidence

- In 10 cases across this batch, lost wages were identified as a demand component
- In a premises liability case, the client was asked to "send the docs for her lost wages" and the firm sent a "salary & wage verification form that her employer needs to sign"
- In MVA cases, PIP adjusters specifically asked "Is he claiming lost wages?" and "Was he working at the time this accident occurred?" during the PIP application process
- Lost wages verification is a separate collection track that can delay demand preparation if the client does not promptly obtain employer signatures
- In WC cases, lost wages overlap with TTD (Temporary Total Disability) benefits and require average weekly wage (AWW) calculations rather than employer verification

## Agent Guidance

1. At intake, determine if the client missed work due to injuries
2. If yes, send the salary and wage verification form to the client with clear instructions
3. Set a 2-week follow-up reminder to confirm the form was returned
4. If the client has not returned the form, escalate — this can block demand preparation
5. For WC cases, lost wages are handled through TTD/AWW calculations, not employer verification forms

## Pitfalls

- Clients often delay getting employer signatures, creating a bottleneck separate from medical records
- PIP adjusters may ask about lost wages during PIP application — ensure the firm has this information ready
- Lost wages in WC cases follow a completely different process (TTD benefits, not employer verification)
- Some employers may be uncooperative or slow to sign, requiring follow-up from the client
