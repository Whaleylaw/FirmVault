# Medicaid Lien Management (Conduent)

> When a PI client is covered by Medicaid, the state asserts a subrogation lien for accident-related medical expenses paid by Medicaid. In Kentucky, Medicaid subrogation is administered by Conduent (a third-party administrator). Medicaid liens differ from private health insurance liens in that they involve government authority, can be substantial (covering hospital stays and emergency treatment), and the lien administrator actively monitors cases and follows up with law firms.

## Pattern

The Medicaid lien lifecycle:

1. **Early Identification**: At intake, determine whether the client has Medicaid coverage. If yes, send a first lien letter to the Medicaid subrogation administrator (Conduent in Kentucky) immediately.
2. **Lien Assertion**: Conduent will respond with a lien amount based on Medicaid payments made for accident-related treatment. Response times are typically faster than VA but slower than private insurers.
3. **Active Monitoring**: Unlike private health insurance lien holders who are largely passive, Conduent actively monitors cases — they will call the firm to check on case status.
4. **Lien Coordination**: Medicaid lien amounts must be coordinated with other payment sources (PIP, KAC, BI settlement). Bills paid by Medicaid cannot also be claimed in the demand as unpaid specials.
5. **Dual Lien Possibility**: A client may have both Medicaid (Anthem Medicaid) and private health insurance (Anthem BCBS) liens on the same case — these are separate entities requiring separate tracking.

### Key Differences from Private Health Insurance Liens

| Aspect | Private Health Insurance | Medicaid (Conduent) |
|--------|------------------------|---------------------|
| Legal basis | State subrogation law / ERISA | Federal/state Medicaid law |
| Administrator | Insurance company's subrogation dept | Conduent (third-party administrator) |
| Monitoring | Generally passive | Active — calls firm to check status |
| Lien amounts | Typically moderate | Can be very large (hospital stays covered) |
| Negotiation | Percentage reductions common | Government program — less flexibility |
| Coordination | With PIP/settlement | With PIP, KAC, and settlement |

## Evidence

- In Case F (auto accident, dental injury), both Anthem Blue Cross Blue Shield and Anthem Medicaid asserted liens of $425.41 on the same case. The firm tracked these as separate liens, using ZipLiens for the health insurance lien. The dual-lien scenario required the firm to identify which entity was the correct recipient for lien correspondence — an error in routing (sending to the wrong Anthem entity) caused delays. This demonstrates that "Anthem" can represent both private insurance AND Medicaid, and the firm must distinguish between them.
- In Case H (auto accident, stolen vehicle/coverage denial), Conduent asserted a Medicaid lien of $20,669.08 — the largest single lien observed in the case. The firm sent a "First Lien Letter faxed to Conduent" at file setup. Conduent later called the firm to follow up on case status (voicemail from Conduent on 2/5/2025). The firm provided the Conduent lien information and medical records/bills to the KAC adjuster during the claims process. The substantial Medicaid lien (covering a multi-day hospital stay at the University of Louisville Hospital) illustrates that Medicaid lien amounts can be proportionally larger than private insurance liens because Medicaid pays a higher share of hospital costs for low-income clients.
- In an MVA case (DOI 8/2024, KY), Conduent asserted a Medicaid lien of $3,830 (amount confirmed). The client had ongoing dental treatment at a university dental school. Conduent called the firm to check on case status — the note read: "Conduent called for status of her case — case is still open — client still having dental treatment." The adjuster from the BI carrier (Progressive) also called to get treatment status information. This case confirms the active-monitoring pattern: Conduent proactively calls for case status updates, unlike private insurers who typically wait passively. The firm must be prepared to provide status updates to both the BI carrier and the Medicaid lien holder concurrently.

## Agent Guidance

1. At intake, ask whether the client has Medicaid coverage. If yes, flag the case as "Medicaid lien likely"
2. Send a first lien letter to Conduent immediately during file setup — do not delay
3. Track the Medicaid lien SEPARATELY from any private health insurance lien, even if both are through "Anthem"
4. When Conduent calls to check on case status, respond promptly — they are actively monitoring
5. Before preparing the demand or settlement math, obtain the current Medicaid lien amount from Conduent
6. Coordinate Medicaid lien payments with PIP and KAC payments — ensure no double-billing
7. When presenting settlement options to the attorney, include the Medicaid lien as a mandatory deduction
8. Use ZipLiens or similar tracking tools to monitor Medicaid liens alongside health insurance liens

## Pitfalls

- Confusing Anthem Medicaid with Anthem BCBS (they are separate entities with different lien processes)
- Not sending the first lien letter to Conduent at file setup (delays lien identification)
- Not responding to Conduent's follow-up calls (they may escalate or assert incorrect lien amounts)
- Not factoring the Medicaid lien into settlement math early (Medicaid liens can be the largest single deduction)
- Assuming Medicaid liens can be negotiated down as easily as private health insurance liens
- Not tracking Medicaid lien payments alongside KAC/PIP payments

### Batch 4 Evidence (23 Medicaid cases)

- In 23 cases from this batch, Medicaid/Conduent interactions were documented. Conduent was the most common Medicaid subrogation administrator, with cases spanning first lien letter faxing, final lien requests, and lien payment.
- In a case (DOI 11/2022, KY), Conduent requested updated payment status months after settlement: "I just received an email from Conduent requesting an update on payment since it has been a few months." The firm then mailed check to Conduent. A second check had to be mailed when the first was not received — demonstrating that Conduent lien payment requires tracking and follow-up.
- In a case (DOI 8/2022, KY), Conduent called "looking for lien funds" — the lien amount remained at $12,000+. The firm mailed a negotiated check for $1,250.67, confirming that Medicaid liens CAN be negotiated down significantly (from $12K+ to $1.25K, approximately 10% of the asserted amount).
- In a case (DOI 7/2023, KY), the Medicaid lien was sent to PIP carrier with instructions to pay: "Send the Medicaid lien to PIP, Kentucky Farm Bureau with instructions to pay." This confirms that PIP carriers can pay Medicaid liens directly, reducing the firm's lien resolution burden.
- In a case (DOI 12/2023, KY), Conduent (Medicaid subrogation analyst) sent repeated urgent requests: "SECOND REQUEST - PLEASE ANSWER TODAY CASE SETTLED - KY MEDICAID LIEN HAS NOT BEEN [paid]." The aggressive follow-up pattern from Conduent confirms they actively monitor settled cases and escalate when payment is delayed.
- In a case (DOI 11/2023, KY), Conduent adjuster correspondence showed detailed email exchanges about lien amounts and payment timing, with the adjuster (Kristy Caldwell) maintaining consistent follow-up.
- In 5+ cases, "First Lien Letter faxed to Conduent" was documented during file setup, confirming the standard workflow of early Medicaid lien identification.
- The Medicaid lien amounts observed ranged from small ($73.48, $211.36) to substantial ($3,047.28, $12,000+), depending on the extent of Medicaid-covered treatment.
