# Medical Bills Going to Collections

> A recurring pain point in PI cases is medical bills going to collection agencies while the case is pending. This causes significant client anxiety about credit impact and creates urgency to resolve the case. The firm must manage both the practical issue (getting bills paid through PIP or other channels) and the emotional impact on the client.

## Pattern

The collections problem follows a timeline:

1. Accident occurs, client receives ER treatment
2. Hospital/provider bills health insurance first
3. If health insurance doesn't cover (or only partially covers), the provider bills the client
4. After 60-90 days of non-payment, the provider sends the bill to a collection agency
5. The collection agency contacts the client
6. Client contacts the firm in distress about credit impact

The gap between accident and collections is typically 3-6 months. This often coincides with the period when the firm is still collecting records and bills, or waiting for PIP to process.

## Evidence

- In Case C, the client brought in collection notices from Credit Bureau Systems for the Baptist Health bill. He was "concerned because he does not want that to impact his credit — as he needs a replacement vehicle." The bill had been submitted to PIP on 11/1/2024 but PIP had made no payments as of 12/31/2024.
- In Case B, the client's husband forwarded a "notice from a debt collection agency" for a radiology bill and a "letter from a debt collection agency" for another bill, asking "Shouldn't they be aware that this is an accident claim?" The firm responded by forwarding bills to PIP for processing.

## Agent Guidance

1. Proactively warn clients at intake that medical bills may go to collections during the case, and this is normal
2. When a bill is submitted to PIP, note the submission date and set a 30-day follow-up
3. If PIP has not processed within 30 days, escalate before the bill goes to collections
4. When a client reports a collection notice, respond within 24 hours with:
   - Acknowledgment of their concern
   - Status of the bill submission to PIP/insurance
   - Expected timeline for resolution
   - Reassurance that this will be addressed through the settlement
5. Request a PIP payment log/ledger to demonstrate to the collection agency that the bill is being processed
6. If possible, contact the collection agency with the firm's LOR and documentation of PIP processing

## Pitfalls

- Dismissing client concerns about collections as unimportant
- Not submitting bills to PIP proactively (waiting for clients to forward bills)
- Slow response to collection notices (increases client anxiety)
- Not following up with PIP when they fail to process bills timely
- Not explaining to clients that accident-related bills follow a different payment path
