# Medical Records and Bills Collection

> Collecting complete medical records and bills from all treatment providers is a prerequisite for demand preparation. This process involves sending HIPAA authorizations, formal record/bill requests via fax and email, and persistent follow-up when providers are slow to respond. Gaps in records collection directly delay case resolution.

## Pattern

The records collection workflow follows this sequence:

1. **Provider Identification**: Confirm all treatment providers with the client, including ER, PCP, specialists, imaging
2. **Request Preparation**: Generate HIPAA medical authorization forms and formal records/billing request letters for each provider
3. **Request Delivery**: Send via fax (using RingCentral or similar) and/or email to the provider's records department
4. **Response Tracking**: Track which providers have responded with records vs. bills vs. both
5. **Follow-Up**: If no response within 2-3 weeks, follow up
6. **Client Assistance**: Ask clients to help obtain records from their patient portal (MyChart) when formal requests are delayed
7. **Completeness Check**: Verify all providers are accounted for before moving to demand

## Agent Guidance

1. Create a provider checklist during file setup with records requested/received dates for each
2. Send HIPAA authorizations and record requests within the first week of file setup
3. Use both fax and email when possible — some providers respond faster to one channel
4. Set follow-up tasks at 14-day intervals for each outstanding request
5. Encourage clients to share records from patient portals as an interim measure
6. Track records and bills separately — receiving one does not mean you have the other
7. Do not move to demand preparation until ALL providers have been accounted for
8. When the attorney asks about case status, have the records collection status ready

## Pitfalls

- Not confirming all providers with the client early (discovering a provider late delays everything)
- Sending requests and not tracking responses
- Not distinguishing between records received and bills received (you need both)
- Letting the client's medical portal records substitute for formal provider records in the demand
- Waiting too long to follow up on non-responsive providers
- Requesting records under a billing entity name that differs from the clinical entity name (causes "no records found" responses)
- Not anticipating that multi-state providers require different request methods and response timelines
