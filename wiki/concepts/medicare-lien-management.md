# Medicare Lien Management

> Medicare conditional payment liens represent a federal government interest in recovering payments made for accident-related treatment. Unlike private health insurance subrogation, Medicare liens are governed by the Medicare Secondary Payer (MSP) Act and carry federal legal authority. The presence of a Medicare lien adds complexity to settlement calculations and lien resolution timelines.

## Pattern

### Medicare Lien Lifecycle
1. **Identification**: Medicare is identified as a payer during records/billing review
2. **Conditional Payment Notice**: Medicare/CMS issues a conditional payment letter quantifying amounts paid
3. **Amount Confirmation**: Firm verifies the conditional payment amount against treatment records
4. **Demand Impact**: Medicare lien amount must be factored into demand calculations
5. **Resolution**: After settlement, firm must satisfy Medicare's conditional payment claim before disbursing to client
6. **Potential Negotiation**: Unlike VA liens, Medicare conditional payments may be subject to reduction through the BCRC (Benefits Coordination & Recovery Center) process

### Key Characteristics
- Federal legal authority (MSP Act) — cannot be ignored
- Conditional payment amounts may include treatment unrelated to the accident — requires audit
- CMS/BCRC is the counterparty, not a local office
- Timeline for final demand letter from CMS can extend months post-settlement
- Overlaps with other liens (Medicaid/Wellcare) creating complex multi-lien scenarios

## Evidence

- In Case H (MVA, air evac), a Medicare conditional payment lien was confirmed at $10,157.36 (notice received 5/17/2024). The case also had a Wellcare of Kentucky (Medicaid managed care) lien at $2,315.49 and an unidentified health insurance lien. The multi-lien landscape (Medicare + Medicaid managed care + unknown) created significant complexity for settlement math. The air evacuation bill alone was $72K+, meaning Medicare's conditional payment was a fraction of total treatment costs but still substantial enough to impact net recovery.
- In Case S (auto accident, government transit defendant, DOI 9/2021), Medicare was identified as a lien holder alongside a Humana health insurance lien ($1,513.45). The client was a Medicare beneficiary with treatment at multiple Louisville-area hospitals and EMS providers. The case involved a government entity defendant (transit risk management), creating a multi-layer complexity: federal Medicare lien + private health insurance lien + government tort claim. The Medicare lien remained in "identified" status throughout the observed activity, confirming the pattern that Medicare liens are often identified early but final amounts take months to confirm. The coexistence of Medicare and Humana liens on the same case confirms that Medicare beneficiaries may have supplemental private insurance, each asserting separate subrogation interests.
- In Case U (auto accident, employer liability/taxi company, DOI 9/2024), Medicare liens from multiple entities were identified: Aetna Medicare and Medicare BCRC (Benefits Coordination & Recovery Center), plus additional "Unknown" Medicare liens. The client was treated at a regional medical center with $31K+ in billings. The presence of both Aetna Medicare (a Medicare Advantage plan) and traditional Medicare BCRC on the same case demonstrates that Medicare Advantage plans assert liens separately from CMS. This creates a dual-Medicare-lien scenario requiring the firm to track and resolve both independently. The case also involved bifurcated litigation issues, adding procedural complexity on top of the lien resolution challenge.

## Agent Guidance

1. During intake and records review, determine whether the client has Medicare coverage
2. If Medicare paid for any accident-related treatment, initiate a conditional payment query with BCRC early
3. Track the conditional payment amount as a confirmed lien in the case file
4. Audit the conditional payment letter against treatment records — challenge any unrelated charges
5. Factor Medicare lien into demand calculations and settlement evaluation
6. After settlement, submit the final demand to BCRC and negotiate reduction if applicable
7. Do NOT disburse settlement funds until Medicare's final demand is resolved
8. When Medicare coexists with other liens (Medicaid, private), calculate total lien exposure before evaluating any offer

## Pitfalls

- Not identifying Medicare as a payer early in the case
- Assuming Medicare liens can be ignored or informally resolved
- Not auditing conditional payment amounts for unrelated treatment charges
- Disbursing settlement funds before Medicare's final demand is issued
- Confusing Medicare conditional payments with Medicaid/managed care liens (different processes)
- Not factoring total lien exposure (Medicare + Medicaid + private) into settlement evaluation
