# Minor Settlement and Court Approval Process

> When a PI client is a minor (under 18), the settlement requires court approval before funds can be disbursed. The process involves filing a motion with the court, obtaining a court order, and depositing funds into a blocked account or STABLE account. This adds weeks to months to the settlement timeline and requires coordination between the firm, the guardian, and the court.

## Pattern

### Court Approval Sequence
1. **Settlement Reached**: Standard negotiation produces an offer the guardian agrees to accept
2. **Motion Filed**: Firm files a motion with the court to approve the minor's settlement
3. **Court Review**: Judge reviews the settlement terms (may request additional documentation like LPOA from both parents)
4. **Order Entered**: Court enters an order approving the settlement
5. **Carrier Notified**: Insurance carrier is informed of court approval and asked to issue the check
6. **Blocked Account / STABLE Account**: Funds deposited into a protected account until the minor reaches majority

### STABLE Accounts (Kentucky)
For clients receiving government benefits (SSI), a STABLE account (stablekentucky.com) can be used to deposit settlement funds without affecting benefit eligibility. Setup requires:
- Client disability information
- Guardian cooperation in setting up the account
- One-time funding check mailed directly to STABLE

### Guardian Documentation
- Both parents may need to provide Limited Power of Attorney (LPOA)
- If parents are together, both may be required to execute documents
- If one parent is absent, the court may require additional documentation
- DocuSign/VineSign used for remote execution

## Evidence

- In a minor MVA case, the court required LPOA from "the other parent" (father) even though the parents were still together. The firm had to obtain the father's email, draft the LPOA, send via DocuSign, and await execution before the court would approve.
- In the same case, the firm repeatedly checked CourtNet for the order approving settlement, with the attorney emailing "Please follow up on the Order approving the settlement. I looked at CourtNet and still nothing" — indicating multi-week delays.
- In a premises liability case involving a client receiving SSI benefits, the firm set up a STABLE account (stablekentucky.com) to protect government benefits. The guardian needed to provide disability information and link accounts. The settlement check was made payable directly to the STABLE program.
- In a companion case, a minor's settlement check was issued to the guardian (mother), who picked it up along with the companion adult's check.
- In a minor auto accident case, the guardian's attorney (separate counsel for the minor) required notification of the settlement and noted that "once a settlement is reached I have to file a motion with the court to approve the settlement."
- In multiple minor cases, settlement checks were smaller amounts that still required the full court approval process, adding disproportionate overhead to small-value settlements.
- In one case, the guardian called the firm asking for proof of the blocked account, indicating the court required confirmation that funds were properly deposited.

## Agent Guidance

1. At intake, flag any minor clients and note their guardian information
2. When settlement is reached for a minor, immediately prepare the motion for court approval
3. Check whether both parents need to sign documents — obtain both parents' contact information early
4. For SSI recipients, begin STABLE account setup before settlement is finalized
5. Monitor CourtNet regularly for the court order — do not wait for the court to notify you
6. Once the order is entered, promptly notify the carrier and request the check
7. Document the blocked account or STABLE account details in the file
8. Expect the court approval process to add 2-8 weeks to the settlement timeline

## Pitfalls

- Not identifying minor status at intake (delays settlement by weeks when discovered late)
- Not obtaining both parents' contact information early (court may require both LPOAs)
- Not monitoring CourtNet for the order (can sit for weeks without the firm knowing)
- Forgetting to set up a STABLE account for SSI recipients (settlement could affect benefits)
- Filing motion without all required guardian documentation (court rejects and requires resubmission)

### Batch 6 Evidence (2 minor closing cases)

- In a minor MVA case (DOI 9/2021, KY), the complete minor settlement blocked account timeline was documented across 18+ months of activity logs. The Peoples Bank blocked account process involved: (1) bank sent account documents to the firm (agreement, disclosures, W-9, online banking forms), (2) guardian needed to provide "wet" (physical) signature — e-signatures not accepted, (3) bank required updated ID and proof of address (utility bill), (4) guardian's address on ID did not match current address — required proof of new address, (5) guardian needed multiple reminders to come to the office and execute documents, (6) bank staff turnover caused additional delays (contacts changed during the process). The entire blocked account setup took 3+ months from initial document receipt to account finalization. The paralegal expressed frustration to the bank: "Is there a reason this has taken 3 months? Is this a normal time frame?" This confirms that blocked account setup is a major bottleneck in minor settlement cases.
- In the same case, the firm sent the court order approving the settlement, then transitioned to the bank document phase. The guardian's new address needed to be updated in the contact system. After bank documents were finally completed, the case moved to Closing with a Google review solicitation — indicating the guardian relationship ended positively despite the delays.
