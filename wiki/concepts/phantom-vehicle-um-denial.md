# Phantom Vehicle UM Coverage Denial

> In Kentucky, uninsured motorist (UM) claims involving phantom vehicles (hit-and-run where the at-fault vehicle is never identified) face a specific coverage hurdle: the UM carrier requires either physical contact between the vehicles or testimony from a disinterested witness. When neither condition is met, the UM carrier denies coverage, leaving the client with only PIP — effectively eliminating the case.

## Pattern

### The Phantom Vehicle Rule
1. **Accident occurs**: Client is involved in an accident caused by an unidentified vehicle (the "phantom")
2. **Police report filed**: Report documents the phantom vehicle but no identification is made
3. **UM claim filed**: Firm files a UM claim with the client's own carrier
4. **Carrier investigation**: UM carrier reviews the police report for two requirements:
   - **Physical contact**: Was there actual contact between the phantom vehicle and the client's vehicle?
   - **Disinterested witness**: Is there a witness (other than the claimant or passengers) who can corroborate the phantom vehicle's involvement?
5. **Denial**: If neither requirement is met, the UM carrier denies coverage

### Coverage Denial Language
The carrier's denial typically states: "Based on the police report there was no contact with the phantom vehicle and there are no disinterested witnesses, therefore the Uninsured Motorist Coverage will not extend for this loss."

### Impact
- PIP remains available for medical bills but provides no pain-and-suffering recovery
- The case must be declined unless the firm can locate a disinterested witness
- Companion cases (passengers in the same vehicle) are all affected by the same denial

## Evidence

- In two MVA cases (DOI 8/2024, KY), involving a driver and two passengers, State Farm denied UM coverage for all three claimants. The denial letter stated: "Based on the police report there was no contact with the phantom vehicle and there are no disinterested witnesses, therefore the Uninsured Motorist Coverage will not extend for this loss." The attorney discussed the situation with the client (driver), explaining that without a witness or contact, the claim "may not be successful." The client mentioned a potential witness who saw the accident but the witness could not be identified or located. All three cases were declined and closed.
- In one of the companion cases, the uninsured motorist claim was the only viable path because the phantom vehicle was never identified — there was no BI carrier to pursue.
- The firm spent approximately 5 months working these cases (intake in August 2024, closing in January 2025) before the UM denial was fully processed and the cases were closed.

## Agent Guidance

1. At intake for any hit-and-run or phantom vehicle case, immediately assess:
   - Was there physical contact between the vehicles?
   - Are there any witnesses other than the client and passengers?
2. If neither condition is met, flag the case as HIGH RISK for UM denial immediately
3. Aggressively pursue witness identification: check the police report for witness statements, ask the client for anyone who may have seen the accident, check nearby businesses for surveillance footage
4. File the UM claim early so the carrier's investigation begins while the firm searches for witnesses
5. If the UM carrier denies coverage and no witness can be found, recommend immediate case decline rather than spending additional months on a case with no viable recovery path
6. Confirm whether the phantom vehicle rule applies in other states — Kentucky law requires contact or a disinterested witness (KRS 304.39-080)

## Pitfalls

- Not asking about witnesses at intake (witnesses become harder to find as time passes)
- Spending months on the case before discovering the phantom vehicle denial
- Not recognizing that passengers in the same vehicle are NOT "disinterested witnesses"
- Assuming UM coverage will apply to all hit-and-run cases (the phantom vehicle rule is a distinct barrier)
- Not declining companion cases simultaneously (all passengers are affected by the same denial)
