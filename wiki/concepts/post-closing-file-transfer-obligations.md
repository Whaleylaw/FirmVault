# Post-Closing File Transfer and Disengagement Obligations

> When a client terminates representation and retains new counsel, the original firm has ongoing obligations: transferring the case file to the new firm, sending a disengagement letter to all carriers so they know the firm no longer represents the client, and ensuring PIP and other active claims are not disrupted. Failure to complete these steps promptly creates real financial harm to the former client and potential malpractice exposure for the firm.

## Pattern

### Post-Termination Obligation Sequence
1. **Client Termination**: Client notifies the firm (email, phone, or via new counsel)
2. **Closing Letter**: Firm sends closing/disengagement letter to client
3. **Carrier Notification**: Firm sends disengagement letters to ALL carriers (BI, PIP, UM) so they know the firm no longer represents the client
4. **File Compilation**: Firm compiles the case file (records, bills, correspondence, photos, police report)
5. **File Transfer**: Firm sends the compiled file to new counsel upon request
6. **PIP Cleanup**: Firm contacts PIP carrier to remove reservations and process remaining bills, or explicitly hands PIP management to new counsel
7. **Lien Notification**: Firm notifies lien holders of the representation change

### Critical Timing Issues
- New counsel cannot effectively represent the client until they have the file
- Carriers will not communicate with new counsel until they receive a new LOR AND a disengagement from the original firm
- PIP bills may be pended (held without payment) while representation is in limbo
- Medical bills may go to collections if PIP is frozen and no one is managing the claim

## Evidence

- In an MVA case (DOI 6/2025, KY), the client terminated the firm in January 2026 and retained new counsel. The new firm repeatedly contacted the original firm requesting the case file over several weeks: "I have now called a few times and continue to follow up... Mr. [Client] has requested his file be sent. Can you confirm if it has been sent?" The original firm was slow to respond — calls went unreturned for weeks. Meanwhile, the client's medical bills went to collections because the original firm had sent an LOR to State Farm instructing them not to pay bills directly to the client, but had not sent a disengagement letter. The client could not get State Farm to resume payments until the original firm confirmed they no longer represented him. The client left a 300+ word voicemail detailing his frustration and financial harm.
- In a companion KAC case (DOI 9/2023, KY), after the firm closed the case, the KAC PIP adjuster continued contacting the firm months later: "I have sent numerous emails and letters concerning Mr. [Client]'s benefits. Please respond as soon as possible and advise as his bills are being pended." The firm responded by asking the adjuster to "remove any remaining PIP reservations and process any remaining bills" — but only after being prompted by the adjuster's persistent follow-up.
- In an MVA case (DOI 7/2024, KY), the firm discovered the client was already represented by another firm (Morgan and Morgan) when calling the BI carrier. The carrier informed the firm of the competing representation. The firm immediately sent a closing letter and drop letter to PIP.
- In an MVA case (DOI 11/2024, KY), the client terminated within days of intake. A "Declined Rep" letter was emailed to the client, and a drop letter was sent to PIP carrier. The rapid turnaround (same day) represents the ideal file transfer scenario — minimal obligations to close out.

## Agent Guidance

1. When a client terminates representation, send a disengagement letter to ALL carriers within 48 hours — not just the client
2. Contact the PIP carrier explicitly to remove reservations or hand off management to new counsel
3. When new counsel requests the file, compile and send within 5 business days
4. Log all file transfer communications — document what was sent, when, and to whom
5. Do NOT assume that sending a closing letter to the client satisfies carrier notification obligations
6. If the former client's medical bills are being pended by PIP, ensure the PIP adjuster knows to resume processing
7. Keep a copy of the complete file for the firm's records before transferring

## Pitfalls

- Not sending disengagement letters to carriers (leaves the client in limbo)
- Ignoring file transfer requests from new counsel (creates malpractice risk and harms the client)
- Not cleaning up PIP reservations (bills are pended and client receives collection notices)
- Assuming "case closed" means no further obligations to the former client
- Not documenting what was transferred (creates disputes about whether the file was complete)
