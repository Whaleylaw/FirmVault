# Post-Settlement Provider and Lien Disputes

> After settlement funds are disbursed, providers and lien holders may contact the firm months or even years later with payment disputes. These disputes include underpayment claims (interest not included), lost checks requiring reissuance, and providers threatening to pursue the client directly through the court system. This creates ongoing firm liability and client anxiety well after the case is considered closed.

## Pattern

1. **Settlement Completed**: Case is settled, checks mailed, client paid
2. **Provider/Lien Holder Contacts Firm**: Months later, a provider or lien holder contacts the firm claiming underpayment or non-receipt
3. **Escalation**: Provider leaves repeated voicemails, sends faxes, and threatens alternative collection routes (court filing against client)
4. **Investigation**: Firm must investigate what was paid, what was agreed, and whether additional payment is owed
5. **Resolution**: Additional check issued, original check traced, or provider redirected

### Common Post-Settlement Dispute Types

- **Interest not included in lien payment**: Provider claims interest was promised but not paid
- **Check not received**: Conduent or other lien holder did not receive the mailed check, requiring reissuance
- **Provider balance remaining**: Treatment costs exceeded PIP/lien payments plus settlement allocation
- **Funeral home balance**: In wrongful death cases, funeral home providers may claim unpaid balances years after settlement

## Evidence

- In a KY MVA case (settled), a funeral home provider left repeated voicemails months after settlement: "I sent in some information showing where the interest was promised... the interest did not get taken care of." The provider escalated: "if there is no funds available... I need to try a different avenue" and threatened to "file a settlement with the court systems." The provider called multiple times over weeks, requesting either a check for the difference or notification that no more funds would be forthcoming.
- In a KY MVA case (DOI 11/2022, KY), the Conduent Medicaid lien payment check was not received. The firm had to mail a second check: "check #7685 (#2) mailed to Conduent" — confirming that lien payment checks can be lost in transit and require re-issuance with new check numbers.
- In a KY MVA case (DOI 8/2022, KY), Conduent called "looking for lien funds" months after the case settled, indicating the firm had not yet paid the Medicaid lien. The firm then mailed a check for $1,250.67 against a $12,000+ lien.
- In a KY MVA case (DOI 12/2023, KY), a Medicaid subrogation analyst sent urgent repeated requests: "SECOND REQUEST - PLEASE ANSWER TODAY CASE SETTLED - KY MEDICAID LIEN HAS NOT BEEN [paid]" — demonstrating that delayed lien payments after settlement create aggressive follow-up.
- Post-settlement provider complaints were observed in 5+ cases where providers or lien holders contacted the firm after settlement to request payment or dispute amounts.

## Agent Guidance

1. Before closing a case file, verify ALL lien payments have been sent AND received (track check clearance)
2. Document any interest or balance agreements with providers in writing before settlement
3. When mailing lien checks, use tracking/delivery confirmation to avoid lost-check disputes
4. Set a 90-day post-settlement follow-up task to verify all checks have cleared
5. When a provider contacts post-settlement, pull the disbursement sheet immediately and compare to claimed amounts
6. Escalate all post-settlement disputes to the attorney for guidance before making additional payments

## Pitfalls

- Not tracking check clearance after mailing lien payments
- Not documenting interest or balance agreements with providers before settlement
- Ignoring post-settlement provider calls (they will escalate to legal action against the client)
- Assuming "case closed" means no further financial obligations
- Not keeping disbursement records accessible after case closure

### Batch 6 Evidence (3 post-close carrier contacts)

- In 3 closing cases from this batch, carriers or lien holders contacted the firm months after the case was declined/closed:
  - In an MVA case (DOI 9/2023), the PIP carrier (Progressive) sent "numerous emails and letters" to the firm about pending bills months after the case was declined and the firm no longer represented the client. The carrier escalated: "I have been unable to get a response." The firm finally responded confirming they no longer represented the client and asked the carrier to "remove any remaining PIP reservations and process any remaining bills."
  - In an MVA case (DOI 1/2024), a Conduent Medicaid subrogation analyst sent a follow-up request months after decline. The firm responded: "Our office has formally declined the case... We no longer represent her."
  - In an MVA case (DOI 2/2024), Carelon (Anthem subrogation) confirmed "File closed 0 paid" — but the firm had to follow up for 2+ months to obtain written confirmation of the zero-lien status.
- These post-close contacts confirm that lien holders and carriers continue to track cases independently of the firm's status. Proactive notification at closing is essential to prevent extended back-and-forth.
