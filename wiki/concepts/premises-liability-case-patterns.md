# Premises Liability Case Patterns

> Premises liability (PL) cases — typically slip-and-fall incidents at commercial properties — follow a different trajectory than auto accident cases. They are more likely to proceed to litigation, involve property owner defense counsel early, and require more extensive expert work (FCE, IME, vocational/economic experts). The liability analysis focuses on property owner notice and maintenance rather than traffic fault.

## Pattern

PL cases differ from auto accident cases in several key ways:

### Liability Analysis
- **Property owner notice**: Was the hazardous condition known to the owner? How long did it exist?
- **Maintenance records**: Were inspection and maintenance protocols followed?
- **Incident reports**: Was an incident report filed at the time of the fall?
- **Surveillance footage**: Commercial properties may have video of the incident; preservation requests should be sent immediately
- **Third-party defendants**: Banks, stores, and other commercial entities typically have dedicated defense counsel (not insurance adjusters)

### Litigation Tendency
- PL cases are more likely to proceed to litigation than auto accident cases
- Defense counsel for commercial property owners may aggressively litigate through discovery, depositions, and motions practice
- Mediation is commonly attempted before trial

### Expert Work
- **Functional Capacity Evaluations (FCE)**: Used to document physical limitations from the injury
- **Independent Medical Examinations (IME)**: May be requested by either side; amendments may be needed as treatment progresses
- **Economist/Vocational Reports**: When the injury affects earning capacity, vocational economics experts are retained

### Timeline
- PL cases tend to run significantly longer than auto accident cases (3-6+ years observed vs. 1-2 years for typical MVA)
- Discovery deadlines, motions practice, and mediation scheduling extend the timeline

## Agent Guidance

1. At intake for PL cases, immediately send a preservation letter to the property owner requesting incident reports, maintenance logs, and surveillance footage
2. Identify the property owner's insurance carrier AND their defense counsel — PL cases often have dedicated law firms representing the property owner
3. Plan for litigation from the start: request certified records (not just copies) from medical providers
4. Budget for expert expenses early: FCE ($500-600+), IME, vocational/economic reports
5. Track discovery deadlines meticulously — interrogatories, document production, deposition scheduling
6. Coordinate mediation scheduling between all parties (mediator, defense counsel, client) well in advance
7. When the attorney directs additional medical evaluation based on IME/FCE findings, prioritize scheduling

## Pitfalls

- Treating a PL case like an auto accident case (PL is more complex and litigation-heavy)
- Failing to request surveillance footage promptly — it may be overwritten
- Not requesting certified records for litigation (regular copies may be insufficient for court)
- Providing incorrect documents to defense counsel (e.g., wrong tax year) — verify all documents before production
- Not coordinating expert evaluations (FCE, IME) early enough — scheduling can take weeks/months
- Allowing mediation to be cancelled without promptly rescheduling
