# Robinson v. Bates Medical Specials Reduction

> In Kentucky PI cases, adjusters commonly cite Robinson v. Bates to reduce the valuation of medical specials. Under this legal principle, the insurer pays only what the "collateral source" (health insurance, Medicaid, PIP) actually paid for treatment, rather than the full billed amount. This reduces the firm's leverage when negotiating, as the adjuster values the case based on allowed/paid amounts rather than provider billing.

## Pattern

1. **Demand Sent**: Firm sends demand with full billed medical amounts
2. **Adjuster Applies Robinson v. Bates**: Adjuster reviews what PIP or health insurance actually paid (via ledger or EOBs) and values medical specials at the reduced amount
3. **Discrepancy Flagged**: Adjuster notes "medical specials were allowed per Robinson vs Bates and therefore allowed what the collateral source paid"
4. **Offer Based on Reduced Specials**: Initial offer reflects the reduced amount rather than the billed amount
5. **Negotiation Impact**: The firm must decide whether to counter based on billed amounts or accept the reduced valuation framework

### When Adjusters Apply Robinson v. Bates

- When PIP ledger shows payments lower than billed amounts
- When health insurance EOBs show contractual adjustments
- When Medicaid paid rates are significantly below provider billing
- Primarily by sophisticated adjusters at major carriers (USAA, Progressive, State Farm)

## Evidence

- In a KY MVA case (DOI 9/2021), the USAA adjuster explicitly stated: "The medical specials were allowed per Robinson vs Bates and therefore allowed what the collateral source paid." The adjuster requested only the one bill not already on file and made an offer based on the reduced specials amount. The firm countered at $5,000 and settled.
- In multiple negotiation cases from this batch, adjusters cross-referenced PIP payment data against demand records, with discrepancies between PIP-paid amounts and demand billing creating delays and lower initial offers.
- The Robinson v. Bates framework was observed in USAA claims and is expected to apply across all Kentucky carriers, as it is a state-level legal standard.

## Agent Guidance

1. When preparing demands, include both billed amounts AND paid amounts (PIP/health insurance) to preempt Robinson v. Bates reduction
2. Obtain the PIP ledger before demand preparation to understand the gap between billed and paid amounts
3. When the adjuster cites Robinson v. Bates, escalate to the attorney for a legal response strategy
4. Consider including non-economic damages arguments to offset the specials reduction
5. Track both "billed" and "allowed" amounts in the case file for settlement math

## Pitfalls

- Not anticipating the Robinson v. Bates reduction when calculating demand amounts
- Sending demands with only billed amounts when the adjuster will immediately reduce them
- Not having the PIP ledger available to verify the adjuster's Robinson v. Bates calculations
- Failing to argue that non-economic damages should not be reduced by the collateral source rule
