# Uninsured Motorist and PIP-Only Cases

> When the at-fault driver has no bodily injury (BI) liability insurance, the PI firm must evaluate alternative recovery paths: the client's own Uninsured Motorist (UM) coverage, the Kentucky Assigned Claims Plan (KAC) for PIP, or a direct suit against the at-fault driver. Cases with only PIP available (no BI, no UM) have severely limited recovery and may need to be declined. Understanding these coverage gaps early prevents wasted effort on unviable cases.

## Pattern

### Coverage Evaluation Hierarchy
When the at-fault driver is uninsured:
1. **Check client's UM/UIM coverage**: Does the client's own auto policy include Uninsured Motorist coverage?
2. **If UM exists**: File a UM claim against the client's own carrier — this becomes the primary claim
3. **If no UM**: Was a UM rejection signed? Request the rejection form from the carrier
4. **KAC (Kentucky Assigned Claims Plan)**: If the client has no auto insurance at all (e.g., was a passenger in an uninsured vehicle), PIP benefits may be available through KAC
5. **Direct suit**: The at-fault uninsured driver can be sued directly, but collectability is usually poor

### KAC Process
- KAC assigns a PIP adjuster (may be from Liberty Mutual, State Farm, or other carrier)
- A recorded statement (RS) is typically required from the client
- The RS must be scheduled between the PIP adjuster and the client
- KAC PIP covers medical bills but does NOT cover pain and suffering

### Cases with Multiple Uninsured Parties
When the accident involves multiple uninsured parties or an unknown driver (hit-and-run):
- Each party's coverage must be investigated independently
- The client may have been a passenger in an uninsured vehicle hit by another uninsured driver
- State Farm or other BI carriers may only be involved for named defendants with coverage

### Decision Point: Decline vs. Pursue
- **Decline if**: No BI, no UM, only PIP — firm cannot recover attorney fees from PIP-only cases
- **Pursue if**: UM coverage exists, even if BI is absent — UM claim can support the case
- **Pursue with caution if**: Direct suit against uninsured driver may be viable (substantial assets)

## Evidence

- In a case involving an uninsured driver (DOI 1/2021), the client was a passenger in an uninsured vehicle. PIP was obtained through KAC, assigned to a Liberty Mutual adjuster. A recorded statement was required and scheduled. State Farm was the BI carrier for the named defendant. The case proceeded to litigation with multiple defendants including the unknown driver, named defendant, and State Farm.
- In another auto accident case (DOI 3/2024), investigation revealed the at-fault driver had no BI or UM coverage — only PIP through KAC. The case manager flagged "No BI or UM only PIP through KAC" and the case was declined after attorney authorization.

## Agent Guidance

1. When the at-fault driver appears uninsured, immediately request: (a) client's own auto insurance declarations page showing UM/UIM status, (b) UM rejection form from the carrier if no UM
2. If the client was a passenger with no auto insurance, initiate KAC process for PIP
3. When KAC assigns a PIP adjuster, schedule the recorded statement promptly — delays in the RS delay PIP benefits
4. Flag "PIP-only" cases to the attorney within the first week of file setup
5. Do not invest significant resources (records requests, lien management) until BI or UM coverage is confirmed
6. For cases with UM coverage, treat the UM claim like a BI claim — demand, negotiation, and settlement follow the same process

## Pitfalls

- Failing to check UM/UIM coverage early — this is the most common coverage gap discovery
- Not requesting the UM rejection form from the carrier (may reveal that UM was not properly rejected)
- Investing months of work before discovering only PIP is available
- Confusing PIP recovery (medical bills only) with BI/UM recovery (includes pain and suffering)
- Not understanding that KAC assigns the PIP adjuster — it is not automatic
