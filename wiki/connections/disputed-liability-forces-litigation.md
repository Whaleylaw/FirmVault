# Disputed Liability Forces Early Litigation

> When the at-fault carrier disputes or limits liability acceptance (e.g., accepting only 25% fault), pre-suit resolution becomes unlikely regardless of medical treatment value. Disputed liability is a distinct litigation trigger separate from low settlement offers or SOL pressure — it forces the firm to file a complaint earlier in the case lifecycle to preserve the client's rights and establish liability through the judicial process.

## Pattern

The connection between disputed liability and litigation operates as follows:

1. **Liability Dispute Identified**: BI carrier communicates partial or denied liability during file setup or early negotiation
2. **Negotiation Stalls**: Without full liability acceptance, the carrier will not make a reasonable offer regardless of treatment value
3. **Litigation Decision**: Attorney determines that filing suit is necessary to establish liability through evidence (dash cam footage, police report, witness testimony)
4. **Early Filing**: Complaint filed earlier than typical (within months of DOI rather than after treatment completion and demand)
5. **Discovery Focus**: Litigation discovery focuses on liability evidence (video footage review, witness depositions) in addition to damages

## Evidence

- In Case I (MVA), BI carrier (State Farm) accepted only 25% liability, claiming their insured had already pulled out into the roadway and was rear-ended by the client. The firm filed a complaint in Jefferson Circuit Court approximately 8 months post-accident. Discovery included defense counsel reviewing dash cam video footage. Depositions of both the client and a companion witness were conducted to establish the accident facts.
- In Case H (MVA), the BI adjuster (Kentucky Farm Bureau) initially had "no liability determined" and needed to "dive back into it." The delayed liability determination combined with a complex accident scene (air evacuation required) led to complaint filing in Barren Circuit Court. The insurer also filed a parallel subrogation action, indicating the carrier was actively contesting fault.

## Agent Guidance

1. When a BI adjuster communicates partial liability acceptance, immediately flag for attorney review
2. Preserve all liability evidence (dash cam footage, photos, police report, witness statements) at intake
3. When liability is disputed, begin litigation preparation early (do not wait for treatment completion)
4. Track the SOL countdown more aggressively in disputed liability cases
5. Ensure complaint adequately addresses the liability facts, not just damages
