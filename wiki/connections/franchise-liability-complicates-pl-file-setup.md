# Franchise Liability Investigation Complicates PL File Setup

> When a premises liability incident occurs at a franchise location (restaurant chains, retail stores), identifying the correct defendant is significantly more complex than standard PL cases. The firm must trace liability from the local store manager to the franchise LLC to the corporate franchisor, often requiring certified mail to corporate offices and registered agents. This investigation can add weeks or months to file setup before any insurance claim can be opened.

## Pattern

The franchise liability investigation follows a chain:

1. **Local Contact**: Firm contacts the store location and speaks with a manager, who provides a phone number for their "boss" or "insurance person"
2. **Dead End**: The initial contact number often leads to voicemail with no callback
3. **Corporate LOR**: The firm sends a Letter of Representation via certified mail to the corporate franchisor's principal office
4. **Franchise Entity Discovery**: The corporate entity responds that the store is a franchise location owned by a separate LLC (e.g., "Golden Palatka LLC")
5. **Second LOR**: The firm must then send a new LOR to the franchise LLC's registered agent
6. **Insurance Identification**: Only after reaching the franchise LLC can the firm identify the correct insurance carrier

## Evidence

- In a restaurant chain PL case, the firm spent 5+ weeks tracing from the store manager to a corporate number (no callback) to certified mail to the corporate office to a franchise LLC to its registered agent before identifying the correct defendant
- The corporate entity explicitly responded that "Store Location is a Franchise Location - Owned by [LLC]" with a different registered agent address
- The firm had to send certified mail twice: once to the franchisor and once to the franchise LLC
- Defense counsel (from a specialized PL defense firm) was not engaged until after the franchise entity was properly identified

## Agent Guidance

1. For PL cases at chain restaurants or retail stores, immediately determine if the location is corporate-owned or a franchise
2. Do not rely on store-level contacts for insurance information — go directly to the corporate office via certified mail
3. Send LOR to both the franchisor (corporate) and, once identified, the franchise LLC
4. Document all certified mail tracking numbers for proof of service
5. Expect 4-8 weeks for the franchise entity identification process

## Pitfalls

- Relying on phone calls to store managers or corporate contacts wastes time — they often do not return calls
- The franchisor may not be the correct defendant — the franchise LLC is typically the responsible party
- Each step in the chain (store → corporate → franchise LLC → registered agent → insurance) adds delay
- Filing a complaint may require naming both the franchisor and franchise LLC until liability is sorted out
