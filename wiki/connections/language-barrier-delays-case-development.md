# Language Barriers Delay Case Development

> When a PI client does not speak English, the firm faces communication bottlenecks at every phase: intake information gathering, treatment verification, settlement discussions, and document execution. This barrier interacts with the companion case pattern and the co-counsel negotiation pattern, creating cascading delays.

## Pattern

Non-English-speaking clients create a distinct delay pattern:
1. **Intake**: Welcome call cannot be completed; case manager notes "client doesn't speak English" and defers to a companion or family member
2. **File Setup**: Companion case must be uploaded first so a bilingual contact can be designated as point of contact
3. **Treatment Verification**: Confirming treatment providers and obtaining information requires going through a proxy
4. **Negotiation**: When co-counsel from other firms are involved (multi-plaintiff or referral cases), language-matched attorneys may handle direct client communication while the primary firm manages the claim

## Evidence

- In an MVA case (negotiation phase), the client did not speak English. During the initial welcome call, the case manager noted the client "doesn't speak English, will wait for the companion's case to be uploaded and will make it as the point of contact." The police report was not yet available on BuyCrash either, compounding the delay. The case eventually involved co-counsel negotiation with two other attorneys (from separate firms), suggesting the language barrier may have contributed to the multi-firm structure. The case took approximately 18 months from DOI to settlement acceptance, with the attorney ultimately communicating settlement details to co-counsel via email: "[total] in meds. Closed head injury. No UIM."
