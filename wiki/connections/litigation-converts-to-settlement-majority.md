# Litigation Converts to Settlement in Majority of Cases

> Of 43 archived cases that involved litigation, 32 (74%) also showed settlement activity — confirming that filing suit is primarily a settlement mechanism, not a pathway to trial. Only 10 cases referenced trial at all. This reinforces the strategic principle that litigation should be viewed as an escalation of negotiation, not an alternative to it.

## Pattern

### Conversion Funnel
- **Total cases with litigation**: 43 (19% of 230 archived cases)
- **Litigation cases with settlement**: 32 (74% of litigated cases)
- **Litigation cases with discovery**: 23 (53% of litigated cases)
- **Cases referencing trial**: 10 (23% of litigated cases)

### Settlement Pathway Comparison
- **Pre-litigation settlement**: 68 cases — the most common path (53% of all settlements)
- **Post-litigation settlement**: 32 cases — still results in settlement (25% of all settlements)
- **Implied non-settlement litigation**: ~11 cases — declined during or after litigation without settlement

### Strategic Implication
Filing suit adds significant cost and complexity but converts to settlement at a 74% rate. The decision to litigate should be evaluated as an investment in settlement leverage, with the expectation that most litigated cases will still settle.

## Evidence

- In 230 archived cases, 43 involved litigation activity and 32 of those (74%) also showed settlement activity, confirming litigation as primarily a settlement mechanism.
- 23 litigation cases had discovery activity (interrogatories, depositions), and 10 referenced trial — suggesting that most litigation settlements occur during or after discovery but before trial.
- 68 cases settled through negotiation alone without litigation, representing the majority path.
- The 10 cases referencing trial represent the furthest progression of the litigation funnel — these are the true trial-track cases, representing only 4% of all archived cases.

## Agent Guidance

1. When recommending litigation to clients, frame it as a settlement strategy with a 74% historical settlement rate
2. Budget and timeline for litigation cases should assume settlement during discovery, not trial
3. The 23% of litigated cases that reference trial require different resource allocation (trial prep, witness coordination)
4. Track litigation-to-settlement conversion as a firm performance metric
