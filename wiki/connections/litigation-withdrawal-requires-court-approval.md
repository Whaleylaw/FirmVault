# Litigation Withdrawal Requires Court Approval Unlike Pre-Suit Closing

> When a firm decides to close a case during active litigation (as opposed to pre-suit), the closing process is fundamentally different: the firm must file a formal Motion to Withdraw with the court, notify opposing counsel, attend a court hearing (Motion Hour), and obtain a judicial Order Allowing Withdrawal. This adds weeks or months to the closing timeline compared to the simple closing-letter process used for pre-suit cases.

## Insight

In a premises liability case against a transit authority (KY, Kenton Circuit Court), the firm filed a Motion to Withdraw and proposed order on April 30, 2025. The motion was scheduled for Motion Hour on May 19, 2025 (19 days later). The Order Allowing Withdrawal was entered the same day. The firm simultaneously:
1. Sent the motion and proposed order to opposing counsel (defense firm)
2. Sent lien information (Optum ledger) to opposing counsel for direct payment
3. Called the client to discuss the withdrawal
4. Handled ongoing fax communications from the court

The total timeline from motion filing to order entry was 19 days — compared to same-day closing for pre-suit cases. Additionally, the firm had ongoing obligations even after withdrawal: forwarding lien payment information to opposing counsel so the client's health insurance lien could be satisfied.

## Agent Guidance

1. If the case is in active litigation, do NOT simply send a closing letter — a Motion to Withdraw must be filed
2. Draft the Motion to Withdraw and proposed Order, then have the attorney review and file
3. Notify all opposing counsel of the motion filing
4. Calendar the Motion Hour date and ensure someone from the firm appears
5. After the Order is entered, proceed with the standard closing workflow (closing letter, carrier notification, file transfer)
6. If lien obligations exist, forward lien information to opposing counsel or the client's new attorney
