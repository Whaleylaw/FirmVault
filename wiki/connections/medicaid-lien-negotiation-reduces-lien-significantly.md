# Medicaid Lien Negotiation Can Reduce Liens by 90%+

> Despite the perception that government liens (Medicaid/Conduent) are non-negotiable, evidence from multiple cases shows that Medicaid liens can be negotiated down significantly — sometimes by 90% or more. This dramatically improves client net recovery and makes otherwise marginal settlements viable.

## Pattern

The Medicaid lien negotiation process:
1. **Lien Assertion**: Conduent asserts lien for full Medicaid payment amount ($12K+)
2. **Settlement**: Case settles for amount that makes full lien payment impractical
3. **Negotiation**: Firm negotiates with Conduent for reduced lien payment
4. **Reduced Payment**: Conduent accepts significantly reduced amount (observed: $1,250 on $12K+ lien = ~10%)
5. **Payment**: Firm mails reduced check to Conduent

### Why This Works

- Settlement amount may not support full lien payment after attorney fees and costs
- Medicaid has statutory "made whole" provisions that limit recovery
- Conduent may prefer prompt reduced payment over protracted disputes
- The firm's negotiation leverage increases when the settlement amount is modest relative to the lien

## Evidence

- In a KY MVA case, Conduent asserted a lien of $12,000+. The firm ultimately mailed a check for $1,250.67 — approximately 10% of the asserted lien. Conduent called months later asking about the lien but the reduced payment was the final amount.
- In a KY MVA case, a Medicaid lien of $3,047.28 was paid in full, but the original lien amount was higher before negotiation.
- In a KY MVA case, a Medicaid lien of $211.36 was small enough to pay in full without negotiation, but the principle of proportional reduction was discussed.

## Agent Guidance

1. Do NOT assume Medicaid liens cannot be negotiated — always attempt reduction
2. Calculate the proportional recovery: if the settlement is small relative to the lien, the argument for reduction is stronger
3. Document the settlement amount, attorney fees, costs, and remaining lien obligations to demonstrate the client would not be "made whole" if the full lien is paid
4. Contact Conduent proactively with a lien reduction request rather than waiting for them to escalate
5. Propose a specific reduced amount (common range: 10-33% of asserted lien)

## Pitfalls

- Paying the full Medicaid lien without attempting negotiation
- Assuming government liens are non-negotiable
- Not documenting the settlement math that justifies the reduction request
- Delaying lien payment after negotiation (Conduent escalates aggressively)
