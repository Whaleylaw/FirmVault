# Premature Closing Forces Retraction to Carriers

> When a case is moved to Closing and decline letters are sent to the client and insurance carriers without the attorney first speaking to the client, the firm may need to reverse the decision and send embarrassing retraction letters. This connection links the case-decline-and-closing-workflow to attorney-review-triggers — confirming that case decline is an irreversible-seeming action that requires attorney authorization before execution.

## Pattern

1. Staff or managing paralegal identifies a case for decline (e.g., liability concerns, coverage gaps)
2. Case is moved to Closing phase and decline letters are sent to client and insurance carriers
3. Attorney reviews the case and disagrees — "Helps if I talk to the client first. Change of plans."
4. Firm must send retraction letters to all carriers: "Please disregard the previous email with the attached declined rep notice."
5. Case is reopened (Closing -> File Setup or Treatment)
6. Staff must re-engage with carriers who now have conflicting correspondence on file

## Evidence

- In an MVA case (DOI 3/2023, KY), the paralegal moved the case to Closing and sent decline representation notices to the client and both insurance carriers via email and fax. Twenty minutes later, the attorney reversed the decision after speaking with the client (who disputed the police report's account of the accident). The firm sent retraction notices to both carriers: "Please disregard the previous email with the attached declined rep notice. We are still representing our client for this motor vehicle accident." The case was reopened from Closing back to File Setup and later progressed through treatment and demand. The case ultimately received a BI denial on liability, confirming the attorney's initial concern was well-founded but the process error was in closing without client discussion.
- In a separate case, a decline letter was sent to the client's new address after the client had already retained another attorney, creating confusion about whether the firm was unilaterally closing or the client had terminated.

## Agent Guidance

1. NEVER move a case to Closing or send decline letters without explicit attorney authorization
2. The attorney must speak with the client before any decline decision — even when the case appears unviable
3. If a decline is reversed after letters have been sent, immediately send retraction letters to ALL recipients
4. Log the retraction in the activity log with a note about why the decline was reversed
5. After retraction, set the case phase back to the appropriate pre-decline status
