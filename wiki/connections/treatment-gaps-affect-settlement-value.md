# Treatment Gaps Affect Settlement Value

> This connection documents how [[treatment-gap-defense]] interacts with [[client-expectations-management]] and [[pre-settlement-funding-obligations]] to create cases where the client receives minimal net recovery despite having a valid claim.

## Connection

Two concepts converge to create low-value outcomes:

1. **Treatment Gap Defense** ([[treatment-gap-defense]]): The adjuster uses the gap in treatment to justify a lower offer. The client may have stopped treatment due to practical reasons (incarceration, work/parenting constraints) rather than lack of injury.

2. **Client Declines Treatment** ([[client-declines-recommended-treatment]]): When a doctor recommends follow-up care (chiropractic, PT) but the client does not follow through, it creates the very treatment gap the adjuster will exploit.

The interaction creates a vicious cycle: the client's circumstances prevent treatment, the lack of treatment lowers the case value, and the lower case value means less recovery for a client who may need it most.

## Evidence

- In Case A, the client had only one treatment provider and a significant gap. The adjuster cited the gap to justify the offer. After subtracting pre-settlement funding ($6,537) and reduced medical bills ($5,397), the client would net less than $3K from an $18K offer.
- In Case B, the client's doctor recommended chiropractic care, but the client could not coordinate treatment around work and parenting. The spouse acknowledged this was "an area of risk."

## Insight

Early intervention on treatment gaps could significantly improve case value. When a client indicates they may stop treatment, the firm should:
1. Document the practical barriers to treatment
2. Counsel the client on the impact of stopping treatment
3. Help find practical solutions (providers with flexible hours, telehealth options)
4. If treatment truly cannot continue, document the reasons thoroughly for the demand narrative

## Agent Guidance

1. Monitor treatment continuity — flag any gap exceeding 14 days
2. When a client indicates they want to stop treatment, escalate to the attorney
3. In the demand letter, proactively explain any treatment gap with supporting documentation
4. Track the treatment gap + financial obligations equation to predict net client recovery early
