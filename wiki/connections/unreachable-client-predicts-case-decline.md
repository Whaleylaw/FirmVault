# Unreachable Client Pattern Predicts Case Decline

> In 230 archived cases, 64 cases showed both unreachable client patterns AND decline/closing activity — representing 55% of all declined cases and 57% of all unreachable cases. This strong correlation suggests that persistent client unreachability is the single strongest predictor of case decline. Detecting unreachability early and intervening aggressively could either save the case or trigger earlier decline, freeing resources.

## Pattern

The connection operates in both directions:

1. **Unreachable → Decline**: Client cannot be reached for intake completion, treatment coordination, or case updates → case stalls → eventually declined
2. **Decline → Unreachable**: Case has viability issues (no coverage) → firm deprioritizes outreach → client appears unreachable

### Statistical Evidence
- 113 of 230 cases (49%) showed unreachable client patterns
- 117 of 230 cases (51%) showed decline activity
- **64 cases showed BOTH** (55% of declines, 57% of unreachable cases)
- The overlap is the highest of any two-pattern combination in the dataset

### Compared to Other Decline Predictors
- No-coverage + decline overlap: 23 cases (20% of declines)
- Client frustration + decline: lower correlation
- **Unreachable + decline: 64 cases (55% of declines)** — nearly 3x the no-coverage predictor

## Evidence

- In 230 archived cases with complete lifecycles, 64 showed both unreachable client patterns and case decline — a 55% overlap rate with declined cases, making unreachability the strongest single predictor of decline.
- 112 cases involved voicemail patterns (VM left, VM full, no answer), and the majority of these overlapped with unreachable patterns.
- 60 cases showed text/SMS communication attempts, confirming that text is the standard fallback channel when phone contact fails.
- The pattern suggests a detection window: if a client becomes unreachable within the first 30-60 days and does not respond to multi-channel outreach (phone, text, mail), the case has a >50% probability of decline.

## Agent Guidance

1. Track unreachable client signals from day one: VM full, no answer after 3+ attempts, phone not in service
2. After 2 weeks of failed contact, escalate to multi-channel: phone + text + email + physical mail
3. After 30 days of no contact, flag for attorney review as a potential decline
4. Do not continue investing significant resources (records requests, provider outreach) on a case with an unreachable client until contact is re-established
5. Use this pattern to prioritize early outreach — cases that establish contact within the first week are significantly less likely to be declined
