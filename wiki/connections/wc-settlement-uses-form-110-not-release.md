# WC Settlement Uses Form 110 Instead of Standard Release

> Workers' compensation case settlements follow a fundamentally different mechanism than PI case settlements. Instead of the standard BI release → check → disbursement workflow, WC settlements require execution of a Form 110 (WC settlement agreement), submission to the Department of Workers' Claims, and a separate Motion for Attorney Fee Approval by the DWC before fees can be collected. This creates a post-settlement administrative phase absent from PI cases.

## Pattern

WC settlement follows this distinct sequence:

1. **Negotiation**: Attorney negotiates settlement amount with WC carrier/defense counsel
2. **Agreement**: Settlement amount agreed (e.g., $12,000)
3. **Form 110 Execution**: Client signs WC Form 110 (settlement agreement form prescribed by KY DWC)
4. **Form 110 Submission**: Executed Form 110 sent to defense counsel for filing with DWC
5. **Motion for Attorney Fee**: Firm files a motion for attorney fee approval with DWC
6. **DWC Approval**: DWC approves the attorney fee (e.g., $2,400 on a $12,000 settlement = 20%)
7. **Distribution**: Remaining funds distributed to client

This is distinct from PI settlements where the attorney fee is deducted from the settlement check before client distribution without court/agency approval.

## Evidence

- In a WC/MVA hybrid case (DOI 2/2024), the case settled at $12,000 with the note: "Waiting on Form 110 from defense"
- The attorney fee was $2,400 (20% of settlement) with $9,600 to client
- A separate "Motion for Attorney Fee Approved" entry appeared weeks after the settlement agreement
- The Form 110 was executed and returned to defense counsel before the motion could be filed
- The WC carrier (ClearPath Mutual) had been paying for chiropractic treatment during the case

## Agent Guidance

1. When a WC settlement is reached, request Form 110 from defense counsel immediately
2. Have the client execute Form 110 promptly — delays here extend the entire process
3. File the Motion for Attorney Fee with DWC concurrently with Form 110 submission
4. Track DWC approval separately — do not distribute funds until fee approval is received
5. Note that WC attorney fees (typically 20%) are different from PI contingency fees (typically 33%)

## Pitfalls

- Do not use standard PI release documents for WC settlements — Form 110 is required
- DWC fee approval can take weeks — factor this into client timeline expectations
- The 20% WC attorney fee rate is lower than PI rates — ensure settlement math reflects this
- WC settlements may not include future medical expenses unless specifically negotiated (the attorney negotiated "keep future medical expenses open" as an alternative to higher lump sum)
